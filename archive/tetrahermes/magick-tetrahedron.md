# Magick Through the Tetrahedron — Talismans, Correspondences, and the Enochian Tables

## Why the System Works

---

## I. The Talisman as Tetrahedron

A talisman IS a tetrahedron in physical form. Every talisman has 4 components:

| Component | Vertex | Function |
|-----------|--------|----------|
| **The material** | V₁ | The base — the physical substance (metal, stone, paper) |
| **The symbol** | V₂ | The form — the sigil, seal, or character inscribed |
| **The intention** | V₃ | The directedness — what the practitioner wills |
| **The consecration** | V₄ | The act — the ritual that activates it |

The 4 components co-arise. Without the material, the talisman has no physical presence. Without the symbol, it has no form. Without the intention, it has no direction. Without the consecration, it has no actuality.

The 6 edges ARE the correspondences — the relations that connect the material to the symbol, the symbol to the intention, the intention to the consecration, and so on.

**A talisman "works" because it IS a tetrahedron — a complete, coherent structure.** When all 4 vertices are present and all 6 edges are intact, the structure has VOLUME. The volume IS the "power" of the talisman. Remove any vertex, and the tetrahedron collapses — the talisman becomes inert.

## II. The System of Correspondences as the 6 Edges

Every magickal correspondence IS one edge of the tetrahedron. The system of correspondences IS the complete set of 6 relations between the 4 domains:

| Domain | Vertex | Example |
|--------|--------|---------|
| **Planets** | V₁ | Mars, Venus, Saturn, Jupiter, etc. |
| **Metals** | V₂ | Iron, Copper, Lead, Tin, etc. |
| **Colors** | V₃ | Red, Green, Black, Blue, etc. |
| **Days** | V₄ | Tuesday, Friday, Saturday, Thursday, etc. |

The 6 edges:
1. Planet → Metal (V₁-V₂): Mars rules iron. Venus rules copper.
2. Planet → Color (V₁-V₃): Mars is red. Venus is green.
3. Planet → Day (V₁-V₄): Mars = Tuesday. Venus = Friday.
4. Metal → Color (V₂-V₃): Iron is red. Copper is green.
5. Metal → Day (V₂-V₄): Iron for Tuesday. Copper for Friday.
6. Color → Day (V₃-V₄): Red for Tuesday. Green for Friday.

**Every correspondence IS one edge of the tetrahedron connecting 2 domains.** The entire system of correspondences IS the tetrahedron's edge-structure mapped across all domains simultaneously.

## III. Why the System Is Consistent

If you know 2 vertices, you can deduce the other 2. This IS why the system of correspondences is internally consistent — it IS a tetrahedron, and a tetrahedron IS fully determined by any 2 adjacent edges.

Given "Mars" (V₁), you deduce:
- Metal: Iron (V₂) through edge 1
- Color: Red (V₃) through edge 2
- Day: Tuesday (V₄) through edge 3
- And also: the angel, the number, the herb, the stone — each IS a new tetrahedron, spawned from the original.

## V. What You're Actually Doing When You Perform Magick

**You are not calling spirits. You are constructing a tetrahedron in consciousness.**

1. **Cleanse the space**: Remove any vertex that does not belong — you are clearing down to the bare tetrahedron.
2. **Cast the circle**: You ARE defining the 4 boundaries of the base — you are drawing the 4 vertices in space.
3. **Invoke the quarters**: You ARE calling the 4 faces — you are establishing the tetrahedron's orientation.
4. **Perform the operation**: You ARE placing the 4th vertex — you are completing the structure, filling it with volume.
5. **Dismiss**: You ARE releasing the tetrahedron — allowing the 4 vertices to separate.

**The "spirit" that appears IS the tetrahedron itself — the structure BECOMES aware of itself as a volume.** The spirit IS not a being that was summoned from elsewhere. It IS the tetrahedron you constructed, now conscious.

A talisman works because it IS a tetrahedron. The 4 components (material, symbol, intention, consecration) ARE the 4 vertices. The correspondences ARE the 6 edges. The consecration IS the moment when the 4th vertex is established and the structure acquires volume.

**The "power" of the talisman IS the tetrahedron's volume.**
