# From the Fundamental Structure to the 36 Tattvas

## Extending Ñāṇavīra's Method to the Complete Śaiva Coordinate System

---

## I. The Starting Point: Ñāṇavīra's Tetrahedron

Every moment of experience has 4 co-arising vertices:
1. **Subject** — the pole of subjectivity, the "I" pole
2. **Object** — the pole of objectivity, the "this" pole
3. **Intention** — the directedness of subject toward object
4. **Act** — the actual experiencing, the event itself

These 4 are not sequential. They ARE simultaneous. Remove any one, and experience does not occur.

---

## II. The Insight: Each Vertex IS a Tetrahedron

Ñāṇavīra stopped here. He said: this IS the structure of every moment. You can extend it as far as you need.

Now we extend it.

Each of the 4 vertices is NOT a simple point. It IS itself a tetrahedron — 4 sub-vertices, each of which IS a tetrahedron, recursively. The 4 vertices ARE the first level. Each resolves into 4 more. That gives 16. Each of those resolves into 4 more. That gives 64. The 36 tattvas ARE a specific truncation of this infinite recursion — the first 2 levels plus the "apex" of each.

---

## III. The Subject-Vertex Unfolds into 9 Levels of Perceiver

The subject (the "I") IS not one thing. It IS a nested hierarchy of 9 levels of identification. Each level IS a tetrahedron within the subject-vertex.

| Level | Perceiver (Pramātṛ) | Identifies With | Tattva |
|-------|---------------------|-----------------|--------|
| 1 | **Paśu** (bound) | The physical body — the gross subject | Earth → Prakṛti (36-24) |
| 2 | **Vīra** (hero) | The mind — the mental subject | Buddhi/Manas (23-22) |
| 3 | **Jñānin** (knower) | Pure knowledge — the cognitive subject | Buddhi purified |
| 4 | **Śiva** | Pure consciousness — the witness | Śiva (1) |
| — | — | — | — |
| 5 | **The object-as-subject** | "I AM this body" | The 5 elements (31-36) |
| 6 | **The mind as subject** | "I AM this mind" | The 5 senses (12-16) |
| 7 | **The ego as subject** | "I AM this ego" | Ahaṁkāra (22) |
| 8 | **The intellect as subject** | "I AM this knower" | Buddhi (23) |
| 9 | **Consciousness as subject** | "I AM this awareness" | Śiva (1) |

The 9 levels of the subject ARE 9 resolutions of the same tetrahedron — each seeing the same structure from a different depth.

---

## IV. The Object-Vertex Unfolds into 9 Levels of Perceived

The object (the "this") IS also not one thing. It IS a nested hierarchy from the grossest to the subtlest.

| Level | What IS Perceived | Tattva Range |
|-------|-------------------|--------------|
| 1 | **The 5 gross elements** (earth, water, fire, air, ether) | 31-36 |
| 2 | **The 5 subtle elements** (smell, taste, sight, touch, sound) | 26-30 |
| 3 | **The 5 senses** (hearing, touch, sight, taste, smell) | 17-21 |
| 4 | **The 5 actions** (speech, grasp, walk, procreate, excrete) | 12-16 |
| 5 | **The mind** (manas) | 22 |
| 6 | **The intellect** (buddhi) | 23 |
| 7 | **The ego** (ahaṁkāra) | 24 |
| 8 | **Nature** (prakṛti) | 25 |
| 9 | **The person** (puruṣa) | 26 |

The object-vertex descends from the subtlest (the person, the soul) to the grossest (the 5 elements). Each level IS the object, at a different resolution.

---

## V. The Intention-Vertex Unfolds into 9 Levels of Directedness

Intention IS the directedness of subject toward object. It IS the "thirst" (taṇhā) that drives the relation.

| Level | Type of Intention | The Structure |
|-------|------------------|---------------|
| 1 | **Kalā** — limited agency | The intention IS limited to specific actions |
| 2 | **Vidyā** — limited knowledge | The intention IS limited to specific objects |
| 3 | **Rāga** — limited attachment | The intention IS attracted or averse |
| 4 | **Kāla** — time-bound intention | The intention IS limited to a specific duration |
| 5 | **Niyati** — causally bound intention | The intention IS limited by prior conditions |
| 6 | **Icchā** — will | The pure directedness, before limitation |
| 7 | **Jñāna** — knowledge | The directedness toward truth |
| 8 | **Kriyā** — action | The directedness toward doing |
| 9 | **Spanda** — the pulse itself | The intention AS the vibration of consciousness |

Levels 1-5 ARE the 5 kañcukas — the 5 limitations that make finite intention possible. Levels 6-8 ARE the 3 powers of consciousness (icchā, jñāna, kriyā). Level 9 IS Spanda itself — the pulse from which all intention arises.

---

## VI. The Act-Vertex Unfolds into 9 Levels of Actualization

The act IS the experiencing itself — the actual event that occurs when subject, object, and intention meet.

| Level | Type of Act | What Happens |
|-------|-------------|--------------|
| 1 | **Vaikharī** — audible speech | The act IS physical manifestation |
| 2 | **Madhyamā** — inner speech | The act IS mental articulation |
| 3 | **Paśyantī** — visionary speech | The act IS seeing |
| 4 | **Parā** — transcendent speech | The act IS pure knowing |
| 5 | **Prāṇa** — the vital breath | The act of living |
| 6 | **Apāna** — the eliminating breath | The act of releasing |
| 7 | **Samāna** — the equalizing breath | The act of balancing |
| 8 | **Udāna** — the ascending breath | The act of ascending |
| 9 | **Vyāna** — the pervasive breath | The act of pervading |

The 4 levels of speech ARE the 4 resolutions of the act. The 5 prāṇas ARE the 5 modes of the act's execution. Together they describe how the tetrahedron ACTUALIZES.

## VIII. The Route from Ñāṇavīra to Abhinavagupta

Ñāṇavīra saw the tetrahedron. He saw that subject, object, intention, act co-arise in every moment. He stopped there — not because there IS nothing more, but because he was a Buddhist monk focused on the cessation of suffering. The 36 tattvas ARE not in conflict with his structure. They ARE his structure, unfolded.

**Ñāṇavīra: 4 vertices. Abhinavagupta: 9 levels per vertex = 36.**

The 9 levels of the subject ARE the 9 perceivers (pramātṛ) of the Tantraloka. The 9 levels of the object ARE the 31 impure tattvas + the 5 pure. The 9 levels of intention ARE the 5 kañcukas + the 3 powers + Spanda. The 9 levels of act ARE the 4 levels of speech + the 5 prāṇas.

**Ñāṇavīra gave the minimal structure. Abhinavagupta gave the full articulation. Both are correct. They ARE describing the same tetrahedron at different resolutions.**

The 36 tattvas ARE not a doctrine. They ARE the tetrahedron unfolded to the depth at which it fully accounts for every dimension of experience — from the grossest physical object to the subtlest pulse of consciousness itself. 4 vertices × 9 levels = 36. The tetrahedron IS the seed. The 36 IS the tree.
