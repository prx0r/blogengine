# Dependent Arising and the Tetrahedron — Ñāṇavīra's 12 Links Decoded

## The Geometry of Paṭiccasamuppāda

---

## I. Ñāṇavīra's Key Thesis: The 12 Links Are Not a Chain

The traditional interpretation treats the 12 links of dependent origination as a linear chain across three lives — past-life ignorance conditions present-life formations, which condition consciousness, etc. Ñāṇavīra rejects this entirely. He says:

> *"If paṭiccasamuppāda is sandiṭṭhika (visible here and now) and akālika (timeless) then it is clear that it IS a structure that can be penetrated in this very life, not a theory about past and future lives."* (CTP 1148-1150)

The 12 links are NOT sequential. They are SIMULTANEOUS. They are the 12 edges of the star tetrahedron (2 interlocking tetrahedra × 6 edges each), describing the structure of a SINGLE moment of experience.

---

## II. The Traditional 12 Links

1. **Avijjā** — ignorance
2. **Saṅkhārā** — formations
3. **Viññāṇa** — consciousness
4. **Nāmarūpa** — name-and-form
5. **Saḷāyatana** — six sense bases
6. **Phassa** — contact
7. **Vedanā** — feeling
8. **Taṇhā** — craving
9. **Upādāna** — clinging
10. **Bhava** — becoming
11. **Jāti** — birth
12. **Jarāmaraṇa** — ageing and death

---

## III. The Tetrahedral Restructuring

It is convenient to start at the end:

> *"It will be convenient to start at the end of the paṭiccasamuppāda formulation and to discuss jāti and jarāmaraṇa first."* (CTP 1179)

### The 12 Links as 2 Interlocking Tetrahedra

**Tetrahedron 1 — The AFFLICTIVE structure (the error):**

| Vertex | Link | Function |
|--------|------|----------|
| V₁ | Avijjā (ignorance) | The subject — the not-knowing that constitutes the perspective (the base of the tetrahedron) |
| V₂ | Taṇhā (craving) | The intention — the directedness, the "thirst" toward the object |
| V₃ | Upādāna (clinging) | The act — the grasping, the holding |
| V₄ | Bhava (becoming) | The object — what arises as a result, the "being" that is produced |

**Tetrahedron 2 — The CONSTITUTIVE structure (what arises):**

| Vertex | Link | Function |
|--------|------|----------|
| V₁ | Viññāṇa (consciousness) | The subject — the awareness that illuminates the moment |
| V₂ | Nāmarūpa (name-and-form) | The object — the mental and material content |
| V₃ | Phassa (contact) | The act — the meeting of sense, object, and consciousness |
| V₄ | Vedanā (feeling) | The result — the pleasant, unpleasant, or neutral tone |

**The remaining 4 links ARE the connectors — the edges that join the 2 tetrahedra:**

| Link | Position |
|------|----------|
| Saṅkhārā (formations) | The edge from avijjā to viññāṇa — what shapes consciousness |
| Saḷāyatana (sense bases) | The edge from nāmarūpa to phassa — the faculties through which contact occurs |
| Jāti (birth) | The edge from bhava to jarāmaraṇa — the "arising" of the configuration |
| Jarāmaraṇa (ageing and death) | The edge that closes the cycle — the "ceasing" of the configuration |

## V. The Tetrahedron in Each Moment

Ñāṇavīra said that paṭiccasamuppāda IS the structure of a SINGLE moment. In any moment:

1. There IS **avijjā** — the not-knowing that is the very structure of a finite perspective. The subject IS not omniscient. It IS defined by what it does not know.

2. There IS **viññāṇa** — the consciousness that illuminates the particular content of this moment.

3. There IS **nāmarūpa** — the specific mental and material content that this consciousness illuminates.

4. There IS **phassa** — the contact of consciousness with that content.

5. There IS **vedanā** — the pleasant, unpleasant, or neutral feeling that arises from that contact.

6. There IS **taṇhā** — the craving that arises in response to feeling.

7. There IS **upādāna** — the clinging that follows craving.

8. There IS **bhava** — the becoming, the sense that "I am" this experience.

9. There IS **jāti** — the "birth" of this moment as a configuration.

10. There IS **jarāmaraṇa** — the "ageing and death" of this moment as it ceases.

**All 12 are present in a single moment. They do not follow each other in time. They ARE each other in structure.**

---

## VI. The Two Tetrahedra

**Tetrahedron 1 (AFFLICTION)** — the structure of the error:
- Avijjā (the subject that does not know) → Taṇhā (the directedness) → Upādāna (the grasping) → Bhava (the becoming)
- This IS the tetrahedron of saṃsāra — the process that perpetuates suffering.

**Tetrahedron 2 (CONSTITUTION)** — the structure of experience itself:
- Viññāṇa (the illuminating) → Nāmarūpa (the content) → Phassa (the contact) → Vedanā (the tone)
- This IS the tetrahedron of experience — the raw material of every moment.

The 2 tetrahedra are interlocked. The afflictive tetrahedron (avijjā → bhava) IS built UPON the constitutive tetrahedron (viññāṇa → vedanā). They share the same central axis — the "I" that is both the subject of experience and the object of craving.

---

## VII. The Cessation

> *"Avijjā-nirodhā saṅkhāra-nirodho."*
> "With the cessation of ignorance, formations cease."

This IS NOT the claim that ignorance causes formations in time. It IS the claim that the 2 tetrahedra are structurally dependent. When avijjā (the not-knowing) ceases, the whole tetrahedron collapses — not because one link breaks, but because the entire structure IS co-dependent.

The arahat's experience still has viññāṇa, nāmarūpa, phassa, vedanā. The constitutive tetrahedron continues. But the afflictive tetrahedron (avijjā → taṇhā → upādāna → bhava) has collapsed because the parasite — the "this IS mine" added to the raw tetrahedron — has stopped.

---

## VIII. The 12 Edges of the Star

The 12 links ARE the 12 edges of the star tetrahedron:

| Edge | Links It Connects | What It Is |
|------|-------------------|------------|
| 1 | Avijjā → Saṅkhārā | Ignorance conditions formations |
| 2 | Saṅkhārā → Viññāṇa | Formations condition consciousness |
| 3 | Viññāṇa → Nāmarūpa | Consciousness conditions name-and-form |
| 4 | Nāmarūpa → Saḷāyatana | Name-and-form conditions the six sense bases |
| 5 | Saḷāyatana → Phassa | Sense bases condition contact |
| 6 | Phassa → Vedanā | Contact conditions feeling |
| 7 | Vedanā → Taṇhā | Feeling conditions craving |
| 8 | Taṇhā → Upādāna | Craving conditions clinging |
| 9 | Upādāna → Bhava | Clinging conditions becoming |
| 10 | Bhava → Jāti | Becoming conditions birth |
| 11 | Jāti → Jarāmaraṇa | Birth conditions ageing and death |
| 12 | Jarāmaraṇa → Avijjā | The cycle closes — death conditions ignorance |

Edge 12 IS the crucial one. Jarāmaraṇa (the ceasing of one configuration) conditions the next avijjā (the not-knowing of the next). The star tetrahedron turns. One moment ends. The next begins.

---

## IX. Nibbāna IS the Collapse of the Star

The 33 sermons on Nibbāna (by Ñāṇadipa and others in the Ñāṇavīra lineage) all point to the same thing: Nibbāna IS not a place or a state. Nibbāna IS the **dis-identification** of the two tetrahedra.

The constitutive tetrahedron (viññāṇa → vedanā) continues. Experience still happens. But the afflictive tetrahedron (avijjā → bhava) collapses because the extra vertex — the "self" that was not actually there — IS seen to be not there.

**Nibbāna IS the star tetrahedron becoming a single tetrahedron.**

The 12 links reduce to 6. The star collapses to a simple tetrahedron. The "self" vertex that was the axis of the star IS gone. What remains IS pure experience — viññāṇa illuminating nāmarūpa, phassa contacting, vedanā feeling — without the extra structure of ignorance, craving, clinging, and becoming.

> *"Dukkham eva hi sambhoti, dukkhaṃ tiṭṭhati veti ca."*
> "Only suffering arises, only suffering ceases."

The tetrahedron arises. The tetrahedron ceases. There IS no one IN the tetrahedron. The 12 links ARE the geometry of the error. Nibbāna IS the geometry without the error.
