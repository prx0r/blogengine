## The Relativistic Light Cone

In special relativity, every observer has a light cone — the region of space-time they can causally affect. It has 4 components:
1. **Past light cone** — everything that can have affected the observer
2. **Future light cone** — everything the observer can affect
3. **Timelike boundary** — the limit of causality
4. **The observer's world-line** — the center of the cone

The light cone IS a tetrahedron when you include the observer as the fourth vertex. Three dimensions of space (past, future, boundary) + the observer = 4 vertices.
