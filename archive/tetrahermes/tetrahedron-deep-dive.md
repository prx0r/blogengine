## I. The Tetrahedron in Quantum Physics

## II. Friston's Free Energy Principle and the Markov Blanket Tetrahedron

### The Markov Blanket

A Markov blanket is the boundary that separates a system from its environment. It consists of exactly 4 sets of states:
- **Internal states** (the system itself)
- **External states** (the environment)
- **Sensory states** (the interface from external to internal — what the system can sense)
- **Active states** (the interface from internal to external — what the system can do)

The Markov blanket IS a tetrahedron. The 4 vertices are internal, external, sensory, active. The 6 edges are the conditional dependencies between them:
- External → Sensory (the environment affects what the system senses)
- Sensory → Internal (the senses affect the internal state)
- Internal → Active (the internal state affects what the system does)
- Active → External (the actions affect the environment)
- Internal ↔ External (mediated through the blanket — never direct)

**The Markov blanket is Ñāṇavīra's tetrahedron in information-theoretic language.**

| Ñāṇavīra | Friston | Function |
|----------|---------|----------|
| Subject | Internal states | The one to whom experience appears |
| Object | External states | What appears — the environment |
| Intention | Active states | The directedness — what the system DOES |
| Act | Sensory states | The experiencing — what the system SENSES |

The free energy principle says: the system minimizes variational free energy by updating its internal states (perception) and acting on the environment (action). This IS the tetrahedron in motion — the 4 vertices adjusting their relations to minimize prediction error.

### Why It's Always 4

A Markov blanket CANNOT have fewer than 4 sets of states. Remove internal states and there IS no system. Remove external states and there IS no environment. Remove sensory states and the system cannot perceive. Remove active states and the system cannot act. Everything with a boundary has exactly these 4 components.

**The tetrahedron IS the minimal structure of any bounded system.** From a single cell to a human to a society — all have Markov blankets. All are tetrahedra.
