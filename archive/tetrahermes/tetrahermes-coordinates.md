## 6. The Ñāṇavīra Foundation

Ñāṇavīra Thera (1963) identified that every moment of experience has exactly 4 irreducible terms connected by 6 relations. He called this K₄ — the complete graph on 4 vertices.

The terms (pada):

| Term | What it is | In teaching: Teacher's experience | In teaching: Student's experience |
|------|-----------|----------------------------------|----------------------------------|
| **Subject** (V₁) | The conscious "I" | The teacher as guide | The student as seeker |
| **Object** (V₂) | What is experienced | The student + the content | The teacher + the content |
| **Intention** (V₃) | The orientation | Pedagogical intent (to help, guide, cut) | Learning intent (to understand, resist, comply) |
| **Act** (V₄) | The engagement | Teaching act (speak, question, silence) | Learning act (respond, question, resist) |

The 6 relations (edges):

| Edge | Relation | Content |
|------|----------|---------|
| S-O | Subject→Object | Intentional awareness — the subject attends to the object |
| S-I | Subject→Intention | Motivation — the subject's reason for engaging |
| S-A | Subject→Act | Agency — the subject enacts |
| O-I | Object→Intention | Elicitation — the object calls forth intention |
| O-A | Object→Act | Directedness — the act is toward the object |
| I-A | Intention→Act | Volition — intention is realized in action |

The crucial point: these 4 terms and 6 relations are not a model of experience. They are the structure of experience itself. Every moment, from the most mundane (typing a sentence) to the most profound (nondual realization), has exactly this structure. No more, no less.

**The HXRMXS mapping is exact:**

| K₄ Term | HXRMXS Phase | What the phase does |
|---------|-------------|-------------------|
| Subject | UNMAKING | Examines, dismantles, or clarifies the "who" |
| Object | REMAKING | Constructs, distinguishes, or delivers the "what" |
| Intention | META | Governs, explains, or questions the "why" |
| Act | SELF-MAKING | Returns agency, demands commitment, the "how" |

| K₄ Edge | Register | What it modulates |
|---------|----------|-------------------|
| S-O | Intensity | How hard Subject is pushed toward Object |
| S-I | Intimacy | Relational distance between teacher (S) and learning intent (I) |
| S-A | Attunement | How sensitive teacher (S) is to the student's engagement (A) |
| O-I | Style | The rhetorical form of the content (O) in relation to purpose (I) |
| O-A | Depth | Conceptual vs somatic — the level of Object addressed |
| I-A | Meta Mode | Whether the Intention is hidden or explicit |

This is not "like" a tetrahedron. It IS the tetrahedron. The four phases ARE the four terms of experience. The six registers ARE the six relations between them. The mapping is exact because the structure of teaching IS the structure of experience, and the structure of experience IS K₄.

## 7. The Tantraloka Expansion: 36 Tattvas as Coordinate System

The Tantraloka gives us 36 tattvas — a coordinate system for locating ANY student state within the K₄ structure. Each tattva is a specific configuration of the four terms.

### Level 0: The 4 Upāyas (the vertices themselves)

These are the ways Subject relates to Object — the 4 pedagogical modes:

| Tattva | Name | Upāya | What it is | UNO Equivalent |
|--------|------|-------|-----------|----------------|
| 1 | Śiva | Anupāya | No-means. The subject IS the object. Direct recognition. | SM_01 (direct_seeing), clarity state |
| 2 | Śakti | Śāmbhavopāya | Will-means. The subject's intention collapses the object. Will alone. | SM_02 (witness_pivot), dawning_awareness |
| 3 | Sadāśiva | Śāktopāya | Mind-means. The subject engages the object through thought, analysis, distinction. | RM_03 (conceptual_distinction), genuine_curiosity |
| 4 | Īśvara | Āṇavopāya | Body-means. The subject engages the object through action, sensation, practice. | RM_04 (instruction_protocol), somatic_activation |

### Level 1: The 11 Pure Principles (tattvas 5-15)

These are the **kañcukas** — the self-limitations that create the appearance of a separate self. Every student state of resistance maps here:

| Tattva | Name | What it limits | UNO Student State |
|--------|------|---------------|-------------------|
| 5 | Kalā (creativity) | I can create — but only partially | resist_intellectually |
| 6 | Vidyā (limited knowledge) | I know — but only partially | defensive_rationalization |
| 7 | Rāga (attachment) | I desire — but conditioned | clinging, attachment_to_deserving |
| 8 | Niyati (causality) | I act — but within causes | helpless_effort, caught_in_logic |
| 9 | Kāla (time) | I am — but in time | anxious_temporality, hopeless_shutdown |
| 10-11 | Māyā + Śūnya | The veil + the void | total_denial, collapsed_dependency |

A student in `defensive_rationalization` is operating from tattva 6 (vidyā — limited knowledge). They know something, but they think they know more than they do. The UNMAKING move is constraint_removal (UM_06): remove the hidden assumption that they already understand.

### Level 2: The 5 Bhūtas + 5 Tanmātras + 5 Karmendriyas (tattvas 16-30)

These are the experiential states — what the student is directly feeling:

| Tattva Range | What | UNO States |
|-------------|------|------------|
| 16-20 | 5 buddhīndriyas (sense capacities) | seeking_clarity, following_logic, genuine_curiosity |
| 21-25 | 5 karmendriyas (action capacities) | resist_intellectually, pushes_back, compliance |
| 26-30 | 5 tanmātras (subtle objects) | guarded, fearful, angry, sad, confused |

### Level 3: The 5 Mahābhūtas (tattvas 31-35) + 1 Body (36)

The most concrete states — somatic, behavioral, fully manifest:

| Tattva | Element | UNO States |
|--------|---------|------------|
| 31 | Ākāśa (space) | overwhelmed, spacious, open |
| 32 | Vāyu (air) | anxious, flighty, scattered |
| 33 | Agni (fire) | angry, irritable, explosive |
| 34 | Āpas (water) | tearful, sad, flowing |
| 35 | Pṛthivī (earth) | stuck, heavy, collapsed |
| 36 | Body | somatic_activation, somatic_panic |

### The 38th Principle (Anākhyā)

Beyond the 36 is the enabling condition — what makes any configuration possible. In UNO, this is `my_thoughts`. It observes all 4 vertices simultaneously, is not itself on any vertex, and is where the teacher's metacognitive self-model lives.

The Green Core (center of the tetrahedron, 550nm, Anāhata) is the point where recognition occurs — when the student sees that they ARE the tetrahedron, not a term within it.

## 8. How It Works: Traversal Paths

A student in crisis needs a SPECIFIC path through this coordinate system — not the same path as a student who's intellectually curious.

### Path A: Crisis → Emotional regulation → Safety → Insight

Student state: `hopeless_shutdown` (tattva 35 — earth, collapsed)

```
Turn 1:   State=hopeless_shutdown
          Lineage=Therapeutic (therapy speak — meet them where they are)
          Function=ME_02 (aporia_validation — normalize the state)
          Mechanism=none (direct statement)
          Register=PR_01, IN_04, AT_03 (gentle, compassionate, high attunement)
          "You are not lost. Your usual distractions are missing. Stay with the bare discomfort."

Turn 2:   State=guarded
          Function=UM_04 (ground_reality_check — drag from abstraction to body)
          Mechanism=none
          Register=PR_02, IN_03, AT_02 (steady, authoritative, medium attunement)
          "Forget the story. What is happening in your body right now?"

Turn 3:   State=somatic_activation (tattva 36 — body engaged)
          Function=RM_03 (conceptual_distinction — separate feeling from story)
          Mechanism=comparison_contrast
          Register=PR_02, IN_02, AT_02
          "There is a sensation in your chest. And there is the thought 'this will never end.' They are not the same thing."

Turn 4:   State=dawning_awareness (tattva 2 — Śakti, will-level shift)
          Function=SM_02 (witness_pivot — who is watching?)
          Mechanism=subject_object_inversion
          Register=PR_02, IN_02, AT_02, PD_04 (nondual depth)
          "Who is aware of this sensation? The sensation is known. The knower is not drowning."
```

Key: start with Therapeutic (crisis needs safety), then transition to REMAKING (distinctions), then to SELF-MAKING (witness pivot). You move from tattva 35 to tattva 2 by traversing through intermediate states.

### Path B: Intellectual confusion → Conceptual clarity → Direct insight

Student state: `intellectual_fixation_on_binary` (tattva 5 — kalā, partial creation)

```
Turn 1:   State=intellectual_fixation_on_binary
          Lineage=Socratic (meet intellectual with intellectual)
          Function=UM_01 (definition_collapse — force precision)
          Mechanism=none
          Register=PR_03, IN_02, AT_01 (friction, collaborative, low attunement)
          "Define exactly what you mean by 'either/or.' Not an example. The definition."

Turn 2:   State=caught_in_logic
          Function=UM_02 (contradiction_exposure)
          Mechanism=recursion_loop (apply their logic to itself)
          Register=PR_03, IN_02, AT_02
          "If your own definition fails, then what supports your either/or?"

Turn 3:   State=dawning_awareness
          Lineage=Advaita (shift to direct seeing)
          Function=RM_03 (conceptual_distinction)
          Mechanism=structural_analogy
          Register=PR_02, IN_03, AT_02, PD_04
          "Both positions are clouds. The sky is neither. Find the sky."
```

Key: start Socratic (intellectual level), then shift to Advaita (nondual) once the intellect is cleared.

### Path C: Spiritual materialism → Letting go → Recognition

Student state: `clinging_to_idealized_reward` (tattva 7 — rāga, attachment)

```
Turn 1:   State=clinging_to_idealized_reward
          Lineage=Advaita (direct pointing)
          Function=UM_06 (constraint_removal — remove the hidden assumption that bliss is an object)
          Mechanism=constraint_collapse
          Register=PR_03, IN_03, AT_02, PD_04
          "It does not become anything. When you stop demanding experiences from it, its quiet joy becomes obvious."

Turn 2:   State=resistance_to_inactivity
          Function=RM_03 (conceptual_distinction)
          Mechanism=comparison_contrast
          Register=PR_02, IN_03, AT_02
          "It feels dull because you are comparing it with the excitement of becoming. The 'dullness' is the mind complaining."

Turn 3:   State=sober_realization
          Function=SM_01 (direct_seeing)
          Mechanism=none
          Register=PR_01, IN_03, AT_03, PD_04
          "You do not find it by decoration, but by subtraction."
```

Key: Advaita doesn't need to use Therapeutic register for spiritual materialism. The attachment is already at the right level — just collapse the constraint directly.

## 9. The Principle: Sequential Lineage Switching

The system doesn't pick ONE lineage. It switches between them based on WHERE THE STUDENT IS:

```
├── Crisis, trauma, emotional flooding → THERAPEUTIC register
│   └── Safety established
│       └── Intellectual confusion remains → SOCRATIC register
│           └── Conceptual clarity reached  
│               └── Identity-level stuckness → GURDJIEFFIAN register
│                   └── Still holding onto subtle self → ADVAITA register
│                       └── Recognition → SELF-MAKING (any lineage)
```

The function selection algorithm:

1. Map student utterance to a tattva coordinate (1-36)
2. Map utterance to a student state (1 of 50 Mātṛkā prototypes)
3. Select lineage based on tattva region:
   - Tattvas 29-36 (body/elements) → Therapeutic (somatic)
   - Tattvas 21-28 (karmendriyas/tanmātras) → Therapeutic or Gurdjieffian (action/emotion)
   - Tattvas 12-20 (guṇas/buddhīndriyas) → Socratic (intellectual)
   - Tattvas 5-11 (kañcukas) → Advaita (identity-level)
   - Tattvas 1-4 (pure) → Advaita or none needed (recognition)
4. Query COs for the (lineage, state) pair
5. Select function/mechanism/register from top-weighted CO
6. Render response using RO content

The lineage is not fixed. It's a dimension of the state like any other. A student can start in Therapeutic, move to Socratic, then to Advaita, all in one conversation. The system just asks: "given where the student is now, and given the target tattva (the Green Core), what's the optimal next step?"

## 10. The Kañcuka Model of Resistance

The 5 kañcukas (tattvas 5-9) are the UNIVERSAL forms of resistance. Every student state of resistance maps to one:

| Kañcuka | UNO States | The Lie | The Move |
|---------|-----------|---------|----------|
| Kalā (partial creativity) | resist_intellectually, pushes_back | "I can't do this" | UM_06 constraint_removal |
| Vidyā (partial knowledge) | defensive_rationalization, intellectual_fixation | "I already understand" | RM_03 conceptual_distinction |
| Rāga (conditioned attachment) | clinging, attachment_to_deserving, compliance | "I need this specific outcome" | UM_05 ego_displacement |
| Niyati (conditioned causality) | caught_in_logic, helpless_effort | "I can't because of X" | UM_02 contradiction_exposure |
| Kāla (conditioned time) | hopeless_shutdown, anxious_temporality | "It's too late / too soon" | ME_02 aporia_validation |

The system can identify WHICH kañcuka is active from the student's language and select the corresponding UNMAKING function. The 6th UNMAKING function (UM_04 ground_reality_check) is special — it operates on ALL kañcukas by dragging from abstraction to immediate experience.

## 11. What the System Actually Does

```
Given: student utterance

1. Classify to Mātṛkā state (1 of 50)
2. Map state to tattva coordinate (1 of 36)
3. Determine kañcuka if resistance is present
4. Select lineage based on tattva region
5. Query CO: (lineage, state) → (phase, function, mechanism, register)
6. If no CO: query geometricengine policy_weights for nearest known state
7. If no weights: similarity fallback to semantic nearest state
8. Register prediction: given (state, function, lineage) → 6 register values
   (this is a trained mapping: certain functions prefer certain register profiles)
9. Render: function + mechanism + register + RO content → natural language
10. Observe next state
11. Update CO success/failure count
12. If failure → Error Book constraint
```

The Tantraloka gives precision (exactly where in the 36-tattva space the student is). Ñāṇavīra gives clarity (the 4-term structure of the phase space). UNO gives the empirical data (738 actual teaching moves with outcomes). The COs bridge them.
