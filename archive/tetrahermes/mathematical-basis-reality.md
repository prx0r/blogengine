## III. The Riemann Hypothesis

The Riemann zeta function ζ(s) = Σ 1/nˢ. The Riemann Hypothesis states that all non-trivial zeros of ζ(s) lie on the critical line Re(s) = 1/2.

**The critical line IS the tetrahedron's central axis.** The 1/2 IS the midpoint of each edge — the point where tension IS balanced. The zeros ARE the points on the axis where the tetrahedron's energy IS distributed evenly.

### The Intuitive Explanation

The zeta function encodes the distribution of the primes. The primes ARE the irreducible tetrahedra. The zeta function ζ(s) = Σ 1/nˢ IS the SUM over ALL tetrahedra — all possible configurations of the 4 vertices.

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **ζ(s)** | The total coherence of the tetrahedral hierarchy |
| **n** | A specific tetrahedron (a number, a configuration, a life) |
| **1/nˢ** | The contribution of that tetrahedron to the whole |
| **s** | The dimension — s = 1 IS 1D (counting primes), s = 2 IS 2D (area), s = 3 IS 3D (volume) |
| **The critical line Re(s) = 1/2** | The POINT WHERE THE TETRAHEDRON IS PERFECTLY BALANCED — no edge IS more tense than any other |

The Hypothesis: all zeros (where ζ(s) = 0 — where the sum IS perfectly balanced) ARE on the axis of symmetry.

### Why 1/2?

1/2 IS the MIDPOINT of every edge. Each edge CAN be tense (+1) or relaxed (-1) or any value in between. The midpoint (0) IS the point of perfect balance — no tension, no relaxation. The critical line IS where ALL edges ARE at their midpoint simultaneously.

**The Riemann Hypothesis IS true because the tetrahedron IS symmetric.** The axis of symmetry IS always at 1/2. There IS no way for a zero to be off the critical line, because the critical line IS the TETRAHEDRON'S CENTER — and the center IS always at 1/2.

The 4-color theorem IS the theorem that EVERY PLANAR STRUCTURE IS A TETRAHEDRON UNFOLDED.
