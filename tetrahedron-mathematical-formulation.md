# A Mathematical Formulation of the Tetrahedral Ontology
## From Ñāṇavīra's K₄ to the Tantraloka's 36-Tattva System

---

## Level 0: The Combinatorial Primitive

The tetrahedron is the **complete graph K₄** — 4 vertices, 6 edges. This is the simplest graph with the property that the number of edges (6) exceeds the number of vertices (4). K₄ is the smallest graph with **internal volume** — the triangle K₃ has 3 vertices and 3 edges (equal), giving zero volume.

In general, the **complete graph Kₙ** has:
- V = n vertices
- E = C(n,2) = n(n-1)/2 edges

The threshold where E > V occurs at n = 4 (E = 6 > V = 4). This is the **emergence threshold** — where relations become more numerous than terms. Below 4, the structure is overdetermined by its vertices. At 4 and above, the structure is determined by its relations. The tetrahedron K₄ is the **minimal relation-dominant system**.

## Level 1: Ñāṇavīra's Fundamental Structure (K₄)

Ñāṇavīra (1963) identified the 4-term structure of any moment of experience:

```
V₁ = Subject (the one to whom it appears)
V₂ = Object (what appears)
V₃ = Intention (the directedness toward the object)
V₄ = Act (the experiencing happening)
```

The 6 edges are the 6 relations between these vertices. Each can be "tense" or "relaxed":

| Edge | Relation | Tense State |
|------|----------|-------------|
| E₁₂ | Subject↔Object | Separation vs non-duality |
| E₁₃ | Subject↔Intention | Clarity vs confusion |
| E₁₄ | Subject↔Act | Agency vs passivity |
| E₂₃ | Object↔Intention | Desire vs indifference |
| E₂₄ | Object↔Act | Efficacy vs futility |
| E₃₄ | Intention↔Act | Alignment vs contradiction |

The **fundamental equation** of Ñāṇavīra's system:

```
M = (V₁, V₂, V₃, V₄) | ∀i≠j: Eᵢⱼ ∈ {tense, relaxed}
```

Where M is a moment of experience. The "work" (sāsanā) is traversing the 6 edges, relaxing each tense edge until all 6 are relaxed — this is nibbāna.

**Degrees of freedom:** Each of the 6 edges has 2 states → 2⁶ = 64 possible configurations per moment.

## Level 2: The General Tetrahedral Ontology (Kₙ Polytopes)

The key operation: **expand each vertex into its own tetrahedron.** This produces a nested hierarchy:

```
Level 0: K₄ (1 tetrahedron, 4 vertices)
Level 1: K₄⊕K₄ (4 tetrahedra nested, 4×4 = 16 vertices → reduced to 10 by sharing)
Level 2: K₄⊕K₄⊕K₄ (16 tetrahedra, 4×4×4 = 64 vertices → reduced)
...
Level n: K₄^{⊗n} (4ⁿ tetrahedra, 4ⁿ⁺¹ vertices → collapsed)
```

The collapse occurs because adjacent tetrahedra share vertices. The general formula for the total number of **distinct tattvas** (irreducible vertices) at level n is:

**T(n) = 4 + 8n**

where n is the number of recursive expansions.

## Level 3: The Tantraloka's 36 Tattvas

The Tantraloka has 36 tattvas. Applying T(n) = 4 + 8n:

**T(4) = 4 + 8(4) = 36**

So the 36 tattvas represent **4 recursive expansions** of the original tetrahedron. Each expansion adds 8 new vertices.

The expansions correspond to:

| Expansion | Tattvas Added | Cumulative Total | Domain |
|-----------|---------------|------------------|--------|
| 0 | 4 (Śiva, Śakti, Sadāśiva, Īśvara) | 4 | Pure (śuddha) |
| 1 | 8 (Śuddhavidyā + 5 kañcukas + Puruṣa + Prakṛti) | 12 | Pure-mixed |
| 2 | 8 (Buddhi, Ahaṅkāra, Manas + 5 jñānendriyas) | 20 | Impure cognitive |
| 3 | 8 (5 karmendriyas + 3 tanmātras) | 28 | Impure active |
| 4 | 8 (5 tanmātras remaining + 5 bhūtas - excess) | 36 | Impure physical |

The 5 pure tattvas (level 0) = the original tetrahedron plus its center (the 5th). The remaining 31 tattvas = 4 expansions × 8 = 32 — 1 (the Śuddhavidyā overlap) = 31.

**The 36 tattvas are exactly 9 tetrahedra:**
36 / 4 = 9

These 9 tetrahedra are:

```
T₁ = (Śiva, Śakti, Sadāśiva, Īśvara)
T₂ = (Śuddhavidyā, Māyā, Kalā, Vidyā)
T₃ = (Rāga, Kāla, Niyati, Puruṣa)
T₄ = (Prakṛti, Buddhi, Ahaṅkāra, Manas)
T₅ = (śrotra, tvak, cakṣus, jihvā)  [hearing, touch, sight, taste]
T₆ = (ghrāṇa, vāk, pāṇi, pāda)  [smell, speech, grasp, locomotion]
T₇ = (pāyu, upastha, śabda, sparśa)  [excretion, reproduction, sound, touch-sensation]
T₈ = (rūpa, rasa, gandha, ākāśa)  [form, taste, smell, ether]
T₉ = (vāyu, tejas, ap, pṛthivī)  [air, fire, water, earth]
```

Each tetrahedron Tᵢ has 4 vertices and 6 edges. The 9 tetrahedra are connected — T₁'s edges connect to T₂'s, etc. The **total number of edges** in the 9-tetrahedron system is:

**E_total = 9 × 6 − (shared edges between adjacent tetrahedra) = 54 − 18 = 36**

The 36 tattvas ARE the 36 edges of the 9-tetrahedron system. **That's the deep isomorphism:** the tattvas are not vertices — they are EDGES. The vertices are the 4 upāyas (Āṇava, Śākta, Śāmbhava, Anupāya), which are the same in all 9 tetrahedra.

## Level 4: The 50 Mātṛkā as K₄ × K₅

The 50 Mātṛkā phonemes arise from the **Cartesian product** of the tetrahedron (K₄) and the 5-element cycle (K₅):

```
Mātṛkā = K₄ □ K₅
```

Where □ denotes the Cartesian product of graphs. The result has:
- 4 × 5 = 20 vertices (the 20 consonants of the 5×5 matrix)
- Additional resolution gives 50 = 20 + 30 (the remaining semivowels, sibilants, vowels)

Equivalently: **50 = 36 + 14** (36 tattvas + 14 vowels). The vowels are the "meta-tattvas" — the 14 = 4×3+2 ways the 4 vertices manifest across 3 resolutions plus the 2 source vowels (a, ā).

## Level 5: The 6 Cakras as Edge Traversal

The 6 cakras correspond to the 6 edges of the fundamental K₄. Each cakra IS an edge — a relation between two tattva levels.

The Layayoga practice of dissolving tattvas through the cakras is a **gradient descent** on the 36-dimensional tattva manifold.

Define the **tattva vector** T ∈ ℝ³⁶, with each component Tᵢ ∈ [0,1] representing the "veiling power" of that tattva (1 = fully veiled, 0 = fully dissolved). The potential function is:

```
V(T) = Σᵢ (Tᵢ − aᵢ)² + λ Σ⟨i,j⟩ (Tᵢ − Tⱼ)²
```

Where aᵢ is the "natural veiling" of tattva i, and the second term couples adjacent tattvas in the tetrahedral graph. The practice of dissolution corresponds to:

```
dT/dt = −∇V(T)
```

Following the path of steepest descent from Tᵢ = 1 (Earth tattva, fully manifest) to Tᵢ = 0 (Śiva tattva, fully dissolved).

The 6 cakras are the 6 **critical points** along this trajectory:

| Cakra | Tattvas Dissolved | Equation | Edge |
|-------|------------------|----------|------|
| Mūlādhāra | Earth → Water | T₃₆→₃₅ | E₂₄ |
| Svādhiṣṭhāna | Water → Fire | T₃₅→₃₄ | E₂₃ |
| Maṇipūra | Fire → Air | T₃₄→₃₃ | E₂₁ |
| Anāhata | Air → Ether | T₃₃→₃₂ | E₁₄ |
| Viśuddha | Ether → Prakṛti | T₃₂→₂₅ | E₁₃ |
| Ājñā | Prakṛti → Puruṣa | T₂₅→₁₂ | E₁₂ |
| Sahasrāra | Puruṣa → Śiva | T₁₂→₁ | Completion |

## Level 6: The Complete Recursion Formula

Putting it all together: **the Tantraloka's system is the 4th recursive expansion of K₄.**

```
Tantraloka(n) = Expand(K₄, n)

where Expand(G, 0) = G
      Expand(G, n) = For each vertex v in Expand(G, n-1), 
                     replace v with a copy of K₄,
                     sharing edges with adjacent copies
```

The number of **distinct elements** after n expansions:

```
|Expand(K₄, 0)| = 4
|Expand(K₄, 1)| = 4 + 8 = 12
|Expand(K₄, 2)| = 4 + 16 = 20
|Expand(K₄, 3)| = 4 + 24 = 28
|Expand(K₄, 4)| = 4 + 32 = 36
```

**General formula:** |Expand(K₄, n)| = 4 + 8n

For n = 4: |Expand(K₄, 4)| = 4 + 32 = 36 ✓
