# Yogi Spotlight — Video Format

**Target length:** 12-18 minutes (15 min sweet spot)
**Series identity:** "the strange life of" meets "here's what it actually means"
**Unfair advantage:** We can decode the weirdness through Tantraloka theory — no one else can do this

## The Format

### 1. The Hook (0:00-0:40)
The insane claim. No context. No name. Just the thing that makes you stop scrolling.

> "In the 1970s, a man walked into a medical lab, sat down, and told the doctors he would stop his own heart. They didn't believe him. Then his pulse flatlined."

No "today we're going to talk about." Just the claim. Name comes after the hook lands.

### 2. Who Was This Person? (0:40-2:30)
Quick orientation — but not "he was born in..."

- Tradition/lineage (1 sentence)
- Why they're famous in their world (1-2 sentences)
- The gap: "almost nobody in the West has heard of him, but in [region/tradition], he's a legend"

The goal: establish that this person matters without over-explaining.

### 3. The Life — What Actually Happened (2:30-9:00)
The story. Chronological or thematic. This is the meat.

- **Early life / before** — what made them who they were
- **The turning point** — renunciation, initiation, the weird thing
- **The practice** — what they actually did (if known)
- **The evidence** — what we know, what's documented, what's legend
- **The peak** — the most extraordinary episode

Keep it moving. Don't get bogged down. Use the source quotes as punctuation, not as the main text.

### 4. The Decode (9:00-13:00)
This is what makes us different from every other "strange life" channel.

- **What Tantraloka says about what happened** — prana, spanda, kundalini, samadhi, the tattvas
- **Why it's not magic** — it's a technology of consciousness that we have the map for
- **What this means about human potential** — this isn't a freak show, it's a demonstration

> "When Swami Rama's heart stopped, what actually happened? In Tantraloka terms, he redirected apana vayu to suspend cardiac function. This is not supernatural. It's a specific yogic technology that works on known principles."

### 5. Why It Matters Now (13:00-14:30)
Connect to the viewer's life. What can they learn from this?

- This person lived a teaching, not just thought it
- The same principles are available
- You don't need to stop your heart — but you can understand what stopping it means

### 6. The Takeaway (14:30-15:00)
Circle back to the hook. One line that lands.

> "He stopped his heart so we could learn something about consciousness. The question is: have we learned it?"

## Length Variations

| Duration | When | Format |
|----------|------|--------|
| 8-10 min | Short version / YouTube Shorts | Hook → Peak → Decode → Takeaway (cut life story) |
| 15 min | Standard episode | Full format above |
| 20-25 min | Deep dive / major figure | Full format + extended decode + multiple quotes |

## Visual Template

| Segment | Visual |

| Who | Still image of the yogi (if exists) or period art |
| The Life | Documentary-style: historical images, locations, maps |

| Takeaway | Fade to black → text |

## Series Structure

The first 3-5 episodes establish the format. After that, the audience knows what to expect:

1. **Swami Rama** — the Menninger experiments (most documented, strongest hook)
2. **Yogi Satyamurti** — 8 days buried alive (best blend of story + science)
3. **Bamakhepa** — the mad saint who scolded the goddess (first purely traditional figure)
4. **Tangtong Gyalpo** — the yogi who built bridges (evidence survives, teaches karma yoga)
5. **Sarvananda of Mehar** — the fool who mastered 10 goddesses in one night (dark horse)

Followed by: Boganathar, Tajuddin Baba, Ra Lotsawa, Muktābāī, The Two Headless Yoginīs.
