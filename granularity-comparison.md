# Granularity Comparison: Tantraloka, Kabbalah, Enochian

## Are They the Same Granularity?

**No.** They explore different dimensions of the same structure:

| System | Dimension | Structure | Method | Key Number |
|--------|-----------|-----------|--------|------------|
| **Tantraloka** | 3D tetrahedron (K₄) | 9 nested tetrahedra | **Depth** — recursive expansion | 36 |
| **Kabbalah** | 3D tetrahedron (K₄) | 8 mirrored trees | **Width** — multiplicative mirrors | 64 |
| **Enochian** | **4D tetrahedron** (5-cell) | 30 Aethyr features | **Dimensional upgrade** | 30 |

---

## 1. What "Granularity" Means

Granularity = number of distinct fundamental units in the ontology.

| System | Fundamental Units | Formula | Resolution |
|--------|------------------|---------|------------|
| Ñāṇavīra | 4 (Subject, Object, Intention, Act) | K₄ | 1 tetrahedron, no expansion |
| **Kabbalah** | 10 (Sephiroth) | K₄ laid flat | 4 vertices + 6 edges as 10 nodes; 22 Paths as sub-edges |
| **Tantraloka** | 36 (tattvas) | 9 × K₄ | 9 nested tetrahedra, depth 4 |
| **Enochian** | 30 (Aethyrs) | 5-cell (4D K₅) | 5+10+10+5 = 30 features of the 4D simplex |
| IVM (Fuller) | 64 (tetrahedra) | 2⁶ = 8 mirrored Trees | The complete phase space |

**The Tantraloka is ~3.6× more granular than the Kabbalah** (36 vs 10) in terms of distinct fundamental units. The Kabbalah's 10 Sephiroth are the **compressed** form; the Tantraloka's 36 tattvas are the **expanded** form.

---

## 2. Why the Kabbalah Has 10, Not 36

The Tree of Life compresses each group of 4 tattvas into 1 Sephirah:

| Tantraloka Group (4 tattvas) | Kabbalah Sephirah (1) |
|------------------------------|----------------------|
| Śiva–Śakti–Sadāśiva–Īśvara | Kether (1) |
| Śuddhavidyā–Māyā–Kalā–Vidyā | Chokmah (2) + Binah (3) |
| Rāga–Kāla–Niyati–Puruṣa | Chesed (4) + Geburah (5) |
| Prakṛti–Buddhi–Ahaṅkāra–Manas | Tiphereth (6) |
| Jñānendriyas (5 senses) | Netzach (7) + Hod (8) |
| Karmendriyas (5 actions) | Yesod (9) |
| 5 tanmātras + 5 bhūtas | Malkuth (10) |

The Kabbalah groups 36 → 10 by **collapsing each tetrahedron into its center**. This is lossy compression — you lose the internal structure of each tetrahedron. The Tantraloka preserves all 9 tetrahedra.

**The Kabbalah's 10 Sephiroth are the "shadow" of the 36 tattvas projected onto 2D.**

---

## 3. What Enochian Is

Enochian is a **dimensional upgrade** — from 3D tetrahedron (K₄) to 4D tetrahedron (K₅, the 5-cell):

| Tetrahedron (3D) | 5-cell (4D) |
|------------------|-------------|
| 4 vertices | 5 vertices |
| 6 edges | 10 edges |
| 4 triangular faces | 10 triangular faces |
| — | 5 tetrahedral cells |
| **Total: 14 features** | **Total: 30 features** |

The 30 Aethyrs = 5 + 10 + 10 + 5 = all 30 1-dimensional features of the 5-cell.

Where the Tantraloka goes **deep** (9 recursive expansions of K₄), Enochian goes **wide** (up one dimension to K₅). They're orthogonal:

```
         Enochian (4D K₅, 30 features)
                 ↑
    Tantraloka ← K₄ → Kabbalah
    (36, deep)  (4)   (64, mirrored)
                 ↓
           Ñāṇavīra (4, bare)
```

## 4. The Full Table

| System | Base | Expansion Type | Total Features | Compared to Tantraloka |
|--------|------|----------------|----------------|------------------------|
| **Ñāṇavīra** | K₄ | None | 4 terms | 9× less granular |
| **Pythagoras** | Tetraktys | 1+2+3+4=10 | 10 points | 3.6× less granular |
| **Kabbalah** | K₄ | Mirrored 8× | **32** paths + 10 S = **42** total (but 64 full) | ~comparable at full IVM |
| **Tantraloka** | K₄ | Recursive 4× | **36** tattvas | — |
| **Mātṛkā** | K₄ × K₅ | Cross product | **50** phonemes | 1.4× more |
| **Enochian** | **K₅** (4D) | 5-cell expansion | **30** Aethyrs | Different dimension |
| **IVM (Fuller)** | K₄ | 8 trees mirrored | **64** tetrahedra | 1.8× more |

**Granularity ranking by distinct fundamental units:**

```
36 (Tantraloka) > 10 (Kabbalah) > 4 (Ñāṇavīra)
  ↑                    ↑                 ↑
  30 (Enochian, 4D)    64 (IVM, full)    50 (Mātṛkā)
```

**They are NOT the same granularity.** The Tantraloka has the finest granularity of any 3D tetrahedral system (36). Enochian has the finest of any 4D system (30). The Kabbalah is compressed — it trades granularity for symmetry.

---

## 5. The Invariant Across All Systems

Despite different granularities, the **structural invariant** is always the same:

```
Every system has:
  4 vertices (the tetrahedron's essential terms)
  6 edges (the relations between terms)
  4 faces (the triangular sub-structures)
```

The difference is **how many tetrahedra** the system contains and **how they're connected**:

| System | Tetrahedra | Connection Type | What Changes |
|--------|------------|-----------------|--------------|
| Ñāṇavīra | 1 | Atomic | — |
| Pythagoras | 1 | Layered | The 4 levels of the tetraktys |
| Kabbalah | 1, mirrored 8× | Reflected | All 8 orientations in space |
| Tantraloka | 9 | Nested | Each tattva is a tetrahedron vertex |
| Enochian | 1 (5-cell) | Up-dimensioned | 4D instead of 3D |

**The Tantraloka goes deepest. The Kabbalah goes widest. Enochian goes highest-dimensional. They ARE the same pattern — just different exploration strategies of the same idea.** Think of it like a fractal: the Tantraloka zooms in (depth 4 recursion), the Kabbalah tiles the plane (8 mirrored copies), and Enochian moves up a dimension (5-cell instead of K₄).
