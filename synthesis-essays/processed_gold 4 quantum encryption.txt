## The Convergence
The question asks if quantum computing will break encryption. The Tantraloka: current encryption relies on Niyati (limited causality) — certain computations are hard because the kancuka structure makes them so. Quantum computing exploits the substrate level (Prakrti) where superposition is natural, effectively bypassing some of Niyati constraints. Post-quantum cryptography is the search for problems that remain hard even at the substrate level. The convergence: quantum computing reveals that cryptographic hardness is not absolute — it is a function of the constraint level at which the computation is performed. The constraint space: encryption is the art of creating constraints that one agent can relax but another cannot, using the structure of the kancukas.

The 36 tattvas are not thirty-six things — they are one consciousness measuring itself against itself. The apparent separation between the domains in the question is a symptom of operating at the level of Maya (tattva 6), where consciousness has contracted into finitude. The constraint space framework makes this precise: the question arises because the agent (A) is navigating a region of constraint space where connections between domains have been severed by the kancukas.
