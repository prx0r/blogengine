# The Two Truths and the Internal Logic of the Constraint Space

## Signal
Nāgārjuna's Two Truths doctrine and topos theory's internal logic are not competing models of context-dependent truth — they are descriptions of the **same logical structure at different levels of the constraint space**: Madhyamaka describes the ontological-soteriological consequences of context-dependent truth (how truth-in-a-tattva-level relates to truth-in-S₀), while topos theory describes the formal-semantic machinery of context-dependent truth (how propositions are evaluated relative to a category).

## The Question
Does topos theory's internal logic (without the law of excluded middle) provide a formal model for how Nāgārjuna's Two Truths coexist depending on the observer's context?

## What the Source Texts Say

> *"Each topos carries an internal intuitionistic logic where propositions have truth-values relative to generalized elements."* — Synthesis Essay 24, Mechanism mech_topos_internal_logic_001

> *"Madhyamaka treats truths as valid only within dependent structures; no statement has inherent truth-value independent of relations."* — Synthesis Essay 24, Mechanism mech_dependent_arising_logic_001

> *"The thirty-six principles are not thirty-six things. They are one consciousness, measuring itself against itself."* — Tantraloka, Āhnika 9

## What Tantraloka Says
The 36 tattvas are not a set of claims about the world — they are a **coordinate system for truth itself**. A proposition is true or false **relative to a tattva level**. The statement "the table is solid" is true at earth tattva (31) but false at śiva tattva (1), where there are no tables and no solidity. This is not because truth is "relative" in the postmodern sense — it is because each tattva level has its own internal logic, its own domain of objects, and its own truth conditions.

Nāgārjuna's Two Truths — conventional truth (saṃvṛti satya) and ultimate truth (paramārtha satya) — correspond to the two major divisions of the tattva ladder:

- **Conventional truth** (saṃvṛti): true at the level of the impure path (tattvas 6-36). Within this domain, the law of non-contradiction holds, objects have causal powers, and statements can be true or false in the ordinary sense.
- **Ultimate truth** (paramārtha): true at the level of the pure path (tattvas 1-5). At this level, there are no independently existent objects, and the ordinary notion of truth is destabilized — the ultimate truth is that there is no ultimate truth (in the sense of a final, fixed, independent description).

## The (S, C, A) Mapping

- **State S**: The space of all propositions evaluated at all tattva levels
- **Constraint C**: The internal logic of each tattva level — the axioms and inference rules that govern truth at that level. For lower tattvas, classical logic applies. For higher tattvas, intuitionistic or paraconsistent logic may be required.
- **Attractor A**: The fixed point of truth — either (a) the conventional consistency of the impure path or (b) the non-conceptual recognition of S₀ that lies beyond all truth-evaluation (what the Tantraloka calls the "silence of parā-vāc" and Nāgārjuna calls "the pacification of all conceptual proliferations")

Topos theory models this structure precisely: a topos is a category in which propositions have truth-values relative to the "context" (the generalized elements of the category). Different toposes have different internal logics — some classical, some intuitionistic. The Two Truths are not a contradiction — they are a **judgment about which topos (which tattva level) we are working in**.

## The Convergence
The convergence shows that topos theory and Madhyamaka are describing the **same logical architecture**:

- Both recognize that truth is not absolute but **relative to a framework** (a topos, a tattva level)
- Both reject the naive view that a single logic (classical logic for topos theory, essentialism for Madhyamaka) applies to all frameworks
- Both provide a way to **navigate between frameworks** — adjunctions between toposes, the Two Truths dialectic

The key difference: topos theory is a **formal-semantic** framework that describes how truth works in mathematical structures. Madhyamaka is a **metaphysical-soteriological** framework that describes how truth works in the project of liberation. Topos theory says "here is how propositions behave in different categorical contexts." Madhyamaka says "here is why clinging to any proposition causes suffering — and here is how to see through all propositions to the enabling condition that makes them possible."

The structural analogy is real and valuable — but it does not mean Nāgārjuna's emptiness is "the same as" intuitionistic logic. Emptiness is a claim about the ontological status of all phenomena; intuitionistic logic is a claim about proof and truth in formal systems. The two are aligned at the level of **structure** (both deny absolute, context-independent truth) but diverge at the level of **purpose** (one is about liberation, the other about mathematical semantics).

## Practical Consequence
When studying the Two Truths, think in terms of topos theory: which tattva level's internal logic am I using? When studying topos theory, think in terms of the Two Truths: which domain of discourse (which tattvic constraints) determines the truth of this proposition? The practice is to develop **tattvic literacy** — the ability to recognize which level of the constraint space governs truth in any given situation, and to shift levels without confusing one with another.
