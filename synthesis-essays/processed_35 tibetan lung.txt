## The Convergence
Tibetan Tsa Lung involves breath retention and movement to manipulate winds (lung) in channels (tsa). Porges polyvagal theory describes the vagus nerve as primary regulator of autonomic state. The Tantraloka maps this at Prana tattva — the vital energy bridging gross physical (annamaya kosha) and mental (manomaya kosha). Tsa Lung is a systematic protocol for navigating the autonomic constraint space: each breath pattern constrains possible nervous system states. The convergence is precise: both describe the same regulatory architecture — Tantraloka provides the phenomenological coordinate system, Porges the physiological mechanism. The constraint space reveals breath as the most direct access point for modulating C at the level of the vital sheaths.

The 36 tattvas are not thirty-six things — they are one consciousness measuring itself against itself. The apparent separation between the domains in the question is a symptom of operating at the level of Maya (tattva 6), where consciousness has contracted into finitude. The constraint space framework makes this precise: the question arises because the agent (A) is navigating a region of constraint space where connections between domains have been severed by the kancukas.
