# The Three Worlds as Tattvic Attractor Basins

## Signal
Shamanic upper, middle, and lower worlds are not literal places or mere psychological projections — they are **phenomenological attractor basins** in the constraint space of consciousness, corresponding to distinct ranges of the 36-tattva ladder, and the neurocognitive implementation of trance is the mechanism of transition between these basins.

## The Question
Are shamanic "upper," "middle," and "lower" worlds best understood as different modes of self-referential processing, or do shamanic cultures treat them as ontologically real places accessed via altered states?

## What the Source Texts Say

> *"Network-level accounts of trance describe absorptive states with altered coupling between internally oriented and externally oriented networks."* — Synthesis Essay 15, Evidence for mech_trance_absorptive_state_001

## What Tantraloka Says
The 36 tattvas form a **vertical hierarchy** — not spatial but ontological. The upper tattvas (Śiva 1 through Śuddhavidyā 5) are the pure path: minimal constraint, maximal awareness. The middle tattvas (Māyā 6 through the five kañcukas 7-11) are the impure path of limitation. The lower tattvas (prakṛti 12 through earth 31) are the gross realm of embodied experience.

The shamanic three worlds map directly onto these three ranges:

- **Upper World** (tattvas 1-5): The pure path — maximally unconstrained awareness, the realm of archetypal forms and cosmic knowledge. This is the plane of śuddhavidyā (pure knowledge) where no limitations apply.
- **Middle World** (tattvas 6-11): The impure path of māyā and the kañcukas — the interface between unconstrained awareness and embodiment. This is the domain of spirits, power animals, and the "as-if" reality of the subtle body (the puryaṣṭaka).
- **Lower World** (tattvas 12-31): The gross realm of the elements, the senses, and the physical body. This is the domain of trauma, instinct, and ancestral patterning.

The shamanic journey is a **tattvic traversal**: moving the locus of identification from the lower tattvas (the body, the emotions) to the middle tattvas (the imaginal, the relational) to the upper tattvas (pure awareness, direct knowledge). The "spirits" encountered are not external entities — they are the **denizens of each tattva level**, the personified constraints and attractors of that region of the constraint space.

## The (S, C, A) Mapping

- **State S**: The configuration of consciousness at any tattva level
- **Constraint C**: The specific limitations of each tattva range — for the lower world, the constraints of the elements (earth, water, fire, air, ether); for the middle world, the constraints of the kañcukas (limited agency, knowledge, attachment, time, causality); for the upper world, minimal constraint
- **Attractor A**: The fixed points of each tattva region — the "spirits" are these attractors, and "contacting" a spirit IS entering the attractor basin

The shamanic world structure is a **phenomenological map of the constraint space** — not a literal geography but a topology of the possible configurations of consciousness and the transitions between them. The drumming and trance techniques are methods for navigating this topology by modulating the constraint parameters (breath, attention, social context).

## The Convergence
The convergence shows that the shamanic three-world cosmology, the neurocognitive model of trance, and the Tantraloka's 36-tattva system are **three descriptions of the same constraint topology**:

- The shamanic tradition provides the **phenomenological map** — the worlds as felt landscapes of experience
- Neuroscience provides the **implementation constraints** — the brain networks that must shift for trance to occur
- The Tantraloka provides the **formal structure** — the tattvas as the coordinate system within which the worlds are arranged

The correction: the worlds are not "in the head" or "out there" — they are **levels of the constraint space**. They are real in the same way that a mountain range is real: you can traverse them, they have structure, and different cultures give them different names. The "spirits" are the attractors of those regions — as real as the concept of a watershed in topography, but not objects in the physical sense.

## Practical Consequence
Stop debating whether shamanic worlds are "real." Instead: learn to recognize your current tattva level by the **constraints you are experiencing**. Lower world = heavy, bound by body and trauma. Middle world = fluid, relational, populated with images and presences. Upper world = spacious, silent, beyond form. The practice is not to visit these worlds as tourist destinations but to learn to **navigate the constraint space** — to recognize that every experience is an attractor in some tattva region, and freedom is the ability to move between them.
