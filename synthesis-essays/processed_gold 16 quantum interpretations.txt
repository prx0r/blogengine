## The Convergence
The question asks for the best QM interpretation. The Tantraloka: each interpretation addresses a different tattva range. Copenhagen operates at Maya (tattva 6) — it describes measurement without explaining it. Bohmian mechanics operates at Niyati (tattva 11) — hidden variables as limited causality. Many Worlds operates at Paramasiva (tattva 37) — the space of all possibilities. QBism operates at Ahankara (tattva 22) — the observer as the reference point. There is no best interpretation — different upayas suit different practitioners. The constraint space reveals that the interpretation debate is the physics equivalent of the upaya classification: each interpretation works for a specific level of constraint, and none captures the full structure.

The 36 tattvas are not thirty-six things — they are one consciousness measuring itself against itself. The apparent separation between the domains in the question is a symptom of operating at the level of Maya (tattva 6), where consciousness has contracted into finitude. The constraint space framework makes this precise: the question arises because the agent (A) is navigating a region of constraint space where connections between domains have been severed by the kancukas.
