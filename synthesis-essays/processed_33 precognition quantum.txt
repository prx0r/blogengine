## The Convergence
The transactional interpretation (Cramer 1986) posits the present moment is a handshake between advanced (future) and retarded (past) waves. The Tantraloka Kala tattva is the constraint creating linear time from non-temporal substrate. Precognition — if real — would be perception of information not yet filtered through Kala kancuka. The constraint space explains this as an agent temporarily operating where Kala is relaxed. The Cramer transaction is the formal physics of what the Tantraloka describes as the simultaneous presence of all tattvas in Siva. The convergence: both describe time as a derivative phenomenon, not a fundamental one.

The 36 tattvas are not thirty-six things — they are one consciousness measuring itself against itself. The apparent separation between the domains in the question is a symptom of operating at the level of Maya (tattva 6), where consciousness has contracted into finitude. The constraint space framework makes this precise: the question arises because the agent (A) is navigating a region of constraint space where connections between domains have been severed by the kancukas.
