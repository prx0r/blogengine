## The Convergence
The question asks if Many Worlds is the most coherent QM interpretation. The Tantraloka: Paramasiva (tattva 37) IS the space of all possible configurations of consciousness — what Everett calls the universal wavefunction. The 38th principle (Anakhya) is the recognition that the space of all possibilities cannot itself be captured by any description within it. Many Worlds eliminates collapse, which the Tantraloka also does — collapse is recognition (pratyabhijna), not a physical process. The constraint space reveals: Many Worlds is the most coherent physical description of the tattva structure because it takes the space of possibilities as fundamental rather than derived. But it misses the crucial element: consciousness is not a branch in the wavefunction — it is the substrate of the wavefunction.

The 36 tattvas are not thirty-six things — they are one consciousness measuring itself against itself. The apparent separation between the domains in the question is a symptom of operating at the level of Maya (tattva 6), where consciousness has contracted into finitude. The constraint space framework makes this precise: the question arises because the agent (A) is navigating a region of constraint space where connections between domains have been severed by the kancukas.
