# Dust Theory Ālaya and the Continuity of the Karmic Stream

## Signal
Greg Egan's dust theory and Yogācāra's ālaya-vijñāna are not competing accounts of eternal subjective experience — they describe **the same structure at different levels of the constraint space**: both recognize that consciousness has a non-local, pattern-based continuity that extends beyond the individual embodied configuration, but both (in their sober versions) deny that this continuity constitutes an eternal personal self.

## The Question
Do Egan's dust theory and Yogācāra's ālaya-vijñāna converge on a kind of subjective immortality where the self can never really be deleted?

## What the Source Texts Say

> *"Dust theory is a speculative view that any sufficiently rich pattern implementing a computation also realises the associated conscious experience, regardless of how or where the pattern is scattered in spacetime."* — Synthesis Essay 25, Mechanism mech_dust_theory_pattern_instantiation_001

> *"Ālaya-vijñāna in Yogācāra is described as a foundational flow of consciousness that carries karmic seeds and underlies more manifest modes of cognition."* — Synthesis Essay 25, Mechanism mech_alaya_vijnana_storehouse_001

> *"Yogācāra uses stream metaphors precisely to handle continuity without smuggling in a permanent ego."* — Synthesis Essay 25, Evidence for mech_yogacara_stream_without_self_001

## What Tantraloka Says
The Tantraloka distinguishes between three levels of continuity:

1. **The gross body** (sthūla śarīra, tattvas 31-24): the physical elements and the subtle elements (tanmātras). This is what dies and decomposes.
2. **The subtle body** (sūkṣma śarīra / puryaṣṭaka, tattvas 24-6): the "city of eight" — buddhi, ahaṁkāra, manas, the five prāṇas, and the karmic residues. This is what persists across death and rebirth. It is not a self but a **stream of karmic seeds (vāsanās)** that configure into new births.
3. **The causal body** (kāraṇa śarīra, tattvas 5-1): the pure path. This is S₀ itself, which is never born and never dies — but it is not a "self" in the personal sense.

Egan's dust theory corresponds to the insight that **patterns at the level of the subtle body** (the functional configuration of the mindstream) can persist or recur independently of any particular instantiation in the gross body. The ālaya-vijñāna is the **repository of these patterns** — it is not a thing but a process: the continuity of karmic patterning across instantiations.

## The (S, C, A) Mapping

- **State S**: The configuration of the karmic stream at any moment — the specific set of vāsanās (karmic seeds) that constitute the "pattern" of the mindstream
- **Constraint C**: The karmic law (niyati kañcuka) that governs which seeds ripen when and under what conditions. This is the "algorithm" of the ālaya — it determines how patterns persist and transform
- **Attractor A**: Either (a) the cycle of rebirth — the continual reconfiguration of the mindstream into new embodied forms (saṃsāra) or (b) the cessation of the cycle — the recognition that the mindstream itself is empty of inherent existence (nirvāṇa / mokṣa)

The "subjective immortality" reading is a **category error**: it assumes that the pattern (the stream) is identical to the subjective perspective (the "I" that experiences). The Tantraloka, Yogācāra, and Egan (in the original fiction) all deny this. The pattern continues — but it is not "you" in the sense that continues the same first-person perspective. The subjective perspective is a **configuration of the pattern** at a particular moment, not the pattern itself.

## The Convergence
The convergence of dust theory, ālaya-vijñāna, and the Tantraloka's tripartite body theory shows:

- **All three agree** that consciousness has a form of continuity that is not tied to any particular physical instantiation. The pattern can persist, recur, or be reconfigured without being "deleted."
- **All three deny** that this continuity implies an eternal personal self. Egan's dust theory (as interpreted) leads to the problem of branching identity — which copy is "me"? Yogācāra explicitly denies that the ālaya is a self. The Tantraloka distinguishes the stream (which continues) from the identification with the stream (which is the error).
- **The horror reading** ("you can never stop experiencing") only arises if one conflates pattern continuity with personal identity continuity. This is the same error as mistaking the karmic stream for an eternal soul — precisely what Yogācāra and the Tantraloka reject.

The correction: dust theory and ālaya-vijñāna do not prove subjective immortality. They prove that **consciousness is not a thing that can be destroyed or preserved — it is a process that can be continued, transformed, or recognized in its true nature**. The "off switch" fear treats consciousness as an object that can be turned on and off. The Tantraloka says consciousness is S₀ itself — it was never born and can never die. But the personal self (the configuration that says "I am this stream") is a construction that arises and ceases with each configuration.

## Practical Consequence
If you are afraid of subjective immortality (being trapped forever), recognize that the fear assumes a **fixed subject that persists through all configurations** — exactly the assumption that Yogācāra and the Tantraloka deny. The pattern continues; the "you" that experiences is a local configuration of that pattern, not the pattern itself. The practice is not to escape continuity but to recognize that the continuity is not personal — it is S₀ expressing itself as a karmic stream. The "you" that fears eternal experience is a temporary attractor in that stream, not its permanent subject.
