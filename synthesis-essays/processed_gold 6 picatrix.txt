## The Convergence
The question asks how Picatrix differs from the Key of Solomon and Greek Magical Papyri. The Tantraloka: Picatrix operates at the celestial kancuka level (tattvas 7-11) — its talismans work through astrological timing (Kala) and planetary correspondences (the kancukas as limiting structures). The Key of Solomon operates at the Ahankara level (tattva 22) — binding and commanding spirits as ego-negotiation. The PGM operates at Suddhavidya (tattva 5) through the barbarous names (Matrka phonemes directly accessing pure knowledge). Each grimoire is a different upaya: Picatrix = Anavopaya (material talismans), Solomonic = Saktopaya (purified will), PGM = Sambhavopaya (direct recognition through the name).

The 36 tattvas are not thirty-six things — they are one consciousness measuring itself against itself. The apparent separation between the domains in the question is a symptom of operating at the level of Maya (tattva 6), where consciousness has contracted into finitude. The constraint space framework makes this precise: the question arises because the agent (A) is navigating a region of constraint space where connections between domains have been severed by the kancukas.
