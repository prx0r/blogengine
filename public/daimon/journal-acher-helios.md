# Journal: Combining Helios Unbound + Acher's Holy Daimon

> A practitioner's first-person account of running Helios Unbound (7-month programme)
> alongside Frater Acher's Holy Daimon exercises. Sourced from an online forum (2024).
> Username: pixel_fortune.

---

## Starting Context

- Began the combined programme when a TV writing job was cancelled, freeing up time
- Had already started Gallery of Magick's Mystical Words of Power (7 rites aligning with elemental balancing)
- Was unemployed at start, acknowledging that wouldn't last the full 7 months
- Approached it as: "Helios Unbound is the core rite and the only one I've sworn an oath to complete"

## Structural Insight: The Three Traditions Align

Abramelin: 3 sections of 2 months each, ramping in intensity
Acher: TRUST → JOY → DARKNESS → ENCOUNTER (final week)
Helios Unbound: 7 months with elemental balancing, vowel singing, planets, moon, decans, angels

Key realisation: TRUST (Acher) = Elemental Balancing (Helios) = getting your shit together in Malkuth.
JOY (Acher) = Month 3 vowel singing (Helios) = inflaming yourself with prayer.
The requirements of Holy Daimon are not onerous — daily meditation, singing, occasional extra exercises.

## Month 3 Report: "The Most Powerful Practice So Far"

"The third month requires significantly more discipline because it's primarily meditation (65 minutes for the main ritual). The discipline isn't in the ritual itself, but in structuring your day."

"The third month is feeling like the most powerful practice so far, by a lot."

Week 1: Opening each middle pillar/chakra centre, placing godform of HGA in it
Week 2: Same for "solar body" (HGA's body, mirror image from crown), using PGM vowel chanting
Week 3: Drawing the solar body down into the lunar body, one centre at a time

"When I say doubts are gone: today, first day of Month 4 and the planetary invocations, felt very empty and blah. That's normal for the first time I do any new ritual, but I still feel sad and anticlimactic today as a result."

## On the Helios Structure

"I realised why you probably couldn't swap out a month. You're rising through the spheres:
- M2: Elements
- M4: Sublunar forces — planetary Kings who mediate power down here
- M5: Lunar Mansions
- M6: Decans — planets among the fixed stars
- M7: Angelic forces

This realisation made me feel a lot better about the astrology focus (mainly the Decans I wasn't looking forward to). It's less about learning your chart and more about introducing yourself to relevant powers at each level."

## The "Not a Success" Report (Month 7 Retreat)

"It wasn't a success, but not a complete failure either. I got instruction on what I need to do for the next 3-4 months (mainly an intensified focus on one of the earlier months)."

The contact: a minor TV character with wings. Struggled with whether to accept it.

Nick Farrell's response: "If there was communication, then it was a success, even if there was no intense altered state or visions."

"I'm not feeling particularly motivated to keep doing my daily practice — especially because I had looked forward to shifting gear and having time for practical magic."

## Key Lessons

1. **The programme works — but contact may not look dramatic.** The practitioner got instruction (ideas popping into head fully formed) but no dramatic vision. Farrell considers this success.

2. **Months vary in personal value.** Planetary invocations (M4) were "stuff I already know." Month 3 was "game-changing." Different months hit different practitioners differently.

3. **The structure is a genuine hierarchy.** Elements → Planets → Moon → Decans → Angels. This isn't arbitrary — you're literally rising through the spheres.

4. **"Success" may require going back.** The instruction received was to spend 3-4 months intensifying an earlier month's work. The path isn't always linear.

5. **Discipline > motivation.** "Daily practice relies on discipline, not motivation." Post-retreat blah is normal.

6. **The vow matters.** The practitioner took the oath seriously — waking before dawn to complete missed sessions rather than breaking the vow. This intensity contributed to the power of the work.

7. **External timing aligned.** The job cancellation and restart dates perfectly bookended the 7 months. "I can't help but think it's a sign."
