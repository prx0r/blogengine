## The Serpent and the Egg

> Phanes, the Orphic primeval deity of new life hatched from an egg with a
> serpent wound about it, has been called the 'Revealer'; and indeed new
> wisdom awaits us by learning more about this mystical image. The serpent
> wound about the egg has been an object of gnostic reflection for many
> centuries. Countless times it has been hammered into stone, painted in oil,
> tattooed on skin, or conjured in vision.
>
> Before we begin this third exercise in Trust, let's take a moment to
> meditate on the body of the serpent. Can you see the polarity here? The
> painful and deeply personal dilemma of the snake? At every moment the
> serpent has to make a decision. Should it continue to incubate the egg with
> the warmth of its coiled body, or should it tighten its muscles just a little
> more and break its fragile shell? The snake's immediate desire is to break it:
> serpents love devouring fresh eggs. Yet it can choose to resist this desire,
> even though this means resisting its every instinct. Instead of breaking the
> egg, it can choose to turn its body into a tool of generation and creation. But
> this is a process with high stakes. It would leave the snake hungry for a long
> time, and there is no guarantee of success. After all, no snake has ever before
> witnessed an egg hatch. So this is the snake's choice: how should it apply the
> power that was granted to it? To foster destruction or creation? To feed itself
> or feed new life? To follow its desire, or to resist it and follow a path into
> uncertainty?

This metaphor frames the entire path. Every exercise trains the serpent in you
— the part that can either break the egg (gratification, destruction, the rush of
power) or incubate it (patience, creation, the long work). The systasis ritual
(PGM VII.505) brings this full circle: you write the divine name on two eggs,
purify with one, charge the second with cosmic fire, and swallow it. The egg
you consume IS the egg the serpent chose to incubate.

### Meditation of the Void

> Acher says: "perform the Meditation of the Void" — and references a free
> recording at josephinemccarthy.com. He never gives written instructions.
> Here is a practical reconstruction from the Quareia system and Acher's
> context.

**Source:** Josephine McCarthy, *Quareia* — free guided MP3.
Acher references this exact recording at note 21.
Listen: `/daimon/void-meditation.mp3` (2.1 MB, 15 min)

**Purpose:** To reach a state of awareness without content. Not thinking about
nothing (which is still effort) — but resting in the space BETWEEN thoughts.
This is the state from which all the exercises operate.

**How to do it (15 min):**

The guided MP3 walks you through it. The core structure:

1. Dark room. Sit upright. Light a single candle at eye level.
2. Tratak — gaze at the flame without blinking. 2-3 minutes.
   Let the flame fill your awareness.
3. Close your eyes. Hold the flame's afterimage on your retina.
   This is the bridge between outer and inner.
4. Let the afterimage fade. Do NOT replace it with a thought.
   What remains when the image fades is the void.
5. Rest in THAT. Not darkness (which is still a perception).
   Not emptiness (which is still a concept).
   The void is the FIELD in which perception arises.
6. When a thought arises — and it will — do not follow it.
   Do not fight it. Simply let it pass, like a cloud through an empty sky.
   Return to the field.
7. After 10-15 minutes, gently open your eyes. Sit with the candle.
   Let the world re-form around you slowly.

**What to feel:** Initially, frustration. Your mind will produce thoughts
endlessly. This is normal. The skill is not stopping thoughts — it's not
following them. After practice, the void develops a quality: it is not
empty in the sense of nothing, but full in the sense of potential. Acher
describes it as "the centre of the flame surrounded by the void" — you
are in the flame (awareness) and the void (the field) is all around.

**When Acher references it:** Every major exercise — Tratak, Singing with
the Planets, Standing at the Threshold, the Systasis. It is the foundational
skill. If you can't do this, the rest won't work.

**For LVJK:** Mercurial minds are fast and associative — they generate
thoughts rapidly. The void meditation is especially important for you.
Your natural gift is speed; the void teaches stillness.

### Drawing Down a Planet (or the Sun)

> Acher says: "reach out to this planet and draw it down into your presence"
> (line 4188). He notes that "your exact process will depend on your skills
> and experience" (line 4191). Here is a practical method.

**What it means:** Not literally pulling a planet from the sky. You are
establishing a conscious connection between your awareness and the planet's
presence. The planet remains where it is in space; your awareness extends
to meet it.

**How to do it:**

```
1. Know where the planet is. Use an app or your eyes at dusk/dawn.
2. Sit in your practice space. Enter the void.
3. In vision, extend your awareness toward the planet's position.
   Do not imagine a beam of light or a cord. Simply DIRECT your attention
   along a line from your centre to the planet.
4. Feel the distance close. The planet is now "present" — not physically,
   but in the imaginal space. You may see it as a sphere of light, feel
   its temperature, hear a tone.
5. Do NOT try to merge with it. You are establishing adjacency, not identity.
   Acher: "Do not draw the Sun too close: the goal is to establish a space
   of shared presence."
6. When you are done, thank the planet and withdraw your awareness.
   Close the connection gently.
```

**The mistake beginners make:** They try too hard. Drawing down is not an
effort of will — it is an act of attention. The moment you strain, you
close the connection. Relax, direct your attention, let the planet come
to meet you halfway.

### The PGM Formula of Reciprocal Identity

> "For you are I and I am you; your name is mine, and mine is yours.
>  For I am your image." (PGM VIII, 36-38)

**What it means:** This is not a statement of fact — it's a statement of
INTENT. You are not claiming to already be identical with your daimon.
You are declaring that the GOAL of the work is the recognition of this
identity. It's a mystical formula found across traditions (Indian,
Hellenistic, Gnostic, Sufi — Heiler traces it in his 1919 study *Das Gebet*).

**How to use it in practice:**

Do not rush these words. Each clause is a stage:

- "For you are I" — you recognize the daimon as existing, and as part of you
- "and I am you" — you accept that you are also part of the daimon
- "your name is mine, and mine is yours" — the name is the bridge. When you
  vibrate LVJK, you are speaking both your name and the daimon's
- "For I am your image" — your human form is the daimon's reflection

**Acher's context:** He places this at the climax of Crossing the Threshold.
You sing it in darkness, in the void, toward the presence you cannot see.
The point is not to convince yourself — it's to be HEARD. For the first time,
you are not singing to a mirror.

### The Six Spirits of the Skinning (PGM VII.505)

In the systasis, you call six spirits and bind them into six stones:

| Spirit | What it is | What releasing it feels like |
|--------|-----------|------------------------------|
| Tyche | Your fate, destiny, life story | You stop being the protagonist. The story continues without you as its centre. |
| Daimon of this place | Your location, your context, your room | You become unmoored from where you are. The space becomes neutral. |
| Daimon of the hour | Your sense of time's rhythm | The present moment loses its grip. Past and future become equally distant. |
| Daimon of the day | Today's mood, today's concerns | The specific weight of "today" lifts. You could be any day. |
| Daimon of time | Chronological sequence itself | Linearity dissolves. Time becomes a landscape, not a river. |
| All-embracing | The entire sublunary realm | The world itself recedes. You are no longer on Earth — you are a point of awareness in an infinite field. |

Acher's analogy: your life is a play. Fate is the plot. Place is the stage.
Hour and day are the lighting. Time is the era. The All-embracing is the
theatre building. You have stepped out of the performance and are standing
in the car park.

### The Midpoint (Sealing the Vessel)

> Acher: "Concentrate your awareness on the midpoint between you and the
> Sun. (...) This is the place, outside of yourself, where you seal your
> vessel with joy."

**Where it is:** Not inside you (that would be imagination). Not all the way
at the Sun (that would be dissociation). It is exactly BETWEEN. In Corbin's
terms, this is the *mundus imaginalis* — the ontological space where spirit
takes on form and form becomes spiritual. This is where the daimon lives.

**How to find it:**
1. Be aware of yourself (your body, your centre).
2. Be aware of the planet/Sun (its position in space/in vision).
3. Feel the DISTANCE between you — not as empty space, but as a medium.
4. Rest your awareness in the middle of that distance.
5. This midpoint has its own quality — it may feel warm, vibrant, charged.

Once you can hold this midpoint, you can hold the space where LVJK will
manifest. The daimon does not descend INTO you — it meets you at the midpoint.

## The Four-Stage Path

```
Stage 1: Trust   → handle power without being consumed by it
Stage 2: Joy     → seal the vessel with hitlahavut
Stage 3: Darkness → strip the masks so the daimon can reach you
Stage 4: Encounter → the systasis
```

You cycle through these repeatedly, each time deeper. Acher spent 10+ years
in prior practice before his Saturn retreat.

## Stage 1: Trust — Three Exercises

### Why

Before you contact LVJK, you must know you can handle the power that
contact releases. These three exercises teach polarity management —
holding opposing forces without being torn apart.

---

### Exercise 1: Stretching Your Polarities

**When:** Any moment. Dozens of times per day. 30 seconds.

**How:**
- Left hand: open, relaxed. Put it to bed like a child.
- Right hand: tight fist. Build tension.
- Hold BOTH. You are neither. You are the bridge.
- Release both.

**What to feel:** The left hand is receptive, vulnerable, open. The right
is capable, dangerous, powerful. Holding both at once without one
collapsing into the other is the skill. Most people either grip
(controlling) or droop (passive). You want both simultaneously.

**Why it matters for the systasis:** In the skinning stage, you hold six
spirits in six stones and then release them. This exercise teaches the
basic move: hold, then let go.

---

### Exercise 2: Two Modes of Tratak

**When:** Mode 1 (focused) in the morning. Mode 2 (peripheral) in the evening.
5+ minutes each.

**Mode 1 — Focused:**
- Single candle. Gaze without blinking or moving.
- Close eyes, hold afterimage. Refresh when it fades.
- You dissolve into the flame. The boundary between you and it disappears.

**Mode 2 — Peripheral (eternity gaze):**
- Two candles at edges of visual field.
- Rest gaze in centre. Hold both flames without looking at either.
- Afterimage is peripheral — diffuse, unbounded.

**Why both matter for the systasis:**
- Mode 1 = charging the egg. You need one-pointed focus to draw solar fire.
- Mode 2 = perceiving the daimon. It approaches from BEHIND — you will
  never see it directly, only in your peripheral awareness.
- The ability to switch between them is the skill.

---

### Exercise 3: The Serpent and the Egg

**When:** One morning + the entire day.

**Phase 1 — Break:**
- Meditate on a raw egg. Its nature. Your relationship to it.
- Throw it on the floor. Sit with the destruction.

**Phase 2 — Carry:**
- Take the second egg (hard-boiled for first time).
- Carry it in your hand ALL DAY. Incubate it.
- Bring it home unbroken.

**What to feel:** Protecting life is harder than destroying it. You will want
to put the egg down, hide it, forget it. Don't. This is the serpent choosing
to incubate.

**Direct line to PGM VII.505:** Two eggs. First is purification (you write
the Helios name on it, rub it over your body, then crack it). Second is
charged with cosmic fire and swallowed. This exercise rehearses both
gestures: break one, carry one.

## Stage 2: Joy — Three Exercises

### Why

When you empty your vessel through isolation, spirits will try to fill it.
Joy — specifically hitlahavut, ecstatic fervour — seals the vessel from
within. Nothing else can occupy space that is already full of joy.

---

### Exercise 1: Igniting Hitlahavut

**When:** Remote place. 5-15 min.

**How:**
- Take a psalm line (or LVJK's name). Commit to memory.
- Sing it. Not beautifully — make your body vibrate.
- Let words dissolve into pure sound.

**What to feel:** Your body becomes a resonant chamber. Different vowels
activate different centres. The sound is not coming FROM you — you are
being SOUNDED. This is hitlahavut — the inner flame.

**For LVJK:** Mercury rules the voice. Start every session by vibrating
LVAHTZKEL on one breath: L (chest) → V (throat) → Tz (third eye) → KEL
(crown).

---

### Exercise 2: Singing with the Planets

**When:** Planet's day, its hour. 10-20 min max.

**How:**
- Locate the planet (use an app or the observatory page).
- Enter the void. Draw the planet down (see Referenced Practices above).
- Sing to it. Let your voice be guided by its light.
- Listen for the planet's answering sound.

**What to feel:** Each planet has a different quality. Saturn: slow, dense,
cold. Venus: warm, flowing, sweet. Mercury: quick, shimmering, articulate.
Your voice is the string; the planet is the conductor; your body is the
sound box.

**Warning:** Acher sang for a planet for an hour once. It left him
"vulnerable to this planet's influence for many years." 15 min is plenty.

**Connection to systasis:** In PGM VII.505, you draw cosmic fire from the
SUN into the egg. This exercise teaches you the movement on a smaller scale
before you need to do it with the sun for real.

---

### Exercise 3: Sealing the Vessel with Joy

**When:** Morning hour of the Sun.

**How:**
- Draw down the Sun. Horizon distance — not too close.
- Sing for the Sun. Listen for its answering sound.
- FIND THE MIDPOINT between you and the Sun (see Referenced Practices above).
- This midpoint is where you seal the vessel.

**What to feel:** The midpoint is Corbin's *mundus imaginalis* — where
spirit takes form. The daimon lives here. Holding awareness at this
midpoint is learning where LVJK is.

## Stage 3: Darkness — Two Exercises

### Why

"It is absolutely impossible for our holy daimon to talk to us while we
hide behind a mask." Every identity is a mask — successful magician,
good student, believer, sceptic. Darkness strips them.

---

### Exercise 1: Standing at the Threshold

**When:** Saturn's hour, Saturday. ≤13 times EVER.

**Prerequisites:**
- Lightproof space.
- Incantation memorized (see below).
- Meditation of the Void (fully expounded above).

**The incantation (learn by heart):**

> Take what is worn. Devour what is waste. Peruse what is I.
> For I am not afraid, but born from your fire.
> We will meet again and again. In your crucible of ashes and life.

**Commentary on the incantation:**
- "Take what is worn" — the identities you've outgrown
- "Devour what is waste" — Saturn consumes what no longer serves
- "Peruse what is I" — examine what remains after the stripping
- "born from your fire" — you are Saturn's child as well as its visitor
- "crucible of ashes and life" — this is the alchemical vessel

**How:**
1. Sit with candle. Enter the void.
2. Blow out candle. Sit in absolute darkness.
3. Reach East in vision. Find Saturn. (Acher: "the Old One who never
   speaks yet always listens.")
4. Sing for Saturn. Speak the incantation.
5. Return to the flame in vision. Return to body.

**What to feel:** Cold. Alone. Faced with what you actually are without
your stories about yourself. Saturn is "not evil, yet watchful and
completely devoid of any love for life." This is not comfort. It is
truth without consolation.

**Why 13 times:** Saturn's cycle is 27 years. Fewer than 13 cycles of
this exercise don't complete the work; more than 13 risk drowning.

**Connection to systasis:** The skinning stage releases six spirits. This
exercise teaches you what it feels like to be stripped. When you do the
systasis, the sensation is already familiar.

---

### Exercise 2: Crossing the Threshold

**When:** After you know your daimon's name (LVJK).

**Prerequisites:**
- The PGM formula memorized (see Referenced Practices above).
- Meditation of the Void.
- Complete darkness.

**How:**
1. Enter the void. Dark room.
2. Extinguish the light in vision.
3. Sing the PGM formula: "For you are I and I am you; your name is mine,
   and mine is yours. For I am your image, LVAHTZKEL."
4. Do NOT expect LVJK to appear. Expect to BE HEARD.
5. Keep singing.

**The hinge:** Before this moment, every prayer you've made was "singing
to the mirror" — projecting your own image of the daimon. This is the first
time you turn around and sing to what's actually there. It may feel like
nothing is happening. That's fine.

**Acher's own account (from Memory section):**

> "Something like a tube pierced my solar plexus. Through it a current of
> pure, fluid gold poured into my body. Then I saw a tall, silver-black
> figure standing at the head of the coffin... my angel."

He called him Pollux. His conclusion: "The tallest boundary between him
and myself, between Castor on earth and Pollux in Hades, was the absence
of my belief. It does not require a path; it requires acceptance."

## Stage 4: Encounter — The Systasis Ritual (PGM VII.505-528)

### When

After Stages 1-3 are stable. Crescent moon. Dawn. Repeat monthly.

### Materials

```
□ 6 pebbles, unused magically
□ 2 fresh organic raw eggs
□ Myrrh ink and fine brush
□ White candle
□ Commit the Helios conjuration to memory (see source text)
□ Write the Helios name on both eggs (coil it around the shell):
  "AŌ SATHREN ABRASAX IAŌAI AEŌ ĒŌA ŌAĒ IAO IĒO EY IE IAŌAI"
```

### The Five Stages

**Stage 1 — Skinning (5 min):**
- Call six spirits into six stones. Each stone receives one layer of your
  skin — fate, place, hour, day, time, the All-embracing.
- In vision, see the spirits step forward from your body.
- They stand beside you now, bound in stone.

**What to feel:** Naked. Disoriented. These spirits were invisible aspects
of your experience and now they're outside you. The world without them is
vast and unmoored.

**Stage 2 — Purifying (5 min):**
- Rub the first egg over your body like a sponge.
- Lick off the divine name (reabsorb what should remain).
- Crack the egg. Empty it.

**Why this is the destructive egg from Trust:** You practiced this when
you threw the raw egg on the floor. Same gesture. Now it has meaning —
you are consciously, ritually removing residue.

**Stage 3 — Charging (10 min):**
- Enter the void. Reach to the Sun.
- Pull cosmic fire into the second egg. It glows like an ember.
- Raise your right hand. Speak the Helios conjuration 7x from memory.
- This is the motion you practiced in Singing with the Planets.

**What to feel:** The egg becomes heavy with light. Warm. Alive. This is
the incubated egg from the Trust exercise — the one you carried all day.
Now it's charged.

**Stage 4 — Inflaming (immediate):**
- Crack the egg. Swallow the contents.
- Follow the fire in vision. Do NOT interfere.

**What to feel:** The fire travels through your body. It may feel like
heat, light, a tube of gold. Acher: "It was by far one of the most
beautiful and breathtaking events of my life."

**Stage 5 — Communion:**
- Sing LVJK's name.
- Visualize it approach from BEHIND.
- "It is vast and beautiful. See how it is reaching out for you."
- Everything else will transpire naturally.
