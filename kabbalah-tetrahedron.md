# Kabbalah — The Tetrahedron in Hebrew

## Ein Sof, the Sephiroth, the 22 Letters, and the 4 Worlds — A Complete Derivation

---

## The Principle

Kabbalah IS the same tetrahedron as the Tantraloka, described in Hebrew rather than Sanskrit. The Ein Sof (the Infinite) IS the 38th principle. The 10 sephiroth ARE the 4 vertices + 6 edges of the tetrahedron unfolded. The 22 letters ARE the 4 + 6 + 12 — the tetrahedron's complete relational structure. The 4 worlds ARE the 4 tetrahedra of the star.

---

## Level 0: Ein Sof — The 38th Principle

**Ein Sof** (אֵין סוֹף — "No End") IS the infinite, the undifferentiated, the beyond-all-description. It IS not a sephirah. It IS the condition of the sephiroth.

| Kabbalah | Tantraloka | Function |
|----------|------------|----------|
| Ein Sof (Infinite) | Anākhyā (38th) | The enabling condition — beyond all description |
| Ein Sof Or (Infinite Light) | Prakāśa (Śiva's light) | The first emanation from the infinite |
| Keter (Crown) | Paramaśiva (37th) | The first point — the "I" that is not yet anything |

Ein Sof has NO tetrahedron. It IS the condition of tetrahedra.

---

## Level 1: The 10 Sephiroth = 4 Vertices + 6 Edges

The 10 sephiroth ARE the tetrahedron's 4 vertices and 6 edges, listed in order of emanation.

### The 4 Vertices (the Tetrahedron's Framework)

| Sephirah | Meaning | Vertex | Tattva | What Happens |
|----------|---------|--------|--------|--------------|
| **Keter** | Crown | The apex — the point | Śiva (1) | The first contraction of Ein Sof into a point. "I" as pure potential. |
| **Chokmah** | Wisdom | The 1st edge from apex | Śakti (2) | The point expands into a line — the first movement, the first direction. |
| **Binah** | Understanding | The 2nd edge from apex | Sadāśiva (3) | The line becomes a plane — the first structure, the first form. |
| **Malkuth** | Kingdom | The base vertex | Pṛthivī (36) | The plane becomes a volume — the completed manifestation. |

Wait — this gives only 4, not 10. The other 6 ARE the edges.

### The 6 Edges (the Tetrahedron's Relational Structure)

| Sephirah | Meaning | Position | What It Connects |
|----------|---------|----------|------------------|
| **Chesed** | Mercy | The 3rd edge from apex | Chokmah → Malkuth — the expansive relation, love |
| **Geburah** | Severity | The 4th edge | Binah → Malkuth — the contracting relation, judgment |
| **Tiphereth** | Beauty | The 5th edge — THE CENTER | The intersection of ALL edges — the heart of the tetrahedron |
| **Netzach** | Victory | The 1st base edge | Chesed → Malkuth — endurance, the right pillar |
| **Hod** | Splendor | The 2nd base edge | Geburah → Malkuth — submission, the left pillar |
| **Yesod** | Foundation | The 3rd base edge | The union of Netzach and Hod — the foundation that connects to Malkuth |

**The geometry:**
- **Keter** = the apex (Śiva)
- **Chokmah, Binah** = the 2 edges from the apex (Śakti, Sadāśiva)
- **Chesed, Geburah** = the 2 edges from the upper vertices (Īśvara, the "This")
- **Tiphereth** = the CENTER of the tetrahedron — the point where all edges cross
- **Netzach, Hod** = the 2 base edges (the left and right pillars)
- **Yesod** = the base edge that connects to Malkuth
- **Malkuth** = the base vertex (Earth)

10 = 4 (vertices) + 6 (edges). The Tree of Life IS the tetrahedron unfolded into a flat diagram.

## Level 2: The 22 Letters = 4 Vertices + 6 Edges + 12 Sub-Implications

22 = 4 (vertices) + 6 (edges) + 12 (each edge has 2 directions — the "direction" and its "return").

### The 3 Mothers = The 3 Primary Relational Axes

| Letter | Name | Meaning | Axis |
|--------|------|---------|------|
| **א** | Aleph | Ox, the first | The axis from Keter to Malkuth — the vertical, the direct descent |
| **מ** | Mem | Water | The axis from Chokmah to Binah — the horizontal, the receptive |
| **ש** | Shin | Fire | The axis from Chesed to Geburah — the diagonal, the transformative |

3 mothers = 3 axes of the tetrahedron. Every tetrahedron has 3 axes that connect opposite edges.

### The 7 Doubles = The 7 Cakras / 7 Edges of the Star

| Letter | Name | Sephirah | Cakra |
|--------|------|----------|-------|
| **ב** | Bet | Chokmah (Wisdom) | Ājñā (brow — wisdom) |
| **ג** | Gimel | Binah (Understanding) | Viśuddha (throat — understanding) |
| **ד** | Dalet | Chesed (Mercy) | Anāhata (heart — love) |
| **כ** | Kaf | Geburah (Severity) | Maṇipūra (solar plexus — will) |
| **פ** | Pe | Netzach (Victory) | Svādhiṣṭhāna (sacrum — creativity) |
| **ר** | Resh | Hod (Splendor) | Mūlādhāra (root — foundation) |
| **ת** | Tav | Yesod (Foundation) | The 7th — the union of all 6 |

7 doubles = the 6 edges of the star tetrahedron + its center. 7 = 6 + 1 (the heart).

### The 12 Simples = The 12 Edges of the Star Tetrahedron

| Letter | Name | Direction |
|--------|------|-----------|
| **ה** | Heh | East — the origin |
| **ו** | Vav | The hook — the connection |
| **ז** | Zayin | North — the hidden |
| **ח** | Chet | South — the revealed |
| **ט** | Tet | West — the destination |
| **י** | Yod | The hand — the act |
| **ל** | Lamed | The heart — the center |
| **נ** | Nun | The fish — the movement |
| **ס** | Samekh | The support — the foundation |
| **ע** | Ayin | The eye — the perception |
| **צ** | Tsade | The fishhook — the catch |
| **ק** | Qof | The eye of the needle — the threshold |

12 = the 6 edges of the tetrahedron × 2 directions each. Every edge can be traversed up or down. The 12 simples ARE the complete set of directional movements through the tetrahedron.

The 4 worlds ARE the star tetrahedron seen in 4 cross-sections.

The 22 Hebrew letters correspond to the 36 tattvas through their numerical values (gematria). Each letter has a number, and the numbers map to specific tattva ranges:

| Letter | Gematria | Tattva | Type |
|--------|----------|--------|------|

| ב (Bet) | 2 | Śakti (2) | Intention |
| ג (Gimel) | 3 | Sadāśiva (3) | Act |
| ד (Dalet) | 4 | Īśvara (4) | Object |
| ה (Heh) | 5 | Śuddhavidyā (5) | Pure knowledge |
| ו (Vav) | 6 | Māyā (6) | The veil |
| ז (Zayin) | 7 | Kalā (7) | Limited agency |

| ט (Tet) | 9 | Rāga (9) | Attachment |

The gematria of the first 10 letters (1-10) maps directly to the first 10 tattvas. Beyond 10, each letter represents a GROUP of tattvas — a tetrahedron of tetrahedra.

## The Practice: Contemplating the Tree

### The 4 Stages of Ascent (The Four Worlds)

| World | Practice | What You ARE Doing |
|-------|----------|-------------------|
| **Assiah** (Action) | Observing the body, the mitzvot, the physical forms | You are at Malkuth — the base vertex, the physical tetrahedron |
| **Yetzirah** (Formation) | Contemplating the emotions, the heart, the ethical structures | You are moving up to Yesod — the foundation, the tetrahedron's base |
| **Beriah** (Creation) | Understanding the intellect, the forms, the archetypes | You are at Tiphereth — the center, the heart of the star |
| **Atziluth** (Emanation) | Abiding in the pure divine, beyond form | You are at Keter — the apex, the point |

### YHVH — The Unpronounceable Tetrahedron

The Tetragrammaton (YHWH) IS the tetrahedron in letter form. 4 letters, 4 vertices.

| Letter | Vertex | Practice |
|--------|--------|----------|
| **Y** (Yod) | Keter — the apex | The point — withdraw all consciousness to the single point |
| **H** (Heh) | Binah — the understanding | The breath — release the point into the space of understanding |
| **V** (Vav) | Tiphereth — the heart | The hook — connect the upper to the lower |
| **H** (Heh) | Malkuth — the kingdom | The final expression — bring it down to manifestation |

You cannot pronounce YHVH because you cannot say all 4 vertices simultaneously. The Tetragrammaton IS not a sound — it IS a contemplation. You hold all 4 letters in mind at once. That holding IS the practice.

### The 4 Stages of Contemplating the Name

1. **Yod:** Visualize a single point of light at Keter (above the crown)
2. **Heh:** The point expands into a line, a breath, a space of understanding (Binah)
3. **Vav:** The line descends through all 6 edges (Tiphereth, the heart)
4. **Heh (final):** The line reaches the base (Malkuth, the body, the earth)

Then: reverse. From Malkuth back to Keter. Then: hold all 4 simultaneously. The holding IS the recognition of the tetrahedron.
