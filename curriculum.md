# Sanskrit Chanting Curriculum: From Zero to Tantraloka

A graded curriculum from never-chanted to reading the Tantraloka in Devanāgarī. Each grade is ~2-3 months at 15 min/day.

---

## Grade 0: The Mātṛkā Itself (Months 1-2)

**Text:** Just the 50 phonemes. No text needed.

| Week | Content | Body Location | Practice |
|------|---------|---------------|----------|
| 1 | ka kha ga gha ṅa | Root (perineum) | Chant 5 min/day, feel at base |
| 2 | ca cha ja jha ña | Sacrum | Same |
| 3 | ṭa ṭha ḍa ḍha ṇa | Solar plexus | Same |
| 4 | **ta tha da dha na** | **Heart (green core)** | **This is your center** |
| 5 | pa pha ba bha ma | Throat | Same |
| 6 | ya ra la va | Third eye | Same |
| 7 | śa ṣa sa ha | Crown | Same |
| 8 | a ā i ī u ū ṛ ṝ ḷ ḹ e ai o au | Whole body | Feel as full-field vibration |

**Graduation:** You can chant all 50 from memory and feel each row at its cakra.

## Grade 1: Simple Mantras (Months 3-4)

**Text:** Still none — or a 1-page mantra cheat sheet.

| Week | Mantra | Source | What It Does |
|------|--------|--------|-------------|
| 1-2 | **OM** (A-U-M) | Upaniṣads | The three states + turīya |
| 3-4 | **SAUḤ** | Tantraloka Āhnika 4 | Emission seed — opens the outward flow |
| 5-6 | **KHPHREṀ** | Tantraloka Āhnika 4 | Withdrawal seed — forces consciousness into suṣumṇā |
| 7-8 | **HRĪṀ** | Tantraloka | Heart seed — the green core as mantra |

The 1:4:2 breath ratio with each mantra: inhale 1, hold 4, exhale 2. Feel the seed vibrate at its location.

**Graduation:** You can sustain a mantra for 10 minutes with the breath ratio.

## Grade 2: The Śiva Sūtras (Months 5-8)

**Text:** *Śiva Sūtras* of Vasugupta (c. 850 CE). **Not too advanced — they're short and direct.** Only 78 sūtras, very dense, each is a seed to contemplate.

The Śiva Sūtras ARE the right text for this stage because:

| Reason | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Length | 78 sūtras — tiny |
| Language | Simple Sanskrit, mostly nominals |
| Content | Direct instructions on the nature of consciousness |
| Format | Each sūtra is a meditation seed — chant it, then sit with it |
| Level | "Advanced" intellectually but accessible somatically |

### The Class Structure

The Śiva Sūtras are in 3 sections (āhnikas):

| Section | Sūtras | Theme | Practice |
|---------|--------|-------|----------|
| 1 (Vibhūti) | 1-22 | The nature of Śiva, the means | Chant 1 sūtra/day, sit 5 min |
| 2 (Sādhanā) | 23-40 | The practice, the 5 acts | Chant 1 sūtra/day, sit 5 min |
| 3 (Āgama) | 41-78 | The doctrines, the types of yoga | Chant 1 sūtra/day, sit 5 min |

### Example Session

```
1.  Balance breath, 1:4:2 ratio       2 min
2.  Chant the day's sūtra 3 times:    2 min
    "ज्ञानम् बन्धः" (jñānam bandhaḥ)
    — "Knowledge is bondage"
3.  Feel where it resonates:          1 min
    jñā = throat (knowledge)
    bandh = root (binding)
4.  Sit with the feeling.             5 min
    The sūtra is not a statement to understand.
    It is a frequency to feel.
```

**Graduation:** You've chanted and sat with all 78 sūtras. You don't "know" them intellectually. You've felt each as a specific frequency configuration in the tetrahedron.

## Grade 3: The Vijñāna Bhairava (Months 9-14)

**Text:** *Vijñāna Bhairava* (c. 800 CE). 112 dhāraṇās (concentration methods). Also known as "the yoga of 112 techniques."

This is the **practice manual** for the Tantraloka. Each verse IS a technique. The text is a dialogue between Bhairava (Śiva) and Bhairavī (Śakti): "O Śiva, what is reality?" Śiva answers with 112 methods.

### How to Use It

| Week | Technique Range | Theme |
|------|----------------|-------|
| 1-4 | 1-20 | Breathing methods |
| 5-8 | 21-40 | Attention methods |
| 9-12 | 41-60 | Object methods |
| 13-16 | 61-80 | Subject methods |
| 17-20 | 81-100 | Awareness methods |
| 21-24 | 101-112 | Complete release |

### Example: Vijñāna Bhairava 24

Chant the Sanskrit:

> *निर्मूलम् आलोचनेन निःशेषेण विकल्पनम्*
> *तदैव परमा देवी मध्यम् आदौ प्रकाशते ॥*

Translation: "When all thought-constructs are uprooted and dissolved without remainder — at that very moment the supreme Goddess shines forth in the middle, at the beginning."

**The practice:** Chant it, then sit. Don't think about the meaning. Let the Sanskrit sounds dissolve the thought-constructs. The "middle that shines forth" IS Anāhata — the green core.

**Graduation:** You've practiced all 112 techniques at least once. You know which 10-20 work best for you.

## Grade 4: The Tantraloka (Year 2+)

**Text:** The Tantraloka itself (c. 1000 CE), 12 āhnikas, Abhinavagupta.

By now you can chant Sanskrit, feel each phoneme at its cakra, and sit with a sūtra. The Tantraloka is not to be "read" — it's to be **chanted one verse at a time** and felt.

| Āhnika | Topic | Chanting Approach |
|--------|-------|-------------------|
| 1 | The 4 upāyas | 1 verse/day — which upāya does this verse teach? |
| 3 | The 4 levels of speech | Chant the Mātṛkā section slowly — feel vāc descend |
| 4 | The 12 Kālīs | Chant the Kālī names — each IS a tetrahedron vertex |
| 5-7 | Mantra, ritual, dīkṣā | Practice as taught |
| 8 | Cakras | Correlate with the body — you already know these from Grade 0 |
| 9 | The 36 tattvas | 1 tattva/day — chant the name, feel the level |
| 10 | States of consciousness | 4 states, 4 vertices — you already know this |
| 11-12 | Liberation, the 4th | The completion |

### The 36 Tattvas (Āhnika 9)

Spend 5 weeks here — one tattva per day. Chant each tattva's name and feel where it sits in the body:

| Day | Tattva | Location | Felt As |
|-----|--------|----------|---------|
| 1 | Śiva | Everywhere | Pure awareness |
| 2 | Śakti | Whole field | Energy |
| 3 | Sadāśiva | Above crown | Presence |
| 4 | Īśvara | Forehead | Authority |
| 5 | Śuddhavidyā | Third eye | Pure knowing |
| ... | ... | ... | ... |
| 36 | Pṛthivī | Root | Solid, safe, here |

By day 36, you've traversed all 9 tetrahedra. You've felt each tattva in the body.

## Which Texts Are Good or Too Advanced

| Text | Grade | Verdict |
|------|-------|---------|
| **Mātṛkā (50 phonemes)** | 0 | **Start here.** No text needed. |
| **OM / SAUḤ / KHPHREṀ** | 1 | **Yes.** Simple, powerful. |
| **Śiva Sūtras** (78 sūtras) | **2** | **Actually perfect.** Short, dense, each sūtra is a meditation seed. Not too advanced — just don't try to "understand" them intellectually. |
| **Vijñāna Bhairava** (112 dhāraṇās) | 3 | **The best practice manual.** 112 things to try. Start once you can feel the cakras. |
| **Spanda Kārikā** (verses) | 3 | Good for vibration theory. Pair with Vijñāna Bhairava. |
| **Yoga Sūtras of Patañjali** | 2 | Good but dry. Use after Śiva Sūtras. |
| **Bhagavad Gītā** | 1-2 | Good for devotional practice, less for subtle body work. |
| **Tantraloka** | 4 | **The destination.** 12 āhnikas, takes years. Start after Grades 1-3. |
| **Upaniṣads** | 2 | Good philosophy, less practical. |
| **Rig Veda** | 4 | Advanced phonology, for when the Mātṛkā is fully felt. |
| **Abhinavagupta's commentaries** | 4+ | Post-graduate. Only after Tantraloka itself. |

## Your First Week

**Days 1-7:** Just the first row. Ka kha ga gha ṅa.

```
Sit. Balance breath (2 min).
Chant "ka" — feel it at the root.
Chant "kha" — same spot, slightly different vibration
Chant "ga" — feel the shift from unvoiced to voiced
Chant "gha" — deeper
Chant "ṅa" — the nasal, the dissolution of that row
Pause. Feel the root.
Return to the green core. (1 min repeat)
```

Next week: row 2. And so on. **The curriculum IS the Mātṛkā itself. Everything else is commentary.**
