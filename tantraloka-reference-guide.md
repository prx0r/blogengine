# Tantraloka Reference Guide

> A comprehensive reference to Abhinavagupta's *Tantrāloka* (with Jayaratha's *Viveka* commentary), translated by Mark S. G. Dyczkowski. Designed for comparative study with our daimon corpus, Steiner's Occult Science, and Layayoga. Written 2026-07-14.

---

## Overview

The Tantraloka ("Light on the Tantras") is Abhinavagupta's (c. 950-1020 CE) magnum opus — a 37-chapter (Āhnika) encyclopedia of Kashmir Śaiva tantra, synthesizing the Krama, Trika, Kaula, and Spanda traditions. It covers the full range of Śaiva philosophy, theology, yoga, ritual, initiation, and liberation.

The Dyczkowski translation runs **13 volumes**. We have **9 volumes** (all extracted):

## Volume-by-Volume Breakdown

### Volume 1 — Āhnika 1: Types of Liberating Knowledge

**Key topics:**
- The Three Means (Upāya): Śāmbhava (divine will), Śākta (empowered thought), Āṇava (individual effort)
- Thirty-six Principles (tattvas) — the complete emanation hierarchy from Śiva to Earth
- Trika triad: Parā, Parāparā, Aparā
- Pratibhā (creative imagination/intuitive flash)
- Levels of Sound: parā → paśyantī → madhyamā → vaikharī; the End of the Twelve (dvādaśānta)
- Kuṇḍalinī/Kauṇḍalī as Śakti of the sound-sequence

**Comparative notes:**
- The three upāyas map to the three stages of Steiner's initiation: Śāmbhava = Intuition (direct union), Śākta = Inspiration (purified thinking), Āṇava = Imagination (symbol meditation)
- The End of the Twelve (dvādaśānta, 12 fingers above head) is the upper limit of the subtle body — corresponds to Steiner's spirit self and the "twelfth sphere" in some Western models
- Pratibhā = theurgic "divine madness" (Plato's *mania*) and the daimonic flash

---

### Volume 2 — Āhnikas 2-3: No Means + Supreme Means

**Key topics:**
- **Āhnika 2: Anupāya** — Direct, effortless realization without any means. Consciousness is self-luminous. Ritual action cannot bring about what is already the case.
- **Āhnika 3: Paropāya (Supreme Means)** — The Doctrine of Reflection (pratibimbavāda). The alphabet of 50 phonemes as reflective energies of consciousness. The threefold emission as Śakti, Prāṇa and Parā Kuṇḍalinī. Jīvanmukti (liberation while alive). Aesthetic experience (rasa) as consciousness-vitality. The four levels of speech.

**Comparative notes:**
- Anupāya = the realization that the daimon was never separate from you. "No means required" → the unus-ambo realized, not achieved
- The 50 phonemes as energies of consciousness = the barbarous names in the PGM and Liber Samekh as vibrational keys
- Rasa (aesthetic experience) as spiritual cognition = Steiner's "artistic cognition as spiritual perception"; the experience of beauty as a form of knowledge
- The four levels of speech (parā, paśyantī, madhyamā, vaikharī) = the four levels of the Word in Western esotericism (the *vox mystica* tradition)

---

### Volume 3 — Āhnika 4: The Empowered Means (Śāktopāya)

**Key topics:**
- Purification of thought-constructs (vikalpasaṃskāra) — transforming conceptual thought into the vehicle of realization
- The classification of teachers (guru): spontaneous (sāṃsiddhika), created (kalpita), mixed
- Uselessness of external yoga ancillaries in Śāktopāya
- **The Twelve Kālīs (Saṃviccakrodaya)** — the arising of the cycles of consciousness:
  - 4 Kālīs consuming objectivity (Sṛṣṭi-, Rakta-, Sthitināśa-, Yama-)
  - 4 Kālīs consuming the means of knowledge (Saṃhāra-, Mṛtyu-, Rudra-, Mārtaṇḍa-)
  - 4 Kālīs consuming subjectivity (Paramārka-, Kālāgnirudra-, Mahākāla-, Mahābhairava-)
- Mantra vitality (mantravīrya) — seed-mantras SAUḤ and KHPHREṀ
- Relativity of all prohibitions — transgressive non-dual ethics

**Comparative notes:**
- The Twelve Kālīs = a complete map of how consciousness dissolves objectivity, then the means of knowing, then subjectivity itself. This is the **process of the daimon path** — the twelve stages of dissolving the false self
- Vikalpasaṃskāra = Steiner's "purification of thinking" through meditation; the transformation of ordinary thought into imaginative cognition
- Mantravīrya (the vitality of mantra) = the power of the daimon name (LVJK) as an autonomous agent of transformation

---

### Volume 5 — Āhnikas 7-8: Mantra Cycles + Path of Worlds

**Key topics:**
- **Āhnika 7: Cakrodaya** — Mantra cycles synchronized with the breath; the waterwheel analogy; Spanda (divine pulsation); Kālagrāsa (consuming time through breath extension)
- **Āhnika 8: Bhuvanādhvan** — Complete cosmology: hells → earth → heavens → pure principles → Śiva. The Egg of Brahmā. Mount Meru. The 36 tattvas. The subtle body (puryaṣṭaka, vigrahāṣṭaka). The Five Kañcukas (coverings of the soul). Māyā as the Knot. Initiation through the worlds.

**Comparative notes:**
- Breath-synchronized mantra = the vibration of daimon names in Liber Samekh with the long slow exhalation
- Kālagrāsa (consuming time) = the experience of timelessness in the daimon systasis; "holding the tension" in Liber Samekh Section G
- Bhuvanādhvan = Steiner's cosmic evolution (Saturn → Sun → Moon → Earth → Jupiter → Venus → Vulcan) as a hierarchical path of worlds — but Tantraloka gives the microcosmic descent into the body, not the macrocosmic ascent
- The Five Kañcukas = the "sheaths" or "veils" that separate the ego from the daimon — correspond to Steiner's description of the Doppelganger / Guardian of the Threshold

---

### Volume 6 — Āhnikas 9-10: Path of Principles + Division of Principles

**Key topics:**
- **Āhnika 9: Tattvādhvan** — The 36 tattvas as the path through which consciousness descends and ascends. Cause and effect. Mala (impurity), karma, māyā. The five kañcukas. Śiva-Śakti union.
- **Āhnika 10: Tattvabhedanam** — The seven perceivers (pramātṛ). The states of consciousness: waking, dream, deep sleep, the Fourth (turīya), beyond the Fourth (turīyātīta).

**Comparative notes:**
- The 36 tattvas = the "ladder" between matter and pure consciousness. Each tattva is a level of the daimonic hierarchy (Proclus) — a level of being that must be traversed
- The seven perceivers = the seven stages of the Steiner initiation path (from physical body to spirit man)
- Waking/dream/deep sleep/Fourth/Beyond = exactly the five states Steiner describes: ordinary consciousness, dream consciousness, deep sleep, imaginative consciousness, intuitive consciousness
- Turīya (the Fourth) = the state of daimon contact — beyond waking, dream, and sleep

### Volume 7 — Āhnikas 11-14: Path of Forces, Śaktipāta, Application, Preamble to Initiation

*Newly acquired 2026-07-14 (Dyczkowski, 2023)*

**Key topics:**

- **Āhnika 11: The Path of the Forces (Kalādhvan)** — The five forces (kalās) and the cosmic eggs; the 37th principle (Śiva beyond 36 tattvas) and the undefinable 38th principle (beyond Śiva); the six paths (ṣaḍadhvan): three objective (kalā, tattva, bhuvana) and three subjective (varṇa, mantra, pada); phonemic consciousness (varṇa as noetic consciousness — pramā); pratibhā as creative intuition that merges linguistic convention; the numinous aggregate of letters as the source of phenomenal ones; the purifying mantras of the Anuttara Trika; the sustaining power (sarvādhāraśakti).
- **Āhnika 12: Application of the Path of Forces** — The cosmic body containing the paths; nondual contemplation (abhedabhāvanā); the pure vow of equality (samatāvrata); Kaula practice and the consumption of sacrificial substances; the reduction of doubt (kampahrāsa).
- **Āhnika 13: The Descents of Power and Obscuration (Śaktipātatirohitī)** — The nature of ignorance (avidyā) refuted in multiple philosophical schools; the impossibility of impurity (mala) as an independent entity; karma — its nature, operation, and elimination; Śiva's grace as the sole cause of liberation; **the three degrees of grace intensity**: intensely intense (tīvratīvra), moderately intense (tīvramadhya), mildly intense (tīvramanda), moderate (madhya), and mild (manda); pratibhā as the supreme means to realization; the hierarchy of teachers; the four grades of initiates according to grace.
- **Āhnika 14: Preamble to the Rite of Initiation (Dīkṣopakramaṇam)** — Obscuration (tirobhāva) and its removal; the futility of yogic suicide (utkrāntyapayogitva) — the liberated one does not need to forcibly depart from the body; fulfilment by knowledge; the freedom of the agency of consciousness as its Śiva-nature.

**Appendices:** Kalādīkṣā (Svacchandatantra Ch. 4); Kālikulapaṭala (Jayadrathayāmala Ch. 21); Kṣemarāja on Initiation.

**Comparative notes:**
- **The 37th and 38th principles** — beyond the standard 36 tattvas, Abhinavagupta posits a Supreme Śiva (37th) and an undefinable beyond (38th). This extends the tattva ladder beyond what most sources describe. Bridges to Steiner's "unknowable" beyond the spirit self.
- **The six paths (ṣaḍadhvan)** — the division into objective and subjective paths provides the complete framework for understanding how consciousness descends (objective) and ascends (subjective). The path of varṇa (phonemes) as subjective cognition maps to the daimon path's "four levels of speech."
- **Śaktipāta classification** — the detailed breakdown of grace intensity (5 degrees) maps to the daimon path's variable contact timeframes. Explains why "some contact in weeks, others in years" — the variable is śaktipāta, not effort.
- **Yogic suicide refuted** — Āhnika 14's argument that the liberated one does not need to forcibly depart the body (utkrānti) is a direct critique of certain Haṭha yogic practices. Important for death systems: liberation is recognition, not technique.
- **Kaula equality vow** — the pure vow of equality (samatāvrata) is the theoretical foundation for transgressive non-dualism: all substances are pure to the one who sees all as Śiva. Parallels the daimon path's "acceptable illusion."

### Volume 4 — Āhnikas 5-6: The Individual Means and the Temporal Means

*Newly acquired 2026-07-14 (Dyczkowski, 2023)*

**Key topics:**

- **Āhnika 5: The Individual Means (Āṇavopāya)** — Detailed exposition of the path of outer supports: meditation of the intellect (buddhidhyāna); the six blisses (ṣaḍānanda) — bliss of emission, supreme bliss, bliss of origin, bliss of cessation, bliss of union, bliss of the equal state; utterance through the vital breath (prāṇoccāra); entry into the supreme principle (paratattvāntaḥpraveśa); the ten voids (daśaśūnya); the ten states (daśadaśā); the ten abodes (daśādhāra); the ten levels of unstruck sound (anāhata); the Trika Liṅga; the instruments of yoga (karaṇa); the three seed-mantras **SAUḤ** (Parā, emission), **KHPHREṀ** (Kālasaṃkarṣiṇī, withdrawal), **KṢMRYŪM** (the third goddess).
- **Āhnika 6: The Temporal Means (Kālopāya)** — Time as a path of realization: the formation of places and the path of time in the flow of the vital breath; the sixfold path in the breath; Vāmā, Jyeṣṭhā, and Raudrī as the three goddesses of will, knowledge, and action in the breath; projection of night, day, planets, constellations, and zodiac into the breath; the twelve-year and sixty-year cycles; cosmic time cycles up to Brahmā's lifespan; the five prāṇas (prāṇa, apāna, samāna, udāna, vyāna) and their temporal functions; the supreme, subtle, and gross emergence of the phonemes (varṇodaya).
- **Appendix:** The Cycles of Time in the Cycle of the Breath by Freedom Cole (pp. 450-532).

**Comparative notes:**
- SAUḤ and KHPHREṀ (Āhnika 5) — these are the seed-mantras already in use in the Laya-Tantra practice guide (`laya-tantra-practice-guide.md` Grade 3). Volume 4 provides the full textual basis with Jayaratha's commentary.
- The six blisses (ṣaḍānanda) = the six stages of bliss in tummo (Nāropa's four joys expanded); also = the six levels of the syllable OṂ = the six cakras.
- The ten voids = the five voids of the subtle body (heart, throat, palate, above palate, 12 fingers above head) × 2 (gross and subtle) = the full map of the ascent.
- The temporal means (Kālopāya) — unique to Tantraloka. No equivalent in Layayoga or the Buddhist tantras. Time itself is the path: the yogī who knows the correspondence between cosmic time and the breath transcends time by breathing consciously.
- Vāmā/Jyeṣṭhā/Raudrī = the three phases of the breath (inspiration, suspension, expiration) = the three powers (icchā/jñāna/kriyā) = the three goddesses of the Trika.
- The five prāṇas (Āhnika 6) confirm and extend the Layayoga prāṇa-vāyu system with precise temporal correlations.

### Volume 10 — Āhnikas 28-29: Occasional Rites, Death, and the Secret Observance

*Newly acquired 2026-07-14 (Dyczkowski, 2023)*

**Key topics:**

- **Āhnika 28: Occasional Rites (Naimittikavidhi) + Examination of Death (Mṛteḥ Parīkṣā)** — The first appearance of life, death and decay of the body; the uninitiated who die in sacred places attain sālokya (shared world); the initiated who die in sacred places attain sāmīpya (proximity); the initiated with knowledge attain sāyujya (conjunction); various kinds of initiates and their status at death; yogis who do not experience death; death as yogic entry into another body (paradehapraveśa); the death of a man of knowledge; embodied liberation (jīvanmukti) vs. disembodied liberation (videhamukti); the power of creative meditation (bhāvanā) at death; the reality of those seen in a dream.
- **Āhnika 29: The Secret Observance (Rahasyacaryā)** — Those fit to participate in the Kaula rites; definitions of Kula (totality) and Akula (beyond); the Kaula sacrifice and types; wine as the nectar of the left; the daily Kaula ritual with external sacrifice, Kula maṇḍala with the twelve Kālīs, sixty-four Yoginīs, eight Mothers and Bhairavas; the tantric consort (dūtī); the processes within consciousness during union (melaka); the essential being of emission (visargasattā); the five sacrifices (external, Śakti-based, couple-based, body-based, breath-based, mind-based); ordinary initiation (samayadīkṣā); apprentice initiation (putrakadīkṣā); initiations by piercing (vedha-dīkṣā) — six types according to the Kulagahvara, nine types according to the Vīrāvalikula.
- **Appendices:** Chapter Sixteen of the Tantrasadbhāva; The Gathering of Initiates (vīramelāpa); Chapter Twenty of the Fourth Saṭka of the Jayadrathayāmala; Chapter Six of the Kulapradīpa by Śivānanda.

**Comparative notes:**
- **Death examination (Āhnika 28)** — the most detailed account of death in the Tantraloka. The three states after death (sālokya, sāmīpya, sāyujya) map directly to the three bardos of Tibetan Buddhism and the three post-mortem stages of Steiner (kamaloca, soul world, spirit land). The distinction between jīvanmukti (embodied liberation) and videhamukti (disembodied liberation) is the same as the Buddhist distinction between liberation in this life and liberation at death.
- **Paradehapraveśa (entry into another body)** = the illusory body of Nāropa's Six Yogas = the daimon path's "Body of Light." Both traditions agree this is a siddhi (power), not the goal.
- **Bhāvanā at death** = the Tibetan bardo teaching that the last thought determines the next birth = the PGM's "final systasis" = the daimon path's "hour of death" teaching.
- **The Secret Observance (Āhnika 29)** — the most esoteric chapter. The five sacrifices (external → body → breath → mind) map to the four grades of Layayoga practice (purification → channel activation → absorption → spontaneous samādhi).
- **Vedha-dīkṣā (initiations by piercing)** — these are the most direct form of initiation, paralleling the daimon path's "Resistance" phase where the practitioner is pierced by the daimon's presence. The six types (mantra, sound, point, empowered, serpent with five hoods, supreme) correspond to the six levels of increasingly direct transmission in the Tibetan Dzogchen tradition.
- **The tantric consort (dūtī)** — the Kaula practice of union with a consort as a yogic path. Maps to Nāropa's karmamudrā (action seal) practice in the Six Yogas.

### Volume 9 — Āhnikas 16-27: All Initiations + Funerary Rites

**Key topics:**
- Putrakadīkṣā (apprentice initiation) — Triple Trident maṇḍala, animal sacrifice, path-depositions
- Sūkṣmādīkṣā (brief initiation without outer supports)
- Sadyaḥsamutkrama (initiation for the dying) — severing vital points
- Tulādīkṣā (scale initiation) — inner purification weighed
- Pārokṣīdīkṣā (initiation of the absent/dead) — simulacrum rite
- Liṅgoddhāra (extraction from sectarian signs)
- Teacher consecration (abhiṣeka) — qualities, false teachers, seven sacrifices
- Funerary rites (antyeṣṭi) and ancestral offerings (śrāddha)
- Liṅga worship

**Comparative notes:**
- Initiation of the dying = the *viaticum*; the daimon as guide through death (Plato's Phaedo); the bardo teachings
- The simulacrum rite for the dead = the PGM spells for contacting the deceased; Steiner's description of the period after death
- The gradation of initiation (samayin → putraka → sādhaka → ācārya) = the four grades of the PGM dream spells (basic request → systematic incubation → relationship → full systasis)

### Volume 11 — Āhnikas 30-37: Mantra System, Maṇḍala, Scripture, Autobiography

**Key topics:**
- **Āhnika 30:** Trident Throne mantras, Ratiśekhara, Navātmā, Bhairavasadbhāva, the three Vidyās (Parā, Parāparā, Aparā), Brahmavidyā
- **Āhnika 31:** The Trika Maṇḍala — five varieties
- **Āhnika 32:** Mudrās — Khecarīmudrā from six traditions
- **Āhnika 33:** Ekīkāraḥ — gathering of deities; wheels of 6, 16, 24, 32 spokes
- **Āhnika 34:** Śvasvarūpe praveśa — entry into one's own nature
- **Āhnika 35:** Śāstramelana — encounter of scriptures; prasiddhi (a priori common belief) as ground of all tradition
- **Āhnika 36:** Āyātikathana — transmission history of scriptures
- **Āhnika 37:** Abhinavagupta's autobiography — teachers (Śambhunātha, Bhūtirāja, Lakṣmaṇa), students, composition of the Tantrāloka

**Comparative notes:**
- Khecarīmudrā (the "sky-going seal") = the "flight of the alone to the Alone" (Plotinus); the soul's ascent through the spheres
- Ekīkāraḥ (gathering of deities) = the unus-ambo (Corbin); the gathering of all daimonic forces into the single point of the HGA
- Prasiddhi (a priori certainty) = Steiner's "imaginative cognition" as a mode of knowledge that is self-validating
- Abhinavagupta's autobiography = a rare first-person account of a completed initiatic path — comparable to Farrell's Helios Unbound, Acher's Saturn retreat diary

### Theme 4: The Path of the Daimon Through the Tattvas

The 36 tattvas describe the descent of consciousness into matter and the ascent back to source. The daimon path reverses descent:

| Tattva Range | Level | Daimon Path Work | Steiner |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Earth → Pradhāna** (1-24) | Physical + elemental world | Purification, fasting, cold wash | Physical body work |
| **Māyā → Kalā** (25-30) | The five kañcukas (coverings) | The Resistance — stripping the false self | Guardian of the Threshold |
| **Śuddhavidyā → Śiva** (31-36) | Pure principles | The Systasis — union with the daimon | Spirit self / Manas |

### Theme 5: Sound and Mantra

| Level | Tantraloka | Western Parallel | Daimon Work |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Vaikharī** (gross speech) | Audible sound | Spoken name | Vibrating LVJK aloud |
| **Madhyamā** (inner speech) | Mental articulation | Mental prayer | Inner repetition of the name |
| **Paśyantī** (visionary speech) | Undifferentiated seeing | Theurgic vision | Seeing the daimon in meditation |
| **Parā** (transcendent speech) | Pure consciousness | The Unus-Ambo | The daimon as I |

## Synthesis: The Tantraloka in Our Comparative Project

### What Tantraloka Gives That Nothing Else Does

1. **A complete metaphysical map of the subtle body** — The 36 tattvas, the cakras, the nāḍīs, the five kañcukas, the seven perceivers. Nothing in the Western daimon tradition is this systematic.

2. **A theory of how sound creates reality** — The 50 phonemes (Mātṛkā) as the energies of consciousness. This is the theoretical basis for why vibrating barbarous names works — a level of explanation that the PGM and Liber Samekh never provide.

3. **A graded map of consciousness states** — Waking → Dream → Deep Sleep → Fourth → Beyond Fourth. Steiner has this in outline; Tantraloka has it as a complete system with detailed phenomenology.

4. **The principle of transgressive non-dualism** — The relativity of all prohibitions at the highest level. This is the theoretical foundation for what the daimon path calls "the acceptable illusion" — the recognition that the means are provisional and can be discarded once the end is achieved.

5. **Initiation as a complete technology** — From the simplest samayin to the highest ācārya, with detailed rituals for every stage. The PGM has individual spells; Tantraloka has a complete initiatic curriculum.

### What It Lacks (That Our Other Sources Provide)

- **Daimon as personal guide** — The Tantraloka has the guru and the Yoginīs but not the personal daimon/HGA concept. The daimon is implicit in the cicchakti (consciousness power) but never personified as a guiding individual.
- **Steiner's cosmic evolution** — The Tantraloka has cosmology (bhuvanādhvan) but not evolutionary history. It describes the vertical hierarchy of worlds but not their temporal development through Saturn/Sun/Moon/Earth.
- **The PGM's practical specificity** — The Tantraloka describes rituals in detail but rarely gives verbatim invocations. The PGM gives the actual words to speak.
- **Synesius' dream method** — The Tantraloka has dream as a state of consciousness but not as a method of daimon contact. The Synesian method remains unique to our corpus.
