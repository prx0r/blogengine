# Trifecta Vision — Multi-Medium Creative Studio

> *"Don't think in terms of 'should we use PIL or historical art or image gen?' Think in terms of 'what is the epistemic job of this shot?' Then the medium becomes obvious."*

---

## The Core Decision Rule

Choose media by **what job the shot has to do**, not by "what looks cool."

| Passage job | Default medium |
|---|---|
| Historical person, manuscript, place, object, lineage | Historical asset |
| Causal process, transformation, comparison, hierarchy | Procedural PIL |
| New visionary atmosphere or symbolic interpretation | Generated image |
| Rich technical plate requiring custom forms | AI base + PIL semantic animation |
| Archive-heavy sequence | Historical slideshow / FableCut |
| Exact climax that recombines earlier systems | PIL or hybrid with PIL controlling motion |

## Decision Order

1. What claim is being made?
2. What must change visibly?
3. Is the image evidence, reconstruction, model, or atmosphere?
4. Does an authentic source asset exist?
5. Does the scene require exact process control?
6. Would generation add useful material richness or merely introduce uncertainty?
7. Can the shot pass silently?

## Medium Rules

### Historical assets — Use when the narration refers to:
- a real yogi, lineage, manuscript, place, statue, ritual object, or historical period
- iconography whose authenticity matters
- a factual claim that should be grounded rather than illustrated imaginatively

Historical images must carry provenance and an evidence role. They should not be stretched into explaining processes they do not actually depict.

### Procedural PIL — Use when the passage contains verbs such as:
- descend, reverse, seal, gather, divide, preserve, suspend
- enter, circulate, exhaust, replenish, transform
- compare, branch, align, phase-lock, recur

PIL is the studio's semantic engine because it can make the causal operation exact.

### Generated imagery — Use when the passage needs:
- a visionary or imaginal scene that does not exist historically
- a custom material world
- a clean technical base plate too expensive to hand illustrate
- a one-off symbolic scene with strong art direction

Generated imagery should be labeled as interpretation or reconstruction and should not masquerade as historical evidence.

### Hybrid shots — The strongest pattern:
```
historical or AI image supplies material richness
→ PIL isolates relevant forms
→ PIL performs the exact transformation
→ FableCut or ffmpeg assembles and times the shot
```

## The Amṛtasiddhi Test Case

A perfect multi-medium essay because it contains: historical textual world + precise yogic process + symbolic/alchemical imagination.

**Recommended breakdown:** 20-30% historical, 45-55% PIL, 15-25% image generation — with many of the best shots being hybrids.

### Chapter-by-Chapter Medium Map (Amṛtasiddhi)

| Chapter | Function | Best Medium | Why |
|---------|----------|-------------|-----|
| A. Opening the World | Establish text, world, transmission | Historical images | Viewer needs to feel "this is a real medieval tradition" |
| B. The Problem: Nectar Lost | Explain descending essence → depletion | PIL procedural diagram | Causal system needs exact visual mechanism |
| C. Yogic Alchemy Vocabulary | Link process to inherited symbols | Historical + PIL annotations | Show symbols as historically inherited, not invented |
| D. Reversal | The dramatic turn: seal and reverse | PIL | Explanatory heart — must pass mute test completely |
| E. Mahāmudrā/Bandha/Vedha | Three operative yogic means | AI technical plate + PIL animation | Multiple distinct operations needing visual distinction |
| F. Central Channel | Dual currents → single central route | PIL | Vector-field logic: phase, convergence, emergence |
| G. Imaginal Body | Body as alchemical apparatus | Hybrid: AI base + PIL flows | Body-vessel needs elegance, not medical or chakra art |
| H. Nectar/Immortality | Preservation, altered relation to decay | PIL + AI imagery | Two jobs: explain (PIL) and evoke (AI) |
| I. Manuscripts/Transmission | Textual history, inheritance | Historical images + PIL lineage | Historical argument, not symbolic mood |
| J. Final Synthesis | All systems together | PIL-dominant hybrid | Recombine continuity systems into one coherent machine |

```
Container/VPS = PIL, ffmpeg, fonts, final muxing
```

## The Global Visual Grammar

To prevent visual chaos when mixing media, enforce:

- white or parchment-white dominant field
- black ink structures
- restrained crimson for essence/flow
- muted gold for awakened/conserved states
- grey for exhausted/ordinary conditions
- minimal typography
- slow, precise motion
- recurring continuity objects across all media

## Recurring Continuity Systems (Amṛtasiddhi)

1. Upper lunar reservoir
2. Descending drop
3. Lower consuming fire
4. Sealed gate
5. Dual side currents
6. Central ascending route
7. Textual folio / transmission marker

These recur across media: a reservoir may first appear as historical iconography, then as PIL diagram, then as generated imaginal moon.

## Medium Scoring Rubric

For each candidate medium, score 1-5 on:

- **Claim fidelity** — does it accurately represent the passage?
- **Transformation expressivity** — can it show change over time?
- **Asset authenticity** — is the source material genuine?
- **Visual impact** — is it compelling to watch?
- **Continuity compatibility** — does it fit with surrounding shots?
- **Silent intelligibility** — can the concept be understood without audio?
- **Factual ambiguity** (negative) — could it mislead?
- **Style drift** (negative) — does it break the visual grammar?
- **Production risk** (negative) — how likely is failure?

## Verdict

> PIL is the semantic spine. Historical art gives truth-weight. Image generation gives imaginal richness. The triad is the whole studio.

Do not think "should we use PIL or historical art or image gen?" Think "what is the epistemic job of this shot?" Then the medium becomes obvious.
