# Rationale Exercise: Malas Pack — Scene-by-Scene Visual Logic

## Scene ml01: "The Three Veils" (overview_filters)

**Narration:** "Pure consciousness contracts through āṇava, māyīya, and kārma filters."

**What the render does:**
Three labeled columns (Āṇava in crimson, Māyīya in indigo, Kārma in teal) extend horizontally across the frame. A gold center dot on the left connects to each column through a chain-link. The columns progressively narrow from left to right.

**Why it represents the narration:**
- The three columns are DISTINCT (crimson/indigo/teal) — not interchangeable "dark veils"
- They sit in sequence (left to right) showing a PROCESS, not a single event
- The gold source dot connects to all three — consciousness is the substrate that gets filtered
- The chain-link structure implies mechanical operation, not moral judgment

**What the viewer understands without words:**
Three different-colored filters act on a single source in sequence.

## Scene ml02: "Pūrṇatva" (fullness_field)

**Narration:** "Pure consciousness begins as complete capacity without lack."

**What the render does:**
A radial burst of 18 gold rays from center, each ray a glowing line. A bright gold-white ellipse at the center. The rays extend to the full field. A warm pale background.

**Why it represents the narration:**
- Radiating lines = "complete capacity" (fullness extends everywhere)
- No constriction, no aperture — nothing is blocked
- Gold-white center = "pure consciousness"
- The rays are even, symmetrical, unchanging — "without lack" means nothing is missing

**What the viewer understands without words:**
A full, radiant source with no boundaries or obstructions.

---

## Scene ml03: "Āṇavamala" (aperture_contraction)

**Narration:** "Infinite capacity is constricted into a fragmentary sense of insufficiency."

**What the render does:**
A crimson aperture ring shrinks over time — outer ring stays at 220px, inner ring constricts from 160px down to 28px. A dark center grows as the aperture closes. The scene feels like looking through a closing iris.

**Why it represents the narration:**
- The aperture SHRINKS — this is not a static state, it's a PROCESS of constriction
- Crimson color = contraction, intensity (vs gold for fullness)
- "Infinite → fragmentary" is shown by the outer ring staying large while the inner opening shrinks
- The viewer WATCHES the constriction happen — they experience "becoming small"

**What the viewer understands without words:**
Something large and open is being squeezed into something small and tight.

## Scene ml04: "Māyīyamala" (prism_split)

**Narration:** "The single field is divided into subject and object."

**What the render does:**
A gold dot on the left (the self/subject). An indigo triangular prism in the center. A displaced light field on the right side. Lines appear to pass through the prism and split.

**Why it represents the narration:**
- The prism is the key visual — it SPLITS light, it doesn't block it
- Indigo = māyīya color (refraction, not destruction)
- Left side is intact (subject), right side is displaced (object) — one field becomes two
- The prism has sharp edges and glass-like transparency — it's a MECHANISM, not a substance

**What the viewer understands without words:**
A single beam of light passes through a prism and comes out as two separate beams.

## Scene ml05: "Kārmamala" (causal_wheel)

**Narration:** "The localized subject is bound into action, consequence, and repetition."

**What the render does:**
Four labeled nodes arranged in a circle: "lack" (crimson), "desire" (rose), "action" (teal), "result" (indigo). Connected by arrows in a loop. A central glow.

**Why it represents the narration:**
- The circular layout = RECURSIVE, no beginning or end
- Four distinct stages = "action, consequence, and repetition" 
- The loop structure = "bound into" (you can't escape the cycle)
- Teal links = causal structure (not moral judgment)
- The viewer sees a FEEDBACK LOOP, not a linear path

**What the viewer understands without words:**
A cycle of four stages that feeds back into itself — you're trapped in a loop.

---

## Scene ml06: "The Bound Observer" (nested_filters)

**Narration:** "The three malas generate the normal fragmented field of experience."

**What the render does:**
Three nested ellipses (crimson āṇava, indigo māyīya, teal kārma) surround a dark central dot. Eight smaller dots orbit outside. Labels identify each veil.

**Why it represents the narration:**
- The ellipses are NESTED (one inside another) — the observer experiences all three simultaneously
- The central dot is dark (slate) — the observer is not luminous, they're defined by what surrounds them
- The orbiting dots = "fragmented field" — many pieces, not one unified perception
- The labels prevent confusion about which veil is which

**What the viewer understands without words:**
A dark center surrounded by three distinct layers of filtering, with fragmented content orbiting outside.

---

## Scene ml07: "Making the Veils Transparent" (filter_transparency)

**Narration:** "Liberation appears as transparency rather than world-destruction."

**What the render does:**
The same three ellipses as ml06, but over time their opacity drops from 180 to ~50. The center brightens from slate to gold-white. Rays extend outward through the now-transparent veils.

**Why it represents the narration:**
- The ellipses DON'T DISAPPEAR — they become TRANSPARENT (opacity decreases but outline remains)
- The center BRIGHTENS — the observer recovers their luminosity
- The rays extend THROUGH the veils — perception flows freely
- This directly embodies the guardrail: "Liberation should render the filters transparent rather than simply destroy the world"

**What the viewer understands without words:**
The filters are still there but you can see through them. The world remains, but it's no longer obscuring.

## Scene ml08: "The Malatraya Seal" (closing_seal)

**Narration:** "The three veils become translucent around the original fullness."

**What the render does:**
Three translucent rings (crimson, indigo, teal) surround a bright gold-white center. An eye/aperture motif arcs above and below. Small dots mark the rings at eight points each.

**Why it represents the narration:**
- The rings are TRANSLUCENT (outline only, no fill) — "veils become translucent"
- The center is bright — "the original fullness" recovered
- The eye/aperture arcs = a SEAL — this is a closing, a resolution
- The rings are spaced evenly — harmony restored, not chaos

**What the viewer understands without words:**
What was once a cage of veils is now a luminous center visible through light membranes. The aperture motif suggests this state is open, not closed.
