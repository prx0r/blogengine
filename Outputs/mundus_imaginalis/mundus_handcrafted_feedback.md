## What's working

- Structure mirrors Corbin's own stated plan (geography → perceiving faculty → examples), which is the right call — you're not imposing your own architecture, you're following his.
- The core thesis lands: imaginal as a third ontological register, active imagination as receptive rather than fantasizing. That's the hardest thing to get right in this essay and you got it.
- Closing quote ("the soul's true country") is a good choice to end on — it's the emotional payoff, not just the intellectual one.

## What's not working

- Quotes 1 and 2 are redundant. Both just say "imaginary is ruined, I need a new word." Cut the second one (starting "I was absolutely obliged...") down to a sentence of your own summary, or cut it entirely. It's not adding information, just repeating the same beat with more words.
- Quotes 8 and 9 (both Swedenborg) say the same thing twice. "Space in heaven = change of interior state" and "distance = difference in inner state" are the same idea. Pick one. You're spending two full quotes on a single point when you could use that space elsewhere — like unpacking "eighth climate," which you name early and then never actually explain. Right now it's a label that gets dropped and forgotten.
- You assert intersubjectivity without evidence. You write "the geography is consistent across visions... not private fantasy" — that's a load-bearing claim for the whole essay (it's what separates imaginal from delusion) and you don't back it with a quote. Corbin doesn't give you an easy one for this either, honestly, but flag to yourself that this line is doing work the sources aren't fully supporting.
- The Swedenborg section is proportionally large for your stated goal. You said you don't want to do comparative work — fair, and technically this isn't you comparing, it's Corbin himself bringing in Swedenborg. So it's not a violation. But it now occupies real estate equal to the "three universes" section, which is the actual philosophical spine of the essay. If you're tight on space, trim Swedenborg to one quote, not two.
- Weakest transition: the jump from "Mountain of Qaf" straight into "Corbin now moves from geography to epistemology" is functional but flat. You could use one sentence linking them — something like "having placed the world, Corbin now asks what organ could possibly see it" — instead of just announcing the section change.

## Bottom line

The argument is intact and correctly weighted where it matters most (the three-universes/active-imagination section). The fixable problems are all redundancy, not comprehension — you understood the essay, you're just quoting it twice in two places where once would do, which is stealing space from "eighth climate" and the intersubjectivity claim, both of which need more room than they're getting.
