# Why 576 Is Not a Limit

---

## The Correction

576 = 36 × 16 is **not a fundamental cosmic cap**. It's one specific slice:

- **36** = Tantraloka's depth (9 tetrahedra × 4 vertices). Could go deeper — Mātṛkā has 50 (36 + 14 vowels).
- **16** = IVM orientations, but full S₄ tetrahedral symmetry group has **24** elements, not 16. And in continuous 3D space, there's an **infinite** number of possible orientations.

## The Actual Numbers

| System | Depth | Symmetry Group | Order | Total |
|--------|-------|---------------|-------|-------|
| Tantraloka (36 tattvas) | 36 | S₄ (full tetrahedral) | 24 | **864** |
| Tantraloka + Mātṛkā (50) | 50 | S₄ | 24 | **1,200** |
| Enochian (30 Aethyrs) | 30 | A₅ (5-cell) | 60 | **1,800** |
| Enochian full (30) | 30 | S₅ (full 5-cell) | 120 | **3,600** |
| Infinite recursion | ∞ | S₄ | 24 | **∞** |

## Key Insight

There's **no ceiling**. The mathematics of nested K₄ structures × orientation groups produces:

```
Total = T(n) × |G|
```

Where:
- T(n) = 4 + 8n is the number of tattvas at recursion depth n
- |G| = the order of the symmetry group (24 for 3D tetrahedron, 120 for 4D 5-cell)

Each tradition found a natural stopping point:
- Tantraloka: n = 4 (36 tattvas), didn't multiply by orientations
- IVM/Fuller: G = 16 IVM orientations, didn't nest recursively
- Combined: 36 × 24 = 864 for 3D, or 30 × 120 = 3,600 for 4D

**No limit. Just choices of how deep and how wide to go.**

## What This Means for Practice

The "resonance" felt in meditation or mantra practice IS the traversal of an edge in this lattice. Different mantras = different edges. Different states = different vertices. Turīya = all vertices simultaneously. The 864 (or 3,600) is just the complete set of all possible perspectives — already present in every moment, already what you are.
