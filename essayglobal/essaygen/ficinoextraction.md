# Ficino Platonic Theology — Extraction Guide

**Source**: *Platonic Theology*, Marsilio Ficino, tr. Michael J.B. Allen et al. (I Tatti Renaissance Library, 18 vols in 6 tomes)

**Format**: Bilingual Latin/English, facing pages. Both languages appear in the .md files.

---

## Handling the Bilingual Text

The files contain both Latin and English interleaved. Extract **ONLY English passages** — the Latin is included for scholarly reference but should not appear in essay sourcematerial.

**How to distinguish**: English passages use modern English syntax and vocabulary. Latin passages use Latin-inflected words (quod, enim, autem, esse, vero, corpus, anima, etc.). The English translation is always the more readable one. When in doubt, skip.

**Chapter structure**: The text is organized by Books (I-XVIII) and chapters within each book. Volume 1 = Books I-IV; Volume 2 = Books V-VIII; etc. English chapter headings appear as "BOOK 1 • CHAPTER 1 •" etc.

---

## Essay-by-Essay Method

1. **Design all essays first** — blueprint with territory, core question, thesis
2. **For each essay**: read the relevant Book/Chapter territory in the source text, extract the best full paragraphs, keep a mental note of where you've been
3. **After all essays**: run a check for overlap — no passage in more than one essay
4. **Final pass**: re-read the source territory for each essay to see if any rich passages were missed

---

## Quality Targets

- 5-12 passages per essay
- Each passage = 80-500 words of English text (full paragraphs)
- Original Latin terms preserved where they carry meaning (e.g., *vinculum naturae totius*, *mens angelica*)
- Context: 2-5 sentences explaining where in the argument we are

---

## Ficino's Key Concepts (High Signal)

These are the concepts that matter most for the user's interests (metaphysics, angels, divine cosmology, imaginal):

1. **Five levels of being**: body (*massa corporis*), quality (*qualitas*), soul (*anima*), angel (*angelus*), God (*deus*)
2. **Soul as middle link**: *vinculum naturae totius* — the soul connects lower and higher
3. **Angelic mind**: *mens angelica* — indivisible, immutable, the direct image of God
4. **The Divine Sun**: the goal of purified mind, the Platonic light-metaphor
5. **Proofs of immortality**: self-motion, infinite desire, independence from body, natural knowledge
6. **Providence and freedom**: God's providence does not impair human freedom
7. **The ascent**: body → quality → soul → angel → God
