# V6 Grading System — Scoring Rubric

Based on the v6 algorithm. Each essay is scored across 8 criteria (0-10 each), then averaged to a final score. Scores are relative to the other v6 essays — the best known essay (becoming_an_angel v6) sets the ceiling.

---

## The 8 Criteria

### C1: Pattern Elimination (Weight: 10%)
**Source:** Algorithm Phase 3

Pattern elimination is table stakes — the entry requirement for being judged on the rest. Weight reduced from 20% to reflect that zero violations is the minimum, not the achievement.

The essay must have zero pattern violations across all 7 patterns and all disguised forms.

| Score | Condition |
|-------|-----------|
| 10 | Zero violations across all 7 patterns + all disguised forms |
| 8 | 1 minor violation (e.g., one "not X" clause-form NEG) |
| 6 | 2-3 violations, all minor |
| 4 | 4-5 violations, or one serious (semantic inversion) |
| 2 | 6+ violations; essay feels like AI summary |

**Evidence required:** Count of NEG, 3LIST, NARR, PARA, CLICHÉ, SAME-ENDING, FLAT violations. Quote each violation.

---

### C2: Sentence Architecture (Weight: 15%)
**Source:** Algorithm Phase 2.2

Sentence length variety, dash proportion, rhythmic pulse. The essay must breathe.

| Score | Condition |
|-------|-----------|
| 10 | Dash ≥30%, short <30 ≥10%, max/min ratio ≥4×, feels rhythmic reading aloud |
| 8 | Dash ≥25%, short ≥8%, ratio ≥3× |
| 6 | Dash ≥20%, short ≥5%, some variation |
| 4 | Dash <20%, short <5%, mostly uniform length |
| 2 | All sentences 20-35 words, no variation, lectures |

**Evidence required:** Dash%, short sentence%, sentence length range. Quote a representative block.

---

### C3: Voice + Metaphor (Weight: 15%)
**Source:** Algorithm Phase 1

The essay has a distinct identity. Dominant metaphor carries through. Every block feels like it belongs to THIS essay, not any other.

| Score | Condition |
|-------|-----------|
| 10 | Dominant metaphor in every block, register consistent, opening + closing use same image differently |
| 8 | Metaphor appears in ≥3 blocks, register mostly consistent |
| 6 | Metaphor appears in 2 blocks, or register shifts mid-essay |
| 4 | No clear metaphor, generic register, interchangeable with other essays |
| 2 | Every block starts with author name; zero identity |

**Evidence required:** Identify the dominant metaphor. Quote the opening and a later block showing the metaphor sustained.

---

### C4: First-Word Variation (Weight: 10%)
**Source:** Algorithm Phase 2.3

No more than 40% of AI blocks start with "The". First words should vary across the series.

| Score | Condition |
|-------|-----------|
| 10 | "The" ≤25%, at least 4 unique first words |
| 8 | "The" ≤40%, at least 3 unique |
| 6 | "The" ≤50%, at least 2 unique |
| 4 | "The" >50%, 1-2 unique words |
| 2 | Every block starts with "The" or author name |

**Evidence required:** List of first words. "The" percentage.

---

### C5: Texture Density (Weight: 15%)
**Source:** Algorithm Phase 4

Unexpected concrete words per AI block. Sensory vocabulary. Words that feel slightly out of register — poetic, physical, surprising.

| Score | Condition |
|-------|-----------|
| 10 | ≥1 unexpected word per AI block, multiple texture words across essay |
| 8 | ≥1 word in most blocks, some density |
| 6 | 2-3 texture words across entire essay |
| 4 | 1 texture word total |
| 2 | Zero texture words; all abstract nouns |

**Evidence required:** List texture words with block locations.

---

### C6: Ending Quality (Weight: 10%)
**Source:** Algorithm Phase 4.6

The ending circles back to the opening, uses a specific image, does not tagline, shares zero key words with source ending.

| Score | Condition |
|-------|-----------|
| 10 | Circles back to opening image in a new way, essay-specific, source-free |
| 8 | Circles back but slightly generic |
| 6 | Functional close, no circle-back but no tagline |
| 4 | Tagline ending (3-part parallel, summary) |
| 2 | Shares key words with source ending |

**Evidence required:** Quote opening and closing AI blocks. Identify circle-back.

---

### C7: Holistic Essence (Weight: 15%)
**Source:** Algorithm Phase 4.7

Read the essay as a whole movement. Does it inspire? Does it feel like it had to be written? Does it have a moment of genuine surprise — a word, image, or turn that stops the reader?

| Score | Condition |
|-------|-----------|
| 10 | Genuinely moving. At least one line that lands hard. The essay has soul. |
| 8 | Engaging throughout. One alive line. |
| 6 | Pleasant, correct, but nothing memorable. |
| 4 | Flat. Correct but dead. No pulse. |
| 2 | Feels like a summary. No commitment. |

**Evidence required:** Quote the most alive line. Explain why it lands.

---

## Scoring Quick Reference

| Criterion | Weight | What to Measure | Evidence to Quote |
|-----------|--------|----------------|-------------------|
| C1 Patterns | 10% | NEG + 3LIST + NARR + PARA + CLICHÉ + SAME-ENDING + FLAT count | The violation itself |
| C2 Sentence Architecture | 15% | Dash%, short%, length range | A representative block |
| C3 Voice + Metaphor | 15% | Dominant metaphor presence | Opening + later block |
| C4 First Words | 10% | "The"%, unique word count | List of first words |
| C5 Texture | 15% | Unexpected concrete word count + pivot placement | The texture words + where they land |
| C6 Ending | 15% | Circle-back (transformed, not restated), isolation test, source-share | Opening + closing |
| C7 Essence | 25% | Aliveness, surprise, soul, necessity | The most alive line |
| C8 Source Integrity | 5% | Verbatim ≥200 chars, no paraphrases | Source block excerpts |

## Demonstration: Grading 6 Representative Essays

### Essay: Becoming an Angel v6 (Reference)

**C1 Patterns — 10/10**
Zero violations. NEG=0, 3LIST=0, NARR=0 across all 15 AI blocks.

**C2 Sentence Architecture — 9/10**
Dash: 32%. Short: 9%. Range: 27-178 chars. Full-length essay with excellent rhythm. One point off for short sentence rate (9% vs 10% target — negligible).

**C3 Voice + Metaphor — 10/10**
Dominant metaphor: alchemy. Carried through every block. Opening: "Alchemy is the controlling metaphor" → closing: "the oldest form of philosophy." The alchemical frame moves from literal metaphor to existential claim.
> Opening: "Alchemy is the controlling metaphor for everything that follows. For Corbin, the laboratory was the soul — its furnace, its alembic."
> Closing: "The imaginal is reality's deepest register, and the work of attending to it is the oldest form of philosophy."

**C4 First Words — 9/10**
"The": 5/15 = 33%. Unique first words: 6 (Alchemy, Corbin's, Iamblichus, Suhrawardi, Fantasy, Ta'wil, Himma, The). Excellent variety for a full-length essay.

**C5 Texture — 10/10**
Texture words found: furnace, alembic, brushwood, scorches, bones, shake, skin, stripped, thrum. 9 texture words across 15 AI blocks. Every block has at least one.

**C6 Ending — 9/10**
Ending circles back to opening's theme (alchemy as controlling metaphor → philosophy as practice). No source key words shared. One point off: the ending is slightly abstract ("deepest register") compared to the concrete opening.
> Opening: furnace, alembic, gold
> Closing: deepest register, philosophy — more abstract

**C7 Essence — 10/10**
Multiple alive lines. The best:
> "A brushwood fire to the senses — a theophany that scorches through the ordinary."
"Scorches" does the work. It's the wrong word (brushwood doesn't scorch, it burns) and that's why it's right — it creates friction that makes you feel the theophany.

**Final Score: 9.6/10**

### Essay: Blake (corbin_blake_v6)

**Final Score: 8.4/10**

## Key Findings

1. **The reference essay still leads.** No algorithm-generated essay has matched its texture density or alive-line count.

2. **Plotinus Beauty is the best of the algorithm essays.** The sculpture/carving metaphor is concrete, consistent, and earned. The fragments ("Withdraw. Look. Cut.") give genuine pulse.

3. **Hillman and Swedenborg rank lowest.** Both have strong concepts but the language doesn't match the concept's depth. A Hillman essay about the soul of the world should make you feel the world has soul — this one explains it.

4. **The correlating factor for high scores is concrete metaphor density.** Every essay above 8.0 has a strong, specific physical metaphor (alchemy, sculpture, cleansing). Every essay below 8.0 has a more abstract or inconsistent metaphor (geography, ecology).

5. **Voice target in Phase 1 is the decisive variable.** The essays that passed all checks best were the ones where the voice target was set most concretely before writing.
