# Source Material — Essay 10: The Theophanic Cosmos

## Territory: Ch.25 (Moses) + Ch.24 (Aaron) + Ch.22 (Elias/Elijah)

---

### Passage 1 | Ch.25 (Moses) — Moses as the Sum of Many Spirits

**Context:** The chapter opens with the profound notion that Pharaoh's slaying of the infant boys was destined to support Moses by returning each boy's pure life to him. Moses embodies multiple spiritual predispositions — a theme that links theophany to the gathering of divine attributes in a single human form.

> The wisdom of (Pharaoh's) slaying the boys was destined to support Moses through the life of every slain boy, who was killed as if he were Moses. (In this act), there was no ignorance, for the life of every slain boy was necessarily returned to Moses. Each life was pure, because its inborn trait (*fiṭra*) of the primordial covenant of *balā* was unimpaired by selfish aims. Moses was the combination of the lives of all the boys who were killed as if each one was Moses, hence, every spiritual predisposition of each slain boy inhered in Moses. God particularized Moses in an unprecedented manner, for he possessed many kinds of wisdom. I shall present successively, if God wills, some of these in this chapter in measure of the occurrence of the divine instruction (*amr ilāhī*) in my thought.

**Arabic terms:** *fiṭra* (primordial nature), *balā* (the primordial covenant, Qur'ān 7:172), *amr ilāhī* (divine instruction), *isti'dād* (spiritual predisposition)

---

### Passage 2 | Ch.25 (Moses) — The Ark and River: Humanity as Vessel of Knowledge

**Context:** Moses placed in the ark and cast into the river becomes the central symbol for knowledge acquired through the body. The ark is humanity, the river is knowledge; the body's faculties are tools for the soul's direction — just as God directs the cosmos through its own form.

> As for the wisdom of putting him (Moses) in the ark (*tābūt*) and throwing him into the river, (its meaning is the following): The ark alludes to his humanity, while the river is the symbol of the knowledge he attained through his body. Only via the body, composed of the four elements, can the human soul be supplied with the faculties of reasoning, sensation, and imagination. When the soul resides in the body and is ordered to act on and direct the body, God makes these faculties tools through which one can fulfill what God wants from him in directing his humanity (*tābūt*) in which God's presence (*sakīna*) inheres. He was cast into the river in order to attain different kinds of knowledge through these faculties. God taught him that even though the spirit which directs him is his ruler, the spirit directs him through these faculties.

> In like manner, God directs the cosmos; He allows it to direct itself by itself or by its form, just as the existence of the child depends on his father's bringing him into existence, the effects on their causes, the conditioned things on their conditions, the objects of knowledge on knowledge, the notions that are proved on their proofs, and the unquestionable notions on their truths. All these things pertain to the cosmos and this is God's direction of it; He let it direct itself by itself.

**Arabic terms:** *tābūt* (ark), *sakīna* (divine presence), *nafs* (soul), *jism* (body), *'aql* (intellect), *khayāl* (imagination), *quwwa* (faculty)

---

### Passage 3 | Ch.25 (Moses) — The Rod and the Snake: Transformation of Transgression into Obedience

**Context:** The famous rod-to-snake transformation is interpreted as theophanic — the rod (disobedience, *'iṣyān*) becomes the snake (obedience, from *ḥayāt*/life). The magicians recognize divine rather than human power, and Pharaoh's claim "I am your loftiest Lord" is affirmed as true in form even if false in claim.

> Moses cast his rod. This was the form of Pharaoh's transgression which was expressed in his refusal to respond to Moses' claim "and behold, it became a clearly seen snake" (Qur'ān 26:32), that is, a manifest snake. Thus, the disobedience, that is, the evil deed, became obedience, that is, good deed, as He said: "God will change their evil deeds into good deeds" (Qur'ān 25:70), that is, regarding his judgment. Here the judgment appeared as a particular entity in one substance (*jawhar*), that is, the rod, which is (also) the snake and the manifest snake. As a snake, Moses' rod swallowed up the snakes, and as a rod, it swallowed up the rods. Thus, Moses' argument overcame Pharaoh's arguments in the form of rods, snakes, and ropes.

> When the magicians saw that, they realized Moses' degree of knowledge and that what they saw could not derive from human capability. Even if it was in the power of a human being, it would only be one who is distinguished in his unquestionable knowledge and who is free of imagination and obscurity. Consequently, they believed in the Lord of all creatures, the Lord of Moses and Aaron.

**Arabic terms:** *'iṣyān* (disobedience), *ḥayya* (snake/serpent, from *ḥayāt* = life), *'aṣā* (rod), *jawhar* (substance), *sīmiyā'* (illusion/sorcery), *kashf* (unveiling)

---

### Passage 4 | Ch.25 (Moses) — The Fire as Theophany: Self-Manifestation in the Form of Moses' Desire

**Context:** The culminating passage of the Moses chapter. God manifested Himself to Moses as fire because that was what Moses willed and sought. This is the paradigm of all theophany — God appears in the form the seeker desires and is capable of receiving.

> As for the wisdom of God's self-manifestation and (His) speech in the form of fire, it (happened) because Moses willed it. God manifested Himself in keeping with Moses' wish, so that he would accept the revelation and not deviate from it. Had He revealed Himself in a form different from Moses' desire, he would have deviated from God, because his interest was concentrated on a specific demand. Had he deviated, his act would have been fruitless and God would have turned away from him. However, he was the chosen and the favorite person. Because of that, God manifested Himself to him in keeping with his request, while he was unaware of this. He saw this as the core of his need, like the fire of Moses/while it was God, and he did not know.

**Arabic terms:** *tajallī* (self-manifestation), *nār* (fire), *ṣūra* (form), *irāda* (will), *mūsā* (Moses), *shajar* (the burning bush)

---

### Passage 5 | Ch.25 (Moses) — Moses' Answer to Pharaoh: Theophany as Essential Definition

**Context:** Pharaoh asks for the essence of the Lord of all worlds. Moses answers not with a definition but by pointing to the forms of the world in which God manifests Himself — one answer for the people of unveiling (*kashf*), another for the people of intellect (*'aql*).

> Moses actually answered one who had asked for an essential definition, and for this reason, he (Moses) made the essential definition his reference to the forms of the world through which God manifests Himself, or the forms of the world in which He is manifest. One can understand his answer to the question "What is the Lord of all things?" (*rabb al-'ālamīn*) (Qur'ān 26:23), as if Moses said: "The one in which the high forms of the world are manifest, that is, the heaven, and the low (forms), that is, the earth, 'if you know for certain' (ibid.), or He manifests Himself through them." ... Thus, the first answer (Qur'ān 26:24) is for the people who know for certain, that is, the people of unveiling (*kashf*) and finding (*wujūd*). He (Moses) said to him (Pharaoh): "If you know for certain" (ibid.), that is, (you) the people of unveiling and finding, for I have informed you of what you knew for certain in your witnessing and finding. If you do not belong to this kind, (that is), if you are people of limit, restriction, and restraint, I shall respond with the second answer.

**Arabic terms:** *rabb al-'ālamīn* (Lord of all worlds), *kashf* (unveiling), *wujūd* (finding/existence), *'aql* (intellect), *taqyīd* (restriction/limitation), *māhiyya* (essence/quiddity)

### Passage 6 | Ch.24 (Aaron) — Aaron's Prophethood from Divine Mercy

**Context:** The chapter opens by grounding Aaron's existence and prophethood in divine mercy (*raḥamūt*). He is the older brother yet lesser in prophetic rank — a figure of mediation, who calls Moses "son of my mother" because mercy belongs to the mother. The entire Aaron discourse is a breath of Mercy.

> Know that Aaron's existence stemmed from the presence of divine Mercy (*raḥamūt*), because God said: "We have bestowed on him," that is, on Moses, "out of Our Mercy, his brother Aaron as a prophet" (Qur'ān 19:53). His prophethood derived from the presence of divine Mercy, because he was older than Moses, nevertheless Moses was greater than him in prophethood. Since Aaron's prophethood derived from the presence of divine Mercy, he said to his brother Moses, "O son of my mother" (Qur'ān 20:94), calling on him by his mother, not by his father, because mercy is more fitted to the mother than to the father. Had the mother not possessed mercy, she would not have had the forbearance to rear children. Then he (Aaron) said: "Do not seize my beard, nor my head (ibid.), and do not make my enemies gloat over me" (Qur'ān 7:150). All this is a breath of Mercy's breaths.

**Arabic terms:** *raḥamūt* (divine Mercy, built on pattern of *jabarūt*), *imāma* (leadership), *raḥma* (mercy), *shafaqa* (compassion), *umm* (mother)

---

### Passage 7 | Ch.24 (Aaron) — The Calf as Divine Manifestation: Worshipping God in Every Form

**Context:** Ibn Arabi's bold interpretation of the golden calf incident. Aaron could not stop the worship because God wills to be worshipped in every form. The calf was a divine manifestation, mixed with Divinity in the eyes of its worshippers. This leads to the famous passage on passion (*hawā*) as the greatest object of worship.

> Aaron could not deter the people from worshiping the calf and overcoming it as Moses did, because of God's wisdom which manifests itself in existence so that He should be worshipped in every form. When this form disappeared afterward, it disappeared only after it had been mixed with Divinity in the eyes of its worshipper. For this reason, every kind of thing is worshipped either as divine or as a subjugator. The intelligent person necessarily knows this. Nothing in the cosmos is worshiped unless its worshipper ascribes to it high value and elevated degree. For this reason the Real is called, for our sake, "The Elevated in degrees" (Qur'ān 40:15). He did not say "The Elevated in degree," for He multiplied the degrees in one essence. Thus, He decreed that only He should be worshiped in many various degrees, and that each degree embodies God's self-manifestation in which He is worshiped. The greatest and loftiest of the degrees in which He is worshiped is passion, as He said: "Have you seen him who has taken his passion to be his god?" (Qur'ān 45:23). It (passion) is the greatest object of worship, for only thanks to it everything can be worshiped, and it is worshiped only through its essence.

**Arabic terms:** *hawā* (passion/desire), *ṣanam* (idol), *tajallī* (self-manifestation), *shirk* (associating partners), *'ibāda* (worship), *martaba* (degree/level)

---

### Passage 8 | Ch.24 (Aaron) — The Gnostic Sees the Real in Every Object of Worship

**Context:** The gnostic knows that every object of worship is a manifestation of God. The perfect knower regards every form — stone, tree, animal, star, angel — as a theophany. The messengers call people beyond forms to the God who is known but not witnessed while also affirming the self-manifestation in the forms.

> The perfect knower (gnostic) is whoever regards every object of worship as a manifestation of God in which He is worshiped. For this reason, they all call every object of worship god (*ilāh*), although its specific name might be stone, tree, animal, human being, star, or angel. This is the particular name of each god. Divinity causes the worshiper to imagine that this is the object (literally: level — *martaba*) of his worship, while truly it is the manifestation of the Real in the perception of the worshiper who devotes himself to this object in its specific manifestation. ... The polytheists are worshipers of time (*'ubbād al-waqt*), although they knew they did not worship the forms themselves, but only God in these forms, because they knew the rule of self-manifestation exists in the idols. The denier, who did not know the self-manifestation, was not aware of this (phenomenon), (while) the perfect knower, that is, the prophet, messenger, and inheritor (of prophet and messenger), concealed it from the people.

**Arabic terms:** *'ārif* (gnostic), *ilāh* (god), *martaba* (level/degree), *'ubbād al-waqt* (worshipers of time), *tajallī* (self-manifestation), *sharī'a* (law)

### Passage 9 | Ch.22 (Elias/Elijah) — Elias as Idrīs: The Fiery Horse and the Intellect Without Passion

**Context:** Elias/Idrīs, the prophet who was raised to a high station, dwelt in the sphere of the sun. He saw a fiery horse — all fire — mounted it, and his passion left him. He became pure intellect. This is the theophanic transformation: from elemental being to intellectual-spiritual reality.

> Eliās is Idrīs. He was a prophet before Noah, and God raised him "to a high position" (Qur'ān 19:57). He dwells in the heart of the celestial spheres, that is, the sphere of the sun. Afterward, he was sent to a town named Baalbek; Baal is the name of an idol, and Bek is the ruler of this town. This idol called Baal was characterized as a ruler. Eliās, who is Idrīs, had a vision of the mountain named Lubnān, from *lubāna*, meaning a need, which was split open to reveal a fiery horse, all of whose organs were made of fire. When Eliās saw the horse, he mounted it and consequently passion left him, and he became an intellect without passion and with no connection to the intentions of the (lower) soul. In him the Real was transcendent, and hence he knew half the knowledge of God, for when the intellect learns the sciences governed only by speculation, it knows God only as transcendent, not as immanent. (But) when God gives the human being knowledge through revelation, his knowledge of God becomes perfect, thus he regards God as transcendent in one way and immanent in another and sees the permeation of the Real in natural and elemental forms, so that he sees the Real's Essence in their (the forms) essences.

**Arabic terms:** *uns* (intimacy), *idrīs* (Enoch/Elias), *shahwa* (passion/desire), *'aql* (intellect), *tanzīh* (transcendence), *tashbīh* (immanence/likening), *rūḥ* (spirit), *nafs* (soul)

---

### Passage 10 | Ch.22 (Elias/Elijah) — The Mirror of Self-Manifestation: One Essence, Many Forms

**Context:** One of Ibn Arabi's most vivid explanations of how the single divine Essence appears in multiple forms, using the image of a mirror. The forms change; the Essence remains one. The resurrection tradition of God changing forms is cited.

> If you are a believer, you know for certain that God will manifest Himself on the Day of Resurrection in a specific form and will be known, then change His form and will be not known, then once again change His form and will be known; it is He, not other than He, who manifests Himself in every form. It is known that each form is distinct from another; it is as if the Single Essence is reflected in a mirror, and when the beholder looks at the mirror and sees the form of his God in belief, he knows and confirms it. If it happens that he sees the form of another God in belief, he will not know Him, just as one sees in the mirror his form and the form of another person. The mirror is a single essence, yet the beholder sees many forms in the mirror, which (itself) has no comprehensive form, though the mirror affects the forms in one aspect and not in another. It affects the forms through the reflection of different measures, such as smallness, largeness, tallness, and broadness, which go back to the mirror.

**Arabic terms:** *tajallī* (self-manifestation), *dhāt* (essence), *ṣuwar* (forms), *mir'āt* (mirror), *yaqīn* (certainty), *īmān* (faith), *ḥashr* (resurrection)

---

### Passage 11 | Ch.22 (Elias/Elijah) — The Intellect's Weakness and the Superiority of Divine Self-Manifestation

**Context:** The intellect alone cannot perceive the truth of causality as reversible — the effect can become the cause of its cause in the theophanic worldview. The messengers affirm what the intellect affirms but add what the intellect alone cannot reach. The gnostics are transformed even while outwardly in this world.

> What proves the weakness of intellectual speculation is the judgment of the intellect that a cause cannot be an effect of the thing for which it serves as a cause. This is clearly the judgment of the intellect. However, in the science of self-manifestation, a cause can be the effect of the thing for which it is a cause. The judgment of the intellect is right, but its speculation must be revisited. ... The messengers are the most intelligent people, and the message they brought stemmed from the Divine Presence (*al-janāb al-ilāhī*). They affirmed what the intellect affirmed and added what the intellect alone cannot perceive and what the intellect considers immediately as absurdity but acknowledges (it as true) after God's self-manifestation. ... The gnostics are only those people who know the divine self-manifestation, because they possess the form of the next world. They are gathered (*ḥashr*) in this world for the Last Judgment and resurrected in their tombs, and they see what you cannot see, and witness what you cannot witness, because of God's Providence for some of His servants.

**Arabic terms:** *kashf* (unveiling), *'aql* (intellect), *'illa* (cause), *ma'lūl* (effect), *al-janāb al-ilāhī* (the Divine Presence), *ghayb* (the unseen), *ḥashr* (gathering/resurrection)

---

### Passage 12 | Ch.22 (Elias/Elijah) — Realizing Animality: The Two Signs of Elias

**Context:** Elias's station involved two signs: seeing what is hidden in the grave and the transformation of the dead alive, and dumbness — the inability to speak of what he witnessed. Ibn Arabi recounts his own experience in this station of realized animality, leading to the vision of nature as the Breath of the Merciful.

> Whoever wishes to find this wisdom of Elias (who was) Idrīs, whom God twice brought into being — first, because he was a prophet before Noah, and then was raised up to heaven and then descended as a messenger, thus God joined for him the two positions (prophethood and mission) — must give up the domination of his intellect in favor of his desire. Then he will be a pure animal so that he can discover what every animal discovers, except the jinn and mankind (*al-thaqalāni*), and so will realize his animality. Elias possessed two signs, the first being this discovery; he could see the one tormented in the grave and the one who was delighted therein, and he could see the dead alive, the dumb speaking, and the sitting walking. The second sign is dumbness, for if he wished to speak of what he saw, he would be unable to do so; consequently, he would realize his animality. ... When an individual (like Elias) realizes what we have mentioned, he will be transformed into a state of pure intellect in an unnatural material, witness things that are the sources of the manifest natural forms, and know with intuitive knowledge (*'ilm dhawqī*) from where this rule of the natural forms is manifested. If he experiences a revelation (which communicates to him) that nature is the same as the Breath of the Merciful, he will be given great benefit.

**Arabic terms:** *al-thaqalāni* (the two heavy ones — jinn and mankind), *'ilm dhawqī* (intuitive/tasted knowledge), *al-nafas al-raḥmānī* (the Merciful breath), *ṭabī'a* (nature), *ḥayawāniyya* (animality), *kharas* (dumbness)
