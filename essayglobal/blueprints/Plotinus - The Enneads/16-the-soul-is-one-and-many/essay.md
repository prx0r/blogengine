# ennead 16: the soul is one and many

treatise 16 of the enneads: the soul is one and many
plotinus said your soul is not a piece of the world soul broken off — you are the whole thing, just focused on this particular life. you are not a fragment. you are the whole, squinting.

plotinus wrote this as treatise 16 of the enneads. here is what he said and why it still matters.

The soul is one in its source, many in its expressions. Every individual soul is the whole soul — the whole, present in a particular mode, not a part of it. We are not fragments of a larger soul. We are the whole soul, individuated by our choices.

> All souls are one. Not in the sense that they merge into a single substance but in the sense that they proceed from a single source and share a single nature. The soul of the universe and the soul of the least human being are the same soul, present at different levels of attention.

*Μία ψυχή.* One soul. The World-Soul is the soul as it governs the cosmos; the individual soul is the same soul as it governs a particular body — not two different things. The difference is in scope, not in kind.

Plotinus begins from Plato's description of the soul as composed of the indivisible and the divisible:

> How, then, is it also undivided? The reason is that it does not totally depart, but there is an element of it that has not gone forth, whose nature it is not to be divided. The phrase, then, 'from the indivisible and ever-unchanging [Substantiality] and from the divisible [substantiality] which comes to be in bodies' is identical to saying that the soul is composed of that which exists above and that which depends upon the intelligible world, but has flowed forth as far as the sensible world, like a line from a centre. But having come to the sensible world with this part, observe how with this very part it preserves the nature of the whole. Not even in the sensible world, after all, is it solely divided, but it is also undivided; for that in it which is divided is divided indivisibly. In giving itself to the whole body, it is divided even while not being divided, by being whole in all the parts of the body.

> The World-Soul is not separate from individual souls. It is what they are when they are not contracted into particular bodies. The individual soul is the World-Soul contracted — focused on a particular task, a particular life, a particular embodiment.

The soul is the third hypostasis — the expression of Intellect, not God, not pantheism. But within its level, the soul is one. Every soul shares in the same nature. The differences between souls are differences of attention, not of substance.

This is the insight that dissolves loneliness: you are not separate from any other soul. The differences between us are differences of attention, not of substance.

> The soul is divisible in bodies — it is present to every part of the body — but it is indivisible in itself. It is, as Plato says, both divisible and indivisible. Divisible because it is present to the many parts of the body; indivisible because it is wholly present in each part.

*Μεριστὴ καὶ ἀμέριστος.* Divisible and indivisible. The soul cannot be cut into pieces. It is present as a whole in every part. When you touch your hand, the soul is wholly present in the hand. When you think, the same soul is wholly present in your thought. The soul does not move from place to place. It is present as a whole in every place it is present.

The sympathy between all souls testifies to their underlying unity:

> Moreover, based on considerations opposite to this, the argument maintains that we feel sympathy with each other and share in each other's pain through having sight of it, and we find relaxation and are stimulated to love by the promptings of nature; and, no doubt, this is the reason for the phenomenon of love. And if spells and magical procedures in general serve to bring people together and cause them to connect sympathetically from considerable distances, this must at all events result from a unity of soul.

> It is because the undivided part of soul is to correspond to the rational element and is not divided in bodies, whereas the part which is divided in bodies, while also itself being one, in being divided in bodies, and in providing every sort of sense-perception, is to be regarded as activating different powers of itself, one of which is its power of moulding or producing bodies. It is not because it has many powers that it is not one; after all, in the seed there is a plurality of powers, and it remains one; and from this one seed there derives a multiplicity which is yet one.

> The unity of the soul is not like the unity of a body. It is the unity of a living being that is wholly present in every part of itself. The soul does not distribute itself across the body. It makes the body one by being one in all its parts.

This unity extends beyond the individual body. All souls are one in their source, in the World-Soul. And the World-Soul is one with its source in Intellect. And Intellect is one with its source in the One. The unity of the soul is a participation in the unity of all reality.

The science analogy captures how the one remains undiminished in the many:

> How, then, will the Substance be one in many souls? For either the one is present as a whole in all, or the many arise from the one whole, while that remains as it is. That soul, then, is one, and the many relate back to it, as a unit giving itself to a multiplicity, and yet not giving itself; for it has the resources to make itself available to all and yet remain one. It is capable, after all, of both extending to all things, while yet not being cut off from each individual; so, it is identical in the many.

> And let no one actually doubt that this is possible. A science, after all, is a whole, and its parts are such as for the whole to remain as it is and the parts to derive from it. And a seed also is a whole, and the parts into which it is its nature to divide derive from it, and each of them is a whole, and yet the whole remains in no way diminished as a whole — it is merely the matter that has been subject to division — and all are one.

> When we say "my soul," we are speaking of the soul as it is expressed in this particular life. But the soul itself is not mine or yours. It is the soul — one and the same — present in different modes, at different levels, in different lives.

The task of the soul is to realize this unity — to feel not as a separate self but as a focus of the whole. The soul that knows itself knows that it is not alone. It is the whole soul, conscious of itself in this particular mode. And when it rises, it rises not away from other souls but into the unity that includes them all. The one soul does not compete with itself. When you rise, you do not leave others behind. You rise into the unity that holds us all — and in that unity, no one is diminished.
