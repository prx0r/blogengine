# ennead 27: being is everywhere whole

treatise 27 of the enneads: being is everywhere whole
plotinus said the one is closer to you than you are to yourself — you have never been cut off from it for a single moment. separation is not a fact. it is a failure of attention.

plotinus wrote this as treatise 27 of the enneads. here is what he said and why it still matters.

How can the One be present to all things without being divided? If it is here, is it not there? If it is in me, is it less in you? The puzzle of omnipresence seems insoluble — until we realize that the One is not a body and its presence is power, not extension.

> The puzzle is this: how can the One be present to all things without being divided among them? If it were divided, it would not be one. If it were not present, nothing would be. The solution is that the One is present as power, not as extension. It is whole in each part, not because it is broken up but because it is not the kind of thing that can be broken up.

*Δύναμις.* Power. The One is present to each thing as the power that makes it be. Not as a substance that sits inside the thing but as the source from which the thing's being flows. The sun is present to the plant not as a substance inside it but as the power that makes it grow. So the One is present to all things.

This is the most comforting and challenging idea in the Enneads: the One does not need to reach you. It is already there. You are the one who needs to turn around.

Plotinus opens the treatise with the precise puzzle:

> Is soul present everywhere to the universe, because the body of the universe has a determinate size, and because soul is naturally divided in bodies? In fact, no, soul in itself is everywhere, and not just where it would be extended to by body. Indeed, does body not find that soul is everywhere prior to body? The result is that wherever body happens to be placed, it finds that soul is already there prior to body's being placed in any part of the universe, and the whole body of the universe is placed in soul which is already there.

> But if soul extends so far that, prior to reaching a body of a determinate size, it filled up all extension, how will it not have a magnitude? Or in what manner would it exist in the universe prior to the universe coming to be, when the universe was not? How would anyone accept that the soul, said to be without parts and without magnitude, is everywhere, if it does not have magnitude?

> The One is everywhere and nowhere. It is everywhere because its power extends to all things. It is nowhere because it is not contained by any thing. The One does not occupy space. Space is something that comes after it.

Plotinus uses analogies to help us grasp this undivided presence:

> A hand, however, could well control a whole body, for example, a piece of wood many cubits in length, or something else, and, while controlling the thing entirely, it is nonetheless not separated into parts equal to what is in its control. The power may seem to extend as far as what is grasped, but nonetheless the hand is limited by its own quantity, not by that of the body that it lifts and controls.

> And what if one were actually to make a small luminous mass, in a way, the centre of a larger transparent spherical body, so that the light from the inside is revealed in all the surrounding parts without any radiance reaching the outer mass from anywhere else? Will we not then say that the inside remains unaffected, but has extended itself to every part of the exterior of the mass while remaining fixed itself, and that light seen in the small mass has taken hold of the exterior?

Light is present to the entire illuminated space, yet it is not divided by the things it illuminates. The same light is whole in every part of the room.

> Light is one and the same throughout the illuminated space. It is not cut into pieces by the objects it falls upon. Each object receives the whole light, according to its capacity. The light is not diminished by being given. It remains whole.

This is how the One is present. It gives itself entirely to each thing, yet remains whole. The thing receives according to its capacity — a stone receives less, a soul receives more, an intellect receives the whole — but the gift remains unmeasured. The difference is in the receiver.

> If you want to understand how the One is present to all things, do not imagine it as something that moves from place to place. Imagine it as a center that is present to every point on the circumference. Each point is equidistant from the center, yet the center is not divided.

The voice analogy makes the same point:

> The voice is everywhere in the air, not as one voice divided, but as a one which is everywhere whole. And in the case of sight, if the air has the shape it has by being affected, then it has it not as divided. Wherever sight is placed, there it has the shape.

> If in this case, the whole voice is not spread through all the air, such that this part of it is conjoined with part of the air, and this part divided together with that, why be incredulous if the one soul is not spread out, having been divided, but is present wholly everywhere it is present, and is everywhere the soul of the universe, without having been divided.

The circle analogy: the center is one, and every point on the circumference is equally related to it. The center does not move to reach the points. The points are what they are because of their relation to the center. So the One is the center of all being, and all beings are what they are by their relation to it.

> We are not cut off from the One, even when we seem to be farthest from it. Our separation is not spatial but attentional. We are not far from the One because we are in a body. We are far because we are looking elsewhere.

The great claim: "We are not cut off." The soul is never truly separate from its source. The separation is an illusion produced by attention. When the soul looks away from the One, it experiences itself as separate. When it turns back, it discovers that it was never separate at all.

Plotinus makes this explicit:

> And we — who are we? Are we what approaches and comes to be in time? In fact, before that coming to be came to be, we were in the intelligible world, since we were both other human beings, and some indeed gods, pure souls, and intellect united with the whole of Substance, parts of the intelligible neither bounded off nor cut off, but by belonging to the whole. For even now we are not cut off; actually, the Human Being has approached that human being, despite his wishing to be another. And having found us — for we are not outside the true cosmos — he has wrapped himself around us and added himself to that human being, who was each of us then.

> The One is not absent from anything. It is we who are absent from it — absent by our distraction, our dispersion, our love of what is less than one. But the One does not need to come to us. It is already here. We need to wake up to its presence.

To be is to be one. And to be one is to participate in the One. Participation is the very structure of being — not something that happens at a distance. Every thing that is, is because it shares in the One, and it shares in the One wholly — the whole One, received according to its capacity, not a part of it.

This is why the soul seeking the One needs to attend — not to travel. The One is not far. It is closer than the soul is to itself. The soul has only to stop looking elsewhere. You are not trying to arrive. You are trying to notice that you have never left. And the moment you notice — the moment you feel the presence that has always been there — you are home.
