# 10-the-salutation-ishraq-and-the-angels-light

Source: avicenna.md, Part II §3 (The Salutation), lines 16219–16372

---

## Passage 1 — The Angel salutes first: "he took the initiative"

**Context:** The soul approaches the Sage (the Angel). The initiative belongs entirely to the Angel; the soul can only make itself ready.

**Source:** avicenna.md:16219–16228

> THE MASTER SAYS: "When I had seen this Sage, I felt a desire to converse with him. From my inmost depths arose a need to become intimate with him and to have familiar access to him. So, with my companions, I went in his direction. When we had approached, he took the initiative; he wished us peace and honored us with his salutation. Then, smiling, he addressed us in words that were sweet to our hearts."

**Original terms:** *salam* (peace, salutation); *al-mubadara* (taking the initiative)

---

## Passage 2 — The salutation as ishraq (auroral illumination) of the Intelligence

**Context:** Corbin's commentary establishes that the Angel's salutation is not a mere greeting but the actual illumination of the Active Intelligence upon the potential intellect. The term ishraq (auroral rising) is used interchangeably with shuruq (sunrise) to describe this Orient of the Light of the Angel.

**Source:** avicenna.md:16166–16177; 16243–16246

> [I]t is better never to reduce ishraq to mere "intellectual clarity"; furthermore, the commentator al-Munawi uses shuruq (sunrise, dawn) and ishraq (auroral illumination) interchangeably for this Orient of the Light of the Angel.

> For the commentator al-Munawi, the salutation is the shuruq or ishraq of the Intelligence, the Orient or rising of its light on the human intellect in potentia.

**Original terms:** *ishraq* (auroral illumination); *shuruq* (sunrise, dawn); *'aql bi'l-quwwa* (intellect in potentiality)

---

## Passage 3 — The influx reaches all equally, each according to capacity

**Context:** The Angel does not discriminate. What varies is the receptivity of the soul.

**Source:** avicenna.md:16229–16236

> Know that by the very condition attached to its being, the Active Intelligence is not such that it displays avarice in respect to some and abstains from showing them the way, while manifesting prodigality in respect to others and showing the way to them alone. No, its influx reaches all equally. Whoever has in himself the aptitude to receive this influx receives it; he in whom this aptitude is absent does not receive it; and each receives it in proportion to his particular capacity.

**Original terms:** *fayd* (influx, emanation); *isti'dad* (aptitude, capacity); *'Aql fa''al* (Active Intelligence)

---

## Passage 4 — The straw-scorpion analogy: making oneself fit to receive form

**Context:** The commentator uses a striking natural analogy — a straw buried in damp ground becomes fit to receive the form of a scorpion from the influx of a celestial Angel. So too, the soul must first make itself fit before knowledge is conferred.

**Source:** avicenna.md:16266–16278

> Let us take some examples. If a straw containing nodes is buried in damp ground and if the sun or any other heat produces a warming of it, the straw becomes fit for a scorpion to hatch from it. This fitness having once been produced in it, the influx of the celestial Angel is added and confers on it the Form of a scorpion, so that finally a scorpion is born. If a hair from the tail of a horse happens to fall into water, a snake will be produced from it. Other things that hatch forth are to be classed in this same order. It is the same with the knowledge that we do not know but that, within ourselves, we have the potentiality of knowing. When we become fit for the knowing of this knowledge, by virtue of our purpose to produce it in ourselves and our resolve to do whatever is necessary to reach a perception of it, the influx of the Active Intelligence is added to us, and this knowledge then becomes ours.

**Original terms:** *isti'dad* (fitness, disposition); *athar-e fereshteh-e asmani* (influx of the celestial Angel); *surah* (Form); *'ilm* (knowledge)

---

## Passage 5 — The soul wakes from the "lethargy of Nescience"

**Context:** The commentator explains what the salutation accomplishes: the soul understands the divine sciences, knows the Angels, and recognizes the Active Intelligence. The Angel's function is to bestow knowledge and awaken human beings from the sleep of unknowing. The salutation is not a greeting but an ontological event.

**Source:** avicenna.md:16355–16368

> "He took the initiative by wishing me peace" — which means: his influx was added to me, so that I understood the divine sciences. I knew the Angels; I understood that the Active Intelligence exists and that it possesses the attributes mentioned here: to bestow knowledge and to awaken human beings from the lethargy of Nescience. The Angel's salutation, then, certainly expresses the moment of the ishraq (auroral illumination) of the Intelligence. At this initial moment of the recital, the question is not that of knowledge of some natural object, but of recognition of the angelic being who actuates knowledge. The Persian commentator: under its influx, that it exists is here eo ipso to understand that it itself reveals itself in this dialogue of mental vision.

**Original terms:** *nawm al-jahl* (sleep of ignorance, lethargy of Nescience); *'ulum ilahiyya* (divine sciences); *ishraq* (auroral illumination); *ma'rifa* (recognition, gnosis); *ru'ya 'aqliyya* (mental vision)
