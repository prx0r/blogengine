# 11-the-name-of-the-angel-vivens-filius-vigilantis

Source: avicenna.md, Part II §4 (Beginning of the Initiation: The Name and Person of the Angel), lines 16373–16527

---

## Passage 1 — "My name is Vivens; my lineage, filius Vigilantis"

**Context:** The Angel reveals his identity. The names are given in Latin equivalents in Corbin's text, corresponding to Arabic Hayy ibn Yaqzan (the Living, son of the Awake).

**Source:** avicenna.md:16375–16386

> THE MASTER SAYS: "Many words were exchanged between us, until at last the conversation led us to such a point that I questioned him about everything to do with his person, and sought to learn from him what his mode of life and profession were, and even his name and lineage and country. Then he said to me: 'My name is Vivens; my lineage, filius Vigilantis; as to my country, it is the Celestial Jerusalem [lit., the "Most Holy Dwelling," al-Bait al-Muqaddas]. My profession is to be forever journeying, to travel about the universe so that I may know all its conditions.'"

**Original terms:** *Hayy* (Living); *Yaqzan* (Awake, Vigilant); *al-Bait al-Muqaddas* (the Most Holy Dwelling, Celestial Jerusalem)

---

## Passage 2 — The meaning of the names: Living (deathless), son of Awake (wakefulness itself)

**Context:** Corbin explains why the Angel is called Vivens (Living) and his progenitor Vigilans (the Awake/Watcher). Living, because angelic substances are exempt from mixture with potentiality; death is due to such mixture. Vigilans, because exemption from sleep is the privilege of the pure, intelligible Living One.

**Source:** avicenna.md:16387–16404

> In revealing his name, the puer-adolescent reveals his angelic identity. Here again we have fixed the thematization of the name in Latin equivalents. Vivens (Hayy), for death is due to mixture with what is in potentia, and the angelic substances are exempt from such mixture. Vigilans (Yaqzan, at once Awakened, Watcher, and Vigilant), because this exemption from sleep is the privilege of the pure, intelligible Living One (cf. al-Munawi).

**Original terms:** *Hayy* (Living, Vivens); *Yaqzan* (Awake, Vigilans); *quwwa* (potentiality); *nawm* (sleep)

---

## Passage 3 — "My country is the Celestial Jerusalem" (al-Bait al-Muqaddas)

**Context:** The Angel's homeland is not an earthly city but the archangelic pleroma, the pure Orient from which the Angel initiates his disciple, contrasted with the Occident of the material world.

**Source:** avicenna.md:16413–16436

> Bait al-maqdis, al-Bait al-muqaddas, is the current Arabic designation for Jerusalem. It here refers to the archangelic pleroma whose sacral substance subsists inviolate and pure (muqaddas) from all infusion in matter (hulul), from all incarnation (al-Munawi's commentary). It is, then, the pure Orient into which Hayy ibn Yaqzan will initiate his disciple, and which contrasts with the Occident, which is the material world and which is often figured symbolically as Egypt (cf. Leisegang, Die Gnosis, pp. 140, 143, 366). Hence we have here explicitly translated it Celestial Jerusalem.

**Original terms:** *al-Bait al-Muqaddas* (the Most Holy Dwelling); *hulul* (infusion in matter); *tajassud* (incarnation); *al-Mashriq al-mahd* (the pure Orient)

---

## Passage 4 — "My profession is to be forever journeying" through all conditions of the universe

**Context:** The Angel defines his profession — an eternal journey through the totality of beings, in knowledge and apprehension.

**Source:** avicenna.md:16511–16514; 16517–16527

> [M]y profession is to be forever journeying — that is, I am in knowledge and apprehension of the totality of beings, and this condition of my being comes to me from God in truth.

> The image that here presents the Active Intelligence as "forever journeying" suggests the "etymological" definition that Plato and Aristotle give of the Ether (Aither) as element of the divinity: Aei thein, incessant motion (cf. Zeller, Philosophie, vol. II, pt. 2, p. 332).

**Original terms:** *sair* (journeying, traveling); *haraka dā'ima* (perpetual motion); *Aei thein* (always running, the Platonic etymology of Aither)

---

## Passage 5 — The face turned toward the Father and the keys to knowledge

**Context:** The Angel's face is turned toward his father Vigilans, the primordial Intelligence. From him he has learned all science and received the keys to every kind of knowledge. The Father is the First Intelligence, the protoparent of all the Cherubs. The Angel's entire being is oriented toward this source.

**Source:** avicenna.md:16438–16455

> My face is turned toward my father, and my father is Vigilans. From him I have learned all science, he has given me the keys to every kind of knowledge. He has shown me the roads to follow to the extreme confines of the universe, so that since my journey embraces the whole circle of it, it is as if all the horizons of all climes were brought together before me.

> Know that God constituted the Angels as endowed with the science and knowledge of all that will come to be in the world, and this science is perpetually present for them; never does it cease in them, especially among the Angels-Cherubs who are called "Intelligences," of whom the Active Intelligence makes one. And in the choir of the Angels-Cherubs there is an Intelligence that is the primordial being that God produced, whereas He produced the other Angels-Intelligences because of this one and through its intermediation, as we shall explain. This primordial Intelligence is, then, as it were, the protoparent of them all. This is why the Sage here declares to the Master: "My face is turned toward my father." And he says further: "I am Vivens filius Vigilantis," because death is the opposite to life, and sleep the opposite to waking. Thus he names himself "Living," while he names the primordial Intelligence "Vigilant," because the latter is on a more eminent degree of being.

**Original terms:** *al-wajh* (the face, the aspect turned toward); *al-Aql al-awwal* (the First Intelligence); *al-ab* (the Father); *mafatih al-'ilm* (keys to knowledge)

## Passage 6 — The Angel is neither situated nor situatable in a sensible place

**Context:** Corbin's commentary explicates the meaning of the Celestial Jerusalem as the Angel's country. The Angel belongs to no sensible location; its "place" is the archangelic pleroma, a mode of being rather than a point in space. The commentary also establishes that the face turned toward the Father refers to the order of emanation — each Cherub in the pleroma is "son" of the preceding one, with the First Intelligence as the protoparent.

**Source:** avicenna.md:16413–16527

> Bait al-maqdis, al-Bait al-muqaddas, is the current Arabic designation for Jerusalem. It here refers to the archangelic pleroma whose sacral substance subsists inviolate and pure (muqaddas) from all infusion in matter (hulul), from all incarnation (al-Munawi's commentary). It is, then, the pure Orient into which Hayy ibn Yaqzan will initiate his disciple, and which contrasts with the Occident, which is the material world and which is often figured symbolically as Egypt. Hence we have here explicitly translated it Celestial Jerusalem.

> "[My country is the Celestial Jerusalem]," which means: I am neither situated nor situatable in a sensible place. Finally, "my profession is to be forever journeying" — that is, I am in knowledge and apprehension of the totality of beings, and this condition of my being comes to me from God in truth.

**Original terms:** *al-Bait al-Muqaddas* (the Most Holy Dwelling, Celestial Jerusalem); *la fi makan* (not in a place); *ghayr mutamakkin* (not situatable); *hulul* (infusion in matter)

## Passage 7 — Connection to the Enochic Watchers / Egregoroi

**Context:** Corbin links the figure of Vigilans/Yaqzan to the "Watchers" (Egregoroi) of the Enochian books, establishing the lineage of this angelology. The "Watchers" are the angels who keep vigil before the divine throne, sleepless and ever-aware — the same quality expressed in Yaqzan (Vigilant, Awake).

**Source:** avicenna.md:16406–16412

> In revealing his name, the puer-adolescent reveals his angelic identity. Here again we have fixed the thematization of the name in Latin equivalents. Vivens (Hayy), for death is due to mixture with what is in potentia, and the angelic substances are exempt from such mixture. Vigilans (Yaqzan, at once Awakened, Watcher, and Vigilant), because this exemption from sleep is the privilege of the pure, intelligible Living One (cf. al-Munawi). This last denomination is certainly not arbitrary here. We may think of the "Watchers," the Egregoroi, of the books of Enoch; cf. Part I and Bietenhard, Die himmlische Welt, pp. 137 f., 210 f.

**Original terms:** *puer-adolescent* (the eternal youth); *Egregoroi* (Watchers); *Yaqzan* (Vigilant, Awake); *quwwa* (potentiality)
