# Draft — Essay 10: Rays, Figures, and the Talismanic Image

## Rough Structure

### Opening
Modern symbol vs Ficinian operative image. Symbol means, represents, decorates. Ficinian image receives, concentrates, exposes, mediates, acts.

### Section 1 — Celestial Figures
World-soul impresses properties on figures in the heavens. The talismanic image is grounded in this cosmology.

### Section 2 — Rays as Transmission
III.16. The heavens emit, transmit, impress power through rays.

### Section 3 — Figures Above and Below
III.17. The right figure below corresponds to a figure above.

### Section 4 — Engraving
III.18. Fixing celestial form in matter. What, when, on what material, under what celestial condition.

### Section 5 — The Figure of the Universe
III.19. The image as microcosmic machine.

### Section 6 — Images and Spirit
III.20. Images act on spirit. Spirit acts on images. The operator's imaginative faculty (phantasia) is part of the circuit.

### Section 7 — The Talisman as Medicine
The talisman is celestial medicine in visual form.

### Closing
The image is not separate from Ficino's medicine. It is medicine by other means.
