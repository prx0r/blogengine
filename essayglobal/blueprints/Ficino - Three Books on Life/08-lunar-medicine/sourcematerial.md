### Drafting Notes

This is the most technical essay in the sequence. It should not try to be beautiful in the same way as the others — its beauty is precision. The goal is to show that Ficino's astrology is operational, not symbolic. The three powers from Book I (natural, vital, animal) return here in astrological key — this is the essay's organising insight.

The Moon is the centrepiece. It mediates between the celestial and the terrestrial. Everything else (fixed stars, dignities, planetary medicines) is extension of this principle.

### Voss on Semiological vs Causal Astrology

From Voss (p.10): "Following Plotinus, Ficino intuitively grasped that semiological astrology, regarding heavenly configurations as signs rather than causes, provided a more philosophically coherent foundation for his practice of natural magic."

This distinction is essential for the essay. Ficino's astrology is not causal in the modern scientific sense — the planets do not "cause" events. They signify, correspond, and participate. The essay should make the reader understand this without apologising for it.
