## Passage 12.1 — Two Kinds of Unfortunate People

**Source**: **De vita** III.23

**Ficino's own words** (from Via Hygeia excerpt): The unfortunate include those who do nothing and those who choose a profession unsuited to their natural bent and Genius.

**Two failures**:
| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Inertia | Doing nothing while the heavens incite activity |
| Misfit labour | Doing work contrary to one's Genius |

**Analysis**: Misfortune is not bad luck. It is misalignment between action, nature, celestial patronage, and daimonic guide. This is not self-help. It is a metaphysical theory of vocation. The cosmos itself is invested in what you do.

## Passage 12.2 — Region, Star, Daemon

**Source**: **De vita** III.23

**Ficino's own words** (from Via Hygeia excerpt): One should investigate what region one's star and daemon designated for habitation and cultivation. The right place refreshes spirit, strengthens sense, improves health, wins support, and favours wishes.

**The triad**:
- **Star** (*sidus*): celestial affinity
- **Genius** or **Daemon**: guiding daimonic principle
- **Place** (*locus*): environment suited to one's nature

**Analysis**: This is extraordinary. Place is not neutral. A person's genius is geographically keyed. The right region restores spirit because person, place, star, and daemon are aligned. Ficino's astrology becomes spatial — vocation is not only what you do but where you are, under which star, with which daemon, in which motion.

## Passage 12.3 — Circular Movements

**Source**: **De vita** III.23

**Ficino's own words**: One should keep in motion and make circular movements like heavenly bodies.

**Analysis**: Links to Essay 11 (song/dance) but is distinct: here movement is not performance but life-preserving imitation of celestial motion in one's proper place.

## Passage 12.5 — Procreation, Meals, Buildings, Dwelling, Clothing

**Source**: **De vita** III.25

**Ficino's own words** (reconstructed from chapter contents): Ficino extends astrology into every domain of life:
- Procreation (timing of children)
- Meals (food timing)
- Buildings (architecture)
- Dwelling (home placement)
- Clothing (what one wears)
- Daily care

Also: the permissible limits of astrological care.

**Analysis**: The built environment is a receiver of celestial influence. What you wear, where you sleep, what you build — all participate in the arrangement of a life. Ficino's astrology becomes a complete art of living.

## The Daimon Context (from Voss)

**Source**: Voss thesis, §2.4 (pp.99-101)

**Plato, Symposium** (the classic daimon passage, quoted in Voss):
"Spirits are half-way between god and man ... they are the envoys and interpreters that ply between heaven and earth, flying upward with our worship and our prayers, and descending with the heavenly answers and commandments, and since they are between the two estates they weld both sides together and merge them into one great whole."

**Plotinus on daimons** (quoted in Voss, p.100): Each soul chooses its guardian spirit according to the character and power that lived and worked in it. After death, souls return to the star akin to their nature.

**Analysis**: Ficino inherits the daimon tradition from Plato and Plotinus. The Genius of III.23 is not personality — it is the choosing-daimon that picks a life before birth. Vocation is destiny recognised.

## Post-Essay Review

### Initial vs Required
The sourcematerial.md for Essay 12 had the III.23 "unfortunate" passage and the star/daemon/place triad identified but treated them as short quotes. "The unfortunate include those who do nothing and those who choose a profession unsuited to their natural bent and Genius."

### The Gap
The finished essay needed the full Latin *Infelices sunt qui nihil agunt, et qui professionem eligunt ingenio naturâque suâ incongruam*, the extended context from the Platonic daimon tradition (*Symposium, Phaedo, Republic*), the III.24-25 practical applications, and the Apology connection. The essay grew to 303 lines — the longest in the series — because the theme required deep treatment.

### Lesson
For essays that strike the core of the text (as Genius/Vocation/Place strikes the core of the whole *Liber de vita*), the sourcematerial.md must be proportionally deeper. Short quotes will not support a 300-line essay.
