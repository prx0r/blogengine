# Draft — Essay 3: The Medicine Cabinet of the Soul

## Rough Structure

### Opening
The shock of the chapter list: phlegm, rheum, headache, sight, taste, syrups, pills, bloodletting, electuaries, sleeplessness, forgetfulness. Why do these belong in a philosophical work? Frame: this is historical medicine as metaphysical evidence, not practical medical advice.

### Section 1 — The Weather of the Head
The head is where the scholar's weather gathers. Vulnerable to flows, vapours, moisture, blockage.

### Section 2 — Sight and Taste as Gates
Sight: the gate of reading. Taste: the gate of nourishment. The senses as thresholds between body and knowledge.

### Section 3 — The Forms of Medicine
Organised by form: syrup = sweetness as vehicle; pill = compact intervention; liquid = fluid correction; bloodletting = evacuation; electuary = food-medicine hybrid.

### Section 4 — When the Body Fails
Sleeplessness, dullness, forgetfulness. The endpoint: the inner guides cannot operate.

### Closing
The body is the workshop where the soul's clarity is either supported or ruined.
