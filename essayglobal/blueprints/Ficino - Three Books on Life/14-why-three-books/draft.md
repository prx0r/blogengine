# Draft — Essay 14: Why Ficino Needed Three Books

## Rough Structure

### Opening
The danger of misreading the work. It can look like health advice, anti-aging manual, astrological magic, Christian apology. The capstone argues these form one progression.

### Section 1 — Book I: Cognition Wounds Its Own Body
Philosophy requires medicine. Melancholy gives and wounds. Study has enemies. Medicine, morals, religion form a hierarchy.

### Section 2 — Book II: Knowledge Needs Time
The epistemological argument. Vital heat and moisture. Food, gold, milk, blood. Aging is cosmic.

### Section 3 — Book III: Life Drawn from a Living Cosmos
World-soul and world-spirit. Catalogue, rays, images, song, genius. Natural magic and Christian defence.

### Section 4 — The Chain at Higher Scales
Blood/spirit/body → heat/moisture/life → world-spirit/celestial things. The same pattern repeats.

### Section 5 — Practical Platonism
What should one eat? When study? Where live? What sing? Ficino's metaphysics asks practical questions.

### Closing
The final tension: Ficino wants the world alive enough to heal the soul, but not so magically alive that Christian order collapses.
