# Draft — Essay 2: The Melancholic Scholar and the Five Enemies of Study

## Rough Structure

### Part I: The Constitutional Wound (I.3–6)

**Opening**: The brilliant sick person. The scholar is closest to divine things and yet most liable to headaches, insomnia, depression, bodily collapse. Why?

**Section 1** — Scylla and Charybdis: phlegm dulls, black bile disturbs. The scholar is trapped between two kinds of cognitive failure.

**Section 2** — The celestial signature: Mercury begins inquiry, Saturn deepens it. The scholar's vocation is written in the stars — and so is the danger.

**Section 3** — The soul withdraws to the centre (the core insight). Inwardness as compression. Concentration as earth-like. Melancholy as the bodily shadow of thinking.

**Section 4** — The mechanism chain: brain dries, spirit disperses, digestion fails, vapours darken, sadness arises. The mental act has a bodily cost.

**Section 5** — Philosophy half-kills the body. The scholar is wounded not by failure but by success.

**Section 6** — Which melancholy aids genius? Temperament vs romantic suffering. The precision of Ficino's distinction.

### Part II: The Daily Enemies (I.7–12)

**Section 7** — Thought has five bodily enemies. A taxonomy of cognitive sabotage.

**Section 8** — The hour of study. Daily rhythm as proto-astrological time.

**Section 9** — The stomach as hidden organ of thought. Digestion → blood → spirit → sense → reason.

**Section 10** — The discipline of study: managing the instrument day by day.

### Closing
The two parts together: the scholar's constitutional wound (melancholy) and the daily regimen that manages it. Book I as a whole is Ficino's answer to the problem of embodied knowing.

## V7 Notes
- **Personal stake**: Anyone who has felt thought exhaust the body. The essay should make the reader recognise their own experience in Ficino's physiology.
- **Extended metaphor**: Inwardness as centering. The image of the soul withdrawing to a centre, and that centre being heavy, dark, earthy.
- **Register shift**: Move from the cosmic (Mercury/Saturn) to the intimate (the feeling of a drying brain, a darkened mood).
- **Part I vs Part II**: The two parts should feel like two movements of a single piece — first the wound, then its daily management.
