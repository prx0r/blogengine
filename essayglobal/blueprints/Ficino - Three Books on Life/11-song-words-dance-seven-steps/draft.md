# Draft — Essay 11: Song, Words, Dance, and the Seven Steps

## Rough Structure

### Opening
Not "planetary music." This is the essay that owns sound and performance so the other essays stop repeating "planetary music" as a vague phrase.

### Section 1 — The Sequence of Imitation
Speech → song → motion → dance → moral behaviour → action. The human being as performative correspondence.

### Section 2 — How Sound Moves Spirit
The Canigiani letter. Sound arises from mind, fantasy, heart → disturbs the air → vibrates the airy spirit of the listener. The mechanism is physical and spiritual at once.

### Section 3 — The First Music
The hierarchy: reason → fantasy → words → song → gesture → dance. The soul's interior harmony descends through stages into embodied expression.

### Section 4 — Song and the Orphic Model
Ficino's own practice. Singing to the lyre to banish vexations and raise the mind to God. Orpheus as the model of the inspired performer.

### Section 5 — The Seven Steps
Prepared spirit receives bodily gifts. Rays, affection, free will receive soul gifts. Prayer, study, conduct produce greater likeness.

### Closing
Human life as performed correspondence. The essay should make the reader feel that to sing is to participate in cosmic order.

## V7 Notes
- **Central quote**: "Sound and song easily arouse the fantasy, affect the heart and reach the inmost recesses of the mind" — this is the essay's engine.
- **Extended metaphor**: The lyre. The instrument as model of the tuned soul.
- **Register shift**: From theoretical mechanism (how sound moves spirit) to personal testimony (Ficino himself sings). Then from Ficino's practice to the reader's own experience of being moved by music.
- **Opening**: Must kill the phrase "planetary music" immediately. Replace it with something precise.
