# Draft — Essay 5: Food, Putrefaction, Gold, Milk, and Blood

## Rough Structure

### Opening
The strangeness of Book II's material. Gold, milk, blood — what do these have to do with philosophy? The deeper themes: preservation, incorruptibility, vital transfer.

### Section 1 — Aging as Preservation Problem
Putrefaction as the enemy. Aging is not abstract decline but the breakdown of the body's capacity to preserve spirit.

### Section 2 — Gold as Incorruptibility
Gold is solar, noble, stable, anti-putrefactive. It images incorruptibility. It belongs to both longevity and solarisation.

### Section 3 — Vital Transfer
Milk and blood represent life as transferable nourishment. Not sensational — historical. Shows how far Ficino's theory goes.

### Section 4 — Dwelling and Custom
Place, climate, habit, continuity. Longevity is not just ingestion.

### Closing
Book II's material metaphysics: all are ways of resisting the collapse of vital order.
