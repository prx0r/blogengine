# Draft — Essay 13: The Apology: Natural Magic, Christianity, and the Magi

## Rough Structure

### Opening
A Christian priest has just described images, celestial powers, songs, dances, stellar timing. He must explain why this is not forbidden magic.

### Section 1 — The Four Pillars
Medicine, astrology, life of the world, Magi. The structure of the defence.

### Section 2 — Natural Magic vs Demonic
"Natural magic ... practised by those who seasonably subject natural materials to natural causes." The boundary is clear.

### Section 3 — The Magi as Precedent
Star-knowledge has Christian precedent. The Magi were guided by a star to Christ. This is Ficino's strongest card.

### Section 4 — The Limit
Where does reception become worship? Where does care become control? The Apology reveals the theological limit of the whole work.

### Closing
Ficino wants the world alive enough to heal the soul, but not so magically alive that Christian order collapses.
