# Draft — Essay 7: The Turn to the Living World

## Rough Structure

### Opening
After repairing the scholar and preserving life, Ficino asks how life may be drawn from the heavens. Why is this not a sudden genre change? Because the cosmos is alive.

### Section 1 — Divine Lures and Magical Baits
Lead with III.1's strongest image. The world is not dead matter waiting to be manipulated. It is a living order that can be attracted, lured, baited.

### Section 2 — The Ladder
One → Intellect → Soul → stars → things. Explain the Neoplatonic hierarchy that makes natural magic possible.

### Section 3 — World-Soul, World-Body, World-Spirit
The three-level structure. The bridge from Book I's human spirit to Book III's world-spirit.

### Section 4 — Human Spirit and Cosmic Spirit
The spiritus definition. The "airy vapour" passage. The same medium that connects soul and body within connects the human being to the cosmos.

### Section 5 — Sun, Jupiter, Venus
The welcoming gate. The beneficent triad. Book III opens not with danger but with gifts.

### Closing
Book III does not abandon medicine. It enlarges it until the whole cosmos becomes medicinal.

## V7 Notes
- **Extended metaphor**: The ladder. Use the Neoplatonic ladder as a structural image: the essay itself climbs.
- **Key quote to build around**: "Our spiritus is in conformity with the rays of the heavenly spiritus" — this is the bridge sentence of the entire book.
- **Register shift**: From metaphysical abstraction (the ladder) to the intimate (spiritus as vapour of blood). Make the reader feel the connection.
