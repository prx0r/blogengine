# Essay 13 — Heaven and Hell from the Human Race

**Sections:** §§290–326

**Context:** These sections describe how heaven is from the human race, the union of heaven with humanity, the wise and the simple in heaven, the rich and the poor, and people from outside the church (non-Christians). Swedenborg explains that the Lord flows into every individual, that angels and spirits are assigned to us to maintain our spiritual balance, and that people from all nations and religions can enter heaven.

---

## Passage 1 — The Union of Heaven with the Human Race (§§291–292)

> It is acknowledged in the church that everything good comes from God and nothing of it from us, and therefore that we should never take personal credit for anything good. It is also recognized that evil comes from the devil. This is why people who talk from the doctrine of the church describe people who are acting well and who are talking devoutly and preaching as being led by God, and say the opposite about people who are acting maliciously and speaking blasphemously. None of this could happen unless we had a union with heaven and a union with hell, and unless those unions were with our volition and our understanding, since it is from these that the body acts and the mouth speaks.
>
> There are good spirits and evil spirits with every individual. We have our union with heaven through the good spirits and our union with hell through the evil ones. These spirits are in the world of spirits, which is intermediate between heaven and hell. When these spirits come to us, they come into our whole memory and from there into all our thinking—evil spirits into the matters of memory and thought that are evil, and good spirits into the matters of memory and thought that are good. These spirits are totally unaware that they are with us. Rather, as long as they are, they believe that all these matters of our memory and thought are actually theirs.

---

## Passage 2 — We Are Kept in Spiritual Balance by Good and Evil Spirits (§§293–294)

> The reason spirits who are in touch with hell are attached to us as well is that we are born into all kinds of evil, so that our first life is made up of nothing else. Unless spirits of the same kind were associated with us, then, we could not live or be led out of our evils and reformed. So we are kept in our own life by evil spirits and restrained from it by good spirits. Through the two kinds, we are kept in a balance; and since we are in a balance we enjoy an appropriate measure of freedom and can be led out of our evils and turned toward good. This good can be sown in us as well, which could never happen except in our freedom; and the freedom could not be granted us unless spirits from hell were acting on the one side and spirits from heaven on the other, with us in the middle.

---

## Passage 3 — The Kind of Spirits with Us Changes as We Grow (§§295–296)

> The kind of spirit that is associated with us is determined by the kind of person we are in respect to affection and love, though good spirits are assigned to us by the Lord while we ourselves summon the evil ones. The spirits with us change, however, as our own affections change. This means we have one kind with us in infancy, another kind during our childhood, another kind as we are growing up and in early adulthood, and still another kind in old age. During our earliest years, spirits who are in innocence are with us, that is, spirits who are in touch with the heaven of innocence, the inmost or third heaven. In later childhood we are in the company of spirits who are engaged in an affection for knowledge and who are in touch with the ultimate or first heaven. As we are growing up, during our early adulthood, spirits who are responsive to affections for what is true and good and therefore with intelligence are with us. They are spirits who are in touch with the second or intermediate heaven. In old age, though, spirits who are in wisdom and innocence are with us, spirits therefore who are in touch with the inmost or third heaven.

---

## Passage 4 — The Lord's Direct Inflow into Every Individual (§§297–298)

> To continue with the general topic of the union of heaven with the human race, we need to be aware that the Lord flows into each one of us according to heaven's design, into our inmost natures as well as into our outmost, and disposes us to accept heaven. He controls our outmost natures from the inmost and the inmost from the outmost at the same time, and in this way keeps everything about us in coherent connection.
>
> This inflow from the Lord is called a direct inflow, while a second inflow that happens through the agency of spirits is called an indirect inflow. The latter is sustained by the former. The direct inflow, an action of the Lord himself, is from his divine human. It comes into our intentions, and through our intentions into our understanding. This means it comes into the good that is present in us from the Lord and through that good into the truth that is there, or into our love and through that love into our faith. For what we intend we call good, and what we understand we call true.

## Passage 5 — Non-Christians and the Wise and Simple in Heaven (§§318–328)

> The people from outside the church who have worshiped a god in human form and have been engaged in thoughtfulness toward their neighbor in keeping with their religious principles are in heaven. They accept the divine truth about the Lord and acknowledge him more easily than people who have been born within the church and have called themselves Christians but have led evil lives. For in the other life, we are led by the Lord away from our false beliefs to the extent that we have focused on good ends. To pretend to believe things we do not believe, though, is not granted.
>
> The people outside the church who form the Lord's heaven are for the most part from Africa and from Asia, particularly from the East Indies and from the islands, and also from America. The people from Africa live in the southern regions of heaven, but the ones from Asia live in the northern zones. As for the state of the people of the earliest church—the church that existed on our planet before the flood—they are in the highest heaven, and among the most distinguished angels there. They are in a state of such preeminent intelligence and wisdom that the other angels think of them as very near to the Lord.
>
> The Lord's church is spread throughout the whole world. It is universal, then, and consists of all individuals who have lived in the virtue of thoughtfulness according to the principles of their religions. In relation to the people outside it, the church where the Word is and the Lord is known through it is like the heart and lungs of the human body, which give life to all the organs and members of the body according to their forms, locations, and connections.
