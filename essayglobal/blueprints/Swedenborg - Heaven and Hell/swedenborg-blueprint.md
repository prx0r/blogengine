# Swedenborg — Heaven and Hell: Blueprint

**Source**: *Heaven and Hell*, tr. George F. Dole (Swedenborg Foundation, 2000) — 949KB, 13,382 lines
**Essays**: 20 focused, covering every major section

---

## Essay 1 — The Lord Is God of Heaven
**Territory**: §§2-6 (The Lord Is God of Heaven, The Lord's Divine Nature Makes Heaven)
The central claim: the Lord alone is the God of heaven. His divine nature — not anything else — is what makes heaven to be heaven. Heaven is not a place but a state of reception of the Lord's love and wisdom.

## Essay 2 — Heaven as the Divine Human
**Territory**: §§7-12 (The Lord's Divine Nature Makes Heaven, The Divine of the Lord is Love and Wisdom, The Divine Human references)
Heaven as an "individual" — the Maximus Homo, the Grand Man. All of heaven corresponds to a single human being. Every community in heaven corresponds to a part of the body. This is the ontological foundation of correspondence.

## Essay 3 — The Two Kingdoms and Three Heavens
**Territory**: §§13-31 (Heaven is Divided into Two Kingdoms, There are Three Heavens)
The celestial kingdom (love) and the spiritual kingdom (wisdom). The three heavens: the inmost or third heaven (celestial), the intermediate or second heaven (spiritual), and the outermost or first heaven (natural). Their differences in love, wisdom, and apparent form.

## Essay 4 — Angels Are Human
**Territory**: §§32-47 (Angels are Human in Form)
Angels are not abstract spirits but human in form. They have bodies, faces, speech, clothing, dwellings — everything that humans have, but in a spiritual mode. Their humanity is the image of the Lord himself.

## Essay 5 — The Power and Life of Angels
**Territory**: §§48-72 (The Power of the Angels in Heaven, Angels' Clothing and Dwellings, Their Language and Written Materials)
Angels have power through love and wisdom, not through their own strength. Their clothing, homes, language, and writing all correspond to their inner state. Angelic language is direct — thought flows into speech without the mediation of words.

## Essay 6 — Time, Space, and Motion in Heaven
**Territory**: §§73-90 (Concepts of Time and Space in Heaven, Representations and Appearances, The Four Quarters)
In the spiritual world, space and time are not absolute. They are appearances that correspond to states of love and wisdom. The "four quarters" of heaven are not geographic directions but aspects of the Lord: east = love, west = wisdom, south = truth, north = obscurity.

## Essay 7 — The Wisdom and Glory of Heaven
**Territory**: §§91-135 (The Garments of Angels, The Beauty and Splendor of Heaven, The Light of Heaven, The Heat of Heaven)
Heaven's light is divine truth. Heaven's heat is divine love. The garments of angels shine in proportion to their wisdom. The splendor of heaven is unimaginable — it exceeds all earthly beauty because it comes directly from the Lord.

## Essay 8 — Heaven Is Not a Place
**Territory**: §§136-152 (The Wives and Married Partners in Heaven, Heaven Is Not a Place but a State)
Marriage in heaven is the union of love and wisdom, the joining of two into one. Heaven is not a location you go to but a state you become. The "place" of heaven is the quality of your love.

## Essay 9 — Correspondence: Earth Mirrors Heaven
**Territory**: §§153-185 (Correspondence of Heaven with All Things on Earth, The Correspondence of Heaven with the Human Being, We Correspond to Heaven)
Everything on earth corresponds to something in heaven. The human body corresponds to the Grand Man — every organ, every function corresponds to some community in heaven. Correspondence is not symbolism but real connection.

## Essay 10 — The Word as Correspondence
**Territory**: §§186-214 (The Correspondence of Heaven with the Word, The Word in Heaven, The Union of Heaven with the Church through the Word)
The Word is written in pure correspondences. Every name, every event, every number in Scripture corresponds to a spiritual reality. The Word in heaven is spiritual — it is the divine truth itself, without the veil of earthly language.

## Essay 11 — Inwardly We Are Spirits
**Territory**: §§215-245 (Inwardly We Are Spirits, We Leave Only the Body Behind, Our Revival from Death, After Death We Are Fully Human)
The inner self is a spirit. Death is not the end of life but the continuation of life in the spiritual world. We do not become angels — we were always spirits; we simply continue after death in the spiritual world.

## Essay 12 — The States of Life After Death
**Territory**: §§246-289 (Our Nature after Death, Our First State After Death, Our Second State After Death, The Third State — Preparation for Heaven)
Three states after death: the first state is the externals (we appear as we were in the world), the second state is the internals (our true nature is uncovered), the third state is preparation for heaven or hell. No one is judged by God; we judge ourselves by what we love.

## Essay 13 — Heaven and Hell from the Human Race
**Territory**: §§290-326 (Heaven and Hell Are from the Human Race, The Wise and the Simple in Heaven, The Rich and the Poor in Heaven)
Heaven is not populated by special beings. It is populated by human beings who have become angels. The wise and the simple, the rich and the poor — all enter heaven according to their love, not according their knowledge or status.

## Essay 14 — The Vastness of Heaven and the Communities of Love
**Territory**: §§327-365 (The Vastness of Heaven, Each Community Is a Heaven, Every Community Reflects the Whole, Countless Communities)
Heaven is vast beyond measure. Every community is a heaven in miniature, and each reflects the whole. There are countless communities, each corresponding to a different aspect of love and wisdom.

## Essay 15 — The Form of Heaven and Its Government
**Territory**: §§366-428 (The Form of Heaven, The Government of Heaven, The Second or Intermediate Heaven, The Inmost or Third Heaven)
Heaven has a divine form derived from the Lord. Its government is the government of love — not law but mutual love. The intermediate heaven is governed by wisdom, the inmost by love. Authority in heaven is service.

## Essay 16 — The Worship of the Lord
**Territory**: §§429-479 (The Worship of the Lord in Heaven, The Unity and Diversity of Heaven, Heaven's Ongoing State, The Innocence of Heaven)
Worship in heaven is not ritual but a life of love and wisdom. The innocence of heaven is the willingness to be led by the Lord. Heaven's state is one of eternal spring — constant growth in love and wisdom.

## Essay 17 — The World of Spirits
**Territory**: §§480-565 (The World of Spirits, The First State, The Second State, The Third State, The Lord Governs the Hells)
The world of spirits is the intermediate realm between heaven and hell. All souls enter this realm after death. Here they are prepared for either heaven or hell. The Lord governs even the hells — they serve the equilibrium that makes freedom possible.

## Essay 18 — The Nature of Hell
**Territory**: §§566-628 (The Hells, The Evil Spirits, The Equilibrium between Heaven and Hell, Hellfire, The Malice of Hell)
Hell is not a place of punishment but a state of self-love. Hellfire is not physical fire but the burning of self-love. The equilibrium between heaven and hell preserves human freedom. Evil spirits choose hell by their nature — they are not sent there.

## Essay 19 — The Equilibrium That Makes Freedom Possible
**Territory**: §§589-603 (The Equilibrium between Heaven and Hell, Freedom through Balance)
Freedom is the balance between heaven and hell. Without the equilibrium of opposing forces, there would be no choice. The Lord maintains this equilibrium, allowing every spirit to act from its own nature.

## Essay 20 — Reaching Heaven Is Not Hard
**Territory**: §§604-615 (Not Hard to Reach Heaven)
The final, most surprising chapter: reaching heaven is not difficult. The difficulty is admitting heaven's love — because heaven's love is love for the neighbor and the Lord, and that love is contrary to self-love. The door is open; the only question is whether we can walk through it.
