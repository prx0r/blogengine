# Source Material — Essay 3: How to Read an Epiphany

**Chapters covered**: *On the Mysteries* II.1-4
**Length tier**: Medium (325 lines)

---

## Passage 3.1 — Appearances Correspond to Essence, Power, Energy

**Source**: *On the Mysteries* II.1

**Context**: Essay 2 gave the ontology — essence, power, energy. Essay 3 shows how this ontology becomes visible in epiphany.

**Iamblichus' own words**: "Their appearances accord with their essences, powers, and energies."

**Greek key terms**: φαντασία (phantasia — appearance, manifestation), οὐσία (ousia — essence), δύναμις (dynamis — power), ἐνέργεια (energeia — energy).

**Analysis**: The thesis statement of the entire essay. Epiphany is not judged by vibes, terror, beauty, brightness, or emotional intensity. It must be read according to rank. A being's appearance expresses its ontological level. This lets you write the essay as a kind of ancient phenomenology: what appears, how it appears, with what quality of light, with what effect on the soul.

**Cross-references**: Directly depends on Essay 2's essence/power/energy framework. The appearances described here are the energies (energeia) of the beings whose essences Essay 2 distinguished. Also prepares Essay 4 — once appearances can be ranked, dreams and possession can be judged by the same criteria.

**Essay use**: Opening thesis — appearance follows being.

## Passage 3.2 — Appearances Produce Different Effects

**Source**: *On the Mysteries* II.2

**Context**: The diagnostic criterion for epiphanies is not visual but transformational.

**Iamblichus' own words**: Divine appearances give "liberation from passions, perfection, divine love, and joy." Archangelic appearances give "purity and intellectual contemplation."

**Greek key terms**: λύσις (lysis — liberation), τελείωσις (teleiōsis — perfection), θεῖος Ἔρως (theios Erōs — divine love), χαρά (chara — joy), κάθαρσις (katharsis — purification), θεωρία (theōria — contemplation).

**Analysis**: The diagnostic test is not merely visual but transformational. A true divine appearance does something to the soul — frees, purifies, elevates, orders. Lower appearances may be luminous, impressive, or frightening, but they do not necessarily produce divine purification. The sign of a higher epiphany is not spectacle but the quality of transformation it produces.

**Cross-references**: This anti-occult point connects to Essay 4 (possession is not frenzy; true divine possession transforms the person) and Essay 5 (theurgy is not coercion but transformation). The same criterion — effect on the soul — judges epiphany, divination, and ritual.

**Essay use**: Central argument — spectacle is not enough.

## Passage 3.3 — Heroic and Archonic Strangeness

**Source**: *On the Mysteries* II.3-4

**Context**: The concrete descriptions of how different orders appear.

**Iamblichus' own words**: Heroic appearances may move parts of the earth and produce sounds. Archonic appearances are encircled by luminous forms. Psychical appearances are linked to air and circumscription.

**Analysis**: This is where the essay gets vivid. Earth trembling, sounds, luminous forms, airy soul-appearances. Each order has a different spatial and luminous signature. Epiphany is not merely "a shining figure." It is a phenomenon whose mode of appearing carries diagnostic information.

**Cross-references**: Prepares Essay 7 — the symbolic system of Egyptian theology also reads visible forms as carriers of invisible meaning. The same hermeneutic applies to epiphanies and to sacred symbols.

**Essay use**: Concrete taxonomy section — the orders with their characteristic modes of appearing.

## Passage 3.4 — The Practical Diagnostic

**Source**: *On the Mysteries* II.4

**Context**: Iamblichus summarises the practical framework for discerning visions.

**Iamblichus' own words**: The diagnostic questions:
- What appears?
- How does it appear?
- What quality of light?
- What effect on the soul?
- Does it purify or disturb?
- Does it stabilise or agitate?
- Is it self-sufficient or dependent on a medium?

**Analysis**: Iamblichus gives a practical manual for discerning the rank of a visionary appearance. The questions are concrete and operational.

**Cross-references**: These diagnostic questions could be applied to the dreams of Essay 4, the divine possession of Essay 5, and ultimately to the discernment of the personal daemon in Essay 8.

**Essay use**: Closing — the practical framework.

## Post-Essay Review

### Lesson
For an epiphany essay, the diagnostic framework (what appears, how, with what effect) is the practical payoff. The concrete details of how each order appears are the most vivid material. The cross-reference to Essay 2's essence/power/energy frame is the conceptual anchor.
