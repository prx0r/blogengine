# Source Material — Essay 5: Theurgy Is Not Coercion

**Chapters covered**: *On the Mysteries* IV.1-3
**Length tier**: Medium (325 lines)

---

## Passage 5.1 — The Scandal of Command

**Source**: *On the Mysteries* IV.1

**Context**: Porphyry's most damaging objection. If theurgy involves invoking superior beings and receiving responses, it looks like the human operator is commanding the god. This would collapse the hierarchy.

**Iamblichus' own words**: "How some of the superior genera, when invoked, are commanded by those that invoke them, as if they were their inferiors."

**Greek key terms**: ἐπίταγμα (epitagma — command, order), κρείττων γένη (kreittōn genē — superior genera), ἐπίκλησις (epiklēsis — invocation), χείρων (cheirōn — inferior).

**Analysis**: Porphyry's objection in its strongest form. Do not soften it. If theurgy means human beings ordering gods around, then Iamblichus' hierarchy collapses. The objection must be made to bite before it can be answered.

**Essay use**: Opening — let the objection land.

## Passage 5.2 — True Invocation Purifies and Liberates

**Source**: *On the Mysteries* IV.2

**Context**: The first answer — invocation is not command but reconfiguration.

**Iamblichus' own words**: Invocations elevate and purify the soul, liberating it from generation and joining it to divine principle.

**Greek key terms**: κάθαρσις (katharsis — purification), ἄφεσις (aphesis — liberation), γένεσις (genesis — generation), ἕνωσις (henōsis — union).

**Analysis**: The directional reversal. The vulgar view says ritual drags gods down. Iamblichus says true invocation raises humans up. Invocation is successful not because the god is moved by human force but because the soul is reconfigured toward the divine.

**Cross-references**: This reversal — the soul is raised, the god is not lowered — is the same principle that governs Essay 6 (sacrifice returns matter to its causes; it does not bring gods down to matter) and Essay 8 (theurgy lifts the soul above fate; it does not bring fate down to the soul).

**Essay use**: First answer to the coercion problem.

## Passage 5.3 — No One Threatens the Gods

**Source**: *On the Mysteries* IV.2

**Context**: Porphyry raised the problem of threatening language in ritual texts.

**Iamblichus' own words**: "No one, however, threatens the Gods."

**Greek key terms**: ἀπειλή (apeilē — threat), φοβερίζω (phoberizō — to threaten), κρείττων (kreittōn — superior).

**Analysis**: Blunt and clear. If threat-language exists in ritual texts, it applies to lower, divided, irrational powers — not to gods or the highest orders. This preserves the hierarchy.

**Essay use**: Section on ritual threats — preserves the hierarchy.

---

## Passage 5.4 — The Theurgist Acts "No Longer as Man"

**Source**: *On the Mysteries* IV.3

**Context**: The key passage — how the theurgist's agency is transformed.

**Iamblichus' own words**: "No longer as man, nor as using a human soul."

**Greek key terms**: ἀνήρ (anēr — man), ψυχή (psychē — soul), χράω (chraō — to use, to act through), ἔνθεος (entheos — inspired, god-possessed).

**Analysis**: The priest does not command as a personal ego. He acts through divine order, signs, and union. As human: inferior. As theurgist: participating in divine symbols. As united to gods: acting from a higher order. This is the difference between theurgy and goetia.

**Cross-references**: This transformation of agency is the key to Essays 6 (the sacrificial priest acts "no longer as man" when returning matter to divine causes) and 8 (the soul united to its daemon acts from a higher order than the merely human).

**Essay use**: Central mechanism — the transformation of agency in theurgy.

---

## Passage 5.5 — Power Through Union and Arcane Symbols

**Source**: *On the Mysteries* IV.3

**Context**: The source of theurgic power explained positively.

**Iamblichus' own words**: Ritual power comes "through a union with the Gods" and knowledge of "arcane symbols."

**Greek key terms**: ἕνωσις (henōsis — union), σύμβολον (symbolon — symbol), ἀπόρρητος (aporrhētos — arcane, secret).

**Analysis**: The power is not egoic. It is derivative. Theurgy is not human power over gods but divine order acting through a prepared human and its own symbols.

**Essay use**: Definition of theurgy versus goetia — participation versus manipulation.
