# Source Material — Essay 6: Why Matter Can Save the Soul

**Chapters covered**: *On the Mysteries* V.1-12
**Length tier**: Long (400 lines)

---

## Passage 6.1 — The Sacrifice Problem

**Source**: *On the Mysteries* V.1

**Context**: Porphyry's simple devastating question: if gods are immaterial, impassive, and superior, how can material offerings matter at all?

**Iamblichus' own words**: "What utility or power they possess in the universe, and with the Gods."

**Greek key terms**: χρεία (chreia — utility, use), δύναμις (dynamis — power), θυσία (thysia — sacrifice), ὕλη (hylē — matter).

**Analysis**: The question that drives the entire essay. Do not soften it. Let it stand.

**Essay use**: Opening — the question.

---

## Passage 6.2 — Gods Are Not Polluted by Vapours

**Source**: *On the Mysteries* V.2

**Context**: Iamblichus clears away bad theories first.

**Iamblichus' own words**: Gods transcend material contact and cannot be affected like embodied beings.

**Analysis**: Removes the crude reading immediately. Iamblichus explicitly rejects that gods are defiled, nourished, or allured by animal vapours.

**Essay use**: Destroys bad theories of sacrifice.

---

## Passage 6.3 — Not Honour, Thanks, or First Fruits

**Source**: *On the Mysteries* V.3

**Iamblichus' own words**: "By no means requisite that sacrifices should be offered for the sake of honour alone… nor for the sake of returning thanks… nor yet for the sake of first fruits."

**Analysis**: Iamblichus rejects civic/social explanations too. Sacrifice is not just a gift economy between human and god. Four bad theories rejected: gods eat offerings, gods are pleased by vapours, sacrifice is honour/thanks, sacrifice is payment.

**Essay use**: Section — "Four Bad Theories of Sacrifice."

---

## Passage 6.4 — Sacrifice Purifies the Soul

**Source**: *On the Mysteries* V.4

**Context**: The real aim of sacrifice, positively stated.

**Iamblichus' own words**: "The purification of the soul, or an emancipation from generation."

**Greek key terms**: κάθαρσις (katharsis — purification), ψυχή (psychē — soul), ἀπαλλαγή (apallagē — emancipation, release), γένεσις (genesis — generation).

**Analysis**: Sacrifice is not about rain, fertility, famine, or pestilence. Its highest function concerns the soul's release from generation. This shifts the essay from anthropology to soteriology.

**Cross-references**: "Emancipation from generation" is the same goal as Essays 4 (divine dreams free the soul from bodily entanglement) and 8 (theurgy liberates from fate). The entire project aims at this emancipation.

**Essay use**: Core — shifts from anthropology to soteriology.

---

## Passage 6.5 — Sympathy Is True but Not Enough

**Source**: *On the Mysteries* V.5

**Context**: Iamblichus engages the correspondence theory of magic.

**Iamblichus' own words**: "We should thus assert something of what is true… yet we should not demonstrate the true mode."

**Analysis**: Iamblichus is not a correspondence-magician. Cosmic sympathy matters. Animals/plants/materials have affinities. But sympathy is not the highest explanation.

**Cross-references**: This is crucial for the wider project. Iamblichus accepts correspondence but subordinates it to divine causality. This is the difference between theurgy and crude natural magic.

**Essay use**: Section — sympathy is subordinate.

---

## Passage 6.6 — Animal Correspondences Are Concauses

**Source**: *On the Mysteries* V.6

**Context**: Concrete examples of the correspondence principle.

**Iamblichus' own words**: Crocodile/sun, dog/moon, cynocephalus, weasel, cock's heart — such things are only "concauses," not the primary cause.

**Greek key terms**: συναίτιον (synaition — concause, co-cause), αἰτία (aitia — cause).

**Analysis**: Use the examples to make the essay concrete. Then show the correction: these correspondences are not false but subordinate. The ritual does not work because "crocodile = sun" in a flat symbolic table. It works because such correspondences are embedded in a larger demiurgic order.

**Cross-references**: The concause concept connects to Essay 4 (birds, stars, and viscera are concauses of divination, not the primary cause) and Essay 7 (Egyptian symbols are concauses of divine presence, not the presence itself).

**Essay use**: Vivid subsection — examples followed by correction.

## Passage 6.7 — Friendship and Familiarity

**Source**: *On the Mysteries* V.7

**Context**: The heart of the essay — the positive mechanism of sacrifice.

**Iamblichus' own words**: "Friendship and familiarity… binds fabricators to the things fabricated, and generators to the things generated."

**Greek key terms**: φιλία (philia — friendship), οἰκείωσις (oikeiōsis — familiarity, affinity), δημιουργός (dēmiourgos — fabricator, maker), γεννητής (gennētēs — generator).

**Analysis**: The heart of the essay. Sacrifice works because generated things preserve relation to their generating causes. The rite takes something generated and turns it back toward its generator. Procession: divine cause → generated thing. Sacrifice: generated thing → divine cause.

**Cross-references**: This is the same logic as Essays 2 (middle genera bind gods and souls — here binding binds fabricators to the things fabricated) and 8 (the soul returns to its source through theurgic ascent).

**Essay use**: Core — the mechanism of procession and return.

---

## Passage 6.8 — Perfect Sacrifice Moves All Causes

**Source**: *On the Mysteries* V.8

**Context**: The strongest technical claim about sacrifice.

**Iamblichus' own words**: "All the causes are moved in conjunction by a perfect sacrifice."

**Greek key terms**: αἰτία (aitia — cause), κινέω (kineō — to move), συνάπτω (synaptō — to join together), τέλειος (teleios — perfect, complete).

**Analysis**: Perfect sacrifice does not touch only one level. It moves physical sympathies, daemonic causes, divine causes, the soul of the sacrificer, the whole order of return.

**Cross-references**: This is the culmination of the sacrificial theory and connects to Essay 8 (the daemon governs all parts at once — perfect sacrifice, like the perfect daemon, touches every level).

**Essay use**: Central diagram — all causes moved together.

---

## Passage 6.9 — Ineffable Communion

**Source**: *On the Mysteries* V.9

**Iamblichus' own words**: "One friendship… producing this bond through a certain ineffable communion."

**Greek key terms**: φιλία (philia — friendship), κοινωνία (koinōnia — communion), ἄρρητος (arrhētos — ineffable).

**Analysis**: Matter is not dead stuff. The cosmos is held together by a divine friendship. Sacrifice activates that bond.

**Cross-references**: The "ineffable communion" echoes Essay 2's "indissoluble bond" of the middle genera. The same bond that holds the hierarchy together is activated by sacrifice.

**Essay use**: Beautiful but precise section.

---

## Passage 6.10 — Fire Transforms Matter Upward

**Source**: *On the Mysteries* V.10

**Context**: The most visually powerful sacrifice passage.

**Iamblichus' own words**: "It elevates to divine, celestial, and immaterial fire."

**Greek key terms**: πῦρ (pyr — fire), ἀνάγω (anagō — to elevate), οὐράνιος (ouranios — celestial), ἄϋλος (aylos — immaterial).

**Analysis**: Fire is the ritual image of ascent. Matter is consumed, heaviness becomes lightness, opacity becomes flame, lower material is assimilated upward. The sacrificer is also rendered more impassive.

**Cross-references**: Fire as ascent connects to Essay 7 (light as the manifestation of hidden divinity) and Essay 3 (divine light as the signature of epiphany).

**Essay use**: Major section — "Sacrificial Fire as the Image of Return."

---

## Passage 6.11 — Prayer and Sacrifice Mutually Reinforce

**Source**: *On the Mysteries* V.12

**Context**: The final element — prayer completes what sacrifice begins.

**Iamblichus' own words**: "Prayer and sacrifice mutually corroborate."

**Greek key terms**: εὐχή (euchē — prayer), θυσία (thysia — sacrifice), βεβαιόω (bebaioō — to confirm, corroborate).

**Analysis**: Sacrifice returns matter. Prayer turns soul and intellect upward. Together they form one sacerdotal operation. This stops the essay becoming mechanical.

**Cross-references**: The mutual corroboration of prayer and sacrifice mirrors the mutual binding of gods and souls in Essay 2 and the mutual implication of fate and freedom in Essay 8.

**Essay use**: Closing — stops the essay becoming mechanical.

## Post-Essay Review

### Lesson
This essay has the richest source material. The "friendship and familiarity" passage is the conceptual core. The fire passage is the most visually powerful. The animal correspondences provide concrete examples. Every passage contributes to a single argument: matter can save because matter is already within divine procession and can be ritually returned.
