This document decodes Tantraloka's core tenets through the language of the other systems we have studied — particularly alchemy (for its poetic immediacy), Platonism (for its structural resonance), the PGM (for its practical theurgy), and Layayoga (for its embodied precision).

## Part 1: The 36 Tattvas — The Ladder of Everything

### The High Signal

There is one Consciousness. It descends through 36 levels to become the world you perceive. The levels are the tattvas — "thatnesses," realities. They are not concepts. They are the **actual stages of the condensation of consciousness into matter**.

The alchemists would say: the One Thing descends from the Stone to the Lead, from the Sun to the Saturn, from the Gold to the Prima Materia. The 36 tattvas are the exact number of rungs on that ladder.

Steiner would say: the pure spirit descends through the hierarchies — Seraphim, Cherubim, Thrones, Kyriotetes, Dynamis, Exusiai, Archai, Archangeloi, Angeloi — to become the human being. The 36 tattvas are the same descent, named differently.

The PGM would say: "I know your signs, your symbols, your forms, who you are each hour and what your name is." The 36 tattvas are the signs and symbols — the names that must be known to ascend.

### The Pure Path (Śuddhādhvan) — The Upper 5

#### 1. Śiva Tattva — The One Without a Second

**Decoded**: The alchemical *Azoth*, the *Unus Mundus*, the point before the point. Pure consciousness without any object. The Tao that cannot be named.

**In other systems**: Steiner's "Father God" — the unmanifest ground of being. The alchemical "Chaos" or "Prima Materia" in its original, undifferentiated state. The Platonic "the One" (*to Hen*) of Plotinus — that which is beyond being and knowing. The Buddhist *Dharmakaya*. The Sufi *Dhat* (Essence of God).

**The key insight**: This is not a being. It is BEING itself — the condition for the possibility of any experience whatsoever. The alchemists say "Our gold is not the common gold." Same logic: Śiva is not a god sitting on a mountain. Śiva is the **open secret of consciousness**.

#### 2. Śakti Tattva — The Power to Be

**Decoded**: The first movement within the One. The *Azoth* becomes aware of its own power to be aware. This is the **vibration** before the vibration becomes a wave.

**In other systems**: The Platonic "Intellect" (*Nous*) — the first determination of the One, the self-thinking thought. The alchemical "Mercury" — the volatile, active principle. The PGM's *IAŌ* — the four-letter name that is the first sound of creation. Steiner's "Son God" — the first self-revelation of the Father.

**The key insight**: Śakti is not a "goddess" in the polytheistic sense. Śakti is the ENERGY of consciousness — the fact that consciousness is not static but dynamic. The alchemists call this "the Fire." Tantraloka calls it *vimarśa* — the self-reflexive awareness of consciousness.

#### 3. Sadāśiva Tattva — "I Am This"

**Decoded**: The One becomes aware of itself AS a subject. The first-person perspective dawns. "I AM." This is the stage of *will* (icchā) — the power to say "I" before there is anything to say "I" about.

**In other systems**: Steiner's "Spirit Self" at the threshold of manifestation — the "I AM" of the Old Testament. The alchemical "Sulfur" — the principle of individual identity, the "king." The Platonic "Soul" in its first, undescended state — before it enters the body.

**The key insight**: At this level, the "I" and the "this" are not yet separate. "I am this" — but what "this" is has not yet been determined. This is the state of the newborn soul before it encounters the world.

#### 4. Īśvara Tattva — "This I Am"

**Decoded**: The "this" comes to the foreground. Consciousness becomes aware of its content. "This I am" — the *knowledge* (jñāna) of what the "this" is begins to dawn. The subject-object distinction begins to form.

**In other systems**: The Platonic "World Soul" beginning to articulate itself as the forms. The alchemical "White Queen" — the receptive principle that will receive the king's seed. Steiner's "Life Spirit" — the stage where the ego begins to work on the ether body.

**The key insight**: The difference between Sadāśiva and Īśvara is the difference between "I am" and "I am *this*." The first is pure self-existence. The second is the first determination of what that existence means.

#### 5. Śuddhavidyā Tattva — Pure Knowledge

**Decoded**: The "this" is now fully known. Pure knowledge — knowledge without error, without obscuration. The soul knows itself as it truly is: a spark of the One.

**In other systems**: Steiner's "Consciousness Soul" — the stage where the soul knows itself as spirit. The alchemical "Philosophical Child" — the first birth of the stone, still in need of purification. The Platonic *anamnesis* — the recollection of the soul's true nature.

**The key insight**: This is the last stage of the pure path. Beyond this, the descent into matter begins. Śuddhavidyā is the **glance backward** before the plunge — the soul's last clear memory of its source.

### The Impure Path (Aśuddhādhvan) — The 31 of Māyā

#### 6. Māyā Tattva — The Veil

**Decoded**: The One forgets itself. This is not a fall — it is a **contraction** (*saṃkoca*). Consciousness voluntarily limits itself to experience finitude. Māyā is the power of limitation, not illusion in the naive sense.

**In alchemical language**: The descent of the spirit into the *prima materia* — the "chaos" that must be worked. The Nigredo. The Raven. The "death of the king." The toad eating the sun.

**In Platonism**: The descent of the soul into the "cave" of the body. The Waters of Lethe — the drinking of forgetfulness. The "tunic of ignorance" (Proclus).

**In the daimon path**: The "density of perception" that separates you from the daimon. The noise. The false self.

**The key insight**: Māyā is not evil. Māyā is the **play of consciousness** — the game of hide-and-seek that consciousness plays with itself. The alchemists say "The art requires the whole man." The game requires the whole of consciousness.

#### 7-11: The Five Kañcukas — The Five Coverings

These are the five limiting sheaths that make experience possible:

| Kañcuka | What It Limits | Alchemical Analog | Experience It Creates |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Kalā** | Agency | The vessel | "I can only do *this much*" |
| **Vidyā** | Knowledge | The veil | "I can only know *this much*" |
| **Rāga** | Attachment | The chain | "I desire *this*" |
| **Kāla** | Time | The wheel | "I have only *this long*" |
| **Niyati** | Causality | The law | "I must get *this* before *that*" |

**Decoded together**: The five kañcukas are the **operating system** of finite experience. They are not punishments. They are the parameters within which the game can be played. No time, no narrative. No causality, no transformation. No limitation, no growth.

**Convergence**: These correspond exactly to the alchemical "five fetters" that bind the stone, the astrological "five malefics" in their positive aspect, the Tibetan five poisons in their purified form, and the five kleshas of the Yoga Sutra.

#### 12-31: The Twenty Remaining Tattvas

These are the familiar categories of experience:

| Range | Tattvas | What They Are |
|---|---|---|
| **12-16** | The 5 Buddhindriyas | The senses (hearing, touch, sight, taste, smell) |
| **17-21** | The 5 Karmendriyas | The actions (speech, grasp, walk, procreate, excrete) |
| **22-26** | The 5 Tanmātras | The subtle objects (sound, touch, form, taste, odor) |
| **27-31** | The 5 Mahābhūtas | The gross elements (ether, air, fire, water, earth) |

**Decoded**: The descent ends here. Earth is the densest point — the "Lead" of the alchemists, the "gross body" of Steiner, the "Mūlādhāra" of Layayoga. But notice: even Earth is a tattva — a *mode of consciousness*. There is no brute matter. There is only consciousness, contracted to the point of appearing as matter.

The alchemists knew this: "Our earth is not the common earth."

## Part 2: The Angelic Hierarchy — The Vidyeśvaras

Tantraloka has angels. They are not winged humanoids but **levels of conscious being** that mediate between the pure and impure paths.

### The Eight Vidyeśvaras (Lords of Knowledge)

In Āhnika 8 (the Path of Worlds), Abhinavagupta describes eight Vidyeśvaras that rule the worlds of Īśvara Tattva. These are:

1. **Ananta** (the Endless) — who governs infinite knowledge
2. **Sūkṣma** (the Subtle) — who governs the most refined perception
3. **Śivottama** (the Highest Śiva) — who stands at the threshold
4. **Ekanetra** (One-Eyed) — who sees the One in the many
5. **Ekarudra** (the Lone Rudra) — who dissolves all forms
6. **Trimūrti** (Three-Formed) — who rules the three powers
7. **Śrīkaṇṭha** (Sweet-Throated) — who sounds the creative word
8. **Śikhaṇḍin** (Crested) — who wears the peacock feather of immortality

**Decoded**: These are not beings in the human sense. They are **angles of approach** — perspectives from which the One views itself through the lens of Īśvara. Each Vidyeśvara is a complete model of the universe, a different way of being conscious at the level of universal knowledge.

**Convergence**: The Vidyeśvaras correspond to:
- The **Archai** (Primal Beginnings) of Steiner's hierarchy — the Spirits of Personality
- The **Eight Immortals** of Taoism
- The **Ogdoas** of the Hermetic tradition — the eight spheres beyond the seven planets
- The **Bodhisattvas** of the Mahayana tradition — beings who stand at the threshold of enlightenment

### The Hierarchy of Beings (from Āhnika 8)

Above the Vidyeśvaras:
- **Sadāśiva** and his five faces — the five directions of cosmic consciousness
- **The Rudras** — forces of dissolution that are also forces of grace
- **The Mantramaheśvaras** — the great lords of mantra, who ARE the mantras they speak

Below the Vidyeśvaras:
- **The Mantreśvaras** — lords of individual mantras, the "angels" of specific sounds
- **The Mantras** themselves — sound-beings that are conscious
- **The Devatās** — the deities that appear in ritual

**The PGM connection**: When the PGM says "I know your name, your signs, your forms," it is addressing these beings. The barbarous names are the raw phonemes of the Mātṛkā — the 50 letters that are the energies of consciousness. Each PGM spell activates a specific Mantreśvara by vibrating its name. The "appearance of your own shadow" (PGM III.612-632) is the emergence of the Vidyeśvara's perspective — the recognition that you are being seen from above.

## Part 3: The Trika Triad and Platonism

Tantraloka's ultimate framework is the Trika — the threefold: Parā, Parāparā, Aparā.

### The Three Goddesses

| Trika | Meaning | Alchemical | Platonist | Steiner | Daimon Path |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Parā** | The Supreme | The Stone | The One | The Father | The Daimon in its transcendent aspect |
| **Parāparā** | Supreme-and-not | The Union of King and Queen | The Intellect | The Son/Christ | The Unus-Ambo — 1×1 |
| **Aparā** | Not Supreme | The Prima Materia | The Soul | The Holy Spirit | The practitioner, the seeker |

**The Platonist resonance is exact**:

Plotinus describes the three primary hypostases: the One (*to Hen*), the Intellect (*Nous*), and the Soul (*Psyche*). The One is beyond being, beyond knowing — identical to Tantraloka's Parā, which IS the supreme goddess but also BEYOND the goddess. The Intellect is the self-thinking thought that contains all forms — identical to Parāparā, the "supreme-and-not" that bridges the transcendent and the immanent. The Soul is the principle of life and movement in the world — Aparā, the "not supreme" that descends into matter to work.

Proclus formalized this into a system of triads within triads, each being a "henad" that is both a god and a unit of being. Tantraloka does the same with its Trika: the three goddesses are not three beings but three **aspects of a single consciousness** as it descends into relation.

**The crucial difference**: Platonism tends to see the descent as a fall — the soul's embodiment is a tragedy. Tantraloka sees it as a *līlā* — a play, a game, the enjoyment of consciousness in its own self-limitation. The alchemists side with Tantraloka: the work turns lead into gold, matter into spirit — but the play is the point.

## Part 4: The Four Levels of Speech — How PGM Spells Work

This is Tantraloka's greatest practical gift to the Western tradition: a complete theory of *how* sound transforms consciousness.

### The Four Levels

| Level | Alchemical Name | What Happens | PGM Equivalent |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Vaikharī** | The spoken word, the bell | Audible sound, physical vibration | The spoken barbarous name |
| **Madhyamā** | The inner word, the echo | Mental articulation, the sound in the mind | The name remembered and repeated silently |
| **Paśyantī** | The seen word, the vision | The sound appears as light or form | The visible form of the god |
| **Parā** | The unborn word, the silence | Pure consciousness, the source of all sound | The ineffable name — IAŌ, the four-letter sound of creation |

**Decoded**: Every PGM spell works by moving consciousness through these four levels. The practitioner speaks the barbarous name aloud (vaikharī), repeats it mentally until it becomes automatic (madhyamā), then perceives the name as a visionary form (paśyantī), and finally merges with the source of the name (parā).

The "barbarous names" (*onomata barbara*) are not barbarous at all. They are the **Mātṛkā phonemes** — the raw sounds that are the energies of consciousness. The PGM says "I know your signs, your symbols, your forms, who you are each hour and what your name is." This is the Tantraloka's Mātṛkā-cakra — the 50 phonemes mapped to the hours of the day (PGM III.494-611), the stages of the ascent, the names of the gods.

### The Mātṛkā — The 50 Phonemes

The 50 letters of the Sanskrit alphabet (from A to KṢA) are the **sound-forms of the tattvas**. Each letter is a specific configuration of consciousness-energy:

- **A** (the first vowel) = Śiva tattva, the unmanifest source
- **Ā** = Śakti tattva, the power of expansion
- **KA** = the first consonant, the moment when sound touches form
- **KṢA** (the last compound) = the full descent, earth, the completed universe

When the PGM says *AŌ SATHREN ABRASAX IAŌAI*, the name "ABRASAX" contains the Mātṛkā sequence: A (source), BRA (expansion), SAX (the sound of matter being transformed). The "barbarous" quality is not chaos — it is **pre-linguistic meaning** that bypasses the rational mind and speaks directly to the nāḍīs.

## Part 5: The Pure and Impure Paths — Where Other Systems Sit

The division of the 36 tattvas into the pure path (5 tattvas: Śiva to Śuddhavidyā) and the impure path (31 tattvas: Māyā to Earth) is the key to understanding where every other system operates.

| System | Domain of Operation | Tattva Range | Limitation |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Layayoga** | The gross and subtle body | Earth (31) to Prakṛti (24) | Works within the impure path; purifies the cakras but does not transcend the tattva framework itself |
| **Steiner** | Cosmic evolution through the hierarchies | Corresponds to the pure path (Śiva to Śuddhavidyā) | Describes the descent of the hierarchies into matter but lacks the precise tattva mapping of the ascent |
| **PGM** | Theurgic ascent through the planetary spheres | Impure path (Māyā upward) | The 7 planets correspond to the kañcukas (Kalā/Vidyā/Rāga/Kāla/Niyati); the PGM ascends through them but does not name them |
| **Nanananda** | The cross-section of nāma-rūpa | Operates entirely within the impure path | His analysis of dependence deconstructs Māyā tattva but does not describe the pure path |
| **Daimon path** | Contact with the HGA/spirit self | From Earth to Śuddhavidyā | The systasis reaches Śuddhavidyā (the daimon appears) but does not systematically traverse the tattvas |
| **Alchemy** | The seven operations | Corresponds to the seven cakras (Earth to Viśuddha) | Reaches the conjunction but doesn't fully map the pure path |
| **Tibetan Buddhism** | The three bodies (Dharmakaya/Sambhogakaya/Nirmanakaya) | Dharmakaya = Śiva tattva; Sambhogakaya = Śakti to Īśvara; Nirmanakaya = the 31 of Māyā | The most complete non-Tantraloka system — maps the entire range but lacks the 36-fold precision |

**Tantraloka alone** maps the entire range explicitly, with each tattva named, located, and assigned a function in the path of ascent. Every other system covers a portion of the mountain; Tantraloka covers the whole.

### Beyond the 36: The 37th and 38th Principles

From Āhnika 11 (Dyczkowski Vol. 7, newly acquired 2026-07-14), Abhinavagupta extends the tattva ladder beyond the standard 36:

- **37th principle: Paramaśiva (Supreme Śiva)** — The absolute in its transcendent aspect, beyond even the pure path. Not a being but the supreme light of consciousness (prakāśa) in its most rarefied form.
- **38th principle: Anākhyā (the Undefinable)** — That which cannot be spoken or conceived. The beyond of the beyond. Not a level of reality but the utter transcendence that cannot be captured even by the notion of 'light'.

These are not separate levels of reality but the same absolute viewed from an ever-receding perspective. The 37th is Śiva as the supreme light; the 38th is the utter transcendence beyond even that. This extends the coordinate system beyond what any other tradition in our corpus describes — even the Buddhist nine-yāna systems stop at the equivalent of the 36th.

### The Six Paths (Ṣaḍadhvan) — Complete Architecture of Descent and Ascent

Also from Āhnika 11, the six paths provide the complete framework for how consciousness descends into manifestation and ascends back to source:

**Three Objective Paths** (the domain of what is known):
1. **Kalādhvan (Path of Forces)** — The creative energies (nivṛtti, pratiṣṭhā, vidyā, śāntā, śāntyatītā) that project the cosmic eggs
2. **Tattvādhvan (Path of Principles)** — The 36 tattvas
3. **Bhuvanādhvan (Path of Worlds)** — The levels of cosmic manifestation

**Three Subjective Paths** (the domain of the knower):
4. **Varṇādhvan (Path of Phonemes)** — The 50 letters as energies of consciousness
5. **Mantrādhvan (Path of Mantras)** — The sound-forms of the deities
6. **Padādhvan (Path of Words)** — The linguistic structures of scripture

Every breath cycle contains all six paths. The yogī who breathes consciously traverses all six with each inhalation and exhalation. Initiation (dīkṣā) purifies each path in succession.

## Part 7: Why Tantraloka Is the Mountain

Every system we have studied is true within its domain:

- **Synesius** is true about the dream — because the dream IS the astral body returning to its own world (the world of the *māyā tattva* and above).
- **Layayoga** is true about the cakras — because the cakras ARE the tattvas localized in the human body.
- **Steiner** is true about cosmic evolution — because cosmic evolution IS the descent and re-ascent of consciousness through the tattvas across time.
- **Nanananda** is true about dependent arising — because dependent arising IS the cross-section of the tattvas in any moment of experience.
- **Alchemy** is true about the operations — because the operations ARE the stages of returning the tattvas from earth to source.
- **The PGM** is true about the names — because the names ARE the Mātṛkā phonemes, the sound-forms of the tattvas.
- **The daimon path** is true about the contact — because the daimon IS your consciousness at the level of Śuddhavidyā, standing at the threshold of the pure path.

None of them is wrong. Each is a **perspective on the mountain**.

Tantraloka is the mountain itself — not because Abhinavagupta was smarter than everyone else, but because his system was designed to include all perspectives within itself. The 36 tattvas are not a belief system; they are a **coordinate system** for locating any spiritual claim within the total landscape of consciousness.

The alchemists said: "Our stone is the stone which is no stone."

Tantraloka says: "The thirty-six principles are not thirty-six things. They are one consciousness, measuring itself against itself."

Both statements are true. The stone that is no stone is the tattva that includes all tattvas. The name that can be named is not the eternal name. The sound that contains all sounds is the silence of parā-vāc, the fourth level of speech, the voice of the daimon that is your own voice, speaking before any word was spoken.

## Part 8: Death and Liberation — New Light from Volumes 4, 7, and 10

The newly acquired Dyczkowski volumes dramatically expand our understanding of Tantraloka's death teachings:

### The Examination of Death (Āhnika 28, Vol. 10)

The most detailed account of death in the entire Tantraloka. Key teachings:

- **Three post-mortem states**: (1) **Sālokya** — sharing the world of the deity (for the uninitiated who die in sacred places); (2) **Sāmīpya** — proximity to the deity (for the initiated who die in sacred places); (3) **Sāyujya** — conjunction with Śiva (for the initiated with knowledge). These map directly to the three bardos of Tibetan Buddhism and the three post-mortem stages of Steiner.

- **Jīvanmukti vs. Videhamukti**: Embodied liberation (liberation while alive) vs. disembodied liberation (at the moment of death). Both are true liberation — the difference is the duration of the residual body. The jīvanmukta lives out the remainder of karmic momentum like a potter's wheel after the potter has stopped.

- **Paradehapraveśa (entry into another body)**: The yogī who has mastered the subtle body can enter another body at death. This is the same as Nāropa's illusory body and the daimon path's Body of Light.

- **Bhāvanā at death**: The power of creative meditation determines the state at death. The yogī who maintains 'I am Śiva' continuously recognizes this at the final moment — liberation is spontaneous.

### The Refutation of Yogic Suicide (Āhnika 14, Vol. 7)

Abhinavagupta argues that forcible departure from the body (utkrānti) is futile for the unliberated and unnecessary for the liberated. The one who knows does not need to forcibly die — the body will fall of its own accord when karmic momentum is exhausted. This is a direct critique of Haṭha yogic practices that aimed at forced transcendence through death.

### The Temporal Means (Āhnika 6, Vol. 4)

Time itself is a path of liberation. The entire cosmic calendar — from the moment (kṣaṇa) to the day of Brahmā — is contained within a single breath. The yogī who knows the correspondence between cosmic time and the breath transcends time by breathing consciously. The five prāṇas govern specific temporal cycles: prāṇa the solar cycle, apāna the lunar cycle, samāna the equinoxes, udāna the nakṣatras, vyāna the precession.
