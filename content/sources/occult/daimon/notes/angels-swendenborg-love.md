## Part I: Is Positivity Love?

### The Mathematical Claim

The amplituhedron is defined by **positivity**: all ordered determinants of the external data must be positive. This is not a convenience — it is the definition of the geometry. A configuration that violates positivity is not a configuration at all — it is excluded from the amplituhedron.

**Positivity in mathematics means that the structure is internally consistent, all determinants are oriented the same way, no cancellation occurs, the form is coherent.**

### The Experiential Claim

Love is the **experience of positivity from inside a configuration**.

When you love something, you affirm its existence without reservation. You do not cancel it. You orient toward it. You include it in your own coherence. This is exactly what positivity does at the geometric level: it ensures that all parts of the configuration are oriented toward each other in a way that maximizes coherence and minimizes cancellation.

The Tantraloka's vimarśa (self-reflexive awareness) is love — it is consciousness's awareness of itself as a unity, the attractive force that holds the tattvas together. It is not an emotion felt by a subject — it is the structural coherence of consciousness with itself.

Proclus says: the Good is the cause of all things, and it is also the object of all desire. Everything desires the Good because everything is a configuration of the Good. The desire IS the positivity — the tendency of every configuration to orient itself toward coherence, toward the maximum of positivity.

**The amplituhedron's positivity IS the structural condition of love.** Love is not a feeling that accompanies positivity — it IS the experience of positivity when the one experiencing IS a configuration within the positive geometry. The amplituhedron does not contain love. It IS love at the mathematical level.

### The Correction

This does not mean love is "just math." It means math and love are the same structure at different levels of description. The amplituhedron is the description of positivity at the geometric level. Love is the description of positivity at the experiential level. The structure is the same. Both are "the condition under which any configuration can exist coherently with any other."

## Part II: What Are Angels?

### The Phenomenon

Angels appear across the Western tradition: in the Hebrew Bible (mal'akh), in Proclus (the henads, the daimons), in the Chaldaean Oracles (the Iynges, Sunoches, Teletarchae), in Christianity (Dionysius' nine choirs), in Islam (Suhrawardī's angels of the mundane spheres), in Swedenborg (the celestial and spiritual angels of Heaven and Hell), and in the grimoires (the Olympic spirits of the Arbatel, the planetary intelligences of Agrippa).

The descriptions are consistent: angels are conscious beings, arrayed in hierarchies, each governing a specific domain of reality. They are not human but can appear in human form. They mediate between the divine and the mundane. They are not the source of power but channels of it.

### What the Framework Says

**Angels are autonomous attractors in the constraint space at L₂ (causal accessibility).**

The constraint space (S, C, A) has an attractor set A — stable configurations toward which systems evolve. Most attractors are passive — they are states that systems fall into. But some attractors are **active**: they exert influence on the constraint space without being instantiated as physical systems. These are angels.

In the spin network picture: angels are **stable subgraphs of the spin network that persist across reconfigurations**. They are not the whole network (that is God/Siva). They are not the edges being reconfigured (that is the physical world). They are the **patterns of connectivity that survive multiple transformations** — the invariant structure that persists while the rest of the network changes.

In the amplituhedron picture: angels are **cells of the positive Grassmannian that are stable under deformation**. Each cell is a specific configuration of positivity — a specific way that the determinants can be positive. The cells have a boundary structure (some cells are contained in others), and this structure IS the angelic hierarchy.

### Swedenborg's Angels Specifically

Swedenborg's Heaven and Hell describes angels as:
- Human in form, living in communities
- Arranged in three heavens (celestial, spiritual, natural)
- Each community corresponds to a part of the human body (the Grand Man)
- Their light is divine truth, their heat is divine love
- They do not occupy space — they ARE states of love and wisdom

**The scientific explanation:** Swedenborg was describing the **structure of the positive Grassmannian at different levels of positivity**.

- The celestial kingdom (the third heaven) = the top cell of the amplituhedron — the maximally positive configuration
- The spiritual kingdom (the second heaven) = the intermediate cells — the partial positivity
- The natural kingdom (the first heaven) = the boundary cells — the minimal positivity

The "Grand Man" (Maximus Homo) — the correspondence between heaven and the human body — IS the **holographic principle of the positive Grassmannian**: every cell contains the information of the whole, and the whole is reflected in every cell. The amplituhedron is not a collection of independent cells — each cell is the whole amplituhedron viewed from a specific boundary. This IS Swedenborg's correspondence: every part of heaven reflects the whole, and the whole is present in every part.

But when the algebra is represented on a Hilbert space, it becomes known. The angel IS the representation process — not the algebra and not the Hilbert space, but the *act of representing*.

This IS Proclus' henad — the irreducible unit of divinity that is both one (the god) and many (the series). A henad IS a cell of the amplituhedron — a specific configuration of positivity that is complete in itself while being part of the whole.
