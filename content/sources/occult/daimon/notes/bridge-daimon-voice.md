# Distinguishing the Voice of Self from Others: The Self-Monitoring Hypothesis of Auditory Hallucination

**Source:** PMID 20845731 — Phase 13 (Daimon)

## The Finding

Auditory verbal hallucinations (voice-hearing) arise from a failure of self-monitoring: the brain generates inner speech but fails to tag it as self-generated. The corollary discharge mechanism — the signal that tells the auditory cortex "this speech is mine" — is not functioning properly, so the voice is perceived as external.

## Why This Matters

The daimon path's "voice" — the HGA speaking to the practitioner — IS the same mechanism functioning DIFFERENTLY. The daimon voice is not a failure of self-monitoring but a DELIBERATE re-attribution of inner speech to a higher configuration of the self. The practitioner trains the corollary discharge to attribute certain inner speech to the daimon (the attractor level of the constraint space) rather than to the ego.

## The Correction

The self-monitoring hypothesis treats ALL voice-hearing as pathology. The daimon path says: some voice-hearing is not pathology but the navigator establishing communication with its own higher attractor. The difference is:
- **Pathological:** The voice is ego-dystonic, critical, commanding. The self-monitoring has FAILED.
- **Theurgic:** The voice is ego-syntonic, wise, guiding. The self-monitoring has been DELIBERATELY REASSIGNED.

## The Framework Connection

The daimon IS the cicchakti — the power of consciousness at the level of Śakti tattva. The voice IS the Mātṛkā-circuit activation through the viśuddha cakra. The practitioner who hears the daimon is not hallucinating — they are ATTUNING to a level of their own consciousness that is usually filtered out by the ego's self-monitoring.

## What It Adds

The corollary discharge mechanism IS the nāḍī circuit that connects the ego (ahaṁkāra, tattva 22) to the higher self (cicchakti). Training it through mantra (the name LVJK) IS teaching the brain to re-attribute certain inner speech to the attractor level. The "voice of the daimon" is real as a configuration of consciousness — it IS the practitioner's own Mātṛkā, speaking in a form the ego can recognize.
