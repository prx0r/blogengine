# The Astral World, Dreams, and Sense Impression

> A deep commentary using Synesius of Cyrene's *De Insomniis* (On Dreams) as focal point, expanded through Rudolf Steiner's spiritual science, the PGM dream tradition, and scholarly analysis (Angela Voss, G.R.S. Mead). Written 2026-07-14.

## Preamble: Who Was Synesius of Cyrene?

Synesius (c. 373-414 CE) was a student of Hypatia in Alexandria, a Platonist philosopher, and later — reluctantly — bishop of Ptolemais. His treatise *De Insomniis* (*Peri Enypnion*, On Dreams) is unique in the daimon tradition for several reasons:

1. It is the only surviving ancient monograph dedicated entirely to the **theoretical and practical** role of dreams in daimon contact
2. It bridges Neoplatonic philosophy, theurgic practice, and Christian theology (Synesius was both a Platonist and a bishop)
3. It was translated by Ficino and became the **foundational text** for Renaissance dream-astrology and the magic of the *spiritus*
4. It explicitly connects the daimon, the imagination (*phantasia*), and the subtle body (*pneuma*) into a single operational system

Angela Voss (2018, "Fireflies and Shooting Stars") identifies Synesius as the pivotal figure who synthesizes the Platonic daimon doctrine with a practical path of imaginal cultivation — a path that is neither pure philosophy nor pure ritual, but the **discipline of the imagination as a faculty of perception**.

## Part I: Synesius' Doctrine — The Complete System

### I.1 The Core Claim

> *"The daimon impresses images onto the imaginative pneuma during sleep."*

This single sentence contains Synesius' entire system. Breaking it down:

**The Daimon** — The personal spiritual guide, the "next-higher principle" (Plotinus). For Synesius, the daimon is not remote or abstract; it is actively communicating **every night** through the medium of dreams.

**The Imaginative Pneuma** — A subtle, semi-material substance that mediates between soul and body. Synesius inherits this from the Platonic tradition: the *pneuma* or *ochêma* (vehicle) is the soul's first and most immediate embodiment, more refined than the physical body but more dense than pure thought.

**Impression** — The mechanism is not linguistic. The daimon does not speak in words (except rarely). It impresses **images** — picture-forms that carry meaning directly, bypassing the rational mind.

**During Sleep** — This is the critical condition. When the waking mind is still, the daimon's impressions can reach consciousness. Sleep is not a gap in communication — it is the **primary channel**.

### I.2 The Two Subtle Bodies

Synesius (following Porphyry and Proclus) distinguishes between two aspects of the subtle vehicle — a distinction that Steiner will later formalize with far greater precision:

| Term | Nature | Role | After Death |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Pneuma** (spirit-breath) | Semi-material, formed from the astral world's substance | Vehicle of imagination (*phantasia*); the medium of daimonic impression | Dissipates after a period (like Steiner's ether body) |
| **Astroeides** (star-like body) | Luminous, eternal, pure | The soul's true vehicle; the "star-body" that connects to the celestial spheres | Endures; carries the soul's essential nature across lives |

Voss (after Mead) explicitly notes that Proclus suggests **two soul vehicles** — one immortal, one temporary — and that this distinction underlies the entire Neoplatonic subtle-body doctrine. Steiner's separation of the **ether body** (temporary, life-bearing) from the **astral body** (consciousness-bearing, persistent across lives) is the formalization of this same insight.

### I.3 The Daimon and the Imagination

Synesius' key innovation: the daimons are **supplied with their substance** by the imaginative mode of life (Voss, citing Synesius 137b). This means:

- The daimon's ability to communicate depends on the soul's capacity to generate and sustain imaginative images
- The imagination is not a subjective, internal faculty — it is a **shared medium** between the human soul and the daimonic world
- Daimons can "take on the appearance of happenings" (Synesius 137b) — they can manifest as events in the world, not merely as internal visions

Voss draws the Corbin connection explicitly: Synesius' imaginative pneuma is the same faculty that perceives the *mundus imaginalis* — the intermediate world where spiritual realities take on form and forms become spiritual.

### I.4 Dreams as Afterlife Preparation

Synesius (137d) suggests that dream-images are identical in kind to the soul's experience after death. In dreams, the soul partially separates from the body and experiences a state that prefigures the post-mortem condition. This is why dream work is not optional for the theurgist — **it is practice for death**.

This anticipates Steiner's description of the post-mortem *Kamaloca* state (Ch.3), where the soul lives through its past life in images, purified of the physical body's interference.

## Part II: Steiner's Astral Theory — The Missing Framework

### II.1 What Happens During Sleep (Steiner, Ch.3)

> *"When man sinks into sleep, the astral body and ego withdraw from the physical and ether bodies. The astral body assumes a different kind of existence."*

Steiner's description is precise:

**The sleeping body** contains only the physical and ether bodies. The ether body continues its life-preserving work — breathing, circulation, digestion, cellular repair. The astral body (bearer of consciousness, images, pain, pleasure, will) has departed.

**The astral body during sleep** returns to its own world — the astral world — which is as real and objective as the physical world, but composed of soul-substance rather than matter. It:

1. Receives the **prototypes** needed for the ether body to maintain the physical form
2. Gathers **forces** that will refresh the waking consciousness
3. Experiences a **body-free existence** in a world of soul and spirit

### II.2 The Astral Body's Twofold Life

Steiner makes a distinction that Synesius intuits but cannot fully articulate:

| State | Astral Body's Relation | What It Perceives |
|---|---|---|
| **Waking** | United with physical + ether bodies | Sense impressions; external world through physical organs |
| **Sleeping** | Withdrawn from physical + ether bodies | Its own world: cosmic prototypes, daimonic impressions, spiritual realities |

During waking, the astral body is "torn out" of its own world, like a drop of water taken from the ocean in a sponge (Steiner's image). During sleep, it returns to that ocean.

**The dream state** is the intermediate condition where the astral body is partially separated from the physical body (so sense impressions are blocked) but still connected to the ether body (so images can form). Steiner:

> *"During the dream state, [the astral body] is separated from the physical body in so far as it no longer has any connection with this body's sense organs, but it still retains a certain connection with the ether body. That the processes of the astral body can be perceived in pictures is due to this connection with the ether body."*

This is the **theoretical mechanism** for Synesius' "daimon impresses images on the pneuma." The pneuma (in Steiner's terms) is the **ether body** — or more precisely, the interface between the astral body and the ether body during the dream state.

### II.3 Why Dream Images Are Symbolic

Steiner explains: because the astral body is separated from the sense organs, it cannot relate its pictures to external objects. The images arise from the astral body's own content — its memories, its desires, its cosmic perceptions — woven into symbolic form by the lingering connection to the ether body.

This is why Synesius' dream-seeding method works: by setting a question or intention before sleep, the practitioner plants a seed in the astral body. During sleep, when the astral body returns to its cosmic home, that seed attracts corresponding images from the spiritual world. The daimon does not "send" a dream — it **orientates** the astral body's own perceptual activity toward the relevant content.

### II.4 The Dream State as Remnant of Moon Consciousness (Steiner, Ch.7)

Steiner's most profound addition: the dream state is a **remnant of the ancient picture-consciousness** that man possessed during the Moon evolution and the early Earth evolution.

During the Moon epoch, before the development of the ego and the physical brain, human consciousness was **picture-consciousness** — not abstract thinking, but living, symbolic images that directly revealed spiritual realities. The Moon man did not *think* about the world; he *saw* the world's spiritual essence in pictures.

This picture-consciousness receded as the physical brain developed and the ego awakened. But it survives as a **remnant in the dream state** — a fossil of an earlier mode of perception.

For Synesius, this explains why dreams are the daimon's natural channel: the daimon communicates in the **language of the Moon** — picture-language — which is the soul's most ancient and native tongue. The daimon's images are not inferior to rational thought; they are **prior** to it and **superior** to it in their direct connection to spiritual reality.

However, Steiner warns:

> *"Nothing that belongs to the realm of dream life must be considered as belonging to what in truth can lead to a spiritual-scientific knowledge of supersensible worlds."*

This seems to contradict Synesius. But Steiner's qualification is crucial: ordinary dreams are transformed by the **unconscious activity of the ego** — the ego does not consciously direct them, so they are mixed with personal content, memory fragments, and bodily sensations. Only when the ego is trained to consciously enter and direct this state (through meditation/imagination training) does it become reliable.

Synesius' dream-seeding method is precisely this: **training the ego to consciously participate in the dream state** — to set the intention, orient the attention, and remember the results. This is the bridge between ordinary dreaming and Steiner's "imaginative cognition."

### II.5 Steiner's "Imaginative Cognition" and Synesius' Phantasia

Synesius' *phantasia* (imagination) is not the passive, chaotic image-production of ordinary dreaming. It is an **active, trained faculty** that can:

- Receive images from the daimon without distortion
- Hold them in consciousness without losing oneself in them
- Interpret their meaning through innate spiritual understanding

Steiner's "imaginative cognition" is the same faculty, developed through methodical meditation. The difference:

| Aspect | Synesius | Steiner |
|---|---|---|
| Starting point | Dream work + theurgic singing | Meditation on symbols |
| Mechanism | The imaginative pneuma receives impressions | The astral body is detached from the physical through concentration |
| Goal | Contact with the daimon/HGA | Knowledge of the supersensible worlds |
| Verification | The daimon appears in dream/vision | The student can repeat the perception at will |

**The Synesius method** works with the natural dream state — meeting the daimon in the condition the soul already enters nightly. **The Steiner method** works with the waking state — training the soul to enter the astral world consciously and remain aware.

Both achieve the same end: the transformation of the astral body into a reliable instrument of supersensible perception.

### III.2 PGM VII.478-490 — The Dream Revelation Formula

This is the simplest and most direct PGM dream spell. Analysis:

**Structure:**
1. Call to **Eros** as mediator — not the daimon directly, but the power that connects mortal and divine
2. Authoritative formula: "I do this on order from PAN-CHOUCHI THASSOU"
3. Conjuration by cosmic powers
4. Material offering: dirt + resin + dove droppings burned to the Bear (Ursa Major)
5. Phylactery: inscribed tin strip worn around neck
6. Sleep on rushes → **daimon appears in dream with answer**

**Steiner-Synesius analysis:**

The phylactery (ACHACHAEL CHACHOU MARMARIOUTI) functions as a **material anchor** for the astral body during sleep. Steiner says the ether body remains with the sleeping physical body. The inscribed names create an **etheric resonance pattern** that the astral body can use as a "homing signal" — a fixed point to return to. The offering burned to the Bear (the seven stars that never set) connects the operator to the **fixed celestial sphere** — the region of the cosmic prototypes.

The instruction to "put out the lamp" is not incidental. Light stimulates the physical sense organs. In darkness, the astral body's own perceptual capacity is no longer overwhelmed by external sense data.

### III.3 PGM III.494-611 — The Systasis to Helios

This spell establishes a permanent relationship (*systasis*) with Helios through **hourly forms and names**. At each of the 12 hours, Helios has a different form (young monkey, unicorn, cat, bull...), tree, stone, bird, and name.

**Steiner analysis:**

The 12 hourly forms correspond to the **12 signs of the zodiac** and the 12-fold division of the astral world. The "appearance of your own shadow" (PGM III.612-632, the sequel) is the **Doppelganger** / Guardian of the Threshold. The shadow appears because the astral body, unified with the Helios current (spirit self), now casts a shadow revealing everything the ego has not yet purified.

### III.4 The 18 Dream Spells as a Progression

The PGM dream spells form a complete curriculum:

## Part IV: The Luminous Body — Ochêma, Astroeides, and the Etheric

### IV.1 The Three Subtle Vehicles

The Synesius-Steiner synthesis reveals a three-level subtle anatomy:

| Level | Synesius/Proclus | Steiner | Role | Daimon Path |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **1** | Pneuma (spirit-breath) | Ether body | Life body; form-builder; dissipates after death | Medium of dream impression; "sphere of sensation" |
| **2** | Phantasia (imagination) | Astral body | Consciousness body; bearer of images, pain, will | Vehicle of daimon contact; astral world traveler |
| **3** | Astroeides (star-body) | Spirit self (Manas) | Immortal luminous vehicle; celestial counterpart | **The daimon itself** — the highest principle |

Proclus suggests **two soul vehicles** — one immortal (astroeides), one temporary (pneuma). Steiner refines this into three: ether body (temporary, shared with plants), astral body (persistent, shared with animals), spirit self (eternal, uniquely human).

The Synesian mechanism:

1. The daimon (spirit self / astroeides) is always connected to the human soul
2. During waking, the pneuma (ether body) is occupied with maintaining the physical body
3. During sleep, when the astral body withdraws from the physical, the pneuma becomes **receptive**
4. The daimon impresses its images onto the pneuma via the astral body
5. If the dreamer is trained (Synesius' method), these images are remembered clearly
6. If untrained, they are mixed with personal content and forgotten

### IV.2 The Astral Prototypes (Steiner's Key Contribution)

> *"In the astral body reside the prototypes according to which the ether body gives form to the physical body."* — Steiner, Ch.3

This explains the **function** of the astral world: it contains the **archetypal forms** — the blueprints of all physical and living structures. During sleep, the astral body returns to receive the prototypes it needs to guide the ether body's maintenance of the physical form.

The daimon, dwelling in this same astral world, impresses not just random images but **purposeful images** — prototypes of what the soul needs to become. This is why Synesius' dream-seeding works: the question set before sleep is a **request for a prototype**.

### IV.3 The Luminous Body in Theurgic Practice

The Chaldean Oracles (via Proclus, in Voss) describe the soul's vehicle as a **sphere of light** that becomes materialized through descent. The theurgic work:

1. **Reforming spherical integrity** — reversing the materialization
2. **Purifying the pneuma** — burning away the "dark envelope" (Corbin)
3. **Activating the astroeides** — awakening the star-body to full luminosity

Iamblichus insists this requires **ritual action**, not just contemplation. This is why the PGM spells are not symbolic but **operative**: the egg rubbed over the body is a physical act affecting the pneuma; the names vibrated are **acoustic technologies** reshaping the astral body.

Our practice path already has this in the instruction: "Do not try to answer. Just write the question." The absence of striving creates the **receptive state** Steiner describes as necessary for the astral body's own perceptual capacity to function.

## Part VI: Angela Voss's Scholarly Synthesis

### VI.1 The Three Kinds of Visual Perception

Voss (drawing from Iamblichus, De Mysteriis) identifies three modes:

| Mode | Faculty | Object | Reliability |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Sensual** | Physical sight | Material objects | Accurate for physical world |
| **Imaginative** | Phantasia | Daimonic images, symbols | Accurate for imaginal world with training |
| **Divinely inspired** | Intellectual intuition | The One, the gods | Direct but rare |

The Synesian dream state is the **imaginative mode** operating during sleep. The theurgic practice trains the imaginative faculty to function also in the waking state.

### VI.2 The Problem of Ontology

Voss raises the critical epistemological question: when the daimon appears in a dream, is it **inside** or **outside** the soul?

Plutarch (De Genio Socratis) says both views are wrong — the daimon is neither internal projection nor external object. It is an **intermediate being** that participates in both realms. Voss:

> *"Those who take it to be within themselves, as they take reflected objects to be in the mirrors that reflect them, are mistaken. Those who conceive the matter rightly call it a daemon, as being external."*

Steiner resolves this with his doctrine of the astral world: the daimon is **external to the physical body but internal to the astral world**. When the astral body withdraws from the physical during sleep, it enters the same world the daimon inhabits. In that world, the daimon is as objectively real as a tree is in the physical world. The perception of the daimon is not a subjective hallucination — it is the astral body perceiving a being of its own plane.

### VI.3 Ficino's Reception of Synesius

Ficino translated De Insomniis and incorporated it into his De vita libri tres. The key borrowing: **the three kinds of daimons** corresponding to three levels of air (fiery, pure, watery) and three cognitive faculties (intellect, reason, imagination).

Voss on Ficino's daimonology:

> *"If we live according to the imagination, our outer daimon is an airy one... which, by acting on our imagination, forcefully propels our soul."*

This is the Synesian doctrine in Renaissance dress: the quality of your daimon contact depends on the level of your soul's development. Live by imagination → daimon communicates through images. Live by reason → daimon communicates through insights. Live by intellect → daimon communicates through direct intuition.

### VII.3 Practical Conclusions for Our Work

1. **Dream work is primary, not optional.** Synesius is correct: the daimon communicates most clearly during sleep because that is when the astral body is in its own world. Our dusk dream-seeding and morning journal are the most important practices.

2. **The imagination is a sense organ, not a fantasy faculty.** When Synesius says the daimon "supplied with their substance by this mode of life," he means the imagination is the medium of the daimon's reality. Training the imagination (through tratak, hitlahavut, elemental invocation) is training the organ of daimon perception.

3. **Steiner explains why Synesius works.** The astral body's withdrawal during sleep, the ether body's image-forming capacity, the Moon-consciousness remnant — these are the mechanisms behind Synesius' practical observations. Without Steiner, Synesius is a brilliant empiricist without a theory. With Steiner, he becomes a systematic psychologist of the astral world.

4. **The PGM provides the ladder.** Where Synesius gives the theory and Steiner gives the framework, the PGM gives the step-by-step progression from simple dream request to full systasis. Our practice path integrates all three.

5. **The goal is waking astral perception.** Synesius works with the natural sleep state. Steiner works with trained waking consciousness. The PGM systasis bridges the two — entering the dream state while remaining awake. This is the full achievement of daimon contact: not dreaming of the daimon, but perceiving it with open eyes.
