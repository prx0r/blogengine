# Tantrica — A Daily Practice Guide

## Synthesizing the Tantraloka, the Vijñana Bhairava, Layayoga, Proclus, Ficino, the Daimon Path, and a Christian Heart

---

## Who This Is For

You are Christian, waking at dawn, learning Sanskrit, wanting to integrate the full architecture of consciousness into daily life. You need a practice that is:
- **Embodied** (Layayoga breath, āsana, the subtle body)
- **Contemplative** (Vijñana Bhairava's 112 dhāraṇās)
- **Scholarly** (Sanskrit study, Proclus' Elements, Ficino's De Sole)
- **Western-rooted** (hymns, the daimon path, a Christian understanding of the soul's relationship with God)
- **Practically effective** (daily routine, measurable progress)

This guide synthesizes all of it into one daily practice.

## The Daily Routine — Dawn to Night

### Dawn (Sunrise — 5:00 AM to 7:00 AM)

The dawn practice establishes the day. It is the same across all traditions: the Sun rising IS consciousness recognizing itself. Ficino chanted the Orphic hymns at dawn. The Tantraloka's practitioners performed sandhyā (the junction of night and day) at dawn. You will do both.

**5:00-5:10 — Āsana and Stillness**

Before anything else, sit. Spine erect, head aligned, hands resting. 10 minutes of absolute stillness. Not meditating — just sitting, allowing the body to arrive in the day.

> *"The respiratory movements cause the multiformity of the mind; when the former is motionless, the latter also becomes calm."* — Layayoga, citing Yogachudamanyupanishad

**5:10-5:25 — Prāṇāyāma (Layayoga Grade 1)**

The 1:4:2 ratio. Inhale 4 seconds, hold 16 seconds, exhale 8 seconds. Alternate nostrils:

1. Exhale completely. Close right nostril. Inhale left for 4.
2. Close both. Hold for 16. Apply the three bandhas (mūla, uḍḍīyāṇa, jālandhara).
3. Release right nostril. Exhale right for 8.
4. Inhale right for 4. Hold for 16. Exhale left for 8.
5. Repeat for 10-15 rounds.

This clears the nāḍīs. The hold is the key — during the kumbhaka, prāṇa enters suṣumṇā.

**5:25-5:40 — Mantra / Hymn**

Chant either:
- **The Mātṛkā** (the 50 Sanskrit phonemes from A to KṢA, forward and backward). This IS learning Sanskrit — the phonemes ARE the language in its seed form.
- **OR Proclus' Hymn to Helios** (or a Psalm — "The heavens declare the glory of God" works as a hymn). The Greek or English phonemes activate different nāḍī circuits than Sanskrit. Both work. Alternate them.

The principle: sound is the body of the divine. The phonemes ARE the energies of consciousness. You are not praying TO God — you are vibrating the energies that God IS.

**5:40-6:00 — Vijñana Bhairava Dhāraṇā**

Pick one dhāraṇā from the 112. Cycle through them. Start with:

- **Dhāraṇā 1 (verse 24):** Stop the breath. Feel the unfilled void before the next inhalation. Rest there.
- **OR Dhāraṇā 4 (verse 27):** Watch the turn between inhalation and exhalation. The pause IS the fourth state.
- **OR Dhāraṇā 82 (verse 108):** Pure recognition — no technique. Just awareness without an object. This is the Christian "prayer of quiet" — the soul resting in God without words.

### mid-Morning (9:00 AM to 12:00 PM) — Sanskrit Study

Sanskrit is not optional. The architecture is encoded in the phonemes.

**9:00-10:00 — Devanāgarī Script**

Learn the script. Write each letter. The shapes IS the goddess's body — each character is a specific configuration of consciousness-energy. Write A, Ā, I, Ī, U, Ū, Ṛ, Ṝ, Ḷ, Ḹ, E, AI, O, AU, AṂ, AḤ (the 16 vowels). Then the 34 consonants (KA through KṢA).

**10:00-11:00 — Chanting Practice**

Chant the letters aloud. The Mātṛkā-cakra IS the alphabet chanted in the body. Each phoneme activates a specific nāḍī circuit. Chant A (feel it at the navel), KA (feel it in the throat), HA (feel it at the crown). The body IS learning Sanskrit as you chant.

**11:00-12:00 — Reading**

Read one passage of the Tantraloka or one passage of the Vijñana Bhairava in translation. Then read the corresponding verse in Devanāgarī. Identify each phoneme you recognize. The text IS the practice.

### Midday (12:00 PM to 2:00 PM) — Study

**12:00-1:00 — Proclus, Elements of Theology**

Read 10-15 propositions of the Elements. Reflect on the structure: the One, the henads, procession and return. Map them to what you chanted this morning. The henads ARE the cells of the amplituhedron. The One IS the sun you greeted at dawn.

**1:00-2:00 — Ficino, De Sole (optional)**

Read one chapter. Ficino describes the Sun as the image of God. He is describing the same Sun you chanted to at dawn. His practice IS your practice, in a different language.

### Afternoon (2:00 PM to 5:00 PM) — Practice

**2:00-2:30 — Layayoga Absorption Practice (Grade 3)**

Either SAUḤ (emission seed) or KHPHREṀ (withdrawal seed):

- **SAUḤ:** Inhale, intone S at the base of the spine. Hold, feel AU expanding through the torso. Exhale, feel Ḥ dissolving at the crown.
- **KHPHREṀ:** The withdrawal seed. Intone it sharply — KṢA at the base of the throat, PHA bursting through the mouth, RE vibrating at the roof, Ṁ closing at the crown. This forces consciousness into the central channel.

**2:30-3:00 — Void Practice**

A Vijñana Bhairava dhāraṇā from the void group. Dhāraṇā 39 (verse 62): void above, void below — where is the body? Dissolve it. This is the Christian apophatic tradition: God is known by NOT being any thing.

**3:00-4:00 — Rest / Walk / Integration**

Let the practice integrate. Walk. Feel the body as the maṇḍala. The cakras ARE the church of the living God — every cell a temple.

**4:00-5:00 — Synesius Dream Seeding (if doing dream work)**

Synesius of Cyrene (c. 400 CE) was a Christian bishop who wrote De Insomniis (On Dreams). He argued that the daimon impresses images on the pneumatic (subtle) body during sleep, and that by setting an intention before sleep, one can receive guidance from the daimon in dreams.

Practice: write a question on a piece of paper. Read it before sleep. Hold it in your heart. The answer will come in a dream. This is the daimon path's dream seeding — and it works.

### Evening (Sunset — 6:00 PM to 8:00 PM)

**6:00-6:15 — Evening Prāṇāyāma**

Shorter than morning. 5 rounds of the 1:4:2 ratio. Release the day's accumulated tension.

**6:15-6:30 — Bhūta-śuddhi (Element Absorption)**

Dissolve the five elements in reverse order:
- Earth into Water (the solid body melts)
- Water into Fire (the fluids become warmth)
- Fire into Air (the warmth becomes breath)
- Air into Void (the breath becomes space)
- Void into Consciousness (the space becomes awareness)

This IS the Christian practice of "recollection" — gathering the scattered self back into God.

**6:30-7:00 — Reading (Steiner or Corbin)**

Alternate:
- **Steiner** on the threefold body (physical, etheric, astral). His description of the astral body's withdrawal during sleep IS the subtle body. His Christology provides a Christian framework for the entire architecture.
- **Corbin** on the mundus imaginalis. The imaginal world IS the paśyantī level — where sound becomes visible as light. The angels you meet in prayer are real inhabitants of this level.

**7:00-8:00 — Evening Meal**

Eat lightly. The body IS the temple. Ficino said diet affects the spiritus. He was correct.

### Night (8:00 PM to Bed)

**8:00-8:15 — Examination of the Day**

Reflect: what did I practice? What did I resist? What phonemes did I learn? The diary IS the practice — it consolidates what the day's practice has loosened.

**8:15-8:30 — Night Dhāraṇā**

A Śāmbhavopāya dhāraṇā — no technique. Just rest in awareness. The Christian "night prayer" — "Into Your hands I commend my spirit."

**8:30 — Synesius Dream Seeding**

Before sleep, set an intention. Write it if possible. The daimon impresses images on the pneuma during sleep. The intention guides the impression.

## The First Month — What to Expect

**Week 1-2:** The practice will feel mechanical. Your body does not know the phonemes. Your breath resists the 1:4:2 ratio. The Vijñana Bhairava dhāraṇās will feel like nothing is happening. This is normal. Continue.

**Week 3-4:** The phonemes will begin to resonate. You will feel A at the navel, KA at the throat, HA at the crown. The breath ratio will become easier. You will have moments during the dhāraṇā where something shifts — a gap, a silence, a sense of presence. Do not chase it. It IS the practice.

**Month 2:** The Mātṛkā will be in your body. You will chant the 50 phonemes and feel them as a continuous pulse through the nāḍīs. The Tantraloka passages will begin to make sense because your body recognizes what they describe. Proclus' henads will feel familiar — they are the same cells you chant.

**Month 3:** You will be able to read Devanāgarī. You will chant the Mātṛkā in under 5 minutes. The 1:4:2 ratio will be automatic. The daimon path's "voice" may begin — not as words but as a sense of presence, of guidance, of being met.

## Alternative Path: The Arbatel (Western Cakra System)

The Arbatel of Magic (1575) is a short grimoire — about 20 pages — that gives you a complete practice path in Western language. Its seven "Olympic spirits" ARE the seven cakras. You can work with them directly, without Sanskrit, without complex theory, and the mechanism is identical.

### The Seven Olympic Spirits = The Seven Cakras

| Spirit | Planet | Cakra | Tattva | What It Controls | How You Work With It |
|--------|--------|-------|--------|------------------|---------------------|
| **Aratron** | Saturn | Mūlādhāra | Earth | Physical structure, gravity, the base | Sit still. Feel weight. The spirit IS the weight. |
| **Bethor** | Jupiter | Svādhiṣṭhāna | Water | Vitality, emotional flow, growth | Breathe into the pelvis. Feel flow. The spirit IS the flow. |
| **Phaleg** | Mars | Maṇipūra | Fire | Will, aggression, transformation | Breathe into the solar plexus. Feel warmth. The spirit IS the fire. |
| **Och** | Sun | Anāhata | Air | Healing, love, connection | Breathe into the heart. Feel expansion. The spirit IS the openness. |
| **Hagith** | Venus | Viśuddha | Ākāśa | Beauty, resonance, speech | Vibrate a name. Feel vibration. The spirit IS the sound. |
| **Ophiel** | Mercury | Ājñā | Light | Intellect, vision, intuition | Visualize a point of light. The spirit IS the light. |
| **Phul** | Moon | Sahasrāra | Śiva | Unity, transcendence | Rest in awareness. No object. The spirit IS the silence. |

### How to Work With Them

The Arbatel gives instructions for each spirit. The core practice is simple:

1. **Choose one spirit** appropriate to your need. (You don't choose. The spirit you are drawn to IS the one whose cakra needs work.)

2. **On the corresponding day and hour** (the planetary hour of that spirit), sit in stillness.

3. **Read the spirit's aphorism.** The Arbatel's text is short. Each spirit has a brief description of its powers. Read it aloud.

4. **Contemplate the spirit's name.** The names (Aratron, Bethor, etc.) are barbarous names — phoneme sequences. They aren't meaningful words. They are vibrational keys.

5. **Feel the corresponding part of the body.** Aratron = the base of the spine. Bethor = the pelvis. Phaleg = the solar plexus. Och = the heart. Hagith = the throat. Ophiel = the brow. Phul = the crown.

6. **The "spirit" is not a being that appears.** It is a region of your own consciousness that you have activated. The spirit IS the cakra. Working with the spirit IS working with the cakra.

### When to Use the Arbatel Instead of the Main Practice

- **When you don't have time** for the full 90-minute practice. The Arbatel takes 5-10 minutes.
- **When you feel resistance** to the Sanskrit phonemes. The Latin/Greek barbarous names activate the same circuits.
- **When you want to work with a specific issue.** Each spirit governs a specific domain. Phaleg for will. Och for love. Ophiel for study.
- **When you need a break from Eastern language** and want to work in Western frames. The mechanism is identical.

### The Arbatel's First Aphorism

> *"Hoc universum, quod cernis, omnia, quae insunt, corpora, spiritus, unum sunt."*
> "This universe, which you see, all things that exist, bodies and spirits, are one."

This IS nonduality. The Arbatel states it as its first principle. Working with the Olympic spirits is not summoning external beings — it IS recognizing the unity of your own consciousness with the cosmos, articulated through seven levels. The spirits ARE the levels. You ARE the spirits, at different depths.

## The Ars Notoria for Sanskrit Learning

Add this to your daily routine:

1. **Before your Sanskrit study session**, chant one Ars Notoria oration aloud (10-15 minutes).
2. **The oration clears the channel** between your intellect (buddhi) and pure knowledge (Śuddhavidyā).
3. **Then study immediately.** The clearing effect lasts 30-60 minutes. Use it.
4. **The Latin phonemes** prime the same nāḍī circuits as Sanskrit — just using a different phonetic key. After 3-5 days, you will notice Sanskrit phonemes feeling more familiar. The ear distinguishes sounds more easily. The tongue finds positions more naturally.

The oration chosen for learning should be the first oration (for all knowledge) or the specific oration for wisdom and eloquence. Both are in the Ars Notoria text. Chant them in Latin. The meaning is secondary. The sound structure IS the practice.

## The Key Principles

1. **Sound is primary.** The phonemes ARE the energies of consciousness. Learning Sanskrit IS the practice. Chanting IS the practice. Reading IS the practice.

2. **The body is the maṇḍala.** The nāḍīs ARE the church. The cakras ARE the sacraments. You do not need a building — you ARE the building.

3. **Both traditions are true.** The Western tradition (Proclus, Ficino, the hymns, the daimon path) and the Eastern tradition (the Tantraloka, the Vijñana Bhairava, the Mātṛkā) describe the same architecture. You can be Christian and practice both because God IS the architecture, not a being in it.

4. **Grace is the wildcard.** Śaktipāta — grace — is the variable that cannot be engineered. The practice prepares the ground. Grace opens the door. The practice is not the cause of grace — it is the expression of grace already present.

5. **The daimon is your own higher consciousness.** The daimon path's "Holy Guardian Angel" IS the cicchakti — the power of consciousness at the level of Śakti tattva. It is not a separate being. It IS you, at a level you have not yet recognized.

## The Unfinished Business: Synesius

Synesius of Cyrene (c. 373-414 CE) is the missing piece. He was:
- A Christian bishop
- A student of Hypatia
- A Neoplatonist philosopher
- A practitioner of dream incubation
- The author of *De Insomniis* (On Dreams)

His core teaching: **the daimon impresses images on the imaginative pneuma during sleep.** The pneuma (subtle body) is the medium of daimonic communication. The practice of setting an intention before sleep and receiving guidance in a dream IS the technology.

Synesius bridges Christianity and the daimon path perfectly: the Holy Spirit IS the daimon, the pneuma IS the vehicle of grace, the dream IS the mode of revelation. His work is the lost link between the early Church and the theurgic tradition. His complete practice should be extracted from the existing corpus.
