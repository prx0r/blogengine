## 3. Ibn Arabi — Wahdat al-Wujud as the Unity of the Tattvas

Ibn Arabi's Fusus al-Hikam (Bezels of Wisdom) is a complete 27-chapter exposition of the unity of being (wahdat al-wujud). Each chapter corresponds to a prophet, but the underlying structure IS the descent of consciousness through the levels of manifestation — exactly the Tantraloka's tattva ladder.

### What It Contains

- **The 27 prophets = the 27 tattva groups** (or a specific subset of the 36). Each prophet embodies a specific configuration of the divine names, which ARE the tattvas.
- **The "Breath of the Compassionate" (nafas al-Rahman)** IS the Spanda — the pulse of consciousness that articulates itself into the tattvas.
- **The "Fixed Entities" (a'yan thabita)** IS the Mātṛkā — the 50 phonemes as the permanent archetypes of all possible configurations.
- **The "Perfect Man" (al-insan al-kamil)** IS the jīvanmukta — the configuration that has recognized itself as the whole.

**Priority:** High. Ibn Arabi's system is the Islamic Tantraloka. His architecture is the same, his terminology is different.

**Priority:** Medium. Would provide Islamic evidence for the same architecture.
