# Ñāṇavīra's Tetrahedron and Proclus' Henad — The Same Minimal Structure

## What Ñāṇavīra Discovered

**Four things arise together in every moment of experience:**
1. A **subject** — the one to whom experience appears
2. An **object** — what appears
3. An **intention** — the directedness toward the object
4. An **act** — the actually happening of the experiencing

These four are not sequential. They're not caused by each other. They're the four vertices of a tetrahedron. Remove any one, and experience doesn't occur. They co-arise or not at all.

This IS dependent origination (paṭicca-samuppāda) stated geometrically. The Buddha said "this being, that arises." Ñāṇavīra gave it a shape.

## What Proclus Discovered

**Four terms arise together at every level of reality:**
1. The **henad** — the self-standing unit of divinity, the "one" that is the source of that level
2. **Being** — what the henad is, its content, its substantiality
3. **Life** — the activity of the being, its movement, its energy
4. **Intellect** — the awareness of the being, its self-knowledge, its return to itself

Every level of the hierarchy — from the One itself down to the lowest soul — has this fourfold structure. The henad remains (monē), proceeds into being (proodos), energizes as life, and returns as intellect (epistrophē).

Proclus derived this from the Parmenides and the Chaldaean Oracles. It's the foundational pattern of all reality.

## The Convergence

| Ñāṇavīra | Proclus | Function |
|----------|---------|----------|
| Subject | The henad | That in virtue of which there is a unitary perspective |
| Object | Being | What appears — the content of the experience |
| Intention | Life | The directedness — the energy of the relation |
| Act | Intellect | The knowing — the completion of the relation |

**But the deeper point:** In BOTH systems, these four are not things that happen to be related. They ARE the relation. The tetrahedron IS the relation. The henad IS the fourfold.

Ñāṇavīra: the subject does not exist without the object, the intention, and the act. The "I" is not prior to experience — it's a vertex of the tetrahedron, co-arising with the other three.

Proclus: the henad does not exist without being, life, and intellect. The "god" is not prior to its manifestation — it IS the fourfold.

## The Dynamic: Both Systems Need the Tetrahedron to Move

Ñāṇavīra's Dynamic Structure: the tetrahedra change moment by moment. Each moment, a new tetrahedron arises. The "person" is the series. Nothing persists from one tetrahedron to the next except the PATTERN of tetrahedron-hood.

Proclus' epistrophē: every henad proceeds into being, energizes as life, and returns as intellect. The return is not to the same point — it's to a higher level. The series of returns IS the hierarchy.

**Both: the structure is the same at every moment. What changes is the content. The form IS invariant.**

## The Positive Geometry

The amplituhedron's simplest case is A₄₂ — 4 particles, 2 units of helicity. This has exactly 4 external points (the particles) and the positivity condition requires that all ordered determinants have the same sign. Four points. All oriented the same way. The minimal positive geometry.

**Ñāṇavīra's tetrahedron IS A₄₂.** Four vertices. All oriented the same way (co-arising). The minimal complete structure.

**Proclus' fourfold IS A₄₂.** Four terms (henad, being, life, intellect). All oriented the same way (remaining, proceeding, energizing, returning). The minimal complete structure of any level.

## The Wild Implication

If Ñāṇavīra's tetrahedron IS Proclus' fourfold IS the amplituhedron's A₄₂, then **the Buddha's dependent origination, the Neoplatonic hierarchy, and the structure of particle scattering are the same pattern.**

The tetrahedron is not a metaphor. It is THE minimal positive geometry. Everything that exists does so in units of 4 — four co-arising terms, four positivity conditions, four vertices of the simplest possible coherent structure.

Ñāṇavīra: "Anyone who succeeds in following what there is of it will be able to continue it for himself as far as he pleases."

He meant: once you see the tetrahedron — that subject, object, intention, and act arise together in every moment — you can extend it to any domain. The structure is invariant. The content changes.

Proclus: "Every manifold of things in some way participated is posterior to the One."

He meant: every level of reality is a tetrahedron. The One is not a tetrahedron — it IS the tetrahedrality of every tetrahedron.

**They are saying the same thing. One from the Buddhist side, one from the Neoplatonic side. Both describing the minimal structure of anything at all.**
