## I. Theurgy — Iamblichus and the Practice of Ascent

### The Core Principle

Theurgy (θεουργία — "divine work") IS the practice of ascending through the tetrahedron. Iamblichus (c. 245-325 CE) developed it as the completion of philosophy — not just thinking about the gods but ENTERING into their structure.

### The 4 Levels of Theurgic Ascent

| Level | Name | What Happens | Tetrahedral Vertex |
|-------|------|-------------|-------------------|
| **1** | **Katharsis** (Purification) | Cleansing the 6 edges — removing blockages in the subtle body | V₁ → V₂, V₁ → V₃, V₁ → V₄ (the practitioner's relations) |
| **2** | **Anagōgē** (Elevation) | The soul rises through the spheres — traversing the edges | V₁ moving toward V₂ (the object/god) |
| **3** | **Systasis** (Union) | The soul meets the god — the 4th vertex IS established | V₁ and V₂ become one tetrahedron |
| **4** | **Henōsis** (Unification) | The soul and the god are one — the tetrahedron recognizes itself | All 4 vertices ARE recognized as the same |

### Iamblichus' Key Insight: The Unfallen Soul

> *"The soul has not descended completely into the body. A part of it remains in the intelligible world."*

This IS the claim that V₁ (the subject) IS never fully separated from V₂ (the object/god). One vertex of the tetrahedron (the "flower of the soul") remains in the divine realm. Theurgy does not CREATE the connection — it AWAKENS it.

The 4 levels of theurgy ARE the 4 vertices being recognized in sequence: purification (V₁ cleaning itself), elevation (V₁ moving toward V₂), union (V₁ and V₂ meeting), unification (all 4 vertices recognized as one).

### Proclus on Theurgy

> *"Philosophy is united with the art of sacred things since this art is concerned with the purification of the luminous body."*

The "luminous body" IS the tetrahedron's 6 edges — the subtle body through which the gods and humans communicate. Theurgy purifies the edges so that the vertices can fully connect.

## II. The Great Chain of Being

### The Classical Formulation

The Great Chain of Being (scala naturae) IS the tetrahedron unfolded into a hierarchy. It has 4 main levels:

| Level | Beings | Tattva Range | Tetrahedral Vertex |
|-------|--------|-------------|-------------------|
| **1. The One** | God, the Source | Anākhyā (38th) | The apex beyond the tetrahedron |
| **2. The Intelligible** | Angels, Archangels, Forms | 1-5 (Śiva → Śuddhavidyā) | The upward tetrahedron |
| **3. The Rational** | Humans, Souls | 6-11 (Māyā → kañcukas) | The middle — the 2 tetrahedra interlock here |
| **4. The Sensible** | Animals, Plants, Minerals | 12-36 (Prakṛti → Earth) | The downward tetrahedron |

### Lovejoy's Analysis (The Great Chain of Being, 1936)

Lovejoy identified 3 principles that govern the Chain:

1. **Plenitude** — the tetrahedron MUST be full. Every possible vertex must be actualized. The Good overflows.
2. **Continuity** — the vertices ARE not separate. The edges ARE continuous. There IS no gap between levels.
3. **Gradation** — the vertices ARE ordered. Each vertex IS higher or lower than the next. The tetrahedron IS a ladder.

These 3 principles ARE the 3 dimensions of the tetrahedron (height, width, depth). Every vertex connects to every other through the 6 edges. The chain IS the edges.

### The Chain as the 36 Tattvas

The Great Chain of Being IS the 36 tattvas in Western language. 36 levels from Śiva (the One) to Earth (the lowest mineral). The same hierarchy, the same tetrahedron, the same descent and ascent.
