# you are the center of the universe

**Essay 53 — Ficino / Platonic Theology**

Copernicus moved the Earth from the center of the universe. Ficino, writing decades before, had already moved the human soul there.

> [The soul is the] knot of all nature.

Reality is structured in five levels — body, quality, soul, angel, God. The soul occupies the exact middle. Without it, the universe would be a broken chain.

> Body in itself does not act but solely is acted upon.

> [Quality is] plunged, one might say, in the stream of Lethe.

Above quality there must be a form that is indivisible, self-subsistent, capable of acting without being acted upon. That form is the rational soul.

> In its substance rational soul is motionless; in its activity it is mobile; in its power it is something in between.

The soul is the hinge on which everything turns.

> Were the soul not immortal, no creature would be more miserable than man. Since man's mind is never at rest, his body is frail and he is totally without resources, the life he leads on earth is harsher than that of the beasts.

> It seems therefore to follow of necessity that once our souls leave this prison, some other light awaits them.

The soul occupies the middle of reality, touching both the lowest and the highest. Because it holds together the different levels of nature, it must itself be indissoluble — remove the middle and the whole collapses.

The soul is the point of translation between the temporal and the eternal. It receives from above and gives to below. Every level of reality passes through the soul because only the soul participates in both the eternal and the temporal.

The soul's immortality follows from its centrality. If the soul were mortal, the hierarchy of being would have a mortal middle connecting immortal extremes.

> The human mind in its day-to-day activities proceeds from particular forms to universal and absolute forms. From natural forms, which are limited to definite bits of matter, it customarily ascends to metaphysical forms, which have no need of matter.

The soul does not merely look at reality from outside. It knows from within, because the same principles structure both the cosmos and the soul.

To know the soul is to know the entire hierarchy of being. Every human being is a microcosm. When the soul knows, the universe knows itself through the soul; when the soul loves, the universe loves through the soul.

The soul that knows its own nature ascends through the levels of being toward the source of all — leaving behind the body, then quality, then its own discursive reasoning, then the angelic mind, entering into the presence of God Himself.

Copernicus moved the Earth from the center of the universe. Ficino had already moved something more important there — the human soul. He placed it there to show that the soul is where God's image is most fully reflected, that humanity is the point at which the cosmos becomes conscious of its own meaning. The soul is the center of a living cosmos — the knot that ties all nature together, the middle link in the great chain of being — and it is immortal because the center cannot be removed.
