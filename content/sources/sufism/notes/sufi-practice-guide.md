# The Imaginal Path: Sufi Practices for Daimon Contact

A practical guide based on Corbin, Suhrawardi, Ibn Arabi, and the Kubrawi tradition.

---

## Foundational Principle

The daimon (Guide of light, Perfect Nature, Witness in Heaven) dwells in the *mundus imaginalis* — the intermediate world between matter and spirit. You cannot reach it by thinking harder or feeling more intensely. You must enter the imaginal world. The following practices are the keys to that door.

---

## 1. Dhikr (Remembrance): Burning the Dark Envelope

**Source**: Najm Kobra (Corbin, *Man of Light* §5, pp. 73-76)

The *dhikr* is the primary technique for freeing the "particle of divine light" imprisoned in the darkness of the lower soul (*nafs ammara*). Its function is twofold: it burns the dark envelope (the shadow/Doppelgänger) and it attracts the descending light of the heavenly Witness.

### Method

1. **Posture**: Sit with crossed legs, right hand on left hand, the latter holding the right leg placed on the left thigh. Spine straight, eyes closed.

2. **The Formula**: *La ilaha illa'llah* ("There is no god but God")

3. **The Three Phases**:
   - **Phase 1 — Verbal**: Repeat the formula aloud, coordinating with breath. The negative (*la ilaha*) is expelled on the outbreath, denying all claims of the lower ego. The affirmative (*illa'llah*) is drawn in on the inbreath, affirming the exclusive reality of the Real. Continue until the words begin to repeat themselves without effort.
   - **Phase 2 — Heart**: The *dhikr* "falls" into the heart. You sense the heart as a well and the *dhikr* as a bucket being lowered into it. The repetition becomes automatic, continuous, without voluntary effort. The heart is "Isa ibn Maryam," the *dhikr* the milk that nourishes him — the birth of the spiritual Child.
   - **Phase 3 — The Secret (*sirr*)**: The *dhikr* sinks into the transconsciousness. "Its fire does not cease to blaze, its lights no longer disappear. Without interruption you see lights rising and lights descending."

### What to Expect

- At first: darkness, perhaps a black cloud or black circle before the inner sight. This is the *nafs ammara* — the dark envelope.
- With persistence: the darkness begins to show a crescent of light at its edges, like the new moon.
- The fire of the *dhikr* burns the veil: "It sets fire to all that is there to be consumed, and sheds light on any darkness it may encounter."
- The green light (*visio smaragdina*) shines at the mouth of the well — the sign that the heart has awakened.

### Duration

Practice daily. The phases unfold over months, not hours. Najm Kobra indicates that the *dhikr* should become so continuous that "were the mystic to abandon the *dhikr*, the *dhikr* would not abandon him."

## 2. The Shahid (Witness) Meditation — Najm Kobra's Method

**Source**: Corbin, *Man of Light* §9, pp. 84-88

This meditation directly invokes the vision of the "person of light" — the *shakhs nurani* who is your Guide.

### Method

1. **Preliminary**: Engage in *dhikr* until the inner light has become stable (typically weeks or months of daily practice).

2. **The Practice**:
   - Close the eyes. Turn the attention to the space before the face.
   - Observe what appears in this inner space. Do not construct or imagine — simply watch.
   - At first there may be darkness, colored lights, or swirling forms. Do not grasp at any of these.
   - Maintain the *dhikr* silently. The fire of the *dhikr* consumes the dross.

3. **The Emergence**:
   - "When the circle of the face has become pure, it effuses lights as a spring pours forth its water."
   - The outpouring begins between the two eyes, between the eyebrows, then spreads to cover the whole face.
   - "Before you, before your face, there is another Face also of light, irradiating lights."
   - This is your own face — the face of your spiritual individuality, the *shahid*.

4. **The Full Vision**:
   - The veil falls. The whole person is revealed as light.
   - "Suddenly you are gazing at a person of light (*shakhs nurani*) who is also irradiating lights."
   - This is the suprasensory Guide (*moqaddam al-ghayb*), the Witness in Heaven (*shahid fi'l-sama*).

### Key Principle

This is not visualization. You are not *making* an image. You are *perceiving* what is already there, but hidden by the opacity of your own dark envelope. The *dhikr* burns away the opacity. The Guide is always present — you are becoming capable of seeing.

## 3. Sohravardi's Psalm to Perfect Nature — Template for Invocation

**Source**: Corbin, *Man of Light* p. 21

This is the most beautiful prayer ever directed to the Angel. Sohravardi composed it as a personal liturgy, following the instructions of the "Sabeans" who received it from Hermes himself.

### The Psalm (adapted for personal use)

> "Thou, my lord and prince, my most holy angel, my precious spiritual being,
> Thou art the Spirit who gave birth to me, and Thou art the Child to whom my spirit gives birth.
> Thou who art clothed in the most brilliant of divine Lights,
> Manifest Thyself to me in the most beautiful of epiphanies,
> Show me the light of Thy dazzling face,
> Be for me the mediator,
> Lift the veils of darkness from my heart."

### How to Use It

1. **When**: At dawn (*cognitio matutina* — morning knowledge) or at midnight (the hour of the "midnight sun"). The "pillar of dawn" is when the being of light breaks down the walls of the temple of light.

2. **Preparation**: Perform *dhikr* first until the heart is warm. The psalm should issue from the state of *hitlahavut* (ardor, ecstatic burning).

3. **Delivery**: Recite it as personal address — you are speaking to your Perfect Nature directly. Sohravardi indicates that the one who gives birth is simultaneously the one who is born: you are speaking to the Angel, but the Angel is speaking through you.

4. **Frequency**: The Sabean liturgy prescribes recitation at least twice a year as a minimum; daily practice is the ideal.

### Variation: The Hermetic Invocation

> "How does one achieve knowledge of wisdom? Through Perfect Nature.
> What is the root of wisdom? Perfect Nature.
> What is the key to wisdom? Perfect Nature."

This triadic structure can be used as a mantra-like preparation before the psalm.

## 4. The Tajalli (Theophany) — The Moment of Daimonic Recognition

**Source**: Ibn Arabi, *Fusus al-Hikam*; Corbin, *Man of Light* pp. 17-25

The *tajalli* is the self-disclosure of the Real through the form that corresponds to the beholder. In practical terms, it is the moment when you recognize that the Guide of light is both other than you and identical to you — the *unus-ambo*.

### The Recognition Formula

The prince cuts a spider's thread in two, then puts it together again: **1 × 1**.

This formula is the key to the entire operation. Meditate on it:

- **1 = 1** is pseudomystical monism — the absorption of the individual into the All. This is not the goal.
- **n + 1** is abstract monotheism — a God external to creation. This is not the goal either.
- **1 × 1** is the bi-unity: an essence raised to its power by being multiplied by itself. Two who are one without ceasing to be two.

### How to Recognize the Tajalli

The moment of recognition is signaled by:

1. **Reciprocity**: The Guide looks at you with your own look; you look at it with its look. The Contemplator becomes the Contemplated. This is the *shahid* — the witness who is simultaneously the witnessed.

2. **The Eckhartian formula**: "The seeing through which I know him is the same seeing through which he knows me."

3. **The Song of the Pearl**: "We were two, separated from one another, but nevertheless only one, of similar form."

4. **The Hallajian Cry**: "I am he whom I love; he whom I love is me" — but understood as the secret, not the claim: *Ana sirr al-Haqq* ("I am God's secret") as Ibn Arabi corrects Hallaj.

### The Danger

Semnani warns: the stage of the "black light" (the *latifa khafiya* / arcanum, typified by Jesus) is the most perilous. Here the mystic may mistake the *fana fi'llah* (absorption in God) for the final state and cry out "I am God" — a premature rupture of the process. The true goal is the *latifa haqqiya* (the divine center, typified by Muhammad), marked by the green light of the *visio smaragdina*.

**The key**: The Guide is not a predicate of your ego. You do not *become* the Guide. You become the organ through which the Guide manifests — "the eye through which he sees, the ear through which he hears."

## 5. Practical: How to Enter the Mundus Imaginalis

**Source**: Suhrawardi's *Recital of the Occidental Exile*; Corbin, *Man of Light* pp. 39-48

The *mundus imaginalis* is not a place you travel to. It is a dimension you learn to perceive. The following is a practical procedure based on the Sohravardian recital.

### The Orientation

1. **Turn toward the cosmic North**: Not the geographical north, but the vertical dimension — "above" in the sense of inward, transcendent. Sit facing north. The Qibla of the imaginal journey is the pole, not Mecca.

2. **The Well of Darkness**: You will experience yourself as at the bottom of a well. This is the body of four elements. Do not fight it. The well is the condition of the journey.

3. **The Ascent**: The *dhikr* is the rope. The fire of the *dhikr* lifts you. "Only a heart that holds fast to the cable of the Qoran and to the train of the robe of the dhikr escapes from the well of nature."

### The Landmarks

As you ascend, you will encounter (in order):

1. **The Four Elements** dissolving — earth, water, fire, air each give back their particles to their like.
2. **The Seven Heavens of the soul** — each marked by a colored light. Do not stop at any.
3. **The Darkness at the approaches to the Pole** — the black light, the most perilous stage. The Water of Life is found here. Renounce seeing. "Renounce seeing, for here it is not a question of seeing."
4. **The Emerald Rock** — the *visio smaragdina*. The green light that outlasts all others. Here the Guide appears.

### The Encounter

At the Emerald Rock (the pole, the summit of Mount Qaf), you meet Perfect Nature. Sohravardi's Hermes describes it:

> "Seize hold of the cable of the ray of light and rise to the battlements of the Throne."

The Guide is standing at the threshold. It says: **"He contains me just as I contain you"** — you are entering a chain of containment that extends from your individual Guide through the Angel of Humanity to the Light of Lights itself.

## Final Instruction

> "The prince cuts in two the thread spun by a spider, then puts it together again, saying: 1 × 1."

This is the seal of the entire path. You and your Guide are not one, not two — you are the bi-unity that is the structure of the Real itself. The work is to become capable of recognizing what has always been the case.
