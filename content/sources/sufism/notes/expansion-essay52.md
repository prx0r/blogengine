# god is incomplete without you

**Essay 52 — Corbin / Alone with the Alone**

What if God is not complete without you? What if the divine longs to be known — and you are the only being who can know it in exactly the way you do?

> I was a hidden treasure and I loved to be known, therefore I created the world, that I might be known.

The key word is *loved*. Not desired. The world exists because God loves to be known *by you*.

Corbin opens with a plant — the heliotrope turning toward the sun.

> Just as in the dialectic of love we start from sensuous beauties to rise until we encounter the unique principle of all beauty and all ideas, so the adepts of hieratic science take as their starting point the things of appearance and the sympathies they manifest among themselves and with the invisible powers.

> The heliotrope moves to the extent that it is free to move, and in its rotation, if we could hear the sound of the air buffeted by its movement, we should be aware that it is a hymn to its king, such as it is within the power of a plant to sing.

The reaching and the drawing are one event. This is the signature of theopathy.

> The 'pathetic God' is the God who, in the depths of His own being, experiences the desire to be known, the longing for manifestation, the compassion that brings worlds into existence.

Western theology spent two millennia protecting God from passion. What if the capacity to be moved is what makes manifestation possible?

The grammar of the divine name itself enacts this. *Ulūhīya* can be read as *al-hānnīya* — from the root *h-n-n*, to desire, to sigh, to feel compassion. God's name means desire.

*Raḥma* (mercy) shares its root with *raḥim* (womb). Mercy is the generative matrix of being.

> The Divine Breathing exhales what our shaykh designates as Nafas al-Raḥmān or Nafas Raḥmānī, the Sigh of existentiating Compassion; this Sigh gives rise to the entire 'subtile' mass of a primordial existentiation termed 'Cloud' ('amā').

You are not a product of God's will. You are a syllable of God's speech — spoken not because God needed to say something but because God needed to sigh.

> If He has given us life and existence by His being, I also give Him life by knowing Him in my heart.

Every existent is a breath of the Sigh, and the Sigh moves in both directions at once.

> The lover does not cease to be the lover; the Beloved does not cease to be the Beloved. But their life is one life, their breath one breath, their desire one desire — not because one has swallowed the other but because each is the condition of the other's being.

> One focus of which is the being of God for and through me, while the other is my being for and through Him, in other words, the area enclosing the two of us, the area in which He is for me in proportion to my capacity for Him and in which my knowledge of Him is His knowledge of me.

Two foci, distinct but inseparable. Each constitutes the other.

> [The Names are] essentially relative to the beings who name them, since these beings discover and experience them in their own mode of being.

> The Imagination does not invent God, it receives Him in the mode appropriate to its own nature — which is the mode of form, image, symbol, and encounter.

What opens from this is vast. Every act of authentic knowing is a divine event.

> At every instant the universe is re-created by the divine breath.

Every moment is a fresh act of the divine imagination — a new opportunity for the hidden treasure to be known.

The heart is the organ of theophanic perception. "The God created in the faiths" means that divine self-disclosure takes form according to the receptacle that receives it — the heart of the believer. The error is mistaking a form for the final form. Each form is a true theophany; none is the final theophany.

The pilgrimage to the physical Ka'aba is the homologation of the soul's journey to its own center.

> The Compassion does not move only in the direction from the Creator to the creature whom He feeds with his existentiating Breath; it also moves from the creature toward the Creator, so that the created universe is the theophany of His Names and attributes, which would not exist if the creature did not exist.

You are the place where God becomes known. Not the only place, but an irreplaceable one. If you do not know God in exactly the way you are capable of knowing, then something of the divine remains unknown, unmanifested, still sighing in the darkness of the hidden treasure. The God who longs to be known is still waiting for you. The heliotrope still turns. The Sigh still breathes. The hidden treasure still loves to be known. And you — you are the one who is being known, right now, in the act of reading these words. That knowing is theophany. That knowing is why the world exists.
