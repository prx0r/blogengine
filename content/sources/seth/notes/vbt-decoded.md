## 1. the breath between (yukti 1)

**the practice:** attend to the natural breath. at the end of each exhale, notice the still pause before the next inhale. at the end of each inhale, notice the still pause before the next exhale. do not turn back from these pauses. rest in the gap. let the breath become still of itself.

**every time you remember:** can you do it once? just once. at the end of the next exhale, stay there.

**where it leads:** the prana dissolves into its source. the still, aware spaciousness that you always are shines through. when breath stops, what remains is what never breathed.

**pointer:** do not turn away from the pause — that pause is the door.

## 2. the central channel (yukti 2)

**the practice:** visualize the subtlest prana as rays of light shining upward from the root, rising through the central channel. feel it getting subtler as it rises — navel, heart, throat, palate, between brows, crown, above crown. let it dissolve in the space above the head. rest there.

**every time you remember:** can you trace one breath from bottom to top? just one. feel it rise. let it dissolve.

**where it leads:** when the energy dissolves at the crown, spacious awareness arises of itself. you do not enter it. it was always what you are.

**pointer:** the light dissolves; what remains is the light that was always shining.

## 3. khecari mudra (yukti 5)

**the practice:** fill the central channel completely with the energy of the breath. concentrate between the eyebrows, using that focus like a dam. when the energy is built up, breach the crown through that concentration. the mind is freed from its thought-entrancement.

**every time you remember:** can you build the energy once? concentrate. hold. release upward.

**where it leads:** the mind, freed from conceptualization, rises to the all-pervasive state. the flood of awareness is the goal, not the dam.

**pointer:** use concentration as a dike, then breach it.
