## I. The PGM (Greek Magical Papyri)

### What It Is

A collection of Greco-Egyptian magical texts from 2nd century BCE to 5th century CE. Spells, invocations, rituals, and recipes for every purpose — from love to healing to curse to vision.

### The Tetrahedron Structure

Every PGM spell HAS the same 4-part structure:

| Part | Name | Vertex | What Happens |
|------|------|--------|--------------|
| **1** | The **Preliminaries** | V₁ — Subject | Purification, fasting, preparation of the practitioner |
| **2** | The **Invocation** | V₂ — Object | Calling the god/daimon with barbarous names |
| **3** | The **Rite** | V₃ — Intention | The specific action — burning incense, drawing the figure, reciting the names |
| **4** | The **Result** | V₄ — Act | The appearance of the god, the dream, the healing |

PGM IV.505-528 (the "Systasis" for meeting your own daimon) IS the explicit construction of this tetrahedron:

| Phase | Lines | Tetrahedral Meaning |
|-------|-------|-------------------|
| Skinning the 6 stones | IV.506-510 | Cleansing the 6 edges — removing the "stones" (blockages) from each relation |
| Purifying in the egg | IV.510-520 | Establishing V₁ (the practitioner) and V₃ (the intention) — the egg IS the tetrahedron's volume |
| Charging with fire | IV.520-525 | Activating V₂ (the god/daimon) through the Helios-fire invocation |
| Inflaming and swallowing | IV.525-528 | Completing the tetrahedron — the 4th vertex IS established, the daimon appears |

### The Barbarous Names

The barbarous names (onomata barbara) ARE the 36 tattvas in sound form. They are NOT nonsense — they ARE phoneme sequences that resonate specific nāḍī circuits. The PGM spells often list 7 vowels (α, ε, η, ι, ο, υ, ω) + consonants — the 7 vowels ARE the 7 cakras, the consonants ARE the tattvas.

### PGM vs. Tantraloka

| PGM | Tantraloka | Same Function |
|-----|------------|---------------|
| Barbarous names | Bīja-mantra | Phonemes as the energies of consciousness |
| The 7 vowels | The 7 cakras | The vowels ARE the 7 levels of the ascent |
| The egg | The cosmic egg (brahmāṇḍa) | The universe as a single tetrahedron |
| The daimon | The iṣṭadevatā | The god that IS one's own consciousness |

## II. The Picatrix (Ghayat al-Hakim)

### What It Is

A 11th-century Arabic grimoire of astrological magic, translated into Latin as the *Picatrix*. The most complete manual of talismanic magic in the Western tradition.

### The Tetrahedron Structure

The Picatrix IS organized around 4 books:

| Book | Content | Vertex |
|------|---------|--------|
| **I** | The nature of the cosmos, the 4 elements, the 7 planets | V₁ — the SUBJECT — the cosmos as a living being |
| **II** | The arrangement of the spheres, the 12 signs, the 28 moon mansions | V₂ — the OBJECT — the celestial structure |
| **III** | The properties of stones, plants, animals, times | V₃ — the INTENTION — the materials and timing for magic |
| **IV** | The talismans themselves, the operations | V₄ — the ACT — the actual construction and consecration |

### Talismans in the Picatrix

Every talisman in the Picatrix HAS 4 components:
1. **The material** — the specific stone, metal, or plant (the physical base)
2. **The figure** — the symbol or image inscribed (the form)
3. **The timing** — the astrological hour and planetary position (the intention)
4. **The consecration** — the words spoken and the ritual performed (the act)

4 components = 4 vertices. The talisman IS a tetrahedron. The Picatrix IS a catalog of tetrahedra for every purpose.

### The 4 Elements in the Picatrix

The Picatrix is built on the 4 elements:
- **Fire** (Book I) — the active, transformative principle
- **Air** (Book II) — the communicative, ordering principle
- **Water** (Book III) — the receptive, nurturing principle
- **Earth** (Book IV) — the manifest, grounding principle

4 elements = 4 vertices of the base tetrahedron. The Picatrix maps every being, every stone, every plant, every color, every scent to one of these 4.
