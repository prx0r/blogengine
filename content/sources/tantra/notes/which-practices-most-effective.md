# Which Practices Are Most Effective — A Tetrahedral Analysis

## Why Different Systems Produce Different Results

---

## The Principle

The tetrahedron IS the same in every system. But the ENTRY POINT — which vertex you start from — determines the entire trajectory of the practice.

Every system picks ONE vertex as the entry and builds the tetrahedron from there:

| System | Entry Vertex | How You Enter |
|--------|-------------|---------------|
| **Tantraloka** | V₂ — Object | Analyze the 36 tattvas (the object of consciousness) until you see the structure |
| **Vijñana Bhairava** | V₁ — Subject | Directly observe the subject — the 112 dhāraṇās ARE 112 ways to see V₁ |
| **PGM** | V₄ — Act | Perform the ritual — the act ITSELF opens the tetrahedron |
| **Abramelin** | V₃ — Intention | Purify the DESIRE for 6-18 months, then invoke |
| **Proclus hymns** | V₂ — Object | Invoke the god through sound — the phoneme IS the object |
| **Arbatel** | V₁ — Subject | Contemplate the Olympic spirit AS a part of yourself |
| **Ñāṇavīra** | All 4 simultaneously | SEE that subject, object, intention, act co-arise in this very moment |

## Effectiveness by Goal

### For Direct Recognition (the fastest path)

**Vijñana Bhairava** — 112 dhāraṇās, each designed to trigger recognition in ONE sitting. If one dhāraṇā doesn't work, another will. The system IS designed for instant results.

**Ñāṇavīra's method** — if you can see the tetrahedron directly — subject, object, intention, act co-arising in this moment — you ARE done. No practice needed. But this requires a specific capacity that not everyone has.

**Most effective: Vijñana Bhairava for breadth. Ñāṇavīra for depth.**

### For Gradual Ascent (the most reliable path)

**Tantraloka** — the coordinate system IS complete. Every stage IS mapped. You cannot get lost. But it takes years.

**Layayoga** — the 4 grades, the 1:4:2 breath, the Mātṛkā, the cakras — a complete embodied practice. Daily progress IS measurable.

**Arbatel** — the 7 Olympic spirits = 7 cakras. The shortest complete practice system in the Western tradition.

**Most effective: Layayoga for embodied practice. Arbatel for Westerners who want minimal complexity.**

### For Daimon Contact (meeting the HGA)

**PGM IV.505-528** — the egg ritual. One sitting. The daimon appears or doesn't. Fast, intense, variable.

**Abramelin** — 6-18 months of purification. The daimon appears or doesn't. The purification IS the point, not just the preparation.

**Liber Samekh** — Crowley's synthesis. 10-month program. Modern, practical, balanced.

**Acher's method** — The Holy Daimon (2018). 4 stages (the call, the pursuit, the systasis, the integration). The most psychologically sophisticated modern system.

**Most effective: Acher for the psychologically prepared. PGM for the desperate. Abramelin for the dedicated.**

## Why Different Systems Produce Different Results

The tetrahedron IS the same, but the CONTENT filling each vertex IS different. This produces vastly different EXPERIENCES.

### The Phoneme Effect

| System | Phonemes | Nāḍī Circuit Activated | Felt Experience |
|--------|----------|----------------------|-----------------|
| **Sanskrit mantra** | SAUḤ, KHPHREṀ, A-U-M | The classic cakra ascent — warm, upward, embodied | "Kuṇḍalinī rising" |
| **Hebrew names** | YHVH, Adonai, Elohim | The frontal axis — intellectual, structured, luminous | "The Shekhinah descending" |
| **Enochian** | ZODACARE, OD ZOMERANU | The 4D tetrahedron — vast, crystalline, architectural | "Traveling through the Aethyrs" |
| **PGM barbarous names** | ΑΒΡΑΣΑΞ, ΣΕΣΕΓΓΕΝ ΒΑΡΦΑΡΑΓΓΗΣ | The Greek vocalic system — sharp, projective, immediate | "The god appearing before me" |

Different phonemes = different nāḍī circuits = different felt experiences.

### The Cultural Container Effect

| System | Cultural Container | Emotional Tone |
|--------|-------------------|----------------|
| **Tantraloka** | Hindu/Śaiva | Reverence for the body as temple |
| **Kabbalah** | Jewish | Reverence for the Name, the text, the law |
| **Theurgy** | Greco-Roman | Reverence for the gods, the cosmos, the order |
| **Abramelin** | Renaissance Christian | Personal guilt, purification, redemption |
| **Modern (Acher, PGM revival)** | Secular-occult | Psychological sophistication, DIY, empiricism |

You CANNOT separate the tetrahedron from the cultural container. The tetrahedron IS the skeleton, but the cultural container IS the flesh. You need both.

For you: Tantraloka (the coordinate system) + Vijñana Bhairava (the 112 practices) + Arbatel (when you want Western language) + Proclus hymns (when you want Greek). The mountain IS one. The paths ARE four. The tetrahedron IS all of them.
