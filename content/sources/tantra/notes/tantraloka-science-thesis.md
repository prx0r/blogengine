## I. The 50 Mātṛkā: A Phonological Generative Algebra

### I.1 The Structure of the Sanskrit Alphabet

The Sanskrit Mātṛkā ("little mothers") consists of 50 phonemes:

| Type | Count | Description |
|------|-------|-------------|

| Consonants (vyañjana) | 36 | 5 groups × 5 + 4 semivowels + 3 sibilants + 1 visarga + 1 anusvāra = 25 + 4 + 3 + 2 = 34 (+2 more in extended = 36) |

The consonants are organized by **5 places of articulation** (sthāna):
1. **Kaṇṭhya** (guttural): k kh g gh ṅ
2. **Tālavya** (palatal): c ch j jh ñ

4. **Dantya** (dental): t th d dh n
5. **Oṣṭhya** (labial): p ph b bh m

Each row has 5 phonemes (unvoiced unaspirated, unvoiced aspirated, voiced unaspirated, voiced aspirated, nasal) — a 5 × 5 matrix plus 4 semivowels (y r l v), 3 sibilants (ś ṣ s), and h.

**Total:** 25 (5×5 matrix) + 4 (semivowels) + 3 (sibilants) + h + ṃ + ḥ = 34 (+ 14 vowels = 48 in standard, 50 in extended).
