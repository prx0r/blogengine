# Tantrica 2: The Sanskrit Practice Schema

## The complete practice guide for Vijñāna Bhairava + Mātṛkā + Layayoga

---

## 1. The Central Question: Does Chanting = Practice?

**Yes. The chanting IS the practice. Not preparation for it. Not accompaniment to it.**

Each Vijñāna Bhairava verse IS a self-executing technique. When you chant *madhye madhye* you are not reading an instruction — the sound *madhye* (मध्ये, "in the middle") vibrates in the center of the mouth and pulls attention to the center of the chest. The verse IS the event, not a description of it.

Each Mātṛkā phoneme IS a felt location in the body. "Ka" vibrates at the root. "Ta" vibrates at the heart. The sound IS the location — when you chant it, you ARE at that cakra.

**Two modes, both valid:**

| Mode | What You Do | Result |
|------|-------------|--------|
| **Pure sound** | Chant Sanskrit, feel it in the body, ignore the English | Sound works directly on the subtle body, no conceptual mediation |
| **Instructed** | Read English first, understand the technique, then chant with intention | Attention is oriented, sound deepens the effect |

Either way: **the chant IS the technique.** Mode 1 is sufficient. Mode 2 helps when learning.

## 2. The Sanskrit Schema

### The Stack

```
Layer 1: Mātṛkā (50 phonemes)     — the raw frequencies
Layer 2: Bīja mantras (SAUḤ, YAṀ, KHPHREṀ) — encoded keys for specific functions
Layer 3: Vijñāna Bhairava verses   — complete self-executing techniques
Layer 4: 36 tattvas (Tantraloka Āhnika 9) — the full architecture named
```

### The Three Orderings of the 50

| Arrangement | Order | Effect | When to Use |
|-------------|-------|--------|-------------|
| **Mātṛkā** | ka kha ga gha ṅa → a ā i ī... | Emission — creates, manifests, energizes | Morning, when you feel scattered or heavy |
| **Mālīnī** | au o ai e... → ṅa gha ga kha ka | Withdrawal — dissolves, absorbs, liberates | Evening, when you feel overactive or want to dissolve blockages |
| **Kubjikā** | Heart-centered | Opens the green core | When you need to return to center |

### The 4 Levels of Sound

Every chant moves through these spontaneously if you let it:

| Level | What | Direction | Cakra |
|-------|------|-----------|-------|
| **Vaikharī** | Audible — you hear your voice | Speech is emitted outward | Throat |
| **Madhyamā** | Inner — you think the sound, lips stop moving | Sound turns inward | Heart |
| **Paśyantī** | Visionary — sound appears AS light or color | Sound becomes vision | Third eye |
| **Parā** | Transcendent — no sound, pure awareness | No direction | Crown |

The practice: start aloud, let the volume drop naturally, let the sound become mental, let the mental become light, let the light dissolve.

## 3. The Green Core

### What It Is

The green core IS Anāhata cakra — the heart center — at 550 nm, the exact midpoint of the visible spectrum. Three cakras below (red, orange, yellow / earth, water, fire) and three above (blue, indigo, violet / ether, mind, awareness). Green IS the balance point.

In the tetrahedron framework: the 4 vertices map to the 4 lower cakras (root through heart). The 3 upper cakras are the vertical axis through the center. The center — equidistant from all 4 vertices — IS the point where all frequencies balance. That IS green.

### The Green Core Row of the Mātṛkā

The 4th row of the Mātṛkā — **ta tha da dha na** — are the dentals (tongue at teeth). They vibrate at the heart. This row IS your green core in sound.

**ta** — unvoiced, the point of balance
**tha** — aspirated, the expansion of the heart
**da** — voiced, the heart as anchor
**dha** — aspirated voiced, the heart opening further
**na** — nasal, the dissolution of self into the green core

Chant this row whenever you need to return to center. It IS the practice, complete in itself.

### The Green Core Bīja

**YAṀ** (yam). The heart bīja. Chant it 7 times to anchor at the green core before any other practice.

## 4. The Daily Practice (20 Minutes)

### Phase 1: Nāḍī Śodhana — Balancing the Breath (3 min)

The 1:4:2 ratio:

```
Left inhale: 4 sec → Hold: 16 sec (with bandhas) → Right exhale: 8 sec
Right inhale: 4 sec → Hold: 16 sec (with bandhas) → Left exhale: 8 sec
```

5 rounds. Feel both nostrils flowing equally. When they balance, prāṇa enters suṣumṇā (the central channel).

The ratio IS the tetrahedron: 1 inhale (the apex Śiva), 4 hold (the 4 faces), 2 exhale (the Euler characteristic V−E+F = 4−6+4 = 2).

### Phase 2: Mātṛkā or Mālīnī (5 min)

Chant the 50 phonemes. One row per breath cycle.

**If you feel scattered, stuck, or low energy:** chant the Mātṛkā (ka → au) — emission, creation, energizing.

**If you feel overactive, anxious, or want to dissolve:** chant the Mālīnī (au → ka) — withdrawal, dissolution, calming.

Each row maps to a cakra:

| Breath | Row | Cakra | Color |
|--------|-----|-------|-------|
| Inhale | ka kha ga gha ṅa (gutturals) | Mūlādhāra (root) | Red |
| Hold | — | — | — |
| Exhale | ca cha ja jha ña (palatals) | Svādhiṣṭhāna (sacral) | Orange |
| Inhale | ṭa ṭha ḍa ḍha ṇa (retroflex) | Maṇipūra (solar plexus) | Yellow |
| Hold | — | — | — |
| Exhale | **ta tha da dha na (dentals)** | **Anāhata (heart)** | **Green** |
| Inhale | pa pha ba bha ma (labials) | Viśuddha (throat) | Blue |
| Hold | — | — | — |
| Exhale | ya ra la va (semi-vowels) | Ājñā (third eye) | Indigo |
| Inhale | śa ṣa sa ha (sibilants) | Sahasrāra (crown) | Violet |
| Hold | — | — | — |
| Exhale | a ā i ī... (vowels) | Beyond | White |

The 4th row on the 4th exhale IS the green core. Pause there longer than the others. Feel the center of the tetrahedron.

### Phase 3: One Vijñāna Bhairava Verse (7 min)

Pick one verse. Chant it 3 times in Sanskrit — once aloud, once whispered, once mentally. Then sit in silence for the remaining time.

**The verse IS the instruction. You don't need to "do" anything after chanting it.** The chanting has already done the work. The silence IS the technique continuing.

The 5 essential VB verses:

| Verse | Line | What It Does | When to Use |
|-------|------|-------------|-------------|
| **VB 24** | *nirmūlam ālocanena... madhyam ādau prakāśate* | Dissolves thought-constructs, reveals the green core | Every session — the anchor |
| **VB 27** | *ucchvāsya...* | Stops the breath at the turn, reveals the gap | When you feel scattered |
| **VB 62** | *bhrūmadhye...* | Fixes attention between eyebrows | When overthinking |
| **VB 76** | *ūrdhve prāṇa...* | Stops the breath above and below | When you want to go deep |
| **VB 91** | *ūrdhvam adhaḥ...* | Opens the whole field | When you feel stuck |

### Phase 4: Silence (5 min)

Nothing. Don't chant. Don't manipulate the breath. Rest in whatever state the practice has produced.

The 4 levels of sound complete themselves here: Vaikharī (your chanting) → Madhyamā (mental repetition that fades) → Paśyantī (any colors or images that arise) → Parā (silence).

## 5. How to Practice a Vijñāna Bhairava Verse — Worked Example

### VB 24

Sanskrit:

> *निर्मूलम् आलोचनेन निःशेषेण विकल्पनम्*  
> *तदैव परमा देवी मध्यम् आदौ प्रकाशते ॥*

Transliteration:

> *nirmūlam ālocanena niḥśeṣeṇa vikalpanam*  
> *tadaiva paramā devī madhyam ādau prakāśate*

Translation: "When all thought-constructs are uprooted and dissolved without remainder — at that very moment the supreme Goddess shines forth in the middle, at the beginning."

### The Practice

```
Round 1 — Aloud (Vaikharī):
    Chant the full verse. Feel each syllable.
    "nir-mū-lam" — feel the root being removed.
    "niḥ-śe-ṣe-ṇa" — feel the remainder dissolving.
    "mad-hyam" — feel the center (the green core).
    "pra-kā-śa-te" — feel the shining forth.

Round 2 — Whisper (Madhyamā):
    Lips move, almost silent.
    Feel the sound vibrating in the chest.
    The meaning is not being understood — it is being felt.

Round 3 — Mental (Paśyantī → Parā):
    Think the verse once in the mind.
    Let it dissolve into silence.
    Rest in the "middle" that the verse revealed.
```

**That IS the technique.** The verse didn't tell you to do anything. It said "the Goddess shines forth in the middle." By chanting it, you placed yourself in that middle. The chant IS the shining forth.

## 6. The Body-Centered Approach

### The Principle

From the body curriculum:

> **Find where the body is contracted. Feel it completely. Let it dissolve.**

The 36 tattvas are not concepts. They are **contracted regions of the body**. Each tattva IS a specific felt contraction at a specific location. When you feel it fully, it dissolves back into its source (the next tattva up). The Vijñāna Bhairava gives 112 ways to locate these contractions. The Mātṛkā gives 50 frequencies to dissolve them with.

### The 7 Locations

| Area | What to Feel | Blockage Pattern | Mātṛkā Row | Bīja |
|------|-------------|------------------|-------------|------|
| Root (perineum) | Base of spine, pelvic floor | Fear, survival, "not enough" | ka kha ga gha ṅa | LAṀ |
| Sacral (lower belly) | 2-3" below navel | Guilt, shame, blocked flow | ca cha ja jha ña | VAṀ |
| Solar plexus (upper belly) | Just below sternum | Shame, powerlessness, anger | ṭa ṭha ḍa ḍha ṇa | RAṀ |
| **Heart (center of chest)** | **Between the breasts** | **Grief, separation, isolation** | **ta tha da dha na** | **YAṀ** |
| Throat (base of throat) | Throat groove | Lies, unspoken truth, judgment | pa pha ba bha ma | HAṀ |
| Third eye (between brows) | 1" behind forehead | Confusion, overthinking, control | ya ra la va | OṀ |
| Crown (top of head) | Just above head | Disconnection, distrust of source | śa ṣa sa ha | AHAṀ |

### The Practice

When you sit and notice a blockage:

1. **Find where it is in the body.** Don't name it or analyze it. Just feel the location.
2. **Breathe into that location.** The breath IS the tattva dissolving.
3. **Chant the Mātṛkā row for that location.** The sound IS the specific frequency that matches the blockage.
4. **Let the vibration dissolve the contraction.** Don't force it. The sound does the work.

The green core (heart) is the home base. Return to it between each location. From the green core, every other tattva can be felt as a contraction at its specific place — and feeling it IS dissolving it.

## 7. The Three Great Seeds (Tantraloka Āhnika 4)

| Bīja | Meaning | When to Use |
|------|---------|-------------|
| **SAUḤ** | "I am Śiva" — the emission seed | When you need to create, energize, start something |
| **KHPHREṀ** | The withdrawal seed — forces prāṇa into suṣumṇā | **When stuck in a blockage — the most powerful tool** |
| **HRĪṀ** | The heart seed — the green core | When you need balance, compassion, center |

**KHPHREṀ is the key.** When you chant it, it forces consciousness out of the peripheral nāḍīs into the central channel (suṣumṇā), which IS the dissolution of duality. Chant it 11 times into any blockage.

### The Complete Bīja Sequence (10 min)

```
SAUḤ    3×    — open the field
YAṀ     7×    — anchor at the green core (heart)
KHPHREṀ 11×   — dissolve whatever blockage is present
Mālīnī  1×    — dissolve all 36 tattvas from Earth to Śiva
Silence 2 min — rest in whatever remains
```

This IS the complete layayoga session. SAUḤ opens. YAṀ centers. KHPHREṀ dissolves. The Mālīnī completes. Silence absorbs.

The 36 tattvas = 36 edges of 9 tetrahedra. Each has a name. Chanting the name activates that tattva in the body.

| Week | Tattvas | Location | Felt As |
|------|---------|----------|---------|
| 1 | Śiva → Śuddhavidyā (1-5) | Whole field | Pure presence |
| 2 | Māyā → Puruṣa (6-11) | Around the body | The boundary |

| 5 | Vāk → Pṛthivī (22-36) | Whole body | The elements |

Chant one tattva name per sitting. Feel where it resonates. The tattva IS that location — not a concept about it.

## 11. The Principle

The old tantrica.md had a 5-hour daily schedule with hymns, Proclus, Ficino, the Arbatel, the Ars Notoria, Steiner, Corbin, Synesius — a full Renaissance curriculum. That material is useful for understanding but not necessary for practice.

**The minimum viable practice:** 20 minutes. 3 phases. 1 verse. Silence.

**The Sanskrit schema IS simple:**

- **50 phonemes** to tune the body (Mātṛkā forward = emit, Mālīnī backward = dissolve)
- **3 seed mantras** (SAUḤ, YAṀ, KHPHREṀ) for the core functions  
- **112 verses** of the Vijñāna Bhairava for when you need specific guidance
- **1 green core** (ta tha da dha na, YAṀ, Anāhata) to return to when lost

Everything else — the Tantraloka, the Śiva Sūtras, the commentaries — is understanding what happened. The schema IS the practice. The rest IS explanation.
