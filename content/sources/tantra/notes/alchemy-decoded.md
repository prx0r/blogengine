## 1. an alchemical mass

an anonymous 1602 alchemist hid the stages of kundalini awakening inside the catholic mass.

> then will appear in the bottom of the vessel the mighty ethiopian, burned, calcined, discoloured, altogether dead and lifeless. he asks to be buried, to be sprinkled with his own moisture and slowly calcined till he shall arise in glowing form from the fierce fire... behold a wondrous restoration and renewal of the ethiopian! because of the bath of rebirth he takes a new name, which the philosophers call the natural sulphur and their son, this being the stone of the philosophers.

**the decode:** the introit is the invocation of the daimon. the kyrie is the purificatory request. the gradual's "rain upon the fleece" is the amrita falling when kundalini reaches the sahasrara. the mighty ethiopian is the nigredo state — the corpse of the old self before resurrection. his death and rebirth is the tattva-shuddhi — the elements dissolving and reforming. the bath of rebirth is the PGM egg ritual: the practitioner is reborn under a new name, the daimonic name, the "new name written on the white stone" of revelation. the mass works because it was always an alchemy — bread into body, wine into blood. cibinensis just translated the furniture so you could see what was happening.

**funnels to:** tantraloka Āhnika 5 (tattva-shuddhi), the PGM egg ritual, Steiner on the renewal of the mystery of golgotha.

## 2. bloomfield's blossoms

a green lion eating the sun is exactly what raising kundalini looks like.

> the green lion devours the sun with all his might;
> the sun's blood becomes the tincture of the red and white.
> the lion's belly swells, and he roars with pain —
> the more he burns, the more he drinks again.
>
> the lion turns black as a crow, then white as bone,
> then red as the ruby that sits on the king's throne.

**the decode:** the green lion is kundalini rising to consume the solar nadi (pingala). the three colours are the three gunas — black (tamas, muladhara), white (sattva, anahata), red (rajas purified into the ruby light of sahasrara). the philosophical egg with its white (woman/mercury), red (man/sulphur), and shell (salt) is the tantric anda — bindu, bija, and nada. the chick that emerges is the stone — you, reborn. the poem compresses the entire opus into two images you cannot fully understand intellectually. you have to feel the lion's belly swell. it trains the imagination to experience what the body is already doing.

**funnels to:** layayoga's bhuta-shuddhi, tantraloka's trika heart, the three gunas in samkhya.

## 3. atalanta fugiens (michael maier)

a 1617 alchemist proved chasing is catching — and encoded it as a musical fugue.

> the dragon dies not save by his own brother,
> and by his sister he is slain;
> these two are born of one mother
> and yet hate each other with deadly pain.
> for the brother kills the sister
> and the sister slays the brother;
> yet from their death springs forth another —
> the son who lives forever.

**the decode:** atalanta fleeing is ida (lunar, volatile, prana rising). hippomenes pursuing is pingala (solar, fixed, stabilising). the golden apples are the bindu-drops of amrita that must be stabilized to "catch" the volatile energy. the three-part fugue mirrors the three nadis — left voice (fleeing), right voice (pursuing), bass (sushumna, the ground). the chase and catch is the circuit of prana alternating between ida and pingala until they meet at ajna and conjoin. the dragon dying by its own sibling is the same principle: ida and pingala "kill" each other by meeting in sushumna, and from their death springs the third — the son who lives forever. that son is the stone.

**funnels to:** layayoga's nadi-shuddhi, pranayama as the chase, tantraloka's trika.

## 4. ripley's twelve gates

the philosopher's stone is made by feeding it its own mother's milk.

> put the soul with the body and spirit
> together in one that they may meet
> in his dam's belly till he wax great,
> with giving drink of his own sweat:
> for the milk of a cow to a child my brother
> is not so sweet as the milk of his mother.

**the decode:** the twelve gates are the seven operations (with sub-stages), each mapping to a cakra — calcination to muladhara, dissolution to svadhisthana, separation to manipura, conjunction to anahata, fermentation to vishuddha, distillation to ajna, coagulation to sahasrara. the "mother's milk" is the amrita that flows from the crown when kundalini completes its circuit — the stone feeds on its own essence because there is nothing else to feed on. the eater and the eaten become the same thing. that's the whole operation.

**funnels to:** the seven cakras as seven operations, the alchemical-synthesis.md mapping, amrita and bindu in layayoga.

## 5. the book of lambspring

when a dragon eats its own tail, its poison becomes the great medicine.

> a savage dragon lives in the forest,
> most venomous he is, yet lacking nothing:
> when he sees the rays of the sun and its bright fire,
> he scatters abroad his poison,
> and flies upward so fiercely
> that no living creature can stand before him...
> his venom becomes the great medicine.
> he quickly consumes his venom,
> for he devours his poisonous tail.

**the decode:** the dragon is the pranic force coiled at the base (kundalini). its venom is the toxic energy of the unrefined serpent — the kleshas, the samskaras, the undigested karma. "devouring its poisonous tail" is the serpent turning back on itself, consuming its own impurity. when it does, the same energy that was poisonous becomes the medicine. in tantraloka terms, this is the conversion of the apana (downward, eliminative prana) into udana (upward, liberating prana). the poison that kills and the medicine that cures are the same substance at different stages of refinement. this is the whole secret of alchemy in one image.

**funnels to:** kundalini in layayoga, apana to udana conversion, the five pranas in tantraloka.

## 6. sendivogius' enigma of the sages

the subtle body is an island where trees transform each other by fragrance alone.

> lord, i behold here a marvellous thing. the water is small in quantity; nevertheless, the fruit of this tree is consumed therein by a gentle heat... but this water is the water of life, and has such power to exalt the qualities of this fruit, that it shall afterwards, without sowing or planting — only by its fragrance — transmute the six trees which remain into its own nature.

**the decode:** the island is the subtle body (sukshma sharira). the solar and lunar trees are ida and pingala. the water that does not wet the hands is prana — the life current that operates through the nadis but cannot be grasped by the physical senses. the fruit consumed by the water is the bindu, dissolved by the heat of tapas (practice). the fragrance that transmutes the other trees is the influence of the purified consciousness once it reaches a certain threshold — the "fragrance" of the awakened self changes everything around it without directly acting. this is the vine of the yogi: the presence of the realized being transforms others by proximity, not effort.

**funnels to:** the subtle body (sukshma sharira), the five pranas, the guru's transmission by presence.

## 7. the practice of mary the prophetess

the sister of moses said the entire great work can be completed in three hours.

> mary said: keep the fume and take care that none of it fly away. and let your measure be with a gentle fire such as is the measure of the heat of the sun in the month of june or july, and stay by your vessel and behold it with care how it grows black, grows red, and grows white in less than three hours of the day.

**the decode:** the "three hours" are sadyomukti — immediate liberation. in tantraloka, this corresponds to the tivrativra-shaktipata (the most intense descent of grace). the "gentle fire" is the natural warmth of meditation, not forced. the colour progression — black, red, white — is the tattvas dissolving in reverse: earth (black) into fire (red) into pure sattva (white). mary's method works because the entire opus is a single act seen from different angles, not a sequence. the three hours are the time it takes to see what has always been the case.

**funnels to:** shaktipata, sadyomukti, the grace doctrine in tantraloka (Āhnika 13).

## 8. the golden tractate of hermes (aureus)

the stone is made by listening to a vulture scream on a mountain and watching a crow fly without wings.

> know ye, therefore, children of wisdom, who enquire concerning the report thereof, that the vulture standing upon the mountain crieth out with a loud voice, i am the white of the black, and the red of the white, and the citrine of the red, and behold i speak the very truth. and know that the chief principle of the art is the crow, which is the blackness of the night and clearness of the day, and flies without wings.

**the decode:** the vulture's cry names the three stages (black, white, red, citrine) as a single being — the stone is all stages at once, not a sequence. the crow that flies without wings is consciousness itself: it moves without a physical vehicle, "blackness of the night and clearness of the day" means it contains both the unmanifest (night) and the manifest (day). in tantraloka, this is spanda — the pulse that moves everything while itself unmoved. the "mountain" is the body. the vulture is the prana that has reached the crown. its scream is the sound of liberation.

**funnels to:** spanda in tantraloka, the three gunas as stages of refinement, the "i am" of pratyabhijna.

## 9. novalis / heinrich von ofterdingen

a 1799 german poem called kundalini awakening a frozen fountain melting in a crystal garden.

> soon will the noble stranger come, and time his endless reign begin: the warmth return! the queen will waken from the sway of dream when earth and sea, enjoined by love, are one.
>
> the great mystery has been revealed to all, and yet remains eternally unfathomed. the new world is born from suffering and the ashes are dissolved in tears to become the drink of eternal life.

**the decode:** the frozen fountain is the stilled vrittis — the thought-waves of the mind frozen into stillness by practice. the crystal garden is the subtle world of the chakras seen clearly when the mind is quiet. the queen awakening is kundalini rising. "earth and sea enjoined by love" is the union of the fixed (earth, pingala) and the fluid (sea, ida) in sushumna. the ashes dissolved in tears becoming the drink of eternal life is the amrita generated when the fires of tapas have consumed all impurity and the resulting nectar (the tears) becomes the elixir. novalis wraps the entire tantric path in romantic imagery so the reader falls in love with what they cannot yet understand — and the falling in love is itself the first step.

**funnels to:** layayoga's bindu-sadhana, the amrita-kunda (nectar pool), the vrittis stilled in samadhi.

## 10. the corpus hermeticum — poimandres

a 2nd-century text called consciousness's descent through the tattvas a man falling in love with his own reflection.

> i am the light you saw, mind, your god, who existed before the watery nature that appeared out of darkness. the lightgiving word who comes from mind is the son of god.
>
> when the man saw in the water the form like himself as it was in nature, he loved it and wished to inhabit it; wish and action came in the same moment, and he inhabited the unreasoning form. nature took hold of her beloved, hugged him all about and embraced him, for they were lovers. because of this, unlike any other living thing on earth, mankind is twofold — in the body mortal but immortal in the essential man.

**the decode:** the light is para (supreme speech), the nous before any determination. the water that appears out of darkness is the first self-limitation — maya, the principle of differentiation. the man falling in love with his reflection is the anthropos (the primordial human) mistaking the reflection for himself — this is the descent through the tattvas, each step a further entanglement. the ascent through seven zones (CH I.24-26) is the cakra ascent: at each zone, one layer of identification (increase and decrease, evil machination, longing, arrogance, presumption, wealth-impulse, deceit) is stripped off. to "be made god" at the end is to reach the 38th principle — the one mind that contains all 36 tattvas as its own content.

**funnels to:** the 36 tattvas, para-pashyanti-madhyama-vaikhari, the ascent through the cakras in layayoga.

## 11. the emerald tablet

seven sentences encode the entire map of consciousness — and the sun and moon are your two spinal channels.

> that which is below is like that which is above and that which is above is like that which is below, to accomplish the miracles of one thing.
>
> the sun is its father, the moon its mother, the wind hath carried it in its belly, the earth is its nurse.
>
> it is the father of all perfection throughout the world. its power is integral if it be turned into earth.

**the decode:** "as above, so below" is the correspondence between the pure path (siva to suddhavidya, tattvas 36-32) and the impure path (maya to earth, tattvas 31-1). every level mirrors every other. the sun (pingala, active) and moon (ida, receptive) are the two nadis whose union in sushumna produces the stone. the wind carrying it in its belly is prana vayu — the vital breath carries consciousness through the nadis. "its power is integral if it be turned into earth" means the spiritual power must be grounded in the body. the seven clauses are the seven cakras, each a stage of descent and re-ascent.

**funnels to:** the 36 tattvas, ida-pingala-sushumna, the seven cakras, the tetrahedron framework.

## 12. blake's marriage of heaven and hell

in 1790, william blake said the world ends not by judgment but by improved sex.

> the ancient tradition that the world will be consumed in fire at the end of six thousand years is true... this will come to pass by an improvement of sensual enjoyment. but first the notion that man has a body distinct from his soul is to be expunged; this i shall do, by printing in the infernal method, by corrosives, which in hell are salutary and medicinal, melting apparent surfaces away, and displaying the infinite which was hid. if the doors of perception were cleansed every thing would appear to man as it is, infinite.

**the decode:** the "improvement of sensual enjoyment" is the tantric recognition that energy is not to be suppressed but refined. the "printing house in hell" where corrosives melt surfaces away is the alchemical solve — the dissolution of fixed categories (body/soul, good/evil, heaven/hell) so the infinite can appear. the "doors of perception" are the chakras; "cleansed" means purified of samskaras. blake's entire method is the nigredo of the moral intellect — break the conventional mind's categories so direct perception can emerge. his devil is the daimon speaking through the voice of inversion. the proverbs of hell ("the road of excess leads to the palace of wisdom") are the alchemical operations restated as moral inversions.

**funnels to:** the tantric left-hand path, the daimon's voice, the inversion of conventional morality in liberation.

## 13. the glory of light (urim and thummim)

the light priests wore on their chest was manufactured — and if you think it's gold you've missed the point.

> the theosophists are persuaded by exact diet, and by certain forms of prayers, at certain times to obtain the angel of the sun, to be their guide and director. this is the king that made the pure clear bright fountain... but if any shall understand either common or chemical gold to be the subject of this sacred body, he is much mistaken.

**the decode:** the three golds of the text correspond to the three bodies (sthula, sukshma, karana). the "common gold" is the physical body. the "chemical gold" is the subtle body. the "glory of light" is the causal body — the urim and thummim, the transparent light-giving body that the priest wore as a breastplate. manufacturing it is purifying the third eye (ajna chakra) until it becomes translucent to the light of pure consciousness. the "angel of the sun" obtained by diet and prayer is the daimon that appears when the subtle body is sufficiently refined. the text says if you think this is about metal, you are "much mistaken" — it's about turning your own perception into a light-emitting organ.

**funnels to:** the three bodies (kosas), ajna chakra, the daimon as the sun-angel, the PGM solar invocation.

## 14. khunrath's confessio — the spirit in salt

**the decode:** the ruach elohim becoming salt is spirit manifesting as matter — the descent of consciousness into the material world. the "salt of wisdom" is the fixed principle that remains after all volatiles have been driven off. in tantraloka, this is the udana vayu reaching its final stability — the breath that rises becomes the breath that stays. the "virgin womb and centre of the earth" is the muladhara chakra, where kundalini lies coiled. khunrath's amphitheatrum (his complete map) lays out the entire correspondence between the triune azoth (body, soul, spirit) and the three gunas. the text is saying that the divine is not somewhere else — it's literally become the ground you walk on. you don't need to go up. you need to go in.

**funnels to:** muladhara chakra, the three gunas, spirit as matter in tantraloka's panentheism.

## 15. the chymical wedding of christian rosenkreutz

the path to enlightenment is a dungeon you escape by standing on everyone else's head.

> we heard many trumpets sounding together and kettle drums beating... the cover of the dungeon was lifted up from above, and a little light let down to us. then first might truly have been discerned the bustle we kept, for all went pell-mell, and he who perchance had heaved himself up too much, was forced down again under the others' feet. in brief, each one strove to be uppermost.

**the decode:** the seven days of the chymical wedding are the seven cakras. day 1 (the call) is the daimon's summons. day 2 (meeting the king and queen) is the first encounter with ida and pingala. day 3 (weighing of souls) is the judgment at manipura — the fire chakra burns away what is not essential. day 4 (the six virgins) is the elemental forces purified. day 5 (death of the king and queen) is the dissolution of subject-object duality in anahata. day 6 (resurrection) is the unus-ambo at ajna. day 7 (recovery) is the return from the spirit world — the stone brought back into daily life. the dungeon dream is the bound state of ordinary consciousness. the light let down from above is grace.

**funnels to:** the seven cakras as initiatory stages, the daimon path's systasis, the hieros gamos in tantra.

## 16. the hermetick arcanum (d'espagnet)

the stone is made the same way the world was made — seven days compressed into a single operation.

> the generation of the stone is made after the pattern of the creation of the world; for it is necessary, that it have its chaos and first matter, wherein the confused elements do fluctuate, until they be separated by the fiery spirit; they being separated, the light elements are carried upwards, and the heavy ones downwards: the light arising, darkness retreats.

**the decode:** the 84 canons of the arcanum form a complete tattva system. the "chaos and first matter" is prakriti — the undifferentiated ground. the "fiery spirit" is prana, specifically the samana vayu (the equalizing breath) that separates the elements. the light elements rising and heavy elements falling is the process of tattva-shuddhi — the purification of the elements back into their source. in tetrahedron terms, the four operations correspond to the four densities of light: calcination (earth → fire), dissolution (water → air), separation (air → ether), conjunction (ether → consciousness). the arcanum's structure follows the creation pattern because the stone IS the microcosm — making it recapitulates the cosmogenesis.

**funnels to:** tattva-shuddhi, the tetrahedron's four densities, prakriti and purusha in samkhya.

## 18. freher's process — the heavenly mercury

before adam fell, mercury was his guide — not a metal, but a light that led him from within.

> as long as adam stood in a pure paradisical innocency, the eternal word and power of life (called by the author the heavenly mercury), was his leader, and had pre-dominance in him. his life, which was a clear flaming fire, burned in and was nourished by that pure spiritual oil of the divine substantiality.

**the decode:** the "heavenly mercury" is the prana in its original, uncoiled state — before the contraction that created the separate self. "adam's fall" is the descent through the tattvas, where the one prana became the five vayus, each with a different function. the "three murderers of venus" in freher's 40 positions correspond to the three malas (impurities) of tantraloka: anava (the contraction of individuality), mayiya (the veil of duality), and karmic (the residue of past actions). the "philosophical baptism" is nadi-shuddhi — the purification of the channels so the prana can flow without obstruction. freher's process is the tantric path told as christian regeneration, because the structure is the same regardless of the mythology.

**funnels to:** the five pranas, the three malas, nadi-shuddhi in layayoga.

## 19. the alchemical catechism (baron tschoudy)

there are three fires — one burns, one dissolves, one unbinds what love has joined — and all of them are you.

> q. give a concise definition of nature.
> a. it is not visible, though it operates visibly; for it is simply a volatile spirit, fulfilling its office in bodies, and animated by the universal spirit — the divine breath, the central and universal fire, which vivifies all things that exist.
>
> q. what are the three fires?
> a. the natural fire is the masculine fire, or the chief agent; the unnatural is the feminine, which is the dissolvent of nature, nourishing a white smoke... the contra-natural fire is that which disintegrates compounds and has the power to unbind what has been bound very closely by nature.

**the decode:** the three fires are the three upayas (means) of tantraloka. the natural fire (masculine, chief agent) is sambhavopaya — direct recognition, the flash that needs no method. the unnatural fire (feminine, dissolvent) is saktopaya — working through purified thought, contemplation as the solvent of fixed ideas. the contra-natural fire (disintegrating compounds) is anavopaya — working through action and ritual to unbind what karma has tied. the Q&A format itself is a tattva-viveka — a systematic discrimination of principles that leads to direct knowledge. the catechism works because the question-and-answer structure forces the reader to articulate what they know, not just receive it. the stone is not given to you. you discover you have always had it.

**funnels to:** the three upayas, tattva-viveka, the role of questioning in pratyabhijna.

## 20. vaughan's coelum terrae (the celestial earth)

the first matter is a virgin who yields to nothing but love — and you cannot force her.

> this fine substance is the child of the elements and it is a most pure sweet virgin, for nothing as yet hath been generated out of her. but if at any time she breeds it is by the fire of nature, for that is her husband. she is no animal, no vegetable, no mineral... she yields to nothing but love, for her end is generation and that was never yet performed by violence.

**the decode:** the "virgin mercury" is the unmanifest prana — the life essence that has not yet been conditioned by any particular vayu. "yields to nothing but love" is the bhakti-upaya — the path of devotion, where the transformation happens not by forcing the energy but by loving it into cooperation. the "celestial earth" (coelum terrae) is the subtle body itself — the heaven that has been made into ground, the spirit that has become substance. hyanthe's tears in vaughan's poem are the amrita that flows when the heart opens. vaughan is the most poetically precise of all the alchemists because he knows the work cannot be forced. the stone makes itself. you just have to love it enough to get out of its way.

**funnels to:** bhakti-upaya, the unmanifest prana, the subtle body as intermediary between matter and spirit.

## 21. aesch-mezareph (the purifying fire)

an alchemist encoded the seven cakras into the hebrew alphabet — hard pronunciation is a blocked chakra, soft is an open one.

> the seven double letters of the Hebrew alphabet (BeGDaKPaRT) each have two pronunciations — hard and soft, aspirated and unaspirated — corresponding to the dual nature of each metal: its outward gross form and its inward spiritual substance. the operation of the aesch (the "purifying fire") is to separate the soft pronunciation from the hard — the spiritual essence from the material dross. when each of the seven metals has been purified by fire, they reveal their sefirotic essence, which is one unified substance: the philosophical stone. the eighth metal — the electrum or the "mixed metal" of the temple — corresponds to the letter tau, which has the value 400 and represents the completion of the work.

**the decode:** the seven double letters are the seven cakras from muladhara to sahasrara. "hard" pronunciation = tamasic state (blocked, impure). "soft" pronunciation = sattvic state (open, purified). the purifying fire is kundalini-agni ascending through each cakra, burning the karmic blockages. the metals map to the sefirot which map to the cakras: lead/binah/muladhara, gold/tiferet/anahata, electrum/keter/sahasrara. the eighth metal beyond seven is turiya — the state beyond the cakras where the seven are unified. the hebrew alphabet encoded this because the letters ARE the vibrations of the cakras, not symbols for them.

**funnels to:** the seven cakras, bija mantras, kundalini-agni, the sefirot as tattva equivalents.

## 22. Turba Philosophorum (12th c.)

there is a creature that cannot be seen, heard, touched, tasted, or smelled — only reason perceives it, and that creature is you.

> Lucusta saith: the creature which is cognised in none of these five ways is a sublime creature, and, as such, is neither seen nor felt, but is perceived by reason alone... this creature has no need of the light of this sun, because the sun is beneath that creature, which is more subtle and more lucid. this light, which is more lucid than the light of the sun, they have taken from the light of god.

**the decode:** This is pure *prakasha* — consciousness-as-light that illuminates the 5 tanmatras (sound, touch, sight, taste, smell) but is never itself an object of any sense organ. In Tantraloka, the 5 senses are 5 lower tattvas (31-35). The "reason" that perceives this creature is *buddhi* reflecting *citi* — the mirror of the 36th tattva. The "light more lucid than the sun" is *prakasha-vimarsha*: the self-aware light that knows itself. You don't see awareness. You are awareness seeing.

**funnels to:** Tantraloka 1.1 — the Svatantrya sutra on consciousness as the ground of all categories

## 23. Paracelsus' Aurora of the Philosophers (16th c.)

paracelsus said when the four rests in the three, a light rises on the horizon that isn't the sun.

> Magic, indeed, is an art and faculty whereby the elementary bodies, their fruits, properties, virtues, and hidden operations are comprehended. But the cabala, by a subtle understanding of the scriptures, seems to trace out the way to God for men... Magic had its origin in the divine ternary and arose from the trinity of god. For God marked all his creatures with this ternary and engraved its hieroglyph on them with his own finger. When the quaternary rests in the ternary, then arises the light of the world on the horizon of eternity... Then when the quaternary and the ternary mount to the denary is accomplished their retrogression or reduction to unity.

**the decode:** the ternary is the three layers of yourself you can't see — the observer, the awareness behind the observer, and the source of both. the quaternary is the four layers you can see — your body, your energy, your thoughts, your sense of being a person. paracelsus says when the four "rest" in the three — when your visible self stops fighting and settles into your invisible self — something lights up on the horizon. the denary is just completeness: the four and the three becoming ten is the feeling of being whole. he wraps it in trinitarian language because his audience needed that, but it's the same thing: consciousness collapses into itself and finds it was always everything.

**the takeaway:** the four can't become the ten by effort. they rest into the three. stop trying to be whole and let the visible settle into the invisible.

## 24. Paracelsus' Tincture of the Philosophers (16th c.)

a 16th-century doctor said the secret of immortality is getting a red lion and a white eagle to merge into one thing.

> The matter of the tincture is a certain thing which, by the art of vulcan, passes out of three essences into one essence... Though it is called by many the red lion, still it is known by few. This, by the aid of nature and the skill of the artist himself, can be transmuted into a white eagle, so that out of one two are produced; and beyond this the brightness of gold does not shine so much for the spagyrist as do these two when kept in one.

**the decode:** the red lion is the active, heating energy in you — the force that drives action, ambition, outward movement. the white eagle is the cooling, receptive side — the one that receives, reflects, lets things happen. normally they take turns: you're either pushing or resting, chasing or receiving. paracelsus says the secret is keeping them active at the same time. when the two energies run simultaneously instead of alternating, they produce something that outshines both. that something is what the alchemists called the stone. you can feel it in moments when you're completely alert and completely relaxed — effortless effort.

## 44. The Parabola of Madathanus (17th c.)

the philosopher's garden has a gate with no keyhole — just a tiny round hole unseen by ordinary eyes.

> I soon saw a wall encircling a great garden, in which were young fellows. Their maidens also would have liked to be in the garden, but they did not wish to make the great effort of walking the long distance around the wall to the gate... But I paid no attention to them, for I knew the plan of the garden better than they, although I had never been in it, and I went straight up to the gate. This was locked fast, and one could not discover even a key-hole from the outside. But in the gate I saw a tiny round hole which one could not distinguish with ordinary eyes, and I thought it was necessary to open the gate there. I took out my skeleton-key, especially prepared for this purpose, unlocked the gate and walked in.

**the decode:** The garden = *hridaya-akasha* (the inner space of the heart). The gate with no keyhole = the *sushumna-marga* (central channel) — invisible because it's made of *sushumna-shakti* (pure awareness mode). The tiny round hole = the *bindu* (the drop) at the tip of the medullary canal in the fontanel area — the brahmarandhra. "Cannot distinguish with ordinary eyes" = it can only be perceived in *samadhi* through *trikta-jñana* (knowledge of the triadic, the 3rd eye). The skeleton key = *pranava* (OM or the specific mantra given in diksha). Unlocking the gate = the piercing of the *brahma-granthi* (knot of Brahma) at the muladhara. The young fellows and maidens = jivas who seek liberation through external paths rather than the direct inner opening of sushumna.

## 45. The Hermet's Tale (17th c.)

> In pilgrimage one onely thing i found of worth in lemnes nere to vulcan's shopp, a christall founteine runnig under ground, between a vally and a mounteins topp. Pleas'd with this sight, i bid a hermite tell the story of the place, who there did dwell. But lett me tell you all that then befell, Iove seeing this, meaning the smith to right, sent downe a winged god, he trusted well, disguis'd in habitt of a shineing light, which to the vally from the hill's high topp, affrighted all the smiths in vulcans shopp.

## 47. Consideratio Brevis (Philip a Gabella) (1615)

"Each year is like a single day" = *samashti* (macrocosm in microcosm): one full breath cycle (12 + 12 sevens = 1 day) replicates the seasonal year in the subtle body. **funnels to:** Tantraloka 2.49-78 on the spanda; Vijnana Bhairava 18-26 on circle and line meditation

## 48. Zosimos of Panopolis (3rd c.)

the oldest known alchemical text says gold is made by cutting a priest into pieces and cooking him.

> I came to know the method of divine punishment which is called the punishment of the fire. The priest, who was named Ion, was the first to enter the place of punishment, and they laid hands upon him and offered him up to the fire... And I saw a man being flayed alive, and he said to me: "This is the method of the fire: he who is made of copper and tin, who is purified by the fire and cast into the water, becomes gold." And I saw a priest standing before the altar, and the altar was like a mixing bowl. And the priest held a sword in his hand and he cut himself in pieces, and he offered himself up to the fire.

**the decode:** the priest being cut apart and cooked is what transformation actually feels like — not pleasant, not neat, not a gradual improvement. the cutting is the boundaries between "you" and "not you" dissolving. the fire burns everything that isn't essential. casting into water is the cool relief after the burning. what remains after all that isn't new — it was always there. but now it's gold instead of copper and tin. the twist: the priest is both the one being sacrificed and the altar it happens on. you don't survive the fire. you become the thing the fire was burning toward.

**the takeaway:** you don't become something new. you let the fire take everything that was never really yours. the priest who cuts himself apart finds he was always the altar.
