## The 7 Operations as the 7 Cakras = The Descent/Ascent

The alchemical *opus* reverses the descent through the tattvas:

```
Calcination  (Lead/Saturn)    → Mūlādhāra   → Burn away the fixed (Earth tattva)
Dissolution  (Copper/Mercury) → Svādhiṣṭhāna → Dissolve into the water (Māyā pierced)
Separation   (Iron/Mars)      → Maṇipūra     → Cut pure from impure (Śuddhavidyā dawns)
Conjunction  (Tin/Venus)      → Anāhata      → Marry Sun & Moon (Īśvara — subject-object union)
Fermentation (Quicksilver/Jup.) → Viśuddha   → The inner ferment awakens (Sadāśiva — "I Am")
Distillation (Mercury)        → Ājñā         → The volatile rises (Śakti — pure awareness)
Coagulation  (Stone)          → Sahasrāra    → The Stone that is no stone (Śiva)
```

## The Deep Structural Convergence

Alchemy's **solve et coagula** = Tantraloka's **sṛṣṭi** (emission) and **laya** (absorption).

| Alchemical Operation | Subtle Body Process |
|---------------------|-------------------|
| **Putrefaction** (nigredo) | Bhūta-śuddhi — elements dissolve into their source |
| **Purification** (albedo) | Nāḍī-śuddhi — channels purified; prāṇa enters suṣumṇā |
| **Projection** (rubedo) | Kuṇḍalinī rises; Iṣṭadevatā appears |
| **Fixation** (citrinitas/lapis) | Śāmbhavopāya — the recognition that was always there |

The Emerald Tablet — "That which is above is from that which is below" — IS the pure/impure path correspondence. The 5 pure tattvas (Śiva→Śuddhavidyā) are "above"; the 31 impure tattvas (Māyā→Earth) are "below." The Stone that is no stone IS the recognition that the 36 tattvas are not 36 things but one consciousness measuring itself against itself.

## New Material from Tantraloka Volumes 4, 7, 10

### Alchemical Correspondences of the Six Paths (Ṣaḍadhvan)

The six paths (Āhnika 11) provide a more complete framework for alchemy than the 7-cakra model alone:

| Path | Alchemical Equivalent | Stage |
|------|---------------------|-------|
| **Kalādhvan** (Forces) | The five operations: Calcination, Dissolution, Separation, Conjunction, Fermentation | The active principles of transformation |
| **Tattvādhvan** (Principles) | The three primes (Salt, Sulfur, Mercury) | The material basis |
| **Bhuvanādhvan** (Worlds) | The seven planetary metals | The cosmic correspondences |
| **Varṇādhvan** (Phonemes) | The green lion devouring the sun — the raw matter of the opus | Sound as transformative agent |
| **Mantrādhvan** (Mantras) | The secret names of the Stone | The verbal technology |
| **Padādhvan** (Words) | The Emerald Tablet | The revealed scripture |

### The Ten Voids as Alchemical Distillation

Āhnika 5's ten voids (daśaśūnya) are the stages of progressive sublimation:
- Voids 1-5 (heart → throat → palate → between brows → crown): The five stages of distillation
- Voids 6-10 (beyond crown to 12 fingers above): The five stages of coagulation
- Beyond the tenth void: The Stone

The Trika Liṅga (Āhnika 5) = the three alchemical principles: Aparā = Salt (body), Parāparā = Sulfur (soul), Parā = Mercury (spirit).

### The Six Bliss and Seven Operations

The six blisses (Āhnika 5) correspond to the seven alchemical operations (with the first being twofold):
1. Emission bliss = Calcination
2. Supreme bliss = Dissolution
3. Origin bliss = Separation
4. Cessation bliss = Conjunction
5. Union bliss = Fermentation
6. Equal bliss = Distillation + Coagulation (the Lapis)

### Temporal Means as Alchemical Time

Āhnika 6's Kālopāya teaches that the alchemical operations are not external processes but temporal cycles within the breath. The "philosophical month" and "philosophical year" of the alchemists are literal descriptions of the breath cycles mapped to cosmic time. The five prāṇas are the alchemist's five furnaces — each with its own temperature, duration, and product.
