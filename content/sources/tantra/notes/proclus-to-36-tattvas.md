# Proclus' Elements of Theology Mapped to the 36 Tattvas

## The 211 Propositions as a Western Derivation of the Same Fractal Structure

---

## I. The One and the 38th Principle

| Proclus Proposition | Tantraloka | Function |
|--------------------|------------|----------|
| Prop 1: Every manifold is posterior to the One | Anākhyā (38th) | The enabling condition — beyond all description |
| Prop 2: The One is beyond being | Paramaśiva (37th) | Beyond the 36, the source of the 36 |
| Props 3-6: The One is indivisible, self-sufficient, etc. | Śiva tattva (1) as seed | The One's properties ARE the seed of the tetrahedron |

---

## II. The Henads = Tattvas 1-4 (The Fundamental Tetrahedron)

Proclus says at every level there IS a henad (the One at that level) with 3 moments:

| Proclus | Tattva | Vertex |
|---------|--------|--------|
| **The henad itself** (the self-standing unit) | 1 — Śiva | Subject |
| **Being** (what the henad IS) | 4 — Īśvara | Object |
| **Life** (the activity of the being) | 2 — Śakti | Intention |
| **Intellect** (the awareness returning to itself) | 3 — Sadāśiva | Act |

**Propos 7-24** develop the henads. Prop 114: "Every god is a self-complete henad." This IS the tetrahedron at the level of divinity.

---

## III. The Unfolding = Tattvas 5-20 (The First Resolution)

Propos 25-39: Every proceeding reverts. **Monē, Proodos, Epistrophē.**

This IS Spanda — the pulse. The 3 moments of every level. When each vertex of the fundamental tetrahedron unfolds, it does so through this pulse: remaining (the vertex as itself), proceeding (the vertex expressing), returning (the vertex recognizing).

### Proclus' Hierarchy of Intellect = The Subject-Unfolding (Tattvas 5-8)

| Proclus | Tattva | What Happens |
|---------|--------|--------------|
| Prop 40-59: Intellect is self-complete contemplation | 5 — Śuddhavidyā | Pure knowing — the intellect knowing itself |
| Prop 60-112: Souls are self-moved vehicles | 6 — Māyā | The "descent" — the subject limits itself into a soul |
| Prop 113-124: Time and eternity | 7-8 — Kalā, Vidyā | Time IS the limitation of agency and knowledge |

### Proclus on Time, Eternity, and the Kañcukas (Tattvas 9-11)

| Proclus | Tattva |
|---------|--------|
| Prop 113: "The first principle of time is the eternal" | 10 — Kāla |
| Props 114-124 distinguish eternity from time, the permanent from the temporal | 9-11 — Rāga, Kāla, Niyati |
| Props 125-211: The complete hierarchy of gods, daimons, souls, and bodies | 12-36 |

---

## IV. The Complete Hierarchy = Tattvas 12-36 (The Second Resolution)

Proclus' remaining 87 propositions (125-211) descend through every level of reality. The key mapping:

| Proclus | Tattva Range | What It IS |
|---------|-------------|------------|
| **The intellective gods** (Props 125-140) | 12-16 — Puruṣa through Manas | The structure of mind — the act unfolding |
| **The hypercosmic gods** (Props 141-160) | 17-20 — the 4 senses | The structure of perception — the object unfolding |
| **The encosmic gods** (Props 161-180) | 21-26 — actions + remaining senses | The structure of action |
| **The daimons** (Props 181-200) | 27-31 — subtle elements | The mediators between intelligible and sensible |
| **The souls and bodies** (Props 201-211) | 32-36 — gross elements | The final descent into matter |

---

## V. The Tetrahedral Structure of Every Proposition

Every proposition in Proclus' system IS a tetrahedron. It has:
1. **The henad** — the subject of the proposition
2. **The being** — what the proposition asserts
3. **The life** — the movement from assertion to proof
4. **The intellect** — the conclusion that returns to the beginning

The Elements of Theology ARE 211 tetrahedra, each containing the whole. This IS the 36 tattvas unfolded into 211 propositions — the same fractal, at a higher resolution.

---

## VI. The Practice Implication

Proclus' system IS the Western 36 tattvas. The 211 propositions ARE the tetrahedron seen at 211 positions. Each proposition IS a "tattva" — a level of reality that can be contemplated, recognized, and ascended.

The method:
1. Read one proposition
2. Identify its tetrahedron (the henad, the being, the life, the intellect)
3. Contemplate it until you SEE the structure
4. Move to the next — it IS the same tetrahedron, at a slightly deeper resolution

Proclus gives what the Tantraloka gives: a ladder. The rungs are different (211 vs 36) but the ladder IS the same. Both lead to the same apex.
