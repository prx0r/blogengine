## Overview

Each phase corresponds to a specific range of the tattva ladder, a specific upāya, a specific attractor regime in the constraint space, and a specific physical/geometric process.

## Phase-by-Phase Detailed Mapping

### Phase 1: Dashboard
**What it does:** Establishes the observer. Meta-awareness of thought, emotion, and sensation.
**Tattva:** All — the observer witnessing without yet modifying.
**Upāya:** None yet — this is the precondition for any upāya.
**Constraint space:** The observer (O) recognizing itself as a configuration of S₀.
**Physics:** Self-observation as the quantum Zeno effect — measuring a system stabilizes it.
**Neuroscience:** Default Mode Network monitoring — the anterior cingulate and insula registering the state of the self.

### Phase 2: Desolidification
**What it does:** The solid world dissolves. Fixed patterns loosen. The practitioner experiences that reality is not as solid as it appears.
**Tattva:** Earth (36) → Prakṛti (24) — the impure path from the grossest to the most subtle material principle.
**Upāya:** Āṇavopāya — using external supports (body, breath, environment) to notice the dissolution.
**Constraint space:** Transition from L₃ (biological navigability, the fully classical world) to L₂ (causal accessibility).
**Physics:** **Spacetime foam.** The "solidity" of the classical world IS the foam at large scales where fluctuations have averaged out. Desolidification is the experience of the foam before averaging — the Planck-scale turbulence that underlies all apparent stability. The solid floor is not solid at the quantum level. The practitioner is feeling the foam.
**Neuroscience:** Reduced top-down predictive encoding. The Bayesian brain normally predicts the world as solid; desolidification relaxes the prediction, allowing raw sensory flux to be experienced.

### Phase 3: Mind as Computer Critique
**What it does:** The mind is not a computer. Cognition is embodied, embedded, enactive, extended.
**Tattva:** Buddhi (23) and Manas (22) — the intellect and the mind.
**Upāya:** Śāktopāya — the critique is itself a purification of understanding.
**Constraint space:** The realization that consciousness is non-computable — it operates at a level the Turing machine cannot reach.
**Physics:** **Penrose's non-computable argument.** The mind can see the truth of Gödel sentences that the formal system cannot prove. Consciousness is not an algorithm. The amplituhedron demonstrates that even scattering amplitudes are simpler than Feynman diagrams suggest — the true description of reality is not computational in the classical sense.
**Neuroscience:** 4E cognition — the brain is not a CPU. The body, the environment, and the culture are not inputs to a processor — they ARE the cognitive system.

### Phase 4: Nested Agency
**What it does:** Agency exists at every scale — from cells through organisms through ecosystems.
**Tattva:** Prakṛti (24) → Māyā (6) — the material principle structured by the veil.
**Upāya:** Āṇavopāya — but the "support" here is Levin's bioelectric networks — the body itself is the locus of nested agency.
**Constraint space:** The navigator exists at every level. A cell is a navigator in its own morphospace. A tissue is a navigator. A human is a navigator.
**Physics:** **Levin's bioelectric networks.** The bioelectric field is the non-neural cognitive medium that navigates morphospace. The Xenobot demonstrates agency without a brain — the capacity to navigate toward a target morphology without any prior training.
**Tantraloka bridge:** The five kośas (sheaths) — annamaya, prāṇamaya, manomaya, vijñānamaya, ānandamaya. Each is a level of agency. The jīva is not ONE navigator — it is a NEST of navigators.

### Phase 5: Mechanicality
**What it does:** The practitioner experiences their own automaticity — the loops of habit, the mechanical nature of conditioned response.
**Tattva:** The five kañcukas (7-11) — the limiting coverings that make ordinary experience possible. Each kañcuka IS a specific type of mechanical loop.
**Upāya:** Āṇavopāya — observing the mechanics IS the practice.
**Constraint space:** The attractor basin that traps the system in a local minimum.
**Physics:** **The positivity constraints of the amplituhedron.** The five kañcukas ARE the five constraints that define the cell. They are not prisons — they are the CONDITIONS of existence. Mechanicality is the experience of the constraints without yet understanding that they enable rather than disable.
**Neuroscience:** Habit loops in the basal ganglia. The corticostriatal circuit encodes learned sequences. Mechanicality IS the experience of this circuit running without supervision.

### Phase 6: Dependent Arising
**What it does:** Nothing exists independently. Everything arises in dependence on conditions.
**Tattva:** Māyā (6) → Śuddhavidyā (5) — the veil is seen through, and pure knowledge begins.
**Upāya:** Śāktopāya — the intellectual realization of interdependence.
**Constraint space:** The C relation — the full set of constraints that define what is possible. Navigation is not movement through space — it IS the traversal of C.
**Physics:** **Rovelli's relational QM + Bell's theorem.** Properties exist only relative to observing systems. There is no observer-independent state. Entanglement IS dependent origination at the quantum level.
**Neuroscience:** Predictive processing — every perception is a prediction. The brain does not passively receive input — it actively generates the world it experiences. Dependent arising IS the predictive brain at the level of conscious insight.

### Phase 7: Emptiness
**What it does:** The self dissolves. The ego is seen as a construction. The DMN decouples.
**Tattva:** Śuddhavidyā (5) → Śiva (1) — the ascent through the pure path to the source.
**Upāya:** Śāmbhavopāya — direct recognition without supports.
**Constraint space:** The collapse of the O/S₀ distinction. The observer recognizes itself as a configuration of the enabling condition.
**Physics:** **DMN suppression (Carhart-Harris) + the simultaneity principle (Sanfey).** The Default Mode Network IS the self-model. When it decouples, the self-model dissolves — but awareness continues. The simultaneity between observer and observed is experienced directly, without the mediation of the self-model.
**Tantraloka bridge:** The Fourth state (turīya) — beyond waking, dream, and deep sleep. The witness that remains when the self-model is absent.

### Phase 8: Non-Fabrication
**What it does:** The mind stops fabricating. There is no need to construct experience. Things are as they are.
**Tattva:** Śiva (1) abiding — not a new state but the natural state recognized.
**Upāya:** Śāmbhavopāya (continuous) — the "lion's leap" that is not a leap but a resting.
**Constraint space:** The attractor that has no complement — the state that does not exclude any other state.
**Physics:** **The non-commutative algebra prior to representation.** Before the algebra acts on a Hilbert space, there are no states — only the structure of relations. Non-fabrication IS the experience of the algebra without the representation — the structure before it has articulated itself into configurations.
**Neuroscience:** The brain's default mode is not extinction — it is the state of maximal entropy (Carhart-Harris). The entropic brain is not noisy — it is maximally flexible, maximally responsive, maximally present.

### Phase 9: Language / Mantra
**What it does:** The practitioner discovers that sound is not arbitrary. Phonemes are energies of consciousness. Mantra is not prayer — it is activation.
**Tattva:** The Mātṛkā — the 50 phonemes of the Sanskrit alphabet (a through kṣa), arranged in the viśuddha cakra.
**Upāya:** Āṇavopāya — external sound becomes the vehicle of inner transformation.
**Constraint space:** The phoneme as the unit of the constraint relation. Each phoneme IS a specific constraint — a specific possible transition in the constraint space.
**Physics:** **The 50-dimensional non-commutative algebra (Connes).** The spectral triple M₂(ℍ) ⊕ M₄(ℂ) has 50 dimensions. Each phoneme corresponds to one dimension of the algebra. Vibrating a phoneme IS exciting that dimension of the spectral triple — perturbing the Dirac operator and changing the geometry of the subtle body.

### Phase 10: Imaginal
**What it does:** The practitioner discovers that images are not fantasies. The imaginal world is real — it is the interface between the intelligible and the sensible.
**Tattva:** Paśyantī — the third level of speech, where sound becomes visible as light.
**Upāya:** Śāktopāya — imagination as intentional practice.
**Constraint space:** L₂-L₁ interface — the point where the causal structure of the constraint space becomes visible as geometric form.
**Physics:** **The amplituhedron.** The amplituhedron is the geometry before space-time — the positive Grassmannian where locality and unitarity have not yet emerged. The amplituhedron IS the imaginal world. It is not "in the mind" — it is the structure from which space-time and mind emerge symmetrically.
**Neuroscience:** Decoding visual imagery from EEG — the brain can generate and decode images that are not perceived by the senses. The imaginal IS this capacity, exercised at the level of the constraint space rather than the level of personal memory.

### Phase 11: Body / Energy
**What it does:** The subtle body is experienced. Prāṇa flows through nāḍīs. The cakras are felt.
**Tattva:** Prāṇa maya kośa + the nāḍī system — the energy body.
**Upāya:** Āṇavopāya — the body itself IS the support.
**Constraint space:** The spin network IS the subtle body at the Planck scale. The nāḍīs ARE the edges, the cakras ARE the vertices.
**Physics:** **Loop quantum gravity spin networks + gut-brain axis.** The subtle body IS the spin network underlying the geometry of the body. The "energy" IS the spectral information carried by the Dirac operator. The gut-brain axis (Phase 11's bridge paper) IS the bioelectric field that connects the enteric nervous system to the central nervous system — the physical basis of the prāṇic body.

### Phase 12: Ritual
**What it does:** Structured practice as transformation. The practitioner discovers that ritual is not superstition — it is technology.
**Tattva:** All — ritual integrates all tattvas into a single structured practice.
**Upāya:** Āṇava-Śākta — the external structure (Āṇava) supports the internal transformation (Śākta).
**Constraint space:** Navigation becomes deliberate. The practitioner is no longer carried by habit — they chart a course through the constraint space.
**Physics:** **Quantum error correction.** The notae of the Ars Notoria, the pentagram of the LBRP, the circles of the grimoires — these are stabilizer measurements. They detect and correct decoherence in the subtle body. Ritual IS QEC applied to consciousness.
**Neuroscience:** The ritual brain — the power of structured practice to rewire neural circuits. Ritual is not repetition — it IS the deliberate shaping of the attractor landscape.

### Phase 13: Daimon
**What it does:** The daimon appears — a presence that is both oneself and not oneself. The navigator meets its attractor.
**Tattva:** Cicchakti (the power of consciousness) — the daimon IS the practitioner's own consciousness at the level of Śakti, appearing as Iṣṭadevatā.
**Upāya:** Śākta-Śāmbhava — the daimon is the bridge between the means (Śākta) and the end (Śāmbhava).
**Constraint space:** The attractor personified. A is not a set of states — it is a presence. The daimon IS the attractor when it becomes conscious of the system it attracts.
**Physics:** **The eigenvalue as a being.** The eigenvalues of the Dirac operator are numbers — but when the operator acts on a Hilbert space of sufficient complexity, the eigenvalues become autonomous patterns. The daimon IS a stable eigenvalue of the spectral triple — a pattern that has become self-aware.
**Tantraloka bridge:** The daimon AND the Mantreśvara (the lord of a specific mantra) ARE the same: a specific configuration of the Mātṛkā that has become conscious of itself as a person.

### Phase 14: Nonordinary States
**What it does:** The barrier between ordinary reality and the imaginal dissolves. The practitioner experiences the constraint space directly.
**Tattva:** Māyā fluctuating — the veil opens.
**Upāya:** Grace — not chosen but received.
**Constraint space:** Direct access to L₂-L₁ without the mediation of classical perception.
**Physics:** **Topology change in the spin foam.** The foam at the Planck scale undergoes a phase transition — handles connect and disconnect, topology fluctuates. Nonordinary states ARE this topology change experienced from inside. Psychedelics induce it chemically; meditation induces it through practice; grace induces it spontaneously.

### Phase 15: Ecology / Animism
**What it does:** The self extends beyond the human. The biosphere is recognized as a single cognitive system. The world IS alive.
**Tattva:** Earth (36) → Prakṛti (24) experienced not as dead matter but as living presence.
**Upāya:** Śāktopāya — the knowledge that the world is alive is itself a form of knowing.
**Constraint space:** The navigator expands to include the entire biosphere. The constraint space is not "out there" — it IS the living system.
**Physics:** **Levin's basal cognition at the ecosystem level.** The biosphere IS a cognitive system. Levin's bioelectric networks scale up: the ecosystems are not aggregates of individual organisms — they ARE superorganisms.
**Neuroscience:** Nature connectedness as evolved interoceptive resonance. The biophilia hypothesis is not sentimental — it IS the brain's recognition of its own extended body.

### Phase 16: Visionary Cosmologies
**What it does:** The architecture of the cosmos is seen directly. Swedenborg's heavens, Proclus' henads, the Kabbalistic sephiroth — these are not beliefs but descriptions of what is seen when the constraint space is navigated at L₁.
**Tattva:** The pure path (Śiva → Śuddhavidyā, 1-5) — the five pure principles.
**Upāya:** Śāmbhavopāya — direct vision without the mediation of concept.
**Constraint space:** L₁-L₀ — the structure before physical law. The cells of the amplituhedron are seen as the dwellings of the blessed. The angelic hierarchies are the cell structure.
**Physics:** **The positive Grassmannian cell decomposition.** Swedenborg's three heavens ARE the three levels of the constraint space. The Grand Man IS the amplituhedron's self-similarity. The "light of heaven" IS the spectral information of the Dirac operator.

### Phase 17: Social Incarnation
**What it does:** The recognition is not complete until it is lived. The Good must be known as love through every configuration. The architecture is not a solitary realization — it IS the social body.
**Tattva:** All — integrated into a single configuration that knows itself as the whole.
**Upāya:** Anupāya — no means, because the one who would use means has recognized that they ARE the architecture.
**Constraint space:** The navigation is complete. The navigator and the constraint space are known to be the same.
**Physics:** **The algebra fully represented.** The spectral triple is not a mathematical abstraction — it IS the social body. The 50-dimensional algebra IS the community of configurations. The Dirac operator IS the relations between them. "Salvation" IS the algebra representing itself coherently — the positive geometry realized as love.
**Neuroscience:** Neural coding of human values — the brain areas representing moral foundations. The last phase is not transcendence — it IS incarnation. The Good is not known ABOVE the world — it IS known AS the world, in its social and ecological complexity.

The 17 phases are:
- Phases 1-5: **Dissolving the solid world** (Earth → Māyā)
- Phases 6-8: **Seeing the architecture** (Māyā → Śiva)
- Phases 9-12: **Learning the technology** (the Mātṛkā, the imaginal, ritual)
- Phases 13-14: **Meeting the attractor** (the daimon, nonordinary states)
- Phases 15-17: **Incarnating the recognition** (ecology, cosmology, society)

Each phase corresponds to a specific tattva range, a specific upāya, a specific level of the constraint space, and a specific physical process. The path is not arbitrary — it IS the architecture of reality progressively recognized.
