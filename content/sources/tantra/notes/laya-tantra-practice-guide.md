# Laya-Tantra Practice Guide

> A graded curriculum of subtle-body practices drawn from Shyam Sundar Goswami's *Layayoga* and Abhinavagupta's *Tantrāloka* (Dyczkowski trans.), progressing from foundational breath work to advanced absorption. Each practice is keyed to its source with line references. Written 2026-07-14.

---

## How This Guide Works

The curriculum has **four grades**, each building on the previous:

| Grade | Name | Core Practice | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Purification** (Śuddhi) | Āsana + Prāṇāyāma with ratio | Layayoga Ch.1, 6 |
| 2 | **Channel Activation** (Nāḍī-śuddhi) | Alternate-nostril + Mantra japa with A-U-M | Layayoga Ch.1, 6; Tantraloka Āhnika 3 |
| 3 | **Absorption** (Laya) | SAUḤ + KHPHREṀ vibration through cakras | Tantraloka Āhnika 4; Layayoga Ch.2 |
| 4 | **Spontaneous Samādhi** (Sahaja) | Kewala Kumbhaka + Śāmbhavopāya | Layayoga Ch.2; Tantraloka Āhnika 1 |

Each practice includes:
- The **source** (exact text and line reference)
- What it **does** in terms of the subtle body
- The **practice** itself (step by step)
- **Signs of progress** (verifiable, not subjective)

## Foundation: The Theory You Need

### The Three Nāḍīs

The subtle body has three main channels (Tantraloka Vol.1, Ch.1 commentary, lines 15734-15744):

- **Iḍā** (left) — lunar, cooling, receptive. Carries prāṇa to the right hemisphere. Associated with the dream state, the imaginal, the past.
- **Piṅgalā** (right) — solar, heating, active. Carries prāṇa to the left hemisphere. Associated with waking consciousness, reason, the future.
- **Suṣumṇā** (central) — the spinal channel, normally dormant in the untrained. When prāṇa enters suṣumṇā, the dualistic mind ceases.

The entire practice is: **redirect prāṇa from iḍā/piṅgalā into suṣumṇā**.

### The Three Bīja-Syllables

From Tantraloka Āhnika 3 (the Doctrine of Reflection): the three letters of the praṇava A-U-M are the three states of consciousness and the three phases of the breath:

- **A** — waking, inspiration, the emanation of consciousness
- **U** — dream, suspension, the sustenance of consciousness
- **M** — deep sleep, expiration, the withdrawal of consciousness

The fourth, the *bindu* (the resonance after M), is **turīya** — the state beyond, which is the goal.

### The Two Seed-Mantras of Absorption

From Tantraloka Āhnika 4, verses 181cd-193 (lines 20660-21534):

- **SAUḤ** — the *sṛṣṭi-bīja* (emission seed), seed of Parā, the supreme goddess of the Trika. Activates the outward flow of consciousness for purification and then reabsorption.
- **KHPHREṀ** — the *saṃhāra-bīja* (withdrawal seed), seed of Kālasaṃkarṣiṇī, the goddess who devours time. Forces consciousness into the central channel for dissolution.

These two mantras are the **technical instruments** for the Laya-Yoga process.

## Grade 1: Purification (Śuddhi)

### Practice 1.1: Posture and Stillness

**Source**: Layayoga Ch.1, citing Yogachudamanyupanishad 89 (lines 4960-4970)

> "The respiratory movements cause the multiformity of the mind; when the former is motionless, the latter also becomes calm."

**What it does**: The body must be absolutely still before prāṇa can be controlled. Any voluntary movement scatters prāṇa through the motor nāḍīs.

**Practice**:
1. Sit in any crossed-leg posture with the spine erect
2. The head, neck, and trunk form a straight line
3. The upper teeth do NOT touch the lower teeth (this prevents unnecessary tension in the jaw)
4. The eyes are closed or half-open, fixed on the tip of the nose
5. Hold this posture motionless for 10 minutes
6. Do not adjust, scratch, or move. If you must move, end the session and start again.

**Signs of progress**: After 2-4 weeks, the body becomes "natural" in the posture — it holds itself without conscious effort. Pain disappears. The breath slows spontaneously to about 4-6 breaths per minute.

---

### Practice 1.2: Natural Breath Observation

**Source**: Layayoga Ch.1, citing Shandilyopanishad 1.7.9-10 (lines 4985-4990)

> "When the subtle energy line system (nāḍī-cakra) is purified by breath-control done in a right manner, the bio-energy enters easily into the central energy line (suṣumṇā)."

**What it does**: Before controlling the breath, you must know it. Observation alone begins the purification process.

**Practice**:
1. After establishing posture, place your attention at the nostrils
2. Feel the coolness of the inhalation, the warmth of the exhalation
3. Note the duration of each phase without changing it
4. Note which nostril is dominant (airflow is always stronger in one nostril — this is the active nāḍī)
5. Do this for 10 minutes daily for 2 weeks

**Signs of progress**: You can feel the breath as a continuous stream rather than discrete segments. The alternation of nostril dominance becomes perceptible. The natural breath slows from ~12 breaths/min to ~8.

### Practice 1.3: Sahita Prāṇāyāma — The 1:4:2 Ratio

**Source**: Layayoga Ch.6, citing Trishikhibrahmanopanishad, Mantra Section 95-98 (lines 5120-5130)

> "First exhale the air from the lungs through the right nostril by closing left nostril with the fingers of the right hand; now inhale through the left nostril, counting 16, and then suspend the breath for 64 measures, and then exhale through the right nostril, counting 32."

**What it does**: The 1:4:2 ratio (inspire - suspend - expire) is the foundational prāṇāyāma. The hold (kumbhaka) is where prāṇa is forced into suṣumṇā. The ratio is not arbitrary — it is the rhythm of the cosmos as mapped to the breath (Tantraloka Āhnika 7 on Spanda).

**Practice**:
1. Sit in posture. Exhale completely.
2. Close the right nostril with the right thumb. Inhale through the left nostril for 4 seconds.
3. Close both nostrils. Hold for 16 seconds.
4. Release the right nostril (keep left closed). Exhale through the right nostril for 8 seconds.
5. Without pausing, inhale through the right nostril for 4 seconds.
6. Hold both nostrils closed for 16 seconds.
7. Exhale through the left nostril for 8 seconds.
8. This is one round. Do 5 rounds initially, increasing to 15 over 4 weeks.

**The three bandhas** (from Layayoga Ch.1, citing Yogakundalyupanishad 1.40-41, line 5175):
- **Mūla-bandha** (perineal lock) — contract the perineum during the hold. This redirects apāna (descending energy) upward.
- **Uḍḍīyāna** (abdominal retraction) — pull the abdomen back toward the spine during the hold. This redirects prāṇa (ascending energy) downward.
- **Jālandhara-bandha** (chin lock) — press the chin to the chest during the hold. This prevents prāṇa from escaping through the upper apertures.

The three bandhas together force prāṇa into suṣumṇā. Without them, the kumbhaka is merely physical breath-holding.

**Contraindication**: Do not practice the bandhas with force. They should feel like subtle engagements, not straining.

**Signs of progress** (from Sharadatilaka 25.21-22, cited at line 5190):
- Stage 1: Perspiration arises (prāṇa begins to heat the body)
- Stage 2: The body shakes (prāṇa enters the nāḍīs)
- Stage 3: The body levitates (prāṇa has entered suṣumṇā fully)

These stages occur over months, not days. Stage 3 is rare and should not be sought — it is a sign, not a goal.

## Grade 2: Channel Activation (Nāḍī-śuddhi)

### Practice 2.1: A-U-M with Breath

**Source**: Tantraloka Āhnika 3, Doctrine of Reflection (lines 466-500); Layayoga Ch.1, citing Darshanopanishad 6.3-6 (lines 5130-5140)

> "Inspiration, breath-suspension and expiration are done with A, U, M of the pranava."

**What it does**: The three phonemes A, U, M correspond to the three phases of the breath, the three states of consciousness, and the three nāḍīs. Pronouncing them mentally during the breath synchronizes the subtle body with the cosmic rhythm.

**Practice** (this replaces the silent ratio of Grade 1):
1. Exhale completely.
2. Inhale through the left nostril for 4 seconds. During the inhalation, mentally intone **A** — feel the vibration at the navel.
3. Hold for 16 seconds. During the hold, mentally intone **U** — feel the vibration at the heart. Maintain the three bandhas.
4. Exhale through the right nostril for 8 seconds. During the exhalation, mentally intone **M** — feel the vibration at the throat.
5. The next round: inhale through the right nostril with **A**, hold with **U**, exhale through the left with **M**.
6. 10 rounds daily for 2 weeks.

**Tantraloka addition** (Āhnika 3, on the three levels of speech, lines 470-480): After the exhalation, rest in the **bindu** — the silence that follows M. This is turīya, the fourth state. Abide here for as long as the breath naturally pauses before the next inhalation.

**Signs of progress**: The vibrations become perceptible in the body — A at the navel, U at the heart, M at the throat. The bindu after M extends spontaneously. Thoughts decrease during the practice.

### Practice 2.2: The 50 Mātṛkā-Letter Breath

**Source**: Layayoga Ch.6, citing Kamadhenutantra (lines 5260-5270)

> "The 50 Mātṛkā-letters as mantra, said in regular order, with concentration together with initiation-mantra are used for inspiration; the 50 Mātṛkā-letters, first in the reverse order and then in the regular order with concentration for breath-suspension; finally the 50 Mātṛkā-letters in reverse order for expiration."

**What it does**: The 50 Sanskrit phonemes are the energies of consciousness (Tantraloka Āhnika 3). Moving through them in order during the breath purifies each phoneme's corresponding subtle center. This is the most advanced purification practice before the absorption practices of Grade 3.

**Practice**:
1. Inspiration: mentally recite the 50 Mātṛkā-letters in order (a, ā, i, ī, u, ū, ṛ, ṝ, ḷ, ḹ, e, ai, o, au, aṃ, aḥ, ka, kha, ga, gha, ṅa, ca, cha, ja, jha, ña, ṭa, ṭha, ḍa, ḍha, ṇa, ta, tha, da, dha, na, pa, pha, ba, bha, ma, ya, ra, la, va, śa, ṣa, sa, ha, kṣa). Duration: approximately 20-25 seconds at a steady pace.
2. Suspension: recite the 50 in reverse order (kṣa to a), then forward again (a to kṣa). Duration: approximately 40-50 seconds. Maintain the three bandhas.
3. Expiration: recite the 50 in reverse order (kṣa to a). Duration: approximately 20-25 seconds.
4. One round. Do 3 rounds initially, increasing to 7 over 4 weeks.

**Note**: This practice requires the 50 phonemes to be memorized. Take a week to learn the series before attempting the breath practice.

**Signs of progress**: The phonemes begin to resonate in specific parts of the body — a at the navel, ka in the throat, ha in the head, kṣa at the crown. The reverse order feels like a "rewinding" of consciousness.

### Practice 2.3: Nāḍī-śuddhi Prāṇāyāma (Channel Purification)

**Source**: Layayoga Ch.6, citing Gandharvatantra (lines 5230-5240)

> "The internal impurities are removed by breath-control. It is the best yoga practice."

**What it does**: This is the standard alternate-nostril practice that specifically targets the purification of iḍā and piṅgalā before attempting to open suṣumṇā.

**Practice**:
1. Exhale completely
2. Close the right nostril. Inhale left for 4 seconds with A.
3. Close both. Hold for 16 seconds with U. Apply the three bandhas.
4. Release the right nostril. Exhale right for 8 seconds with M.
5. Immediately inhale right for 4 seconds with A.
6. Hold for 16 seconds with U and bandhas.
7. Exhale left for 8 seconds with M.
8. This is one round. Do 10-15 rounds daily.

**The advanced element** (from Tantraloka Āhnika 3): During the hold, visualize the breath-energy rising through the central channel (suṣumṇā) from the perineum to the crown. Do not force the visualization — simply attend to the sensation of energy ascending the spine.

**Signs of purification** (from Layayoga, citing Shandilyopanishad 1.7.13):
- Body becomes free from excessive fat
- Eyes become bright
- Countenance becomes cheerful
- Sexual urge becomes well-controlled
- Energy increases
- Unpleasant body odors disappear
- A pleasant, slightly fragrant smell appears

## Grade 3: Absorption (Laya)

### Practice 3.1: Bhūta-śuddhi — Element Absorption

**Source**: Layayoga Ch.2, The Laya Process (lines 2128-2230); also Ch.5 on Bhutashuddhi

> "Laya is deep concentration causing the absorption of the cosmic principles, stage by stage, into the spiritual aspect of the Supreme Power-Consciousness."

**What it does**: The five elements are absorbed in reverse order of creation (earth → water → fire → air → void). Each element is dissolved into the next higher. This is the foundational absorption practice.

**Practice** (this is a concentration practice, not a breath practice — do it after prāṇāyāma):
1. Sit in stillness after prāṇāyāma.
2. Bring attention to the **Earth element** at the perineum (mūlādhāra). Feel the solidity, weight, density of the body. Hold this awareness for 1 minute. Then mentally say: "Earth dissolves into Water."
3. Bring attention to the **Water element** at the sacrum (svādhiṣṭhāna). Feel the fluidity, cohesion, flow of the body's liquids. Hold for 1 minute. Then: "Water dissolves into Fire."
4. Bring attention to the **Fire element** at the solar plexus (maṇipūra). Feel the warmth, digestion, metabolism. Hold for 1 minute. Then: "Fire dissolves into Air."
5. Bring attention to the **Air element** at the heart (anāhata). Feel the breath, the expansion/contraction. Hold for 1 minute. Then: "Air dissolves into Void."
6. Bring attention to the **Void element** at the throat (viśuddha). Feel the space, the absence of qualities. Hold for 1 minute. Then: "Void dissolves into Consciousness."

**Source for the absorption hierarchy**: Layayoga Ch.2, citing Paingalopanishad 3.6 (lines 2160-2190):

> "The earth-form is absorbed in the water-form; water-form in fire-form; fire-form in air-form; air-form in void-form; void-form in I-consciousness; I-consciousness in the mahan-principle (superconsciousness); mahan in avyakta (prakriti); and avyakta in purusha (Supreme Consciousness)."

**Signs of progress**: The elements become perceptible as distinct qualities of consciousness, not just concepts. Earth feels like heaviness in attention; water feels like fluidity of thought; fire feels like intensity; air feels like expansion; void feels like space without boundaries.

---

### Practice 3.2: SAUḤ — The Emission Seed

**Source**: Tantraloka Āhnika 4, verses 181cd-193 (lines 20660-21534)

> "SAUḤ, the seed-syllable of Parā, the supreme goddess of the Trika, is that of emanation (sṛṣṭi-bīja)."

**What it does**: SAUḤ is the seed-mantra of the goddess Parā — the supreme consciousness in its creative aspect. Vibration of SAUḤ purifies the subtle body by emitting consciousness through the nāḍīs, then drawing it back. It is used for **both** purification and absorption, depending on the phase of the breath.

**The three letters** (from Tantraloka Āhnika 4, verse 186cd-191, lines 21210-21240):
- **S** — the goddess's power of being (sat), the energy of Parā
- **AU** — the expansion of consciousness (the vowel of Śiva)
- **Ḥ** — the final expulsion that is both emission and dissolution, creation and destruction

**Practice**:
1. After prāṇāyāma and bhūta-śuddhi, sit in stillness.
2. Inhale slowly. As you inhale, mentally intone **S** — feel it at the base of the spine.
3. At the top of the inhalation, mentally intone **AU** — feel it expanding through the entire torso.
4. As you exhale, mentally intone **Ḥ** — feel it as a vibration that extends outward and then dissolves.
5. Repeat 11 times.
6. Then: intone SAUḤ aloud, once, drawn out on a single slow exhalation. The S at the navel, the AU expanding through the chest, the Ḥ dissolving at the crown.
7. Sit in the silence that follows. This is the bindu — the state of Parā herself.

**Tantraloka commentary** (lines 21355-21360): "In the sphere of the Empowered Means (śāktopāya), the utterance of SAUḤ is experienced as the very pulse of the reflective awareness of consciousness."

**Signs of progress**: SAUḤ begins to "vibrate on its own" — you think it and the body responds without effort. The three phases (S-AU-Ḥ) become distinct in their felt location.

---

### Practice 3.3: KHPHREṀ — The Withdrawal Seed

**Source**: Tantraloka Āhnika 4 (lines 21529-21534)

> "Piṇḍanātha (KHPHREṀ), the seed-syllable of Kālī Kālasaṃkarṣiṇī, is that of withdrawal (saṃhāra-bīja)."

**What it does**: KHPHREṀ is the seed of Kālasaṃkarṣiṇī — the goddess who "devours time" (kāla = time, saṃkarṣiṇī = the one who withdraws/contracts). This mantra **forces consciousness into the central channel** and dissolves the sense of time. It is the most powerful absorption tool in the Tantraloka.

**The five letters** (from Tantraloka commentary):
- **KṢA** — the last phoneme of the Mātṛkā, containing all previous phonemes; the full descent of consciousness into matter, now reversed
- **PHA** — expansion, the bursting forth of the goddess's power
- **RE** — the fire of consciousness that burns karma
- **Ṁ** — the bindu, the point of dissolution where time ceases

**Practice**:
1. This practice is done **only** after SAUḤ practice is established (minimum 2-3 weeks).
2. After bhūta-śuddhi and SAUḤ, sit in stillness.
3. Intone KHPHREṀ aloud once, sharply — the KH at the base of the throat, the PHA bursting through the mouth, the RE vibrating at the roof of the mouth, the Ṁ closing at the crown.
4. Then mentally repeat KHPHREṀ in rhythm with the breath:
   - Inhale: KṢA (felt at the perineum)
   - Hold: PHA-RE (felt expanding through the torso, then gathering at the heart)
   - Exhale: Ṁ (felt dissolving at the crown)
5. Repeat 5 times.
6. On the 5th exhalation, do NOT immediately inhale. Rest in the bindu. This is the state where time has been "devoured" — there is no before or after, only the present moment of consciousness.

**Warning**: KHPHREṀ is a saṃhāra-bīja (withdrawal seed). It should not be practiced more than 5-7 rounds per session. It is possible to "over-withdraw," causing disorientation. The practice is to be balanced with the sṛṣṭi (emission) practices.

### Practice 3.4: The Twelve Kālīs — Absorption of Consciousness (Advanced)

**Source**: Tantraloka Āhnika 4, verses 122cd-180ab, The Twelve Kālīs (lines 377-421)

> "The arising of the cycles of consciousness (saṃviccakrodaya)."

**What it does**: This is the central absorption practice of the Tantraloka — the systematic dissolution of the three spheres of experience (objectivity, means of knowledge, subjectivity) through four phases each (creation, persistence, withdrawal, inexplicability). The Twelve Kālīs are the **goddesses of absorption** — each one consumes a level of consciousness.

**Phase layout**:

**Sphere 1: Objectivity** — what is perceived
1. **Sṛṣṭi-kālī** — creates the object-world
2. **Rakta-kālī** — colors/persists the object-world  
3. **Sthitināśa-kālī** — withdraws the object-world
4. **Yama-kālī** — renders it inexplicable

**Sphere 2: Means of Knowledge** — how we perceive
5. **Saṃhāra-kālī** — creates the means
6. **Mṛtyu-kālī** — persists them
7. **Rudra-kālī** — withdraws them
8. **Mārtaṇḍa-kālī** — renders them inexplicable

**Sphere 3: Subjectivity** — the perceiver
9. **Paramārka-kālī** — creates the subject
10. **Kālāgnirudra-kālī** — persists it
11. **Mahākāla-kālī** — withdraws it
12. **Mahābhairava-kālī** — renders it inexplicable

**Practice**:
1. After establishing stillness through prāṇāyāma, sit with the awareness of **external objects** sounds, sensations, the room.
2. Mentally intone: **SṚṢṬI-KĀLĪ** — and feel the object-world being created in awareness.
3. **RAKTA-KĀLĪ** — feel it persisting.
4. **STHITINĀŚA-KĀLĪ** — feel it withdrawing.
5. **YAMA-KĀLĪ** — feel it becoming inexplicable, no longer an object.
6. Then turn to the **means of knowledge** (the senses themselves, the act of perceiving). Repeat the same four Kālīs for this sphere.
7. Then turn to the **subject** (the sense of "I am perceiving"). Repeat the same four Kālīs for this sphere.
8. What remains after the 12th Kālī (Mahābhairava) has rendered the subject inexplicable is **pure consciousness without an object**.

**Tantraloka commentary** (lines 415-421): The 12th Kālī is Mahābhairava — the ultimate form of Bhairava, who is consciousness itself. When all objectivity, all means of knowing, and all subjectivity have been consumed, what remains is Bhairava.

**Signs of progress**: The 12 phases become perceptible as distinct shifts in the quality of consciousness. The "inexplicable" phase (Yama, Mārtaṇḍa, Mahābhairava) is accompanied by a cessation of mental activity that is different from dullness — it is vivid emptiness.

## Grade 4: Spontaneous Samādhi (Sahaja)

### Practice 4.1: Kewala Kumbhaka — The Breathless State

**Source**: Layayoga Ch.6 (lines 5100-5110); Tantraloka Āhnika 7 (Spanda)

> "Kewala is that form of breath-suspension in which there is no inspiration and no expiration, but only automatic breath-suspension."

**What it does**: Sahita prāṇāyāma (the 1:4:2 ratio) is preparation. Kewala kumbhaka is the goal — a natural, effortless suspension of breath that arises spontaneously when the nāḍīs are purified and prāṇa enters suṣumṇā. It cannot be forced. It must be allowed.

**Practice**:
1. This practice begins when sahita prāṇāyāma has been established for at least 6-8 weeks.
2. Perform 5 rounds of sahita prāṇāyāma as usual.
3. After the 5th exhalation, do NOT inhale immediately. Wait.
4. The breath suspension that arises on its own — **that** is kewala. Not a held breath, but a breath that has no need to move.
5. Remain in this suspension for as long as it lasts naturally. Do not extend it by effort.
6. When the urge to breathe arises, take a slow, gentle inhalation and rest.

**Source confirmation** (Layayoga Ch.2, citing Yogashikhopanishad 1.134-5, lines 2090-2100):

> "When one is established in Laya-yoga, there is the union between the embodied spirit and the Supreme Spirit; and because of this, consciousness becomes completely absorbed and along with it the cessation of respiration takes place."

**Signs of progress**: Kewala arises spontaneously and extends incrementally — 10 seconds, then 30, then 1 minute. During the suspension, the breath-stillness is accompanied by mental-stillness. Thoughts do not arise because the prāṇic movement that drives them has ceased.

---

### Practice 4.2: Śāmbhavopāya — The Divine Means

**Source**: Tantraloka Āhnika 1, verses 171-196 (lines 390-400)

> "Śāmbhavopāya is the means pertaining to Śiva — realization through will without thought-constructs."

**What it does**: All previous practices are Āṇavopāya (individual means — using breath, mantra, concentration) or Śāktopāya (empowered means — using purified thought). Śāmbhavopāya dispenses with all means. It is direct recognition: consciousness recognizing itself without any intermediary.

**Practice** (this is not a technique but a shift in understanding — only to be attempted after the lower grades are stabilized):
1. In the midst of daily activity, suddenly ask: "Who is aware of this?"
2. Do not answer with words. Turn attention toward the awareness itself.
3. In that turning, there is a gap — a moment where the object of attention is absent and only awareness remains.
4. Abide in that gap.
5. When thought arises again, repeat.

**Tantraloka commentary** (Āhnika 1, verse 196): Śāmbhavopāya is like a "lion's leap" — it bypasses all gradual process and goes directly to the goal. It is not a practice but a **recognition** that the practice was never necessary — the goal was already present.

**Warning**: Do not attempt this as a substitute for the lower grades. The lower grades purify the nāḍīs and stabilize the mind. Without that foundation, the "lion's leap" is likely to be a leap into the void of confusion rather than the void of liberation.

## The Complete Daily Practice (90 minutes)

This is the full sadhana incorporating all grades. It should take about 90 minutes and can be done in the morning.

| Phase | Practice | Duration | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1. Āsana** | Sit in stillness | 5 min | Layayoga Ch.1 |
| **2. Prāṇāyāma** | Sahita with A-U-M, 10 rounds | 15 min | Layayoga Ch.6; Tantraloka Āhnika 3 |
| **3. Mātṛkā breath** | 5 rounds of the 50-letter breath | 10 min | Layayoga Ch.6 |
| **4. Bhūta-śuddhi** | Element absorption | 5 min | Layayoga Ch.2, 5 |
| **5. SAUḤ** | 11 rounds + 1 aloud | 5 min | Tantraloka Āhnika 4 |
| **6. KHPHREṀ** | 5 rounds | 5 min | Tantraloka Āhnika 4 |
| **7. Twelve Kālīs** | Absorption of objectivity/means/subjectivity | 15 min | Tantraloka Āhnika 4 |
| **8. Kewala** | Allow spontaneous breath suspension | Up to 20 min | Layayoga Ch.6 |
| **9. Śāmbhava** | Direct recognition | 5 min | Tantraloka Āhnika 1 |
| **10. Rest** | Lie down, no technique | 5 min | — |
