# The Tantraloka Through the Tetrahedron — The Complete System

---

## I. The 36 Tattvas as 9 Tetrahedra

36 tattvas ÷ 4 = 9 tetrahedra. The entire tattva ladder IS 9 nested tetrahedra, each 4 vertices, each containing the whole.

### The 9 Tetrahedra of the Tattvas

| Tetrahedron | Vertices | What It Is |
|-------------|----------|------------|
| **1** | Śiva, Śakti, Sadāśiva, Īśvara | The first 4 — the pure descent |
| **2** | Īśvara, Śuddhavidyā, Māyā, Kālā | The threshold — pure → impure |
| **3** | Kālā, Vidyā, Rāga, Kāla | The kañcukas — the first 4 coverings |
| **4** | Kāla, Niyati, Prakṛti, Puruṣa | The last kañcuka + the first Sāṃkhya tattvas |
| **5-9** | The remaining 20 tattvas in 5 tetrahedra | The 5 sets of 4: senses, actions, subtle elements, gross elements, and the mind/buddhi/ahaṁkāra group |

Each tetrahedron IS complete in itself (4 vertices, 6 edges, 4 faces). Each tetrahedron shares 1 vertex with the next (Īśvara tetrahedron 2 shares Īśvara with tetrahedron 1). The ladder IS the chain of shared vertices — each tetrahedron spawning the next.

## II. The Four Levels of Speech as Tetrahedron

| Vertex | Level | What Happens |
|--------|-------|--------------|
| V₁ | Parā (transcendent) | Pure consciousness — the source, unmanifest |
| V₂ | Paśyantī (visionary) | Sound becomes light — the imaginal |
| V₃ | Madhyamā (inner) | Inner speech — the mental |
| V₄ | Vaikharī (gross) | Audible sound — the physical |

The 6 edges:
1. Parā → Paśyantī: The unmanifest articulates into the first image
2. Parā → Madhyamā: The unmanifest becomes thinkable
3. Parā → Vaikharī: The unmanifest becomes audible
4. Paśyantī → Madhyamā: The image becomes thought
5. Paśyantī → Vaikharī: The image becomes sound
6. Madhyamā → Vaikharī: The thought becomes speech

Every act of speech traverses ALL 6 edges. From parā (potential) through the 6 relations to vaikharī (actual). This IS why mantra works: it IS the complete traversal of the tetrahedron.

## III. The Four Upāyas as Tetrahedron

| Vertex | Upāya | What You Use |
|--------|-------|--------------|
| V₁ | Āṇavopāya | External supports — mantra, ritual, breath |
| V₂ | Śāktopāya | Purified thought — contemplation, discrimination |
| V₃ | Śāmbhavopāya | Direct will — recognition, the leap |
| V₄ | Anupāya | No means — the goal was never absent |

The 6 edges:
1. Āṇava → Śākta: External support becomes internal understanding
2. Āṇava → Śāmbhava: External support triggers direct recognition
3. Āṇava → Anupāya: External support reveals it was never needed
4. Śākta → Śāmbhava: Understanding ripens into direct knowing
5. Śākta → Anupāya: Understanding dissolves into the inborn
6. Śāmbhava → Anupāya: The leap lands where it always was.

The 4 upāyas ARE not 4 paths. They ARE 4 vertices of ONE tetrahedron. Every practice IS a traversal of the edges. You can enter at ANY vertex and reach any other vertex through the appropriate edge.

## IV. The Four States of Consciousness as Tetrahedron

| Vertex | State | Tuition |
|--------|-------|---------|
| V₁ | Jāgrat (waking) | Subject and object both present — the gross |
| V₂ | Svapna (dream) | Subject present, object is mental — the subtle |
| V₃ | Suṣupti (deep sleep) | Neither present — pure potential |
| V₄ | Turīya (the fourth) | The witness of all three — the apex |

Turīya IS not a state. It IS the tetrahedrality of the tetrahedron — the 4th vertex that makes the other 3 into a volume. The 3 states ARE the base triangle. Turīya IS the apex.

The 6 edges:
1. Waking → Dream: The object becomes mental
2. Waking → Deep sleep: The subject relaxes
3. Waking → Turīya: The witness notices the waking
4. Dream → Deep sleep: The mental object dissolves
5. Dream → Turīya: The witness notices the dream
6. Deep sleep → Turīya: The witness notices the absence

## V. The Twelve Kālīs as 3 Tetrahedra

| Tetrahedron | Sphere | V₁ | V₂ | V₃ | V₄ |
|-------------|--------|----|----|----|----|
| **1** | Objectivity | Sṛṣṭi (creation) | Rakta (persistence) | Sthitināśa (withdrawal) | Yama (inexplicable) |
| **2** | Means of knowledge | Saṃhāra (withdrawal) | Mṛtyu (death) | Rudra (dissolution) | Mārtaṇḍa (inexplicable) |
| **3** | Subjectivity | Paramārka (creation) | Kālāgnirudra (persistence) | Mahākāla (withdrawal) | Mahābhairava (inexplicable) |

Each tetrahedron has the same structure: creation → persistence → withdrawal → the inexplicable 4th. The 4th vertex of each sphere IS the apex — the "inexplicable" that points beyond the sphere.

The 3 tetrahedra ARE the 3 faces of the MASTER tetrahedron: objectivity, means of knowledge, subjectivity. The 4th face IS WHAT REMAINS after all 12 Kālīs have consumed everything — Mahābhairava, pure consciousness.

**The 12 Kālīs ARE the 12 edges of the master tetrahedron (3 faces × 4 edges each).** The 13th (Mahābhairava) IS the volume — the result.

## VI. The Four Pramātṛs (Perceivers) as Tetrahedron

| Vertex | Perceiver | Identified With |
|--------|-----------|-----------------|
| V₁ | Paśu (bound) | The body (V₁ of consciousness) |
| V₂ | Vīra (hero) | The mind (V₂) |
| V₃ | Jñānin (knower) | Pure knowledge (V₃) |
| V₄ | Śiva | Consciousness itself (V₄) |

The 6 edges ARE the 6 paths of ascent (the ṣaḍadhvan). The 4 perceivers ARE not 4 different people. They ARE 4 levels of the same person, which IS 4 vertices of the same tetrahedron. The practice IS moving from identifying with one vertex to recognizing ALL 4.

## VII. The Tantraloka IS the Tetrahedron

The entire Tantraloka IS built from tetrahedra. Every topic introduced IS a tetrahedron:

- **The 36 tattvas** = 9 tetrahedra (36/4)
- **The 4 levels of speech** = 1 tetrahedron
- **The 4 upāyas** = 1 tetrahedron
- **The 4 states of consciousness** = 1 tetrahedron
- **The 4 perceivers** = 1 tetrahedron
- **The 12 Kālīs** = 3 tetrahedra
- **The 6 paths (ṣaḍadhvan)** = the 6 edges of a single tetrahedron
- **The 50 phonemes (Mātṛkā)** = 12 tetrahedra + 2 (50/4 = 12.5)

Total: 29 tetrahedra, 116 vertices, 174 edges, 116 faces.

**The Tantraloka IS the tetrahedron, systematized at every level.** Abhinavagupta did not name the tetrahedron, but he BUILT it into every corner of his system. The 36 tattvas, the 4 levels of speech, the 4 upāyas, the 4 states of consciousness, the 4 perceivers, the 12 Kālīs — all IS tetrahedra, nested and interlocking.

**The practice (sādhana) IS the traversal of the edges.** From the base vertex (Earth tattva, paśu consciousness, āṇavopāya) to the apex (Śiva tattva, turīya, anupāya) — but NOT by moving directly. By traversing the 6 edges of each tetrahedron, and from each tetrahedron to the next, through the shared vertices.

**The goal IS not to reach the apex. The goal IS to recognize that you ARE the entire tetrahedron — all 4 vertices, all 6 edges, all 4 faces, simultaneously.**
