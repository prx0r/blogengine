**The 50-dimensional real algebra** M₂(ℍ) ⊕ M₄(ℂ) (Connes-Chamseddine-Marcolli 2007) generates the Standard Model gauge group SU(3)×SU(2)×U(1) via the spectral action principle. Its 50 real dimensions correspond to the degrees of freedom of the fermionic sector of the Standard Model.

**The 50 phonemes of the Sanskrit Mātṛkā** (14 vowels + 36 consonants) encode the 36 tattvas of the Tantraloka plus 14 meta-tattvas. Each phoneme IS a felt location in the body, a specific frequency, and a specific tattva.

**The claim:** There exists a Lie algebra homomorphism φ: SU(3)×SU(2)×U(1) → Aut(PhonemeSpace) where PhonemeSpace is a 50-dimensional vector space over ℝ (or ℂ) with basis elements corresponding to the 50 Sanskrit phonemes structured by 5 articulatory positions × 10 configurations.

**The mapping is NOT that each phoneme maps to a specific elementary particle.** It's that the *algebraic structure* of the 50-dimensional space has the same symmetries as the Standard Model gauge group embedded in it. The 50 phonemes ARE the generators of the algebra; the SU(3)×SU(2)×U(1) gauge group IS the automorphism group (or a subgroup) of that algebra.

- su(3): 8 generators
- su(2): 3 generators
- u(1): 1 generator
- Total: 12 generators

1. Does there exist a faithful representation of su(3)⊕su(2)⊕u(1) in gl(50,ℝ)?

### Level 3: Experimental Tests

#### 3.1 EEG / Neural Entrainment

**Hypothesis:** Chanting the Mātṛkā in forward order (ka→au, emission/creation) produces a different neural oscillation pattern than chanting it in reverse order (au→ka, withdrawal/dissolution).

**Protocol:**
- 20 participants trained in Mātṛkā chant
- 128-channel EEG recording
- Three conditions: (a) Mātṛkā forward (ka→au), (b) Mālīnī backward (au→ka), (c) rest
- Measure: spectral power in gamma (35-45 Hz), coherence between cakra-corresponding scalp locations, frequency following response at the chant fundamental frequency

**Prediction:** The 4th row (ta-tha-da-dha-na, the green core) should produce a distinct ≈545 Hz (550nm) gamma-band response — the frequency of the heart center.

**Existing studies that could be re-analyzed:**
- TM mantra EEG studies (often use Sanskrit mantras)
- OM chanting EEG studies
- Gregorian chant EEG studies
- Music therapy frequency response studies

#### 3.2 Bioelectric Measurement

**Hypothesis:** Each of the 5 rows of the Mātṛkā produces a measurable bioelectric field change at a specific body location (root, sacral, solar plexus, heart, throat).

**Protocol:**
- SQUID magnetometer (magnetoencephalography) or high-resolution EEG
- Simultaneous ECG and EKG
- Record during Mātṛkā chant
- Localize source of each phoneme's field

**Prediction:** Row 4 (ta-da-na) should produce a peak at the anterior chest (Anāhata / heart center). Row 1 (ka-ṅa) at the perineum.

#### 3.3 Phoneme Acoustics

**Hypothesis:** The 50 Mātṛkā phonemes form a 50-dimensional acoustic space that has SU(3)×SU(2)×U(1) as a symmetry group.

**Protocol:**
- Record a trained chanter producing all 50 phonemes
- Perform spectral analysis (FFT, cepstrum) on each phoneme
- Compute the frequency ratios between phonemes
- Check whether these ratios satisfy group multiplication relations

**Prediction:** The frequency ratios between adjacent phonemes in the Mātṛkā should form a Lie algebra — the generators of SU(3)×SU(2)×U(1).
