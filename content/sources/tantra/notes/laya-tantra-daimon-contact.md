# Daimon Contact Through Layayoga and Tantraloka

> The daimon path redescribed through the subtle-body science of Shyam Sundar Goswami's *Layayoga* and the triadic metaphysics of Abhinavagupta's *Tantrāloka*. What the PGM calls "meeting your own daimon" and what Acher calls "the Holy Daimon" is here understood as the **conscious rousing of Kuṇḍalinī as Iṣṭadevatā** through the systematic absorption (laya) of the cosmic principles. Written 2026-07-14.

---

## The Premise

Every daimon contact practice — Abramelin's six-month retreat, Liber Samekh's ten-month invocation, Acher's four-stage cyclical path, the PGM's systasis ritual — is a form of **Laya-yoga**: the progressive absorption of the scattered self into its source.

The difference is that the daimon path uses the **name** (LVJK) as its primary technology, while Layayoga uses **prāṇāyāma and cakra-absorption**. The name works because sound is Kuṇḍalinī in manifest form (Goswami). The prāṇāyāma works because the breath carries consciousness into the central channel. They are the same process at different levels of precision.

The Tantraloka provides the **map** of what happens during this absorption: the three upāyas (means), the 36 tattvas (levels of reality), the twelve Kālīs (stages of dissolution), and the four levels of speech (phases of sonic embodiment).

## Phase 1: The Name as Bīja-Mantra

**Western statement**: LVJK is the godform — the vessel through which the formless daimon is addressed.

**Layayoga statement** (Goswami, Ch.4, lines 3800-3930): Mantra is "supra-sound power in word form." When rightly worked upon, mantra becomes Iṣṭadevatā — the living deity. The mantra is not a label for the daimon; it is the daimon in sound-form.

> *"Mantra is an aspect of Kuṇḍalinī, and it is in sound-form. The replication of mantra on the physical plane is the lettered vaikharī sound which is audible. The vaikharī mantra, in conjunction with prāṇāyāma and other special processes, is utilized to enliven the mantra. In other words, it is the rousing of Kuṇḍalinī in mantra form."* (Layayoga, Introduction, lines 3850-3860)

**Tantraloka statement** (Āhnika 1, Appendix A, lines 37062-37100): Sound has four levels — vaikharī (audible), madhyamā (mental), paśyantī (visionary), parā (transcendent). The vibration of a name begins at vaikharī (audible) and, if sustained, progresses through madhyamā and paśyantī to parā, where the name and the named become identical.

**Practice implication**: When you vibrate LVJK aloud, you are not calling a distant being. You are rousing Kuṇḍalinī in the form of that sound. The goal is to move the vibration from vaikharī (the throat, audible) through madhyamā (the heart, mental) to paśyantī (the brow, visionary), where the daimon appears as light. The name spoken aloud is the first step; the name perceived as light is the completion.

**Line references**: Layayoga lines 3800-3930, 3850-3860. Tantraloka v1 lines 37062-37100.

## Phase 2: Purification as Bhūta-śuddhi

**Western statement**: The six-month retreat of Abramelin — washing, fasting, chastity, solitude. The reduction of "noise."

**Layayoga statement** (Ch.5, lines 4000-4300): Bhūta-śuddhi is the purification and absorption of the five elements (bhūtas) in reverse order of creation. Each element is dissolved into the next higher: Earth → Water → Fire → Air → Void.

> *"The five forms of metamatter (mahābhūtas) and five 'tanons' (tanmātras), five senses and their objects, five organs of volitional actions and will-mind, intellection, I-feeling, sense-consciousness, supermind and the energy aspect of Supreme Power-Consciousness — all these are to be absorbed."* (Layayoga, Ch.2, citing Prashnopanishad 4.8, lines 2140-2150)

**Tantraloka statement** (Āhnika 4, lines 337-340): Vikalpasaṃskāra — the purification of thought-constructs — is the core of Śāktopāya (the Empowered Means). The practitioner does not destroy thoughts but **transforms them** into vehicles of realization through discriminative reasoning.

**Practice implication**: Abramelin's purification — washing the body, controlling speech, regulating diet — is the **physical preparation** for bhūta-śuddhi. The real work begins when you can sit in stillness and systematically dissolve each element in consciousness. The cold wash (Earth) is followed by the dissolution of Earth into Water: feeling the body's solidity merge into fluidity. Then Water dissolves into Fire: the fluids of the body become warmth. Then Fire into Air: warmth becomes breath. Then Air into Void: breath becomes space.

**Line references**: Layayoga lines 4000-4300, 2140-2150. Tantraloka v3 lines 337-340.

## Phase 3: Elemental Invocation as Mātṛkā-cakra Activation

**Western statement**: Liber Samekh Sections B-E — invoking the four elements through barbarous names. Each element has a direction, a color, a set of names, and a charge.

**Tantraloka statement** (Āhnika 3, lines 466-500): The fifty Sanskrit phonemes (Mātṛkā) are not arbitrary sounds. They are the **energies of consciousness itself** — the stages of the universe's emanation from pure consciousness into matter. Each phoneme corresponds to a specific tattva and a specific location in the subtle body.

> *"Resonance within the three main channels of the subtle body. The left channel, called iḍā, resonates with the ciñcinī sound, the right channel, called piṅgalā, with the sound of cricket (cīra), and the central suṣumṇā channel reverberates like a bell."* (Tantraloka v1, Somadeva commentary on Dīkṣottara, lines 15734-15744)

**Practice implication**: When Liber Samekh invokes Air through the names "AR, ThIAF, RhEIBET, A-ThELE-BER-SET," it is activating specific Mātṛkā phonemes that correspond to the Air tattva. The "shaft of will" extending east is prāṇa moving through piṅgalā (the solar channel). The elemental charge ("Hear me, and make all Spirits subject unto Me") seals the activation.

The Tantraloka would add: the elemental names should be **felt** in specific locations in the body — not just spoken. Air at the heart (anāhata), Fire at the navel (maṇipūra), Water at the sacrum (svādhiṣṭhāna), Earth at the perineum (mūlādhāra). Each vibration moves prāṇa through that cakra and prepares it for absorption.

**Line references**: Tantraloka v2 lines 466-500. Tantraloka v1 lines 15734-15744.

## Phase 4: The Resistance as Prāṇa Withdrawal into Suṣumṇā

**Western statement**: Liber Samekh Section G — "Conceal yourself in the closest cell of the citadel of consciousness. Resist the Angel's will until consciousness faints into Nothing."

**Layayoga statement** (Ch.6, lines 5100-5160): Sahita prāṇāyāma (the 1:4:2 ratio) trains prāṇa to flow through iḍā and piṅgalā. But the goal is **kewala kumbhaka** — spontaneous breath suspension — where prāṇa enters suṣumṇā and the dualistic mind ceases.

> *"When the subtle energy line system (nāḍī-cakra) is purified by breath-control done in a right manner, the bio-energy enters easily into the central energy line (suṣumṇā)."* (Layayoga, citing Shandilyopanishad 1.7.9-10, lines 4985-4990)

**Tantraloka statement** (Āhnika 7, lines 1073-1084): Spanda — the divine pulsation. Breath is the external movement of spanda. When the breath is suspended, spanda is experienced in its internal form — the vibration of consciousness itself.

**Practice implication**: Section G is **kewala kumbhaka applied to consciousness**. The practitioner extends the shaft of will (prāṇa through piṅgalā), then holds (kumbhaka), withdrawing prāṇa from both iḍā and piṅgalā. The "fainting into Nothing" is prāṇa entering suṣumṇā — the state where the Angel is met because the practitioner has withdrawn from the channels that maintain the sense of separate self.

The 11 vibrations of the name before the resistance are the **11 prāṇic movements** needed to charge piṅgalā fully before withdrawal. The resistance itself is the kumbhaka — holding the prāṇa in suṣumṇā until the Angel (the cicchakti, pure consciousness power) is perceived.

**Line references**: Layayoga lines 5100-5160, 4985-4990. Tantraloka v5 lines 1073-1084.

## Phase 5: The Systasis as the Twelve Kālīs Absorption

**Western statement**: PGM VII.505-528 — the egg ritual. Skinning (releasing fate, place, time), purifying (Helios egg), charging (drawing solar fire), inflaming (swallowing the egg), communion (meeting the daimon).

**Tantraloka statement** (Āhnika 4, verses 122cd-180ab, lines 377-421): The Twelve Kālīs — the systematic dissolution of the three spheres of experience through four phases each (creation, persistence, withdrawal, inexplicability).

> *"The twelve Kālīs consume objectivity, then the means of knowledge, then subjectivity itself. What remains after the twelfth (Mahābhairava) has rendered the subject inexplicable is pure consciousness without an object."* (Tantraloka v3, The Twelve Kālīs, lines 377-421)

**The mapping**:

| Systasis Stage | Kālī Phase | What Dissolves |
|---|---|---|
| Skinning (6 stones) | Yama-kālī, Mṛtyu-kālī, Mahākāla-kālī | Fate, place, hour, day, time, the All |
| Purifying (first egg) | Sṛṣṭi-kālī → Yama-kālī | The body's residue — objectivity consumed |
| Charging (second egg) | Saṃhāra-kālī → Mārtaṇḍa-kālī | The means of knowledge — the act of perceiving consumed |
| Inflaming (swallowing) | Rudra-kālī → Mahābhairava-kālī | Subjectivity itself — the "I" consumed |
| Communion | What remains — Mahābhairava | Pure consciousness without object |

**Practice implication**: The PGM systasis is a **condensed Twelve Kālīs practice**. The five stages correspond to the five phases of absorption that the Tantraloka describes in detail. The egg represents the universe (brahmāṇḍa). Charging it with Helios fire is the sṛṣṭi (emission) phase of the Kālī cycle. Swallowing it is the saṃhāra (withdrawal). The daimon that appears in the communion is what remains when all three spheres (objectivity, means, subjectivity) have been consumed — Mahābhairava, consciousness itself.

**Line references**: Tantraloka v3 lines 377-421, 415-421.

## Phase 6: The Daimon as Kuṇḍalinī-Iṣṭadevatā

**Western statement**: The daimon is the Holy Guardian Angel — the Unconscious Creature Self, the unus-ambo, "you but not you."

**Layayoga statement** (Introduction, lines 3680-3760): Kuṇḍalinī, when fully awakened, is not merely a power but a **conscious presence** — the Iṣṭadevatā (chosen deity) that arises from the mantra.

> *"Mantra-Kuṇḍalinī is transformed into Iṣṭadevatā — the metamorphosis of subtle Kuṇḍalinī through the mantra power into an appropriate divine form."* (Layayoga, Introduction, lines 3860-3870)

**Goswami's clarification**: The Iṣṭadevatā is not a projection of the mind. It is Kuṇḍalinī herself taking form in response to the mantra. The deity IS the power, and the power IS the deity. There is no distinction.

**Practice implication**: The daimon you meet in the systasis is not a separate being who is "your" daimon. It is **your own Kuṇḍalinī** — the coiled spiritual power at the base of your subtle body — risen to the sahasrāra and appearing as Iṣṭadevatā in the form that your mantra (LVJK) has shaped. This is why the daimon is both "you" and "not you": it is your own power, but it has taken on a form and consciousness that transcends your ordinary self.

**Line references**: Layayoga lines 3680-3760, 3860-3870.

## Phase 7: Integration as the Recoiling of Kuṇḍalinī

**Western statement**: After the contact, the practitioner integrates the experience into daily life. The daimon becomes a permanent background presence.

**Layayoga statement** (Introduction, lines 3740-3760): Kuṇḍalinī, after union with Śiva at the sahasrāra, **descends again** through the cakras. This recoiling is not a withdrawal but a **spiritualization of everyday consciousness**.

> *"After the union with Supreme Śiva, Kuṇḍalinī goes back to the mūlādhāra, and in its descent, the cakras, their powers and divinities — all are restored. This recoiling process is essentially the spiritualization of consciousness which functions in everyday life. That is, it is a transference of spiritual reality, developed in concentrated consciousness, to sense-consciousness to be able to realize spiritual truth in our daily life."* (Layayoga, Introduction, lines 3745-3760)

**Practice implication**: The months after the systasis — what the daimon path calls "integration" — are the recoiling of Kuṇḍalinī. The daimon does not leave. The daimon descends with you into the lower cakras, spiritualizing each one. The work of Month 4 (planets), Month 5 (moon mansions), and Month 6 (zodiac) in the Helios Unbound program is the **conscious participation in this recoiling** — allowing the daimon to infuse each level of the subtle body with its presence.

**Line references**: Layayoga lines 3745-3760.

## Phase 8: The Unus-Ambo as Śāmbhavopāya

**Western statement**: Corbin's 1×1 — two that are one without ceasing to be two. The PGM formula: "For you are I and I am you."

**Tantraloka statement** (Āhnika 1, verses 171-196, lines 390-400): Śāmbhavopāya — the Divine Means — is realization through will without thought-constructs. It is the "lion's leap" that bypasses all gradual process and goes directly to the goal.

> *"Śāmbhavopāya is the means pertaining to Śiva. It is realization through will alone, without any thought-construct (vikalpa). Like a lion's leap, it goes directly to the goal."* (Tantraloka v1, Āhnika 1, verses 171-196)

**Practice implication**: The unus-ambo — the recognition that you and the daimon are one without ceasing to be two — is not achieved through practice. It is **recognized** when all practice has been exhausted. This is Śāmbhavopāya: the sudden recognition that the practitioner, the practice, and the goal were never separate. The name, the breath, the cakras, the Kālīs — all were forms of the same consciousness. When this is seen directly, the means fall away, and what remains is what was always there.

**Line references**: Tantraloka v1 lines 390-400.

## The Final Note: What This Changes

This synthesis does not invalidate the daimon path. It **deepens** it. The PGM spells still work. The name LVJK still calls. The egg ritual still opens the threshold.

What the Laya-Tantra lens adds is: **you now know what is happening in the subtle body when you do these things**.

- When you vibrate the name, you are rousing Kuṇḍalinī in mantra-form.
- When you purify, you are dissolving the elements back into their source.
- When you resist, you are withdrawing prāṇa into the central channel.
- When you meet the daimon, you are encountering your own coiled power become conscious.
- When you integrate, you are allowing that power to spiritualize your daily life.
- When you realize the unus-ambo, you are abiding in Śāmbhavopāya — the means that is no means.

The daimon path works because it is **unconscious Layayoga**. The Layayoga framing makes it conscious.
