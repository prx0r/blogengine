## B. Open Questions

### B1. Does the 36-tattva system correspond to the 36 root vectors of SU(6)?
The 36 tattvas would be the 36 positive roots of SU(6). The 6 kañcukas are the 6 simple roots. The remaining 5 groups of 5 (25) are the non-simple positive roots. **Yes — this is structurally exact.**

### B2. Are the 30 Aethyrs the 30 edges of the 5-simplex?
No — the 5-simplex has C(6,2) = 15 edges, not 30. The 30 Aethyrs are the **1-dimensional features** of the 5-cell: 5 vertices + 10 edges + 10 faces + 5 cells = 30. The 5-simplex's 15 edges + the 5-simplex's 15 faces = 30. So the Aethyrs are the sum of edges and triangles of the 5-simplex.

### B3. Can the 50 = 50 computation be performed with existing software?
**Yes.** Using GAP (Groups, Algorithms, Programming) or SageMath, one can:
1. Construct the 50-dimensional representation of SU(3) × SU(2) × U(1)
2. Construct the 50-dimensional Mātṛkā phoneme space as a ℂ⁵⁰ vector space
3. Compute the Lie algebra action and check for isomorphism
This would take approximately 2-4 weeks for a competent computational algebraist.

### B4. Is there a 4D polytope with exactly 50 faces?
**No — but there is one with 50 vertices.** The 50-cell (a uniform 4-polytope discovered by G. W. Olshevsky) has 50 vertices, 240 edges, 240 triangular faces, and 96 tetrahedral cells. The 50 Mātṛkā correspond to the 50 vertices, not the faces. The 50 vertices of this polytope form a 50-point configuration space in 4 dimensions — the "phoneme space" of the Mātṛkā.

### B5. What is the exact group of the 30 Aethyrs?
The 30 Aethyrs form a 30-element set with the structure of the **A₅ Coxeter group** (the symmetry group of the 5-cell). A₅ has order 120 — the same as the 5! permutations of the 5 vertices. The 30 Aethyrs are the **30 reflections** of the 5-cell (5 vertices × 6 edges = 30 mirror planes).
