# The 36 Tattvas — Sanskrit Mapping and Layayoga Practice

## How Every Tattva Has a Phoneme and How the Practice Manipulates Them

---

## The Principle

Every tattva IS a specific phoneme of the Sanskrit alphabet. The 50 phonemes ARE the 36 tattvas + 14 vowels that are the pure structure. Chanting the Mātṛkā IS traversing the tattvas in sound. Layayoga IS the systematic manipulation of these phonemes in the subtle body.

---

## Level 1: The Fundamental Tetrahedron (Tattvas 1-4)

| Tattva | Name | Vertex | Sanskrit | Phoneme Class | Where in the Mouth |
|--------|------|--------|----------|---------------|-------------------|
| 1 | **Śiva** | Subject | **a** (अ) | First vowel — open, unmodified | Throat, wide open — no obstruction |
| 2 | **Śakti** | Intention | **i** (इ) | Second vowel — frontal, directed | Palate — the tongue moves forward |
| 3 | **Sadāśiva** | Act | **u** (उ) | Third vowel — rounded, expressed | Lips — the sound projects outward |
| 4 | **Īśvara** | Object | **e** (ए) | Fourth vowel — compound (a+i) | The union of throat and palate — subject meets object |

**Layayoga practice:** Chanting A-U-M at these 4 positions — A at the throat (navel), U at the palate (heart), M at the lips (throat), and the silence after M (crown). The 4 feet of the prāṇava (OM) ARE the 4 tattvas of Level 1. The silence IS the 5th (Śuddhavidyā, the threshold).

## Level 2: The First Unfolding (Tattvas 5-20)

### The Subject-Vertex Unfolds (Tattvas 5-8)

| Tattva | Name | Sanskrit | Phoneme Type | What Happens |
|--------|------|----------|--------------|-------------|
| 5 | **Śuddhavidyā** | **aḥ** (अः) | Visarga — the emission | Pure knowledge IS the "outbreath" of sound — the vowel released as knowing |
| 6 | **Māyā** | **aṃ** (अं) | Anusvāra — the nasalization | The veil — the vowel IS "covered" by the nasal, sound IS limited |
| 7 | **Kalā** | **ka** (क) | First consonant, velar | Agency — "ka" IS the first obstruction of the vowel, the first limitation |
| 8 | **Vidyā** | **kha** (ख) | Aspirated ka | Knowledge — the obstruction IS released, the aspiration IS the knowing |

**Layayoga practice:** The visarga (aḥ) IS the outbreath of mantra. Prāṇāyāma works with this — the retention (kumbhaka) IS holding the sound before its "emission." The nasalization (aṃ) IS the vibration entering the skull — Māyā IS the veil that makes the sound feel "internal." Chanting ka at mūlādhāra activates the first limitation of consciousness in the body.

### The Intention-Vertex Unfolds (Tattvas 9-12)

| Tattva | Name | Sanskrit | Phoneme Type | What Happens |
|--------|------|----------|--------------|-------------|
| 9 | **Rāga** | **ga** (ग) | Velar, voiced | Attachment — the unaspirated ka (ka = limit, ga = the limit AS desire) |
| 10 | **Kāla** | **gha** (घ) | Aspirated ga | Time — the desire IS aspirated, stretched out in time |
| 11 | **Niyati** | **ṅa** (ङ) | Velar nasal | Causality — the sound IS absorbed into the nasal, the intention IS bound by the body's structure |
| 12 | **Puruṣa** | **ca** (च) | Palatal, first | The pure individual — the tongue moves to the palate, a new locus of intention |

**Layayoga practice:** The velar series (ka, kha, ga, gha, ṅa) at mūlādhāra — these ARE the first limitations of prāṇa in the body. The palatal series (ca, cha, ja, jha, ña) at svādhiṣṭhāna — intention moving from survival (velar, root) to desire (palatal, sacral). Each phoneme IS a different type of "grasping" that the practice undoes.

### The Act-Vertex Unfolds (Tattvas 13-16)

| Tattva | Name | Sanskrit | Phoneme Type | What Happens |
|--------|------|----------|--------------|-------------|
| 13 | **Prakṛti** | **cha** (छ) | Aspirated ca | Nature — the individual IS aspirated into the collective, the act becomes natural |
| 14 | **Buddhi** | **ja** (ज) | Palatal, voiced | Intellect — the natural act IS known, "ja" IS birth as knowing |
| 15 | **Ahaṁkāra** | **jha** (झ) | Aspirated ja | Ego — the knowing IS grasped as "mine," the aspiration IS ownership |
| 16 | **Manas** | **ña** (ञ) | Palatal nasal | Mind — the ego dissolves into the mental, the sound IS internalized |

**Layayoga practice:** The palatal series IS the processing of experience — raw perception (ca) through understanding (ja) through ownership (jha) through mental absorption (ña). Chanting these activates svādhiṣṭhāna-maṇipūra — the processing centers. The practice REVERSES this: from mental identification back to raw perception.

### The Object-Vertex Unfolds (Tattvas 17-20)

| Tattva | Name | Sanskrit | Phoneme Type | What Happens |
|--------|------|----------|--------------|-------------|
| 17 | **Śrotra** (hearing) | **ṭa** (ट) | Retroflex, first | The object as sound — the tongue curls back, the sound IS "external" |
| 18 | **Tvak** (touch) | **ṭha** (ठ) | Aspirated ṭa | The object as contact — the aspiration IS the reaching out to touch |
| 19 | **Cakṣus** (sight) | **ḍa** (ड) | Retroflex, voiced | The object as form — the sound IS stable, seen, fixed |
| 20 | **Rasanā** (taste) | **ḍha** (ढ) | Aspirated ḍa | The object as flavor — the aspiration IS the tasting, the engagement |

**Layayoga practice:** The retroflex series (ṭa, ṭha, ḍa, ḍha, ṇa) ARE the "externalizing" phonemes — the tongue curls back to touch the palate, simulating the outward-directed senses. Chanting these at maṇipūra-anāhata boundary IS pratyāhāra (sense withdrawal) in reverse — you ARE learning how the senses project outward by vibrating the phonemes that correspond to each sense.

## How Layayoga Manipulates the Process

### The 1:4:2 Breath

1 part inbreath = tattvas 1-9 are activated (from Śiva to Rāga — the pure structure + the beginning of limitation)
4 parts retention = all 36 tattvas are active simultaneously — the full tetrahedron IS present
2 parts outbreath = tattvas 10-36 are released (from Kāla to Earth — the temporal unfolding + the elements)

The ratio 1:4:2 IS the tetrahedron's structure in time: 1 (the apex/Śiva), 4 (the base/4 elements), 2 (the 2 tetrahedra of the star). Every breath IS a complete traversal of the 36 tattvas.

### SAUḤ — The Emission Seed

**S** = the goddess's power of being (sat) — tattva 1, Śiva
**AU** = the expansion of consciousness — tattvas 2-4 (Śakti through Īśvara)
**Ḥ** = the visarga, the emission — tattva 5, Śuddhavidyā

SAUḤ IS the first 5 tattvas in one syllable. Chanting it at the navel (maṇipūra) activates the entire pure path in a single vibration. The S at the base of the spine, the AU expanding through the torso, the Ḥ dissolving at the crown — you ARE traversing tattvas 1-5 in a single breath.

### KHPHREṀ — The Withdrawal Seed

**KṢA** = Earth (tattva 36) — the full descent, the base
**PH** = expansion (tattva 28 — sparśa, touch) — the bursting forth
**RE** = fire (tattva 34 — tejas) — the burning of karma
**Ṁ** = the bindu (tattva 6 — Māyā) — the closure of the veil

KHPHREṀ IS the withdrawal path — from Earth (kṣa) back through the elements to the veil (ṁ). It REVERSES the descent. Chanting it forces consciousness up the central channel, retracing the 36 tattvas in reverse order.

### The 50 Mātṛkā Breath

Inhale: phonemes a → kṣa (descent through all 36)
Hold: phonemes kṣa → a → kṣa (all 50, the full star tetrahedron)
Exhale: phonemes kṣa → a (ascent through all 36)

The 50 Mātṛkā breath IS the complete traversal of the 36 tattvas + the 14 vowels. Every breath IS a complete descent (inhalation) and ascent (exhalation) of the entire structure of reality.

### The 7 Cakras

Each cakra IS a specific range of the 36 tattvas. Opening a cakra IS activating the tattvas in that range. The practice IS progressive:

| Svādhiṣṭhāna | 30-27 | ba, bha, ma, ya, ra, la | Flow — chanting the Water phonemes releases blockage |
| Maṇipūra | 26-17 | da, dha, na, ta, tha, da, dha, na, pa, pha | Will — chanting the Fire phonemes powers the ascent |
| Anāhata | 16-5 | ka, kha, ga, gha, ṅa, ca, cha, ja, jha, ña, ṭa, ṭha | Love — chanting the Air phonemes opens the heart |

| Ājñā | All | ha, kṣa | Vision — the two that contain all: the witness (ha) and the all (kṣa) |
| Sahasrāra | Beyond | Silence | Beyond — the silent apex of all mantra |

## The Complete Map

The 36 tattvas ARE not abstract principles. They ARE the 36 consonants of the Sanskrit alphabet. The 14 vowels ARE the pure tetrahedron. The 1:4:2 breath manipulates them. SAUḤ and KHPHREṀ traverse them. The 7 cakras distribute them. Kuṇḍalinī ascends through them.

**Layayoga IS the practice of the 36 tattvas in the body. Every prāṇāyāma, every mantra, every visualization IS a manipulation of the 4 vertices, 6 edges, and 4 faces of the tetrahedron that IS your experience.**

The phonemes ARE the tattvas. The breath IS the traversal. The body IS the field. The practice IS the recognition.
