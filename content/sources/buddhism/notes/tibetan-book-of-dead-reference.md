# Tibetan Book of the Dead Reference Guide

> *The Great Liberation by Hearing in the Intermediate States (Bar-do thos-grol chen-mo)*. Translated by Gyurme Dorje, edited by Graham Coleman with Thupten Jinpa. Penguin Classics edition. Written 2026-07-14.

---

## Overview

The Tibetan Book of the Dead is a cycle of Nyingma Buddhist terma (treasure) texts revealed by Karma Lingpa (14th c.), originally concealed by Padmasambhava in the 8th century. It belongs to the *Zab-chos zhi-khro dgongs-pa rang-grol* (Peaceful and Wrathful Deities: Natural Liberation through Recognition of Enlightened Intention) cycle.

**Core purpose**: A guide through the three intermediate states (bardos) between death and rebirth, read aloud to the deceased to facilitate liberation through recognition.

**Root text**: Guhyagarbha Tantra (6th c.) — first work to portray the 42 peaceful and 58 wrathful deities as the purified forms of ordinary psycho-physical states.

---

## The Six Bardos (Intermediate States)

| Bardo | Tibetan | When | What Happens |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1. Living | *skye gnas bar-do* | Lifetime | Ordinary waking consciousness |
| 2. Dream | *rmi lam bar-do* | Sleep | Dreaming consciousness |
| 3. Meditative | *bsam gtan bar-do* | Meditation | Concentrated awareness |
| 4. Death | *'chi kha'i bar-do* | Time of death | Element dissolution → Inner Radiance |
| 5. Reality | *chos nyid bar-do* | ~14 days after death | Peaceful & Wrathful deity visions |
| 6. Rebirth | *srid pa'i bar-do* | Until ~49 days | Judgment, realm selection, womb entry |

## The Three Death Bardos in Detail

### Bardo 1: Chikhai Bardo — Time of Death

**Element dissolution sequence** (Ch. 11, Part 1):

| Element | External Sign | Internal Experience | Conceptual Patterns Ceased |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Earth** dissolves into **Water** | Body heavy, skin sags | Feeling of sinking | — |
| **Water** dissolves into **Fire** | Throat dry, saliva flows | Oscillating clarity | — |
| **Fire** dissolves into **Wind** | Eyes roll up, warmth converges | Turbulence, mirage visions | — |
| **Wind** dissolves into **Space** | Breath wheezes, limbs quiver | — | — |
| **Redness (increase)** | All appears red | — | 40 attachment-based patterns cease |
| **Whiteness (attainment)** | All appears white | — | 33 aversion-based patterns cease |
| **Blackness (near-attainment)** | All appears black | Swoon | 7 delusion-based patterns cease |
| **Inner Radiance (clear light)** | — | Mother radiance dawns | All 80 patterns ceased |

**If recognized = Liberation into Dharmakaya**. If missed → Chonyid Bardo.

### Bardo 2: Chonyid Bardo — Reality

**Days 1-7: 42 Peaceful Deities**

| Day | Buddha Couple | Direction | Light | Wisdom | Temptation Realm |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Vairocana** + Akasadhatvisvari | Center | Blue | Reality's expanse | God realm |
| 2 | **Aksobhya-Vajrasattva** + Buddhalocana | East | White | Mirror-like | Hell |
| 3 | **Ratnasambhava** + Mamaki | South | Yellow | Sameness | Human |
| 4 | **Amitabha** + Pandaravasini | West | Red | Discernment | Antigod |
| 5 | **Amoghasiddhi** + Samayatara | North | Green | Accomplishment | Animal |
| 6 | All five together | All | Multicolor | Combined | All six realms |
| 7 | Awareness Holders + Dakinis | — | Rainbow | All cognitions | Animal |

**Days 8-14: 58 Wrathful Deities**

| Day | Heruka Couple | Peaceful Counterpart | Direction |
|---|---|---|---|
| 8 | **Buddha Heruka** + Buddhakrodhesvari | Vairocana | Center |
| 9 | **Vajra Heruka** + Vajrakrodhesvari | Aksobhya | East |
| 10 | **Ratna Heruka** + Ratnakrodhesvari | Ratnasambhava | South |
| 11 | **Padma Heruka** + Padmakrodhesvari | Amitabha | West |
| 12 | **Karma Heruka** + Karmakrodhesvari | Amoghasiddhi | North |

Plus: 8 Matarah, 8 Pisaci (animal-headed), 4 Gatekeepers, 28 Isvari

**Key recognition teaching**: These terrifying forms are "the buddha-body of your own intrinsic awareness" — the peaceful deities appearing in wrathful form because the mind, having failed to recognize them peacefully, now experiences them as terrifying.

### Bardo 3: Sidpa Bardo — Rebirth

**Features:**
- **Mental body** — subtle body of karmic propensities, invisible, can pass through matter
- **Yama's judgment** — mirror of past actions, counting of white/black pebbles
- **Six lights** — deceased drawn toward the realm matching their karma:
  - Dull white = god realm (pride)
  - Dull red = antigod (envy)
  - Dull blue = human (desire)
  - Dull green = animal (delusion)
  - Dull yellow = hungry ghost (greed)
  - Dull smoky = hell (anger)
- **Womb entrance** — vision of copulating parents; attraction/aversion determines gender
- **Five methods of obstructing the womb** if liberation is sought
- **Choosing a favorable womb** if obstruction fails

## Three Kayas (Buddha Bodies) and Their Bardo Correlations

| Buddha Body | Nature | Realized In | Result |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Dharmakaya** | Formless, empty, radiant | Chikhai Bardo (Inner Radiance) | Ultimate Buddhahood |
| **Sambhogakaya** | Luminous, immaterial forms | Chonyid Bardo (deity visions) | Rebirth in pure land |
| **Nirmanakaya** | Physical emanations | Sidpa Bardo (controlled rebirth) | Continued practice |

---

## Consciousness Transference (Phowa)

Four modes:
1. Into **Dharmakaya** — highest practitioners, direct realization
2. Into **Sambhogakaya** — intermediate, rebirth in pure buddha field
3. Into **Nirmanakaya** — controlled human rebirth
4. **Ordinary beings** — avoidance of lower realms

---

## Judgment Mechanism

- **Two internal witnesses**: innate good/bad conscience counting virtues/non-virtues
- **Yama Dharmaraja** — not a punishing deity but a reflection of one's own karma
- **Mirror of past actions** — every deed reflected vividly
- **Key insight**: Yama and acolytes are "natural forms of emptiness" — recognizing this = liberation

## Comparative Parallels

| System | Chikhai Bardo | Chonyid Bardo | Sidpa Bardo |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Steiner** | Etheric body detachment | Kamaloca (purification, life review) | Spiritual world → new birth |
| **Egyptian** | First hours of night | Judgment Hall of Osiris | Field of Reeds / new existence |
| **Tantraloka** | Initiation for the dying | Soul's journey through bhuvanas | Funerary rites, ancestral offerings |
| **Daimon path** | The Threshold approached | The Resistance (Section G) | Integration / new life orientation |

**The deepest convergence**: All systems agree that what the soul encounters after death is a **reflection of what it has become during life**. The deities, judges, gates, and hells are not external places but **the soul's own content rendered as experience**.
