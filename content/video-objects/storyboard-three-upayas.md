## SEG 1 — Title + Hook (0:00-1:00)

**Visual:** GC17 (Three upāyas as mountain paths — a mountain with three paths to the summit, winding trail with stations, series of doors, straight line up a sheer cliff)
  → S2 (Seeker sits in meditation — cross-legged, upright, chest rising and falling)

**Narration:**
"If you have ever tried to meditate, you know that some days it works and some days it doesn't. Some practices feel natural. Others feel like fighting your own mind. The Tantric tradition recognized this. They said there are not one but three fundamental paths to liberation — and the right one depends on who you are."

**Audio:** Low ambient drone throughout. No music.

**Clip: GC17** (30s) → **S2** (15s) → still art (15s over GC17 held frame)

*LTX clips used: GC17, S2*

## SEG 2 — Three Paths, One Mountain (1:00-2:30)

**Visual:** GC17 continues — camera slowly orbits the mountain, following each path
  → D (Quote card: "Three upāyas, one samāveśa — immersion into divine Reality")

**Narration:**
"The mountain is a central metaphor in Abhinavagupta's Tantrāloka. Three paths lead to the same summit. The first is winding, with stations along the way — postures, fire offerings, mantras placed on the body, breath exercises. The second is a series of doors, each needing a key — a false belief unlocked by correct view, a trauma by forgiveness, a limitation by recognition. The third is a straight line up a sheer cliff — an invisible guide pulling you up without effort. All three arrive at the same place."

**Audio:** Bell tone at quote card.

*LTX clips used: GC17*

## SEG 3 — Śiva, Śakti, Nara — The Three Correspondences (2:30-4:00)

**Visual:** AC2 (Śiva becomes Śakti — crimson thread spirals outward from gold point, weaving around it)
  → AT7 (Geometric form constructs itself — triangle, tetrahedron, rotating gently)

**Narration:**
"The three upāyas correspond to three aspects of reality. Śāmbhavopāya — the divine means — corresponds to Śiva, pure awareness itself. Śāktopāya — the empowered means — corresponds to Śakti, the energy of consciousness. Āṇavopāya — the embodied means — corresponds to Nara, the individual, the embodied self. These are not three different paths chosen at random. They are the path of spirit, the path of energy, and the path of body — and every human being contains all three."

**Audio:** Quiet.

*LTX clips used: AC2, AT7*

## SEG 4 — Āṇavopāya — The Embodied Means (4:00-5:30)

**Visual:** BR21 (Shadowed guru: Yoga teacher — figure in lotus under a tree, silhouette against sky, utterly still)
  → S5 (Seeker kneels before light — head bowed, light washes over them)
  → AT1 (Fire burns slowly — amber and gold flames, embers floating upward)

**Narration:**
"Āṇavopāya means 'the individual means.' It is practice for the embodied self — for those who need to work with what is tangible. This is yoga as it is commonly understood: postures, breath control, mantra repetition, ritual, nyāsa — placing mantras on the body. The āṇava practitioner works with the physical body and the subtle body. They use their hands, their breath, their voice. They do things. And through doing, they transform."

**Audio:** Subtle fire crackle during AT1 (LTX T2A generated).

*LTX clips used: BR21, S5, AT1*

## SEG 5 — Āṇavopāya in Practice — Body, Breath, Nyāsa (5:30-7:00)

**Visual:** BR3 (Shadowed guru: Kashmir Shaiva teacher — seated in stone cave, oil lamp at side, face in shadow)
  → AT5 (Dust particles in light — shaft of warm light, particles drift and catch the light)

**Narration:**
"The practices of āṇavopāya are many. Haṭha yoga. Prāṇāyāma — working with the breath. Japa — repetition of mantra. Nyāsa — the placement of divine energy in the body through touch and visualization. Homa — fire offerings. The body itself becomes the instrument of liberation. Every cell, every breath, every sound is a vehicle. For the practitioner who feels disconnected from the subtle, this is where you start — with what you can feel, touch, and do."

**Audio:** Silence.

*LTX clips used: BR3, AT5*

## SEG 6 — Śāktopāya — The Empowered Means (7:00-8:30)

**Visual:** AC13 (Golden thread — two points in darkness, a thin gold thread extends from left to right, both pulse in unison)
  → YS9 (Teacher surrounded by students — luminous figure at center, dark figures in semicircle, threads of light extending to each)

**Narration:**
"Śāktopāya — the empowered means — works with the energy of thought itself. This path is for those who can work with their mind directly. Where āṇavopāya works with the body, śāktopāya works with vikalpa — mental constructs, beliefs, the stories we tell ourselves. The practitioner does not suppress thoughts. They engage with them. They examine a limiting belief, see through it, and replace it with a view that is more aligned with reality. This is not positive thinking. This is cognitive alchemy."

**Audio:** Quiet.

*LTX clips used: AC13, YS9*

---

## SEG 7 — Śāktopāya in Practice — Undermining False Constructs (8:30-10:00)

**Visual:** YS3 (Guru and disciple — two figures facing each other in darkness, thin gold thread from teacher's chest to student's chest, student's chest begins to glow)
  → TR16 (Guru points, student sees — guru points upward, student sees nothing; guru points at student's chest, chest glows, guru gone)

**Narration:**
"The work of śāktopāya is to undermine false or disempowering mental constructs by cultivating views that are profoundly empowering because they are aligned with the nature of things. This is the path of study, contemplation, and dialogue with a teacher. The teacher does not give you answers. They show you where to look. And when you see — not as a concept, but as a direct recognition — the construct dissolves. This work naturally leads to the ability to dissolve mental constructs altogether."

**Audio:** Bell tone at the moment of recognition in TR16.

*LTX clips used: YS3, TR16*

## SEG 8 — Śāmbhavopāya — The Divine Means (10:00-11:30)

**Visual:** AC14 (Ladder of being — seven platforms in darkness, a point of light ascends through each, revealing luminous space within)
  → AC5 (Liberation expands — small dim point at center, rings of gold light emanate outward, center becomes light itself)
  → S4 (Seeker walks forward through darkness — slow, deliberate, toward unseen light)

**Narration:**
"Śāmbhavopāya — the divine means — is the path of no practice. This is not for everyone. But for those who are ready, it is the most direct. There is no method. There is no technique. There is only recognition — an immediate, non-conceptual intuition of one's own nature as divine consciousness. The practitioner learns to open to grace, to rest in non-conceptual awareness of their inner state, to internalize the essence of mantra without words. In śāmbhavopāya, you do not seek liberation. You recognize you were never bound."

**Audio:** Long pause after "you recognize you were never bound."

*LTX clips used: AC14, AC5, S4*

## SEG 9 — The Hierarchy Is Descriptive, Not Prescriptive (11:30-13:00)

**Visual:** GC17 returns (Three mountain paths — camera now at the summit, looking down at all three paths converging)
  → S2 (Seeker sits — calm, centered)

**Narration:**
"It is easy to hear 'divine means' and think it is better than 'embodied means.' But the tradition is clear: the hierarchy is descriptive, not prescriptive. The divine means is not superior — it is simply what works for those who can receive it. The embodied means is not inferior — it is essential for those who need it. Most practitioners, in fact, need all three. No spiritual path can be complete that emphasizes one upāya to the exclusion of the other two."

**Audio:** Silence.

*LTX clips used: GC17, S2*

---

## SEG 10 — The Three Malas — Why We Need All Three (13:00-14:30)

**Visual:** AC15 (Threshold — simple archway floating in darkness, figure approaches from dark side, pauses, steps through into gold light)
  → YS5 (Death and rebirth — dark form on ground cracks along geometric lines, gold light emerges, form breaks apart and reassembles more luminous)

**Narration:**
"The three upāyas correspond to three kinds of impurity — mala. Āṇavamala — the impurity of individuality, the sense of being a separate self. Māyīyamala — the impurity of limitation, the veils of māyā. Kārmamala — the impurity of action, the residue of past doings. Each upāya addresses a different mala. Āṇavopāya purifies the body and its karma. Śāktopāya dissolves the limitations of māyā. Śāmbhavopāya reveals the individual self was never separate. All three malas must be addressed."

**Audio:** Quiet.

*LTX clips used: AC15, YS5*

---

## SEG 11 — Samāveśa — The Common Goal (14:30-16:00)

**Visual:** AC5 returns (Liberation expands — now held at full brightness, the entire frame is golden light)
  → AT7 (Geometric form — tetrahedron now fully luminous, each face transparent, light shining through from within)

**Narration:**
"All three upāyas lead to the same goal: samāveśa — continuous immersion in divine reality. As Abhinavagupta says, the nectar of blissful self-awareness floods and overflows the internal dams that divide us, dissolving all distinctions. Then you become an undivided self — a mass of blissfully self-aware consciousness, spontaneously responding with the whole of your being to the whole of each moment. Whether you arrived through the winding path, the doors, or the cliff — you are here."

**Audio:** Long pause. Silence.

*LTX clips used: AC5, AT7*

---

## SEG 12 — Closing (16:00-18:00)

**Visual:** S5 (Seeker kneels before light — brief, 8s)
  → D (Quote card: "Do what works, and do everything that works.")
  → BR3 (Shadowed guru: Kashmir Shaiva teacher — returns, the oil flame flickers once, the cave wall is rough stone, the teacher does not move)

**Narration:**
"The root teaching of the upāyas is simple: do what works, and do everything that works. Upāya means 'skillful means' — situationally sensitive methodology. A given practice is right for a certain person at a certain stage. And it ceases to be right when the stage passes. If you are fighting your mind, try a different path. The mountain has three routes. They all lead to the summit. And the summit is not a place. It is what you have always been."

**Audio:** Bell tone at quote card. Long fade on cave image. End.

*LTX clips used: S5, BR3*
