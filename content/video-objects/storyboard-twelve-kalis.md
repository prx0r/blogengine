## SEG 2 — Time Is Not a Line (1:00-2:30)

**Visual:** TR9 (Knot of time unties — figure wrapped in three ropes, luminous hand touches the knot, it dissolves)
  → S3 (Seeker looks up)

**Narration:**
"We normally think of time as a line — past, present, future — one thing after another. The Krama system of Kashmir Shaivism says something radically different. Time is not a container in which things happen. Time is a power of consciousness. It is the energy by which experience unfolds. And that power has a name and a face: Kālī. Not a goddess of destruction. A goddess of consumption — time as the force that consumes every experience so that a new one can arise."

**Audio:** Single bell. Quiet.

*LTX clips used: TR9, S3*

---

## SEG 3 — The Krama System — A Different Approach (2:30-4:00)

**Visual:** GC5 (Wheel of twelve Kalis — the triple ring, outer ring earthy tones, middle ring with eyes, inner ring radiant)
  → GC14 (Wheel of powers — great wheel turning, hub white, spokes will/knowledge/action)

**Narration:**
"The Krama system is the esoteric heart of the Trika. Where the Trika gives you three goddesses, the Krama gives you twelve — twelve Kalis arranged in three groups of four. Each group corresponds to a domain of experience: objectivity, the means of knowledge, and subjectivity. And each group has the same structure: creation, persistence, withdrawal, and a mysterious fourth state that transcends the other three. The twelve Kalis are not beings. They are the stages of every moment of perception."

**Audio:** Quiet drone.

*LTX clips used: GC5, GC14*

---

## SEG 4 — The Four Kalis of Objectivity (4:00-5:30)

**Visual:** GC5 (Wheel — outer ring with earthy tones, the first four goddess-figures)
  → AC3 (36 tattvas descending — the lower tattvas in shadow, the objective world)

**Narration:**
"The first four Kalis consume the sphere of objectivity — everything that appears to you as an object. Sṛṣṭikālī — the Kali of Creation — emanates objectivity into being. Raktakālī — the Red Kali — makes it persist, gives it duration. Sthitināśakālī — the Kali of the Destruction of Persistence — withdraws the object from perception. And Yamakālī — the Kali of Restriction — marks the inexplicable state where the object is gone but not yet replaced. Every object you perceive goes through these four phases in every moment of perception."

**Audio:** Soft pulse at each name.

*LTX clips used: GC5, AC3*

---

## SEG 5 — Sṛṣṭikālī — Creation (5:30-7:00)

**Visual:** GC6 (SAUḤ emanation — egg S cracks, AU expands into tree of light, H scatters into stars)
  → AC1 (Emanation — gold light flowing downward)
  → K1 (Abhinavagupta's cave)

**Narration:**
"Sṛṣṭikālī is the Kali of Creation. She is the power of consciousness to project objectivity — to make something appear as if outside of awareness. Abhinavagupta says: 'This Goddess, who is pure and unsullied by any form, becomes eager to manifest objectivity in this way, and she ideates and brings about emanation.' She is not creating something from nothing. She is making apparent something that was always latent in consciousness. Every object you see is Sṛṣṭikālī at work."

**Audio:** Expansion sound (subtle, LTX T2A).

*LTX clips used: GC6, AC1, K1*

---

## SEG 6 — Raktakālī — Persistence (7:00-8:30)

**Visual:** AC4 (Māyā contracts — grey rings closing inward, dimming the figure)
  → AT4 (Stars pulse — steady, persistent)

**Narration:**
"Raktakālī — the Red Kali — is the power of persistence. Once an object appears, it must be maintained. Raktakālī is the energy that holds your attention on something, that makes a perception last more than a flash. She is the redness of life, the flow of experience continuing. But persistence is not permanence. Raktakālī knows that what persists will also pass. She holds the object in awareness — and she holds it knowing it will be released."

**Audio:** Subtle heart pulse. Quiet.

*LTX clips used: AC4, AT4*

---

## SEG 7 — Sthitināśakālī — Destruction of Persistence (8:30-10:00)

**Visual:** AC10 (World dissolves — landscape breaks into particles, drifting upward, settling into stillness)
  → AT1 (Fire burns slowly — amber and gold, steady consumption)

**Narration:**
"Sthitināśakālī — the Kali of the Destruction of Persistence. She is the power of withdrawal — the dissolution of the object back into consciousness. Every perception fades. Every thought ends. Every moment passes. Sthitināśakālī is not destruction in the violent sense. She is the natural release, the healthy end of an experience. Without her, you would be stuck in the first moment forever. She is the power that allows you to let go. And in letting go, to be free for the next moment."

**Audio:** Soft dissolve — long exhale (LTX T2A). Silence.

*LTX clips used: AC10, AT1*

---

## SEG 8 — Yamakālī — The Inexplicable Fourth (10:00-11:30)

**Visual:** AC5 (Liberation expands — rings of gold light emanating, filling the frame)
  → S3 (Seeker looks up)

**Narration:**
"Yamakālī — the Kali of Restriction or the Inexplicable. She is the fourth state, the gap between the end of one perception and the beginning of the next. She is restriction because nothing is appearing — awareness is empty, waiting. She is inexplicable because this gap cannot be described. It is pure subjectivity without an object. In the Krama system, this fourth state is the most important. It is the taste of awareness itself, object-free. To recognize Yamakālī is to recognize consciousness without content."

**Audio:** Long pause. Silence.

*LTX clips used: AC5, S3*

---

## SEG 9 — The Four Kalis of Means of Knowledge (11:30-13:00)

**Visual:** GC5 (Wheel — middle ring with eyes and mantras, the second group of four)
  → AC3 (36 tattvas — the middle tattvas lighting up)
  → S2 (Seeker sits)

**Narration:**
"The second group of four Kalis consumes the sphere of the means of knowledge — the cognitive processes themselves. Saṁhārakālī — withdrawal, the emergence of the means of knowledge. Mṛtyukālī — death, the persistence of the cognitive process. Rudrakālī — the fierce, the withdrawal of cognition. Mārtaṇḍakālī — the Kali of the Sun, the inexplicable state of the means of knowledge. The Sun represents the extroverted form of the subject — you, looking out at the world. These Kalis consume the act of perception itself."

**Audio:** Bell at each name.

*LTX clips used: GC5, AC3, S2*

---

## SEG 10 — The Four Kalis of Subjectivity (13:00-14:30)

**Visual:** GC5 (Wheel — inner ring, radiant white-gold, the final Kali with terrifying beautiful face surrounded by flames)
  → TR6 (Thousand faces of the divine — shifting rapidly, male, female, animal, star)
  → BR1 (Kashmir mountain mist)

**Narration:**
"The third and innermost group consumes subjectivity itself — the sense of self. Paramārkakālī — the Kali of the Supreme Sun — the emanation of the subject. Kālāgnirudrakālī — the Kali of Rudra the Fire of Time — the persistence of the subject. Mahākālakālī — the Kali of Great Time — the withdrawal of the subject. And Mahābhairavaghoracaṇḍakālī — the Horrific and Fierce Kali of the Great Bhairava — the inexplicable state of the subject. This is the Kali that consumes the sense of 'I.' And when the 'I' is consumed, what remains is pure awareness."

**Audio:** Soft flame at the final Kali. Silence.

*LTX clips used: GC5, TR6, BR1*

---

## SEG 11 — SAUḤ and KHPHREṀ — The Seed Mantras (14:30-16:00)

**Visual:** GC6 (SAUḤ emanation — the seed mantra in full display)
  → GC7 (KHPHREṀ withdrawal — cosmos drawn inward)
  → AT1 (Fire burns)

**Narration:**
"The twelve Kalis are accessed through their seed mantras. SAUḤ is the seed of Parā, the goddess of emanation. Its three letters — S, AU, H — span the entire spectrum of reality, from the first stirring of consciousness to its final emission into objectivity. KHPHREṀ is the seed of Kālasaṁkarṣiṇī, the goddess of the Krama, the seed of withdrawal. Fire burns through form. Vibration remains. The sky of consciousness opens. The field of the three powers — will, knowledge, action — condenses to a point. The point vanishes. Mantra meditation on the Kalis is not about reciting syllables. It is about becoming the Kalis themselves."

**Audio:** Fire at KHPHREṀ. Bell. Silence.

*LTX clips used: GC6, GC7, AT1*

---

## SEG 12 — Closing (16:00-17:00)

**Visual:** GC5 (Wheel of twelve Kalis — full wheel, the three rings rotating, then gradually slowing, dissolving)
  → D (Quote card: "Time is not a line. Time is a goddess with twelve faces.")
  → AT4 (Stars pulse in dark sky, long fade)

**Narration:**
"The twelve Kalis are not a theology. They are a description of your experience, right now. Every perception is their wheel turning. Every moment contains creation, persistence, withdrawal, and the inexplicable. Recognize them, and time is no longer a line that traps you. It is a goddess whose face you can meet in every experience. Iti śivam."

**Audio:** Bell. Stars pulse. Fade to silence. End.

*LTX clips used: GC5, AT4*
