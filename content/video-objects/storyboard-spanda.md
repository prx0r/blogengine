It is not a vibration of something. It is vibration itself — consciousness as a throb."

## SEG 2 — What Is Spanda? (1:00-3:00)

**Visual:** AC6 (Spanda pulses — return to this as the recurring motif, 10s)
  → S2 (Seeker sits in meditation — the inner witness, 15s)
  → D (Quote card: "Spanda is not a vibration of something. It is vibration itself.")

**Narration:**
"The Sanskrit word spanda comes from the root spandi — to throb, to pulsate, to vibrate. But the Kashmiri masters were not talking about physical vibration. Kallata, the 9th-century author of the Spanda Karika, defined spanda as the innate pulsation of one's own true nature — svarupa-spanda. It is the universal pulsation of consciousness, the substrate of all experience. Think of it this way: when you are aware of a thought, there is something in you that makes awareness possible. When you feel an emotion, that same something is present. Spanda is that something — the pulse of awareness itself."

**Audio:** Quiet. Subtle breath at the pause before quote card.

*LTX clips used: AC6, S2*

## SEG 3 — The Wheel of Powers (3:00-4:30)

**Visual:** GC14 (Wheel of Powers — hub of white light, spokes of Will/Knowledge/Action, wheel turns slowly, 25s)

**Narration:**
"The Spanda Karika opens with a magnificent image: the Wheel of Powers — shakti-chakra. Imagine a wheel. At its center, a steady unmoving point of white light — this is pure awareness, the hub. From it radiate three spokes: Will, Knowledge, and Action. These are not separate from the hub — they ARE the hub in motion. Kallata writes: 'We praise the Beneficent Source of the all-pervasive activity of the Wheel of Powers, the expansion of which is the arising of the world and the contraction of which is its dissolution.' The entire universe of your experience is the wheel turning."

**Audio:** Low drone continues. No bells.

*LTX clips used: GC14*

## SEG 4 — Expansion and Contraction (4:30-6:00)

**Visual:** GC14 continues (wheel expands, galaxies appear on rim — then contracts, all multiplicity collapses back into hub, 20s)
  → AC1 (One emanating — point of gold light sends three streams downward, 15s)

**Narration:**
"Expansion — unmesha — is the creation of the world of your experience. Every moment, consciousness expands outward into forms: thoughts, perceptions, sensations, the entire cosmos. Contraction — nimesha — is the dissolution of that world. When you fall asleep, the world contracts. When you die, it contracts completely. But here is the secret: both are the same motion. The wheel does not choose to expand or contract. It simply turns. Your true nature is the hub, not the rim. The hub does not move. It watches the wheel turn."

**Audio:** Subtle expansion sound (LTX T2A generated) at the expansion moment.

*LTX clips used: GC14, AC1*

## SEG 5 — Every Pulse Contains Both (6:00-7:30)

**Visual:** AC6 (Spanda pulses — now we see the pulse differently: each expansion is immediately followed by contraction, 15s)
  → AC2 (Shiva-Becomes-Shakti — gold point with crimson thread spiraling outward, 15s)

**Narration:**
"Here is the most profound insight of the Spanda teaching: every pulse contains both expansion and contraction. They are not two movements — they are one movement with two phases. When you take a breath, the inhale is unmesha, the exhale is nimesha. When you have a thought, its arising is expansion, its passing is contraction. When you feel an emotion — love, anger, grief — it expands into your awareness, then contracts back into the depths. The pulse of spanda is happening constantly. You just haven't learned to feel it yet."

**Audio:** Single slow breath — inhale and exhale — timed to the narration.

*LTX clips used: AC6, AC2*

## SEG 6 — The Flaming Heart (7:30-9:00)

**Visual:** GC15 (Flaming Heart — human chest opens, heart-flame pulses, expanding to universe then contracting to point, 20s)

**Narration:**
"The Tantric masters located the seat of spanda in the heart — not the physical pump, but the hridaya, the 'heart of consciousness.' Abhinavagupta called this the flaming heart of awareness. Picture your chest opening to reveal a heart that is simultaneously a flame. With each pulse, the flame expands to encompass the entire universe. With each contraction, it returns to a single point. This heart-flame is both personal and universal. It is your heart. It is also the heart of all existence. The pulse you feel in the deepest part of yourself — that is the same pulse that moves the galaxies."

**Audio:** Deep resonant tone. Long pause at the end.

*LTX clips used: GC15*

## SEG 7 — Sleep and Waking as Natural Spanda (9:00-10:30)

**Visual:** GC16 (Sleep and waking — split screen: left side sleeping person, world fading; right side dream-world expanding, 20s)
  → UT2 (Moon in water — reflection ripples, settles, 10s)

**Narration:**
"Kallata says something remarkable: even in sleep, even in dreams, the wheel is turning. When you fall asleep, the world of your waking experience dissolves — a nimesha. When you dream, a new world arises — an unmesha. When you wake, the dream dissolves and the day-world arises. You do this every single night. You create and dissolve entire universes. This is not a metaphor. The same power that brings the cosmos into being is the power that brings your dreams into being. Spanda is not something that happens to you. It is something you ARE."

**Audio:** Soft, dreamy. Fading in and out.

*LTX clips used: GC16, UT2*

## SEG 8 — Spanda in Everyday Experience (10:30-12:00)

**Visual:** S3 (Seeker looks upward — particles drift down, 12s)
  → AT5 (Dust particles in light — contemplative, 20s)

**Narration:**
"So how do you feel spanda in everyday life? Start with the breath. Notice the moment where inhale becomes exhale — that pause, that turning point. That is spanda. Notice a thought arising, lingering, dissolving. The space between thoughts — that is spanda. Notice a sound — a bell, a bird, the hum of a fan. The awareness in which the sound appears and vanishes — that is spanda. Kallata says that spanda is not an object of perception. It is that in which all perception occurs. You cannot grasp it. But you can recognize it. And once you do, the whole world begins to pulse with you."

**Audio:** Subtle bell tone at "a bell, a bird, the hum of a fan." Silence otherwise.

*LTX clips used: S3, AT5*

## SEG 9 — Spanda and Mantra (12:00-13:30)

**Visual:** K1 (Abhinavagupta's cave — manuscripts, oil lamp, 15s)
  → D (Quote card: "Mantras, attaining that innate power, become suffused with Divine force." — Spanda Karika 2.26)

**Narration:**
"The Second Flow of the Spanda Karika reveals the secret of how mantras work. Kallata says mantras become powerful not because of correct pronunciation or ritual purity, but because they access the innate power of spanda itself. When a mantra is awakened — when it becomes alive in your awareness — it is because it has attuned itself to the universal pulse. The mantra vibrates not on your lips but in the very substrate of consciousness. This is what the tradition calls mantra-chaitanya — the awakening of the mantra. It is not the practitioner who makes the mantra powerful. It is spanda itself, expressing through the practitioner."

**Audio:** Soft OM drone, barely audible, throughout the segment.

*LTX clips used: K1*

## SEG 10 — The Third Flow — Sovereign Power (13:30-15:00)

**Visual:** AC5 (Liberation expands — small dim point emanates rings of gold, fills frame, 20s)
  → S6 (Seeker dissolves into light — 15s)

**Narration:**
"The Third Flow describes the experience of the awakened being — one who has recognized spanda as their own nature. For such a being, Kallata says, even desire becomes a divine power. 'The One who ordains all things,' he writes, 'when entreated with an impulse of pure desire, causes the moon and sun to rise and bestows those goals residing in the heart.' This is not magic. It is the natural functioning of consciousness when it no longer identifies with limitation. When you know yourself as the pulse of the universe, your will becomes the universe's will. Your desire becomes creative power. Not through force — through alignment."

**Audio:** Rising ambient warmth. A moment of silence.

*LTX clips used: AC5, S6*

## SEG 11 — Spanda Across Traditions (15:00-16:30)

**Visual:** TR15 (Breath of the universe — vast form breathing, seeker within the breath, expanding and contracting, 25s)
  → AT4 (Stars pulse in dark sky — 10s)

**Narration:**
"This pulse is not unique to Kashmir Shaivism. The Sufis speak of nafas al-rahman — the breath of the All-Merciful. The Taoists speak of the Tao that cannot be named but that moves all things. Heraclitus said the universe is flux. Modern physics speaks of quantum fields vibrating at the base of matter. But the Spanda Karika makes a claim that none of these do: that this pulse is not just a property of the universe — it is what you are. Not what you have. Not what you experience. What you ARE. The pulsation of consciousness is your own true nature."

**Audio:** Ambient drone. Stars pulsing.

*LTX clips used: TR15, AT4*

## SEG 12 — Closing — Recognizing the Pulse (16:30-18:00)

**Visual:** S2 (Seeker sits in meditation — 10s)
  → AC6 (Spanda pulses — one final pulse, slow and deep, 15s)
  → D (Quote card: "iti śivam — may it be for a blessing")
  → AT2 (Incense smoke spirals upward, 20s — end)

**Narration:**
"Close your eyes for a moment. Feel the breath moving. Feel the thoughts arising and dissolving. Feel the awareness in which all of this happens. That awareness is not still — it is pulsing. It is the spanda. You have always felt it. You just called it something else — life, consciousness, presence. The Tantric masters gave it a name so you could recognize it. Once recognized, it never leaves you. Because it IS you. Iti śivam — may it be for a blessing."

**Audio:** Bell tone at "iti śivam." Long fade on incense smoke. End.

*LTX clips used: S2, AC6, AT2*
