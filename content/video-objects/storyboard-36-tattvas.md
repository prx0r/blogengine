## SEG 1 — Title + Hook (0:00-1:00)

**Visual:** K1 (Abhinavagupta's cave — slow push-in toward manuscripts on stone shelves, oil lamp flickering)
  → AT7 (Geometric form constructs itself — a golden tetrahedron draws itself line by line, rotates gently)

**Narration:**
"You can observe almost anything. The movement of your breath. The thoughts in your mind. The stars in the sky. But there is one thing you cannot observe: the point from which observation happens. The Kashmir Śaiva masters called this point Śiva. And they mapped everything that unfolds from it — 36 categories, from pure consciousness to the earth beneath your feet."

**Audio:** Low ambient drone throughout. No music.

**Clip: K1** (15s) → **AT7** (20s) → still art (25s over K1 image)

*LTX clips used: K1, AT7*

## SEG 2 — Sāṅkhya vs Trika — 24 vs 36 (1:00-2:30)

**Visual:** D (Quote card: "36 tattvas is the entrance point into the teachings of Kashmir Shaivism. — Swami Lakshmanjoo")
  → Y1 (Himalayan ashram at sunrise — Sāṅkhya world establishing)

**Narration:**
"Swami Lakshmanjoo, one of the last living masters of the Kashmir Śaiva lineage, said that the 36 tattvas are the entrance point into these teachings. If we understand the process of manifestation, we understand everything about our nature. We understand all limitations. We understand how we are at once this individual and this godhead. But to understand the 36, we must first understand the 24 — the system they built upon."

**Audio:** Bell tone at quote card.

*LTX clips used: Y1*

## SEG 3 — Sāṅkhya Foundation — Puruṣa, Prakṛti, Three Guṇas (2:30-4:00)

**Visual:** AC1 (The One emanating — gold light flows downward from a point at top center in three streams)
  → D (Quote card — three guṇas appear one by one: Sattva, Rajas, Tamas)

**Narration:**
"Sāṅkhya philosophy is dualistic. It posits two fundamental realities: puruṣa — eternal spirit, consciousness — and prakṛti — eternal matter, nature. Prakṛti performs all its actions through three powers called guṇas. Sattva — equilibrium, light, knowledge. Rajas — motion, passion, force. Tamas — inertia, structure, ignorance. These three rotate constantly — in the seasons, in your body, in your mind. Yoga and Āyurveda are built on this foundation."

**Audio:** Ambient quiet.

*LTX clips used: AC1*

## SEG 4 — The Inner Organ — Buddhi, Ahaṁkāra, Manas (4:00-5:30)

**Visual:** AC3 (The 36 Tattvas descending — geometric diagram of 36 nodes, golden clay lines, light pulses from top downward, first nodes lighting up)
  → S1 (Seeker stands alone — small geometric figure in darkness, breathing slowly)

**Narration:**
"From prakṛti, the first thing to emerge is buddhi — intellect, the capacity for discernment. From buddhi comes ahaṁkāra — the sense of 'I,' the self-referencing principle. And from ahaṁkāra comes manas — mind, the faculty that processes sensory information. Together, these three form the inner organ — the antaḥkaraṇa. They are the instruments through which consciousness experiences a world."

**Audio:** Quiet. Single breath at the pause.

*LTX clips used: AC3, S1*

---

## SEG 5 — Senses, Elements, and the 24 Tattvas as the Base (5:30-7:00)

**Visual:** AC3 continues — the light reaches the lower nodes, illuminating the full 24-tattva structure
  → S3 (Seeker looks upward — golden light illuminates face, particles drift down)

**Narration:**
"The Sāṅkhya system continues unfolding: from ahaṁkāra emerge the five organs of perception — the jñānendriyas — and the five organs of action — the karmendriyas. Then come the five subtle elements — tanmātras — and the five gross elements — mahābhūtas. That gives us 24 tattvas in total. It is a complete map of the manifest world — from the first movement of intellect all the way down to earth. But it leaves something out."

**Audio:** Long pause after "leaves something out."

*LTX clips used: AC3, S3*

## SEG 6 — Kashmir Adds: Śiva, Śakti, Sadāśiva, Īśvara, Śuddhavidyā (7:00-8:30)

**Visual:** AC2 (Śiva becomes Śakti — crimson thread spirals outward from gold point, weaving complex pattern)
  → AC3 reverse — new nodes appear ABOVE the existing 24, light pulses upward from the top

**Narration:**
"What Sāṅkhya leaves out is consciousness itself — not puruṣa as a passive witness, but consciousness as the active ground of all that exists. Kashmir Śaivism adds five tattvas above the 24. The first is Śiva — pure consciousness. From Śiva, Śakti — its own power, the dynamism inherent in awareness. Then Sadāśiva — the first sense of 'I am this.' Īśvara — the sense of 'this is me.' And Śuddhavidyā — pure knowledge, the recognition that subject and object are one."

**Audio:** Quiet wonder.

*LTX clips used: AC2, AC3*

## SEG 7 — Māyā and the Five Kañcukas — How the Pure Becomes Impure (8:30-10:00)

**Visual:** AC4 (Māyā contracts — greyish rings close inward one by one around a warm figure, each ring dims the light)
  → GC10 (Five kañcukas binding — dark armor pieces clamp onto a luminous human silhouette: gauntlets, visor, chain, clock-mechanism, shackles)

**Narration:**
"Below Śuddhavidyā, something remarkable happens. Māyā — not illusion, but the power of contraction — begins to operate. It is the Lord's own power, voluntarily limiting itself. Through Māyā, five kañcukas — five limitations — wrap around infinite consciousness. Kalā — limited agency. Vidyā — limited knowledge. Rāga — attachment, desire. Kāla — time. Niyati — causality, necessity. The infinite is now finite. A boundless awareness has become a bounded person."

**Audio:** Subtle low tone as each kañcuka is named.

*LTX clips used: AC4, GC10*

## SEG 8 — The Seven Perceivers — Levels of Recognition (10:00-11:30)

**Visual:** GC11 (Seven perceivers — single flower shown from seven perspectives simultaneously in a horizontal panel)
  → AC12 (The veils part — translucent veils lift one by one, form behind becomes more luminous)

**Narration:**
"One of the most beautiful teachings of this system is the seven perceivers. The same reality is experienced differently depending on the level of awareness of the perceiver. The śakala sees the world as separate, solid, desirable. The pralayākala experiences it as memory. The vijñānākala as pure perception. At higher levels, the mantreśa and mantreśvara see everything as mantra and radiant energy. The sadāśiva perceives 'I am this.' And Śiva — perceives the universe as his own body."

**Audio:** Silence.

*LTX clips used: GC11, AC12*

## SEG 9 — Sāṅkhya Says Separate — Trika Says Recognize (11:30-13:00)

**Visual:** D (Quote card: "Sāṅkhya separates wheat from chaff — Trika recognizes they were never separate")
  → TR8 (Whole world is your body — small figure at center, cosmic outlines of mountains and stars arranged around them, figure breathes and cosmos responds)

**Narration:**
"Sāṅkhya says the goal is to separate puruṣa from prakṛti — to disentangle spirit from matter. The noble goal of the Kashmir Śaiva masters was different. They said: recognize that puruṣa and prakṛti were never separate. The 36 tattvas are not a list. They are a derivation — each step follows necessarily from the one above. And at every level, it is the same consciousness, appearing differently. The goal is not escape. The goal is recognition."

**Audio:** Bell tone at quote card.

*LTX clips used: TR8*

---

## SEG 10 — Why 36? Consciousness Must Account for Itself (13:00-14:30)

**Visual:** AT7 returns (Geometric form — now fully built tetrahedron, rotating gently, each face shows a different level of reality)
  → K3 (Liṅga in still water — golden light from above, perfect reflection, ripples move outward then settle)

**Narration:**
"Why 36? Because consciousness must account for itself. The 24 tattvas of Sāṅkhya map objectivity — the world as it appears to a knower. But they do not account for the knower himself, nor for the process by which the knower forgets and remembers his own nature. The 12 additional tattvas in Kashmir Śaivism add the missing dimensions: the pure ground, the power of contraction, the veils that make limitation possible, and most importantly — the possibility of return. The system is self-sealed."

**Audio:** Quiet.

*LTX clips used: AT7, K3*

---

## SEG 11 — The Self-Sealed System — Creation and Creator as One (14:30-16:00)

**Visual:** S1 (Seeker stands alone — small, breathing)
  → GC12 (Māyā as the knot — dark knot-like structure with three chambers, golden thread passing through, light pierces and collapses the knot)
  → AC5 (Liberation expands — dim point at center, rings of gold light emanate outward, center becomes luminous, light fills the frame)

**Narration:**
"This is why Swami Lakshmanjoo called the 36 tattvas the entrance point. Understanding the process of manifestation is understanding everything about our nature. We understand how we are at once this individual and this godhead. The knot of māyā — the māyāgranthi — is not a barrier to be destroyed. It is a contraction to be released. And when it releases, what was hidden is not something new. It is what you have always been."

**Audio:** Long pause after "what you have always been."

*LTX clips used: S1, GC12, AC5*

## SEG 12 — Closing (16:00-18:00)

**Visual:** S3 (Seeker looks upward — brief, 8s)
  → D (Quote card: "36 tattvas is the entrance point. Understanding manifestation, we understand everything. — Swami Lakshmanjoo")
  → K1 (Abhinavagupta's cave — slow pull-back from the manuscripts, the oil lamp flickers, the cave opening reveals a sliver of mountain sky)

**Narration:**
"The Kashmir Śaiva masters gave us not an open-to-cracks system, but a completely self-sealed system of how creation sits in relation to creator. And they did it beautifully. The 36 tattvas are not ancient history. They are the architecture of your own experience — right now — as you sit here, observing. And the one thing you cannot observe is the only thing that is real."

**Audio:** Bell tone at quote card. Long fade on cave image. End.

*LTX clips used: S3, K1*
