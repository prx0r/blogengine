## SEG 2 — What Is a Yogini? (1:00-2:30)

**Visual:** K4 (Shiva-Parvati carving — goddess imagery, warm light shifting)
  → AC2 (Shiva-Shakti — crimson thread spiraling around gold point)
  → AT2 (Incense smoke)

**Narration:**
"The word yoginī comes from the same root as yoga — yuj, to unite. A yoginī is one who unites. But the term does not mean what you might expect. In the Tantric tradition, a yoginī is not a human woman who practices yoga. A yoginī is a power — a specific, named energy of consciousness. The Yoginis are the feminine powers of the awakened mind. They are not beings in the conventional sense. They are functions of the luminous awareness that arise when consciousness recognizes itself."

**Audio:** Bell. Quiet.

*LTX clips used: K4, AC2, AT2*

## SEG 3 — Not a Goddess to Worship — A Power to Become (2:30-4:00)

**Visual:** TR4 (Seeker becomes deity — continues from hook, the transformation sequence)
  → S5 (Seeker kneels before light)
  → AC5 (Liberation expands — rings of gold light)

**Narration:**
"This is the radical teaching of the Yoginis. In most traditions, goddesses are beings you worship — you pray to them, you ask for their favor. In the Kaula and Krama traditions, the Yoginis are not external beings. They are powers you internalize. The practice is not to worship the Yogini. The practice is to become the Yogini. You meditate on her form, her name, her power — until you recognize that form, name, and power as your own consciousness. The Yogini is not other than you. She is what you are when you are fully awake."

**Audio:** Bell at transformation.

*LTX clips used: TR4, S5, AC5*

## SEG 4 — The 64 Yoginis (4:00-5:30)

**Visual:** GC5 (Wheel — the many goddess-figures, circles within circles)
  → TR6 (Thousand faces of the divine — shifting rapidly, male, female, animal, bird, star)

**Narration:**
"The tradition speaks of 64 Yoginis. They are arranged in circles — cakras — each Yogini a distinct power, a distinct name, a distinct form. They are not random. The 64 Yoginis correspond to the full spectrum of conscious experience — every possible state, every possible perception, every possible transformation. Some are fierce, some are gentle, some are beautiful, some are terrifying. They are all aspects of one reality. The 64 Yoginis are the complete map of the awakened mind."

**Audio:** Bell at each grouping.

*LTX clips used: GC5, TR6*

## SEG 5 — The Yogini as Mistress of the Senses (5:30-7:00)

**Visual:** AC4 (Māyā contracts — grey rings closing inward, dimming the figure)
  → AT5 (Dust particles in light — the senses as particles of consciousness)
  → S2 (Seeker sits — the one who masters the senses)

**Narration:**
"The Yoginis are described as the mistresses of the senses. They are not mistresses in the sense of controlling the senses externally. They are the senses themselves — when the senses are recognized as powers of consciousness rather than outward-directed faculties. The Yogini of sight is not a goddess of vision. She is vision itself — when vision is recognized as a self-luminous power of awareness. To become a Yogini is to recognize that the senses are not doors to the world. They are doors TO consciousness."

**Audio:** Bell. Quiet.

*LTX clips used: AC4, AT5, S2*

## SEG 6 — External Deities and Internal Powers (7:00-8:30)

**Visual:** K4 (Shiva-Parvati carving — goddess form)
  → GC8 (Trident throne of Para — the goddess as both external and internal)
  → AC2 (Shiva-Shakti — the internal dynamic)

**Narration:**
"The Yoginis exist on two levels simultaneously. On the external level, they are deities — you can visualize them, chant their mantras, make offerings to their images. On the internal level, they are the powers of your own consciousness — you can feel them as sensations, recognize them as thoughts, experience them as states of awareness. The external level is not false. It is the gateway. The internal level is not abstract. It is the reality. The practice moves from external worship to internal recognition — from 'I worship the Yogini' to 'I AM the Yogini.'"

**Audio:** Bell at transition.

*LTX clips used: K4, GC8, AC2*

## SEG 7 — The Kaula Tradition — Sacralization of the Body (8:30-10:00)

**Visual:** AC8 (Kundalini rises — crimson serpent uncoiling, rising through coloured bands)
  → AC3 (36 tattvas descending — the body as tattva-structure)
  → AT2 (Incense smoke)

**Narration:**
"The Kaula tradition — which means 'of the family' or 'of the body' — teaches that the body is not an obstacle to liberation. It is the seat of liberation. The Yoginis are not somewhere else. They are in the body — in the cakras, in the nāḍīs, in the breath. The Kaula path sacralizes the body by recognizing that every part of it is a Yogini. The instincts are Yoginis. The emotions are Yoginis. The perceptions are Yoginis. The body is the Yogini-mandala. To know the body is to know the goddess."

**Audio:** Quiet.

*LTX clips used: AC8, AC3, AT2*

## SEG 8 — The Kubjika Tantra (10:00-11:30)

**Visual:** TR6 (Thousand faces — the many forms of the goddess, the Kubjika principle)
  → GC5 (Wheel — the Yogini mandala)
  → S3 (Seeker looks up)

**Narration:**
"The Kubjika Tantra is one of the most important texts of the Kaula tradition. Its central deity is Kubjika — the 'crooked one' or 'bent one' — a Yogini who embodies the coiled power of Kundalini. The tradition teaches that Kubjika contains all the Yoginis within her — she is their source and their totality. Her bent form represents the coiled potential of consciousness. When she straightens, the universe unfolds. The Kubjika Tantra describes elaborate rituals for activating the Yogini powers in the practitioner's body — nyāsa, visualization, mantra."

**Audio:** Bell.

*LTX clips used: TR6, GC5, S3*

## SEG 9 — Yogini Cults (11:30-13:00)

**Visual:** YS4 (The descent — entering the cave, the Yogini circle as initiation)
  → BR3 (Shadowed guru Kashmir — the esoteric transmission)
  → AT5 (Dust particles in light — the secret, subtle nature of the teaching)

**Narration:**
"The Yogini cults of medieval India were secret, initiatory traditions. They met in circles — Yogini-cakras — in which the powers were invoked and recognized. These were not theological conferences. They were direct experiences of the Yogini powers — through ritual, through visualization, through the presence of awakened practitioners. The Yogini circles were dangerous — they could destroy the unprepared. But for the initiated, they were the fastest path. The secrecy was not about exclusivity. It was about protection — for the tradition and for the practitioner."

**Audio:** Quiet. Subtle drum (LTX T2A, distant).

*LTX clips used: YS4, BR3, AT5*

## SEG 10 — The Yogini as Awakened Shakti (13:00-14:30)

**Visual:** AC8 (Kundalini rises — the serpent reaching the crown, the flower opening)
  → AC5 (Liberation expands — rings of gold light)
  → S6 (Seeker dissolves into light)

"The Yogini is the awakened Śakti. She is not a power you invoke. She is the power of consciousness recognizing itself through you. When Kundalini rises, the Yoginis awaken. When the cakras open, the Yoginis appear. The practice of the Yoginis is not about gaining power over others. It is about releasing the power that is already you — the power that created the universe, the power that sustains every perception, the power that will dissolve everything back into awareness. To become a Yogini is to become that power."

**Audio:** Bell. Long silence.

*LTX clips used: AC8, AC5, S6*

  → TR4 (Seeker becomes deity — the full sequence, becoming the complete Yogini)

The practitioner who has integrated all the Yoginis has integrated all of themselves — the fierce and the gentle, the creative and the destructive, the transcendent and the embodied. To sit in the center of the Yogini circle is to sit as consciousness itself. The Yoginis are not around you. They ARE you."
