## the four styles

each element gets its own visual style. the style IS the element.

| Element | Style | Look & Feel | Motion | What It Contains |
|---------|-------|------------|--------|-----------------|
| **EARTH** (prithvi) | **alchemical toy / storybook** | whimsical, antique, painted tin, clockwork, visible brushstrokes. like a wind-up toy diorama come to life. warm ochre, terracotta, moss green, faded gold. | mechanisms turning, gears meshing, shoots growing slowly, waterwheels rotating. weight, patience, repetition. | tiny windmills, gear-trees, miniature stone temples, clockwork animals, painted landscapes, toy forts, moving books |
| **WATER** (apas) | **flow / process** | fluid, translucent, rippling. pipes carrying water, wheels turning, currents visible through suspended particles. deep blue, teal, silver, pale green. not standing water — active flow, cycles, causation. | water flowing through channels, wheels turning, bubbles rising in chains, streams branching and joining, a diving bell descending. | aqueducts, waterwheels, rivers seen from above, bubble chains, irrigation pipes, diving bells, ink dispersing in water |
| **AIR** (vayu) | **liminal dream** | mist-soaked, soft edges, colours desaturated. things emerge from fog and dissolve back. like memory. pale grey, lavender, cream, silver-blue. | clouds forming and unforming, mist drifting, fabric moving in wind, breath visible in cold air, smoke curling, silk unfurling. | cloud layers, mist through trees, drifting silk, smoke spirals, fog over landscape, breath, wind through grass |
| **FIRE** (tejas) | **sharp imaginal** | crisp, luminous, high contrast. cutting. beings of light with sharp features. like a stained-glass window lit from behind. warm gold, deep crimson, amber, white. | sparks striking, flames steadying, light spreading, geometric structures folding into existence. | beings of light, angelic forms, geometric light structures, sparks, flame, luminous architecture |

## the tantraloka mapping

each element maps to a tattva group AND a type of motion:

| Element | Tantraloka | Motion | Contemplation | Visual signature |
|---------|------------|--------|---------------|-----------------|
| Space | akasha tattva (ether) | stillness within which all moves | the container, the void | deep black, distant light |
| Air | vayu tattva (wind) | invisible movement, contact | the between, the subtle | mist, clouds, particles |
| Fire | tejas tattva (fire) | transformation, upward | illumination, change | warm gold, spark, flame |
| Water | apas tattva (water) | flowing, dissolving, reflecting | depth, the unconscious | dark blue, ripples, bubbles |
| Earth | prithvi tattva (earth) | solidifying, holding, growing | stability, foundation | stone, moss, soil, rock |

the 5 elements = the 5 lower tattvas of the tantraloka. the 15 visuals rise through them.

## the dream temple concept

the user returns to certain spaces across essays. these are the **dream temples** — recurring environments that feel familiar across videos.

the three primary dream temples:

1. **the stone chamber** (earth) — appears in essays about tradition, the body, the ancient path. always the same chamber, different light.
2. **the corridor of light** (space) — appears in essays about transition, the between, recognition. always receding forward.
3. **the reflection pool** (water) — appears in essays about self-knowledge, the mirror, the daimon. always still until a single ripple.

these three are the backbone. if you only generate three clips, generate these.

## the match-striking loop

the user mentioned a match being lit, a candle catching, the flame burning — looped as a gif.

this works as a doorway AND a recurring motif:

```
black screen: 1s
striking sound: spark appears
orange glow ignites: candle wick catches
flame steadies: warm gold light fills frame
loop back to steady flame → this is the loop point
```

the viewer sees it at the start of every alchemy short. it becomes a signature. the match striking IS the ignition of attention.

## production prompts

### Prompt template

```
[STYLE] Stop-motion animation, handcrafted clay-textured aesthetic, warm parchment tones, subtle grain, shallow depth of field, volumetric lighting, no characters, no people, no figures.

[WHAT IT INVITES] what the essay is asking the viewer to do or feel — the insight

[ENVIRONMENT] every surface, every light, every material. be literal.

[ACTION] what happens, in what order. the moment of illumination emerging from dark.

[CAMERA] static or locked-off. no camera movement unless specified.

[AUDIO] describe the sounds: a match being struck, a drop hitting water, wind passing.
```

### insight-action mapping

each visual has a **what it invites** line. this connects it to the essay content. the action is the manifestation of that insight.

### Batch 1: The Dream Temples

**1. STONE_CHAMBER** — invites you to sit in the ancient, to let the tradition hold you.

```
Stop-motion animation. No people, no figures, no characters.

A square stone chamber. Four walls of rough-cut dark grey blocks, uneven surfaces. A floor of worn grey stone slabs with thin dark gaps between them. The ceiling is not visible — lost in complete blackness above. At the center of the chamber, a single vertical beam of warm golden light falls from somewhere high above, striking the stone floor. The beam is about two feet wide. Inside the beam, tiny dust particles float upward slowly, each catching the light for a moment before disappearing into the darkness above. The stone walls around the beam are in deep shadow — the corners of the room are completely black. No windows. No doors. Just stone and light and drifting dust.

The light beam does not move. The stone does not move. The only motion is the dust drifting upward through the gold light. One particle catches the beam and glows brightly for a moment, then fades as it rises beyond the light. Another particle follows.

Static camera, locked-off. The beam is in focus; the stone walls soften into shadow.

Sound of utter stillness: a faint low stone-resonance hum. The dust makes no sound.
```

**2. CORRIDOR_OF_LIGHT** — invites you to walk toward the source, to know there is a direction home.

```
Stop-motion animation. No people, no figures, no characters.

A long straight corridor receding into infinite distance. The floor is pale warm stone, slightly reflective. No walls are visible — the corridor opens on both sides into warm golden-white haze that has no surface, no boundary. No ceiling is visible — just the floor below and the white-gold glow ahead. At the far end of the corridor, a warm gold-white light source glows steadily. The floor has faint joint lines crossing it at regular intervals — they get closer together as they recede, marking infinite depth.

The corridor is perfectly still. The light at the end does not flicker. Nothing moves. Then, very slowly — over four seconds — the light at the end brightens by perhaps ten percent. Not a pulse. Not a flicker. A gradual deepening of intensity, as if someone has turned their attention toward you. Then it holds at that brightness. The corridor returns to stillness.

Static camera, locked-off, slightly low angle so the floor leads the eye forward. Deep focus — everything from the near floor to the distant light is sharp.

Sound: a single low brass tone, very distant, very sustained, that begins as the light brightens and holds.
```

**3. REFLECTION_POOL** — invites you to wait for the moment recognition arises from stillness.

```
Stop-motion animation. No people, no figures, no characters.

A perfectly still dark pool of water. The pool is a circle about six feet across set into a smooth grey stone floor. The water is so dark it appears black — it reflects nothing. The stone floor around it extends to the edges of frame and then into darkness. Above the pool, a warm gold light source is present but invisible — it illuminates the stone floor but does not yet touch the water.

Four seconds of complete stillness. Then, from somewhere above, a single drop of water falls. It strikes the exact center of the pool. A small circular ripple expands outward from the impact point. As the ripple spreads, the water surface briefly catches the gold light above, creating a momentary crescent of reflection. The ripple reaches the edge of the pool and vanishes. The water returns to perfect stillness. The reflection is gone. The pool waits.

Static camera, locked-off, looking straight down at the pool from above. The water surface is in focus. The stone edges soften.

Sound: a single drop falling, hitting water, then silence as the ripple fades. The reverb lasts six seconds.
```

### Batch 2: The Elements

**4. STAR_FIELD** — invites you to see that the darkness contains infinite points of light, each one real.

```
Stop-motion animation. No people, no figures, no characters.

Complete darkness. Pure black. Three seconds of nothing. Then a single point of warm white light appears in the upper-right of frame. It is small, distant, steady. It does not twinkle. Two seconds later, a second point appears lower-left, slightly dimmer. Then a third, above center. Over the next ten seconds, lights continue appearing one by one, at irregular intervals — never more than one at a time. Each appears from nothing, already lit, already steady. Each is a slightly different warmth: some gold-white, some pale blue-white, one faint amber. They do not form patterns. They do not move. By the end, perhaps twenty lights are scattered across the frame. The spaces between them remain pure black.

The lights hold. Nothing else emerges.

Static camera. Focus at infinity. No depth of field.

Sound: the faintest possible high-frequency shimmer, like ice settling, present only when no new light is appearing.
```

**5. MIST_OVER_WATER** — invites you to accept that presence can exist without being visible.

```
Stop-motion animation. No people, no figures, no characters.

Dark still water fills the lower third of the frame. The water is deep grey-black, perfectly smooth, no ripples at all. Above it, thick pale grey-white mist fills the frame to the top. There is no horizon — the mist obscures everything. The mist is not uniform; it has density variations, patches of slightly thinner grey that drift slowly from right to left. Nothing is visible through the mist. The water reflects nothing because no light reaches it.

The mist continues its slow drift. At one point, a thin patch passes through center frame. For a moment, a faint pale glow penetrates from above the mist — not bright, just a slightly lighter grey, like sunlight through cloud. The glow touches the water surface once, briefly illuminating a small patch of dark water. The mist closes. The glow is gone. The water returns to black.

Static camera, locked-off. Shallow depth of field — the mist in the middle distance is in focus, the near mist and water soften.

Sound: very low distant wind. The mist makes no sound. The water makes no sound.
```

**6. CANDLE_FLAME** — invites you to steady your attention until it becomes still.

```
Stop-motion animation. No people, no figures, no characters.

Extreme close-up of a single candle flame burning in complete darkness. The flame occupies the center of frame. Below it, the top of a cream wax candle is visible — a shallow pool of clear melted wax surrounds the black wick. The flame is about an inch tall. Its inner core is deep blue, nearly invisible. The middle is translucent warm gold. The outer edge is pale yellow-white. The flame is almost still — barely a micro-waver, as if in air that has not moved for a long time.

The flame holds steady. Then, very slightly, it wavers once — a small adjustment — and returns to stillness. A single bead of melted wax at the edge of the pool begins to move. It runs slowly down the side of the candle, a thin clear trickle catching the gold light as it descends. The bead reaches a small ridge on the candle side and stops.

The flame is still again. The wax bead has landed.

Static camera, extreme-close-up. The flame is perfectly in focus. The background is pure black.

Sound: a faint barely-audible crackle. The wax makes no sound as it moves.
```

**7. MATCH_STRIKING** — invites you to ignite attention, to begin the work.

```
Stop-motion animation. No people, no figures, no characters.

Complete darkness. Pure black for two seconds. Then, at the bottom-center of frame, a small orange spark appears — brief, sharp, a single point of light that flares and fades in half a second. The sound of a match being struck against rough stone. A second spark follows, slightly larger, catching. A small flame appears at the base of the spark, flickering rapidly, uncertain, blue then orange then yellow. The flame grows as it catches the match head. The match head is visible now — a small wooden stick tipped with dark red, the flame spreading across it. The flame steadies, burning upward, becoming a warm gold teardrop shape. The flame illuminates the immediate surroundings — a small circle of warm light on darkness, the wooden matchstick now clearly visible, the flame burning steadily at its tip.

The flame holds steady. The match burns slowly. The light holds.

Static camera, close-up on the match head and flame. The flame is in focus. The darkness around it is absolute.

Sound: the scrape of a match against stone. A brief hiss as the sulphur catches. Then the steady faint sound of flame consuming wood.
```
