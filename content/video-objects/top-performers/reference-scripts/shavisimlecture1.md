### Four Iterations of Contemplative Yoga

**1. Upaniṣads — Yoga of Knowledge (Jñāna Yoga)**
Yoga = union between ātman (inmost self) and brahman (the divine). The mahāvākya "tat tvam asi" (that thou art). From the Kaṭha Upaniṣad: "When all the senses are still, when the mind is at rest, when the intellect wavers not — then, say the wise, has reached the highest state. This calm of the senses and mind has been defined as yoga."

**2. Classical Yoga of Patañjali — Yoga Sūtras**
Yoga = stilling of the fluctuating states of the mind (*citta vṛtti nirodhaḥ*). Samādhi — complete absorption. These first two iterations required intense introversion; primarily for monastics and contemplatives who withdrew into the forest.

**3. Bhagavad Gītā — Game Changer**
Yoga defined twice: "Yoga is evenness of mind" (equanimity) and "Yoga is skill in action." Four paths: Jñāna Yoga, Dhyāna Yoga, Bhakti Yoga, Karma Yoga. For active people, not monastics.

**4. Tantra — The Integrative Approach**
Tantra means at least three things:
- A body of texts (Tantras) oriented toward practice
- Expansion — expanding what has been constricted
- Continuum — connecting polarities (spirit/matter, masculine/feminine)

Why practice Tantric Yoga? It connects spirit to matter in the body, honors the divine feminine, provides an array of practices integrating emotional, intellectual, active, and vital parts of being, and integrates masculine and feminine principles.

### Śiva and Śakti

Śiva = consciousness. Śakti = creative energy. They are two aspects of one singularity, two poles of one reality. The way to Śiva (formless consciousness) is through Śakti — through form and energy.

### The Sages

**Vasugupta** (8th-9th c.) — Dreamed Śiva told him to turn over a rock in Kashmir; found the 77 sūtras of the Śiva Sūtras underneath. Also associated with the Spanda Kārikās (Stanzas of Divine Pulsation).

**Abhinavagupta** (10th-11th c.) — The greatest of all. Wrote the Tantrāloka (Light on the Tantras) — 5,800 verses, 37 chapters, not yet fully translated into English.

**Kṣemarāja** — His disciple, wrote the Pratyabhijñā Hṛdayam (Heart of Recognition Sūtras) — 20 sūtras that compress the entire philosophy.

**Vijñāna Bhairava Tantra** — 112 portals/contemplations through ordinary experiences.

### The Śiva Sūtras

1. *Caitanyam ātmā* — The Self is consciousness
2. *Jñānaṁ bandhaḥ* — Ordinary knowledge is bondage (partial, limited knowledge)
3. *Yonivarghaḥ kalāśarīram* — Contemplation of the inner self frees one from suffering

The remaining 74 sūtras show how limited knowledge expands, the discovery of the witness, and the upsurge of joy, wisdom, and spiritual awakening in active embodied life.

### Pratyabhijñā Hṛdayam (Heart of Recognition)

20 sūtras. Key concept: Śiva (universal consciousness) contracts into jīva (individual soul). Spiritual practice reverses this — the contracted sense expands, and jīva becomes Śiva.

Quote: "He or she is a bound soul who has poverty of śakti. With the unfoldment of his or her śakti, he or she becomes Śiva."

How? Through inner movement — meditation, contemplative practices — and through unfolding the central channel (suṣumṇā) in which kuṇḍalinī śakti rises.

### The Distinctive Claim of Kashmir Shaivism

Phase 1: Merge with the Self (ātman) — introverted concentration
Phase 2: Merge with the world — the ultimate aim. Merging with Śiva is a profound re-engagement with the world as a dancing, vibrating world of great splendor. Liberation in the body — *jīvanmukti*.

### Vijñāna Bhairava Tantra

112 portals — ordinary experiences as contemplative gates: breath, music, food, sensuality, spaciousness of the sky, strong emotion (even anger). The key is to engage the experiences fully. Presented as a dialogue between Śiva and the Goddess.

Reading from *The Radiance Sutras* (trans. Lorin Roche):

> One day the Goddess said to her lover Bhairava:
> "I have been wondering — what is this delight-filled universe into which we find ourselves born? What is this mysterious awareness shimmering everywhere within it? What are these energies undulating through our bodies, pulsing us into action? What are these dancing particles of condensed radiance? What is this power we call life, appearing as the play of flesh and breath? Lead me into the wholeness beyond all these parts."
>
> Bhairava replied:
> "Beloved, your questions touch the heart of wonder. The path of intimacy with all life — weaving together body and soul, sex and spirit, individuality and universality — this is my cave of secrets. I am always here."

### The Three Contractions (Malas) & Practice

Three primary contractions in body/mind: emotion, knowledge, and action. Correlate with the three granthis (knots) in yoga — at the navel, heart, and third eye. They block us, creating "poverty of śakti."

The practice: simply name the contraction. "I am angry. I am sad. I feel powerless." These are "A statements" — accurate statements of present feeling.

Four chakras for inquiry:
- **Navel:** Personal will, desire — "I let go"
- **Heart:** Love, resentment — "I forgive myself / I accept myself"
- **Throat:** Expression, communication — "I express myself completely and freely"
- **Third eye:** Wisdom, awareness — "I expand my awareness"

B statements (beneficial) shift from contracted to expanded. G statements are like mahāvākyas: "Everything is consciousness," "I am the Self."

### Guided Meditation (conducted)

The meditation portion uses mantra (*so'ham* or "I am") with a tanpura drone, followed by the chakra healing meditation with affirmations at each center. (~20 minutes of practice)
