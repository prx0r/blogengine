## SEG 4 — Mātṛkā vs Mālinī — Two Arrangements (4:30-6:00)

Mālinī — the 'Garland' — is the same 50 phonemes in a secret transposed sequence beginning with NA and ending with PH. Mālinī is the aroused form of the alphabet, used for inner, more powerful depositions. She embodies the goddess in her supreme form as Parā and as Kālasaṁkarṣiṇī — the Attractress of Time."

**Audio:** Quiet drone.

*LTX clips used: GC1, AT7*

Paśyantī — Seeing Speech — is the first differentiation. Madhyamā — Intermediate Speech — is inner mental speech. Vaikharī — Articulate Speech — is audible vocalization."

**Audio:** Soft ambient.

Abhinavagupta identifies Parā Vāc with the supreme goddess — the dynamic creativity of consciousness that, by reflecting on itself, gives rise to all the phonemes, all the mantras, all the worlds. Abhinavagupta says: 'The Lord united with emission is all things as the fifty energies of the letters.' The utterance AHAṀ does not describe the self. It IS the self, vibrating."

KHPHREṀ is the seed of Kālasaṁkarṣiṇī, the goddess of the Krama — the withdrawal of all manifestation back into the source. HRĪM is the heart mantra, the fiery power of attraction and transformation. The 50 phonemes are not tools for describing reality. They are the body of the goddess. To chant a mantra with full attention is to become the deity. The alphabet is not something you use.
