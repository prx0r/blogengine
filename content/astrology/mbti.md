## Astrology Theorem

The most essential small portion of the full developing theorem. "Arc" refers equally to House & Sign.

### Deducing All Meanings in Astrology

All meanings are according to the circle. Two aspects:

**Time / The cycle of life** — by the number of the House/Sign:
- 1st = Beginning, 12th = End
- Beginning relates to Past, End to Future
- Descending the circle (2nd/IC Quadrant) = movement toward Past
- Ascending the circle (4th/MC Quadrant) = movement toward Future
- 3rd/DC Quadrant = Present time

**The physical relationships in the circle**:
- Opposite Arcs are basic Complementaries
- AC Quadrant (1st, 12th, 2nd) = you
- DC Quadrant (7th, 6th, 8th) = external reality

### Quadrants

Two ways to view Quadrants:
- Between Cardinal Points (AC, IC, DC, MC)
- After each Cardinal Point — the 2 Arcs are Yin & Yang

Arc roles in Quadrants:
- **Prospect** — forward from Cardinal Cusp, most Natural/Real
- **Retrospect** — backward from Cardinal Cusp, most Artificial (learning)
- **Middle** — hologram made of the 2 Arcs around it

## MBTI Theorem

### Combined Functions by Orientation

**Introverted Functions** — ultimate Structure is Abstract, interconnected. 3D structure.
**Extroverted Functions** — ultimate Structure is Expansive, free/creative. 2D structure.

**Feeling**: Fi = Depth (levels behind the square). Fe = Breadth (adjacent squares).
**Intuition**: Ni = single 3D structure built from pieces over life. Ne = pieces arranged 2-dimensionally.
**Sensing**: Si = abstract external reality from life experiences. Se = vast range of experiences.
**Thinking**: Ti = basic eternal principles. Te = how things actually work in the real world.

### Levels

1. **Function** — Automatic brain operation
2. **Intentional** — What Functions cause as result
3. **Learned** — Lifelong development, requires external means
4. **Dependent** — What everything leads to, can be abused

### INFJ Analysis

- **Ni** (dominant): Super Intuition, sudden realizations, pattern recognition. Follows Inner Vision (Dharma).
- **Fe** (auxiliary): Empath, mirrors others, no stable personality, Multiple Personalities-like experience.
- **Ti** (tertiary): Basic Eternal Principles, searching for single united model of underlying mathematical structure.
- **Se** (inferior): Needs novel experiences (ADHD, Highly Sensitive Person, Multipotentialite).

**Ni vs Ti**: Ni is astronomical complexity (chaotic real-world mirroring). Ti is simple and orderly (table with clearly defined relations). Combined, they allow each other into the other's domain.

### MBTI Test Design

Direct questions checking Combined Functions, Orientation, then Placement:
- F: stable personality (Fi) vs adaptive/mirroring (Fe)
- N: creativity/scenarios (Ne) vs intuition/insight (Ni)
- T: practical principles (Te) vs eternal principles (Ti)
- S: experience/action (Se) vs memory/routine (Si)
