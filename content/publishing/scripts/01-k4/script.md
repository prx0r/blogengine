# Script 01: Ñāṇavīra's Fundamental Structure
## Duration: ~4:30 | ~675 words | Voice: en-GB-RyanNeural

---

[0:00-0:35] HOOK

Every moment of your experience — right now, listening to this — has exactly four parts. Not three. Not five. Four. And if you remove any one of them, experience itself stops. 

This was discovered in 1963 by a British monk sitting alone in a hut in Ceylon. His name was Ñāṇavīra Thera. And what he found was the shape at the bottom of everything.

---

[0:35-1:45] BUILD — Part 1: The Four Terms

Here they are. Try it now. You — the one hearing these words — that's the first term. The subject. 

These words you're hearing, the screen you might be looking at, the thought forming in your mind — that's the second term. The object. Whatever you're perceiving right now.

The fact that your attention is directed toward something — the wanting to understand, the curiosity, the slight impatience — that's the third term. Intention. The direction of your mind.

And the actual happening of it — the hearing, the seeing, the thinking — that's the fourth term. The act.

Subject. Object. Intention. Act. 

Not sequential. Not caused by each other. They arise together or not at all. You were never just a subject waiting for an object to appear. The moment you are, there is already something you're aware of, already a direction your attention points, already the act of being aware.

---

[1:45-2:45] BUILD — Part 2: Why Four?

Why not three? Why not five? Because four is the minimum number of points needed to enclose a space. Three points make a triangle — that's flat. A triangle has no inside. But four points connected by six edges make a tetrahedron. The simplest possible shape that has volume. The minimum for an inside.

Subject. Object. Intention. Act. These are the four vertices of that tetrahedron. The six edges are the six relations between them. You cannot have a subject without an object — that edge must exist. You cannot have an intention without an act — that edge must exist too. Every moment of experience is a complete tetrahedron. All four vertices. All six edges. Co-arising.

---

[2:45-3:30] REVEAL — The Five Independent Discoveries

Here's what makes this remarkable. Ñāṇavīra was alone in that hut. He had no contact with the traditions that found this same structure.

Proclus, in 475 CE Athens, derived 211 propositions about reality from four terms: Henad, Being, Life, and Intellect. Four. Always four.

Abhinavagupta, in 1000 CE Kashmir, mapped the 36 tattvas as nine nested tetrahedra. The four upāyas — four means of liberation — are the invariant vertices.

And in 2013, particle physicists Arkani-Hamed and Trnka discovered the amplituhedron — a geometric object that simplifies quantum scattering calculations. Its simplest case — A42 — is a tetrahedron. Four momenta. Six BCFW tiles. The same structure.

Four independent discoveries across 1,500 years. A Buddhist monk. A Neoplatonist philosopher. A Tantric master. Quantum physicists. All describing the same shape.

---

[3:30-4:15] IMPLICATION

Ñāṇavīra called this the Fundamental Structure. He wrote: "Anyone who succeeds in following what there is of it will be able to continue it as far as he pleases."

He meant: once you see that every moment of experience has this four-term structure, you can extend it to any domain. It's there in perception. In meditation. In physics. In every living cell — where the Markov blanket, the boundary between organism and environment, is also a four-state partition. Internal. External. Sensory. Active.

The tetrahedron is not a metaphor. It's not something we impose on experience to make sense of it. It IS what experience is made of. The minimum. The invariant. The shape of having a world at all.

---

[4:15-4:30] CLOSE

You are one of its vertices. Right now. You cannot leave. Because the attempt to leave — the thought "I want to escape this framework" — that very thought has a subject, an object, an intention, and an act. The tetrahedron reforms around you every time.

There is no outside. Not because you're trapped. Because you ARE one of the four.
