# Beat Map — Script 07: Free Energy Principle — Why Your Brain Minimizes Surprise
## Scene Type: Canvas 2D particles with attractors (process visualization)
## Duration: ~4:00

---

### B1 [0:00-0:12] "Your brain has one job. Not to think. Not to feel."
**VISUAL:** Pure black void (#0D1117). A single gold (#D4A574) particle appears center-screen, suspended, still. Three dim grey (#6B7280, opacity 0.3) potential-particle-shapes pulse briefly around it and dissolve — "think" (a thought-bubble silhouette), "feel" (a heart shape), "create" (a star-burst shape). Each gets an X through it. They vanish. Only the gold particle remains.

**AUDIO:** Three short, rejected tones — dissonant minor second intervals — each one cut off abruptly. Then silence. A single low fundamental (98Hz, G2) emerges from the silence with a 3s attack. Volume -30dB.

---

### B2 [0:12-0:30] "Minimize surprise."
**VISUAL:** The gold particle begins to pulse — expanding and contracting rhythmically. Around it, a boundary ring appears — a thin purple (#A855F7) circle. This is the Markov blanket primitive. Inside the ring: gold particle (the model). Outside the ring: white noise particles (#E6E1DC, opacity 0.3) drifting randomly — the environment, unpredictable. When an outside particle approaches the boundary, the ring flexes slightly inward and the gold particle shifts position to avoid the disturbance. The system is actively maintaining its difference from the environment.

**AUDIO:** A steady low drone at 98Hz. When an environmental particle nears the boundary, a brief spike of white noise (50ms, -38dB) sounds — the "surprise" that the system minimizes. After each spike, the drone re-stabilizes. The pattern: disturbance → correction → stability.

---

### B3 [0:30-1:00] "An organism is a pocket of low entropy..."
**VISUAL:** The single particle becomes a cluster of 20-30 gold particles, tightly organized in a hexagonal lattice — ordered, structured. Outside the purple boundary ring, environmental particles swirl chaotically in Brownian motion. The contrast is stark: inside = order, outside = entropy. The boundary ring thickens and becomes multi-layered — three concentric purple rings (cell membrane, skin, blood-brain barrier). White labels fade beside each ring. Gold particles inside the boundary occasionally drift toward it and are repelled back — the system actively resists dissipation.

**AUDIO:** Inside the boundary: a pure sine chord (C3-G3-E4, major triad in just intonation) — ordered, consonant. Outside: filtered noise with sporadic, chaotic overtones — entropy. The boundary ring has its own faint harmonic: a thin, high 2093Hz (C7) ring at -38dB. Volume: inside -30dB, outside -40dB.

---

### B4 [1:00-1:15] "The organism doesn't know the outside world directly."
**VISUAL:** A new particle enters from outside — a single white particle that approaches the outermost boundary ring. As it touches, the ring deforms slightly — a perturbation. This perturbation propagates inward through the three ring layers, each layer passing a transformed signal to the next. The innermost gold particle cluster responds — it shifts configuration slightly. Text labels appear at each step: "Sensory input" (outer ring), "Encoding" (middle ring), "Model update" (inner cluster). An arrow overlay shows the direction: environment → boundary → internal model. NOT internal model → environment (top-down prediction is shown in B5).

**AUDIO:** Each perturbation propagation adds a slight pitch-shift: outer ring: +5 cents, middle ring: +10 cents, inner cluster: returns to exact pitch — the model corrects the deviation. The process sounds like a brief wobble resolving to stability.

---

### B5 [1:15-1:45] "You don't perceive the world. You predict it."
**VISUAL:** The scene now shows BOTH directions simultaneously. Purple arrows travel downward (top-down prediction): internal model → boundary → world. Gold arrows travel upward (prediction error): world → boundary → internal model. The purple prediction creates a ghost-image — a faint predicted cup outline — projected onto the outside world. When the actual sensory input (a gold particle shaped like a cup) arrives at the boundary, the predicted ghost and the actual input are compared. The difference — the mismatch — glows red briefly and then the internal model updates. On the next cycle, the prediction ghost and the actual input are identical — zero prediction error — the system has learned.

**AUDIO:** Top-down prediction: a descending arpeggio (G4 → E4 → C3) — the model projecting outward. Prediction error: an ascending dissonant interval — the discrepancy. When error hits zero, a perfect fifth (C3-G3) locks in. The cycle repeats 3 times, each time with less error signal.

### B6 [1:45-2:00] "This is why you don't see your blind spot."
**VISUAL:** A visual field is shown — a circular aperture representing the field of view. At the edge, a small dark patch appears — the blind spot. Gold particles flow into the dark patch from the surrounding area, filling it with predicted content — the model "paints in" what should be there. The blind spot becomes indistinguishable from surrounding vision. Then: an optical illusion — two identical grey squares. One appears lighter, one darker. The model's priors (context, surrounding colors) are shown as faint purple prediction rays striking the squares, changing their perceived appearance. White text: "What you consciously experience is the prediction that survived the error-check."

**AUDIO:** Blind spot fill: a soft "bloom" sound — a gentle rise in the drone, like the model breathing into the gap. Optical illusion: two tones at identical pitch (440Hz) are played — but one is surrounded by slightly higher harmonics and one by lower harmonics, making them sound subtly different despite being the same fundamental. Volume -32dB.

### B7 [2:00-2:15] "Your entire conscious experience is a controlled hallucination..."
**VISUAL:** The scene pulls back dramatically. The boundary ring now encloses a full human silhouette outline (white, opacity 0.2), seated. Inside the boundary: the gold particle model of reality — a miniature, simplified world-model that the silhouette is running. The model includes simplified versions of a cup, a table, a face, a tree — all rendered in gold particle outlines. Outside the boundary: the "actual" world — rendered in higher-detail white noise particles, chaotic and information-dense. Predictive arrows stream constantly from the internal model outward. Sparse error signals flow back in. White text fades bottom-center: "You are a model of the world, running on a slab of meat, trying to stay alive."

**AUDIO:** The internal model has its own sonic signature — a soft, simplified arpeggio (3 notes cycling: C3-E3-G3). The external world is represented by filtered pink noise at -40dB. The prediction stream is audible as a low, steady hum. The sparse error signals are occasional high chirps (like a modem handshake, but musical). Volume -30dB overall.

### B8 [2:15-2:40] "Friston's free energy is structurally identical to karma."

White text appears center: "Free energy = Karma." Then fades.

**AUDIO:** Two short melodic phrases play simultaneously — one in a Western minimalist style (FEP side), one in a North Indian classical raga style (karma side). Same notes, same rhythm, different timbre and ornamentation. They lock into unison at the center — proving they're the same melody.

### B9 [2:40-3:00] "The person who expects hostility perceives hostility..."
**VISUAL:** Split screen. Left side: the internal model is configured with hostile priors — the gold particles are jagged, sharp, defensive. The boundary ring is thick and rigid. When neutral environmental particles approach, the model interprets them as threats — they appear red (#EF4444 tint) to the model. Right side: the internal model is configured with benevolent priors — the gold particles are soft, rounded, open. The boundary ring is thin and permeable (opacity 0.3). The same neutral environmental particles appear as opportunities — they appear green (#166534 tint) to the model. White text bottom: "Same world. Different priors. Different experience."

**AUDIO:** Left side: a minor second interval (C-C#) — dissonant, tense. Right side: a major sixth interval (C-A) — open, warm. Same fundamental (C3), different harmonic context. Each side's audio panned 60% left/right.

### B10 [3:00-3:30] "Liberation — mokṣa, nirvāṇa — is what happens when the model stops defending itself."
**VISUAL:** The boundary ring from the hostile model begins to thin. The jagged particles soften. The ring becomes transparent — opacity drops from 0.8 to 0.1. Gold particles from inside begin to drift outward, and environmental particles drift inward — freely exchanging. The boundary is still present (there's still a body, a perspective) but it no longer blocks. The internal model and the external world begin to merge into a single particle field — one unified flow in gold (#D4A574). At the center, a single point glows green (#166534). White text fades: "Minimizing free energy to zero." Then: "The end of suffering."

**AUDIO:** The dissonance from B9 gradually resolves — the minor second slowly bends to a unison (C-C# → C-C). The boundary-rigidity sound (a thin, tense high frequency at 4186Hz/C8) fades to silence. What remains is a single, pure C3 drone with its full harmonic series — open, unbounded. Volume -28dB. The drone breathes (amplitude modulation at 12 cycles/minute).

### B11 [3:30-4:00] "What model are you running?"
**VISUAL:** The unified particle field from B10 fades. A single gold particle remains at center — the same particle from B1. A white question mark fades in beside it. The particle pulses once, then fades slowly to black. White text appears faintly at bottom: "Not because the world changed. Because the model did."

**AUDIO:** The C3 drone holds for the first 15 seconds of this beat. Then, slowly, it fades — over 15 seconds — to complete silence at 4:00. The final sound: a single soft bell at C5 (523Hz), struck once, allowed to ring for 5 seconds in silence.
