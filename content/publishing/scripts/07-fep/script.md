# Script 07: Free Energy Principle — Why Your Brain Minimizes Surprise
## Duration: ~4:00 | ~600 words | Voice: en-GB-RyanNeural

---

[0:00-0:30] HOOK

Your brain has one job. Not to think. Not to feel. Not to be creative or intelligent or wise. One fundamental, inescapable job: minimize surprise.

Every perception, every action, every belief you hold — all of it is in service of this single imperative. Keep the difference between what you expect and what happens as small as possible.

This is the Free Energy Principle. It was formulated by Karl Friston, one of the most cited neuroscientists alive. And it may be the closest thing Western science has to a theory of everything for living systems.

---

[0:30-1:15] BUILD — The Problem of Being Alive

An organism is a pocket of low entropy in a universe that trends toward maximum entropy. You are an improbable arrangement of matter. The second law of thermodynamics says you should disperse. Every living thing is in active rebellion against equilibrium.

How? By maintaining a boundary — a Markov blanket. A statistical separation between what's inside and what's outside. Your cell membranes. Your skin. The blood-brain barrier. These are Markov blankets. They don't just separate — they define. "Inside" and "outside" are not physical facts. They're statistical ones, maintained by active processes.

The organism doesn't know the outside world directly. It only knows its own sensory states — the perturbations on its boundary. From these perturbations, it must infer what's out there. And it must act to keep its internal states within viable bounds.

This is the free energy imperative: minimize the discrepancy between your model of the world and the sensory evidence. In other words: minimize surprise.

---

[1:15-2:15] REVEAL — Perception Is Controlled Hallucination

Here's the radical implication. You don't perceive the world. You predict it. And you correct your predictions when they're wrong.

When you look at a cup, your brain is not passively receiving visual data. It is generating a top-down prediction of what should be there — a cup-shaped expectation — and then checking the actual sensory input against that prediction. The difference — the prediction error — is what gets passed upward for correction. What you consciously experience is not the raw sensory data. It's the prediction that survived the error-check.

This is why you don't see your blind spot. Your brain predicts what should be there and fills it in. It's why optical illusions work — they exploit the gap between prediction and input. It's why dreams feel real — the predictions are running without sensory correction.

Your entire conscious experience is a controlled hallucination — constantly updated by sensory evidence, but fundamentally generated from the top down. You are not a camera recording the world. You are a model of the world, running on a slab of meat, trying to stay alive.

---

[2:15-3:00] IMPLICATION — Free Energy = Karma

Now the bridge. Friston's free energy is structurally identical to the Buddhist and Hindu concept of karma.

Karma is not cosmic punishment. Karma is the momentum of your model of reality. Your accumulated actions and beliefs have shaped your predictions. Those predictions shape what you perceive. What you perceive shapes what you do. What you do shapes your future predictions. This is a feedback loop — a free energy minimization loop.

The person who expects hostility perceives hostility, acts defensively, and confirms their model. The person who expects benevolence perceives benevolence, acts openly, and confirms their model. Same world. Different priors. Different experience.

Liberation — mokṣa, nirvāṇa — is what happens when the model stops defending itself. When the prediction error is not resisted but fully met. When the Markov blanket becomes transparent. Friston would call this minimizing free energy to zero. The Buddha called it the end of suffering.

---

[3:00-4:00] CLOSE

The free energy principle says: you are a model. A prediction machine. A hypothesis about the world that updates itself to stay alive.

The spiritual traditions say the same thing in different language. You are a configuration. A pattern of tendencies. A set of expectations that filter what you can perceive and what you can do.

The question is: what model are you running? What do you expect? Because what you expect, you perceive. What you perceive, you become. Minimize your prediction error — or, as the ancients put it, know yourself — and the world you see will be a different world.

Not because the world changed. Because the model did.
