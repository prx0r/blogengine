# god is incomplete without you

What if God needs you as much as you need God? What if the divine is incomplete without human awareness to witness it? This is the central nerve of a metaphysical system so radical that most of its interpreters still flinch from its implications.

---

The famous expression *wahdat al-wojud* refers to the transcendental unity of being: the act of being remains unique while multiplying itself in the actualities of the beings that it causes to be — an unconditioned Subject which is never itself caused-to-be.

There is only one act of being. Your existence and the existence of a stone and the existence of God are but the intensity of awareness that receives being varies from near-total opacity to near-total transparency.

---

I created perception in Thee only that therein I might become the object of my perception.

This is the shortest possible summary of why you exist. Not to achieve something, not to evolve, to serve as the mirror in which the divine can see itself. You are the eye that God made so that God could look into a face and say "I."

---

Man in Ibn 'Arabi's anthropogony is likewise intermediate: situated between being and non-being, between Light and Darkness, at the same time responsible and respondent to both sides; he is responsible for the Darkness to the extent that he intercepts the Light, but he is responsible for the Light to the extent that he prevents the Darkness from invading and governing it.

You are which way you lean — determines which force flows through the world. Every choice you make is a choice about which world you are midwifing into existence. There is no neutral ground.

---

God has no like (*mithl*), but He has an Image, a typification (*mithal*), declares Lahiji. This is the secret of the Prophet's vision tirelessly meditated upon by so many Sufis: 'I have seen my God in the most beautiful of forms,' attesting that the divine Being, without form or modality, is present to the eye of the heart in a particular form, modality, and individuation.

The formless takes form in the heart that contemplates it. And the form it takes is it is the shape of the beholder's deepest love, his truest longing. The act of seeing God *as* something is the very mechanism of theophany.

---

The perfection of contingent being is to regress to its basic negativity, and to come to know through its own unknowingness. It means to know with the certainty of experience that the summum of knowledge is unknowingness, for here there is infinite disproportion... In reality, there is no knowledge of God by another than God, for another than God is not.

If you think you have arrived at a correct concept of God, you have not arrived. The only adequate knowledge of the infinite is to know that you do and to know that the one who does not know is not separate from what is known. The knower, the knowing, and the unknown are the same act. This is why the path of Ibn Arabi unsays everything you have said and unlearns every image you have formed — rather than accumulating information about God — until the one who is trying to know and the one who is known collapse into each other.

---

Absolute non-being is manifested only in and through absolute being. For any other than Perfect Man access to this degree is difficult, for Perfect Man is the most perfect of beings and the very cause of the coming into existence of the world.

The Perfect Man is the being in whom the divine self-disclosure reaches completion. You are that being in embryo. The entire cosmos groans toward the birth of this single individual, and that individual is it is someone you will become.

---

From ascent to ascent, the return of the man of light to his original pleroma postulates the idea of a non-specific individual, of archetypal individuality whose soaring flight and power, by assuming all the virtualities of a species, itself becomes a unique example. The idea of an individual who is himself his species is the idea of the Angel.

The goal of the path is the individual who is his own species, a category of one like the Angel — the Perfect Man creates the category that only he can fill, becoming a new possibility for being itself, rather than fitting into categories that already exist.

---

Each time you hear in the Book words addressed to Adam, listen to them through the organ of the subtle body... Meditate on that with which they symbolize, and be very sure that the esoteric aspect of the passage relates to you, just as the exoteric aspect relates to Adam in that it concerns the horizons... Only then will you be able to apply the teaching of the divine Word to yourself and to cull it as you would a branch laden with freshly opened flowers.

Every scripture, every myth, every story is a letter addressed to you personally. Adam is the faculty in your own soul that fell and forgot — rather than a figure in the distant past. Every prophet is a power of your being that needs to be awakened. The entire cosmos is a book, and you are the reader for whom it was written.

---

The God who needs you is for love requires a beloved, and the beloved is the mirror in which love sees itself. You are and even infinity, without a witness, is only a potential.

The tradition calls this the Secret of Secrets. But the secret is simple: you are so that there might be sight.

Consider what this means for prayer, for ethics, for the purpose of human life. If God needs you, then your presence in the world is purposeful — rather than accidental. You are an organ of the divine body, a necessary component in the self-completion of being — not a stranger passing through a foreign land. When you wake up in the morning, you are entering a world that is incomplete until you arrive — rather than one that is finished without you. The air you breathe is the breath of God experiencing finitude. The thoughts you think are the divine mind reflecting on itself from within the constraint of a skull. The love you feel is God loving God through the medium of a human heart.

This is the claim that everything is God. It is something stranger and more intimate: the claim that everything is God's self-disclosure, and that you are the place where that disclosure becomes conscious of itself. The God who needs you is the God who made you free, because only a free being can offer the gift of recognition freely. A puppet that praises its maker is no praise at all. But a being who can turn away and chooses to turn toward — that being gives God something God cannot give to himself.

You are that being. You have always been that being. The only question is whether you will know it, and in knowing it, complete the circle that has been open since the first particle of light separated from the darkness.
