# remember enlightenment?

Enlightenment is something you remember. Something you have always known.

*Pratyabhijñā*. Recognition. Seeing what you have always been.

Abhinava begins his presentation of the Supreme Means with an exposition of the Doctrine of Reflection. This maintains that all that is objectively perceived, whether outer objects, sensory perceptions or inner cogitations, shines within consciousness at one with it as do reflections with the mirror in which they are reflected. But while the reflections in a mirror are those of an original object outside the mirror, these reflections are produced by the inherent power of the mirror of Bhairava's consciousness. The Buddhist idealists had made reflection a cornerstone of their idealism, but for them it serves to illustrate that the world is just an unreal appearance. However, Abhinava takes care to stress that the reflections are real.

A mirror that generates its own images — the face it shows comes from its own depth. That is the hinge between a glass mirror and consciousness.

> "All this mass of phenomena is just a reflection in the Sky of Consciousness. It possesses the characteristic mark of a reflection: it cannot manifest separately from the medium of reflection. All things are reflections within the Light of the Supreme Lord. The cause of reflection is the Lord's power, otherwise called freedom."

A reflection cannot exist apart from the mirror. A wave cannot exist apart from the ocean. Your sense of being a separate self — a reflection in the mirror of consciousness. The mirror is what you are.

This is the characteristic mark of a reflection: it cannot manifest separately from the medium of reflection, and it manifests mingled with other reflections. That is a reflection, like the form of a face in a mirror, like taste in saliva, smell in the nose, an erotic touch in the organ of touch, and an echo in the sky. All things are reflections within the Light of the Supreme Lord. The cause of reflection is the Supreme Lord's power, otherwise called freedom. Free and completely full is this Lord, the omnipresent Bhairava. There is nothing that exists that He does not make manifest in the pure mirror of His own nature.

Saliva carries no independent taste — it borrows flavour from what touches it. Consciousness is the same: the world tastes real only because consciousness lends it reality.

Reflections carry weight, carry presence, carry the texture of lived experience. Their substance is the mirror that holds them — inseparable as heat from a coal. The Buddhist idealists named them unreal. The Tantric eye finds them real in the only way anything can be real: as the mirror's own content, shimmering.

Having somehow attained the state of a servant of the Great Lord (maheśvara), which is the cause of attaining all that is of value, and wishing to render service to humanity, I am establishing by reasoned argument the Recognition of that reality. A state devoid of craving within oneself — craving, which is desire, consists of the denial of a particular object and the acceptance of what remains. One who is completely fulfilled by his condition as a servant of the lord, there is nothing at all within himself that needs to be done that is desirable. His will is unrestricted because its nature is consciousness.

A fulfilled person does not reach. Their hands are open because holding nothing costs nothing.

He who enters repeatedly into this state of penetration (samāveśa) into pure Bhairava consciousness free of thought constructs, attains Bhairava's state, otherwise called liberation in this life (jīvanmukti). The liberated soul's only purpose is to elevate others. The yogi free of thought constructs, beholding that Śiva also reflected in the noble and uncreated (akalpita) mirror of consciousness, becomes by himself spontaneously Bhairava.

A mirror does being whatever arises, without residue.

The letter A is a measure of consciousness, which does the initial outpouring of consciousness and the final reflective awareness of all things. The letter A stands for Anuttara. Its power is the unfolding perception of all things in their fundamental nature as the flow of pure self-awareness: AHAṀ ('I') of Bhairava/Śiva, the supreme perceiver. It encompasses the energies of the letters from A to H, forms of reflective awareness (parāmarśa) that extend through the reality levels from Earth to Śiva in a perpetual cycle.

The alphabet of consciousness is one word: AHAṀ. Every perception, every thought, every sensation is that one syllable conjugating itself into the world's grammar. The letters themselves are acts of recognition.

Abhinavagupta's doctrine of reflection holds that the world is a reflection in the mirror of Śiva's consciousness. Matter is a real manifestation of consciousness, sharing in its reality — the reflection grants the world ontological weight while maintaining its dependence on consciousness. Abhinavagupta names the world a living seam of light and density. Advaita calls it false appearance — the same river, different maps entirely.

A dream and a reflection — the dream dissolves when you wake, the reflection stays as long as the mirror faces the light. The world is more like the second: dependent but dense, real but still woven into the mirror's own fabric.

The Awakened One should apply the fifty kinds of reflective awareness free of thought constructs, here called the Divine Means. Beholding the Earth principle reflected within one's own nature by means of this reflective awareness free of thought constructs, it assumes Bhairava's nature. This same procedure is applied to all the other principles also, from that of Water onwards right up to the last, where the yogi reaches the supreme principle beyond all obscuring coverings, which pervades all things, is free, omniscient, and conceived to be Śiva.

Each tattva — from earth up to consciousness — is a different face of the same mirror. To see a stone as a reflection of awareness is to see through the stone — and the stone remains whole, perhaps more whole than before.

The Power that Sustains Everything is the ground of all manifestation. Everything appears within Śiva like a reflection or image in a dream or notion of the mind. The relationship between the sustainer and the sustained is that of consciousness to its own self-manifestation. By His freedom, Śiva can manifest what does not exist; all is one with Him. The śakti that appears as the ground is itself one with Śiva, not separate from Him.

The ground does just quieter, stiller, more itself.

Matter is a real manifestation of consciousness, sharing in its reality — the world carries ontological weight even as it depends on consciousness. Abhinavagupta names it a wondrous, incandescent expression. Camatkāra — wonder or astonishment — is the central aesthetic category here. The world is a living expression of the divine: every stone, every breath, every seam of light.

The weight of a stone is real. So is the light that reveals it. The two are they are the same reality at different densities.

The liberated soul's only purpose is to elevate others. The yogi free of thought constructs, beholding that Śiva is reflected in the noble and uncreated (akalpita) mirror of consciousness, becomes by himself spontaneously Bhairava. From this point onwards the notion that there is a means and a goal persists only in a subtle form. Even the distinction between the one who knows and the knowing itself begins to blur. The uncreated mirror does it is the act of reflection itself, without beginning, without end.

The mirror shows that seeing and being are the same act.

All this mass of phenomena is just a reflection in the Sky of Consciousness, because it possesses the characteristic mark of a reflection, namely, that it cannot manifest separately from the medium of reflection, and that it manifests mingled with other reflections. The cause of reflection is the Supreme Lord's power, otherwise called freedom. Free and completely full is this Lord, the omnipresent Bhairava. There is nothing that exists that He does direct as a flint-strike — is Pratyabhijñā.

When you see that the sky does is the space that allows them to be, you have understood. Consciousness is the sky. Thoughts, feelings, the world — clouds. And the sky has never been touched by a single one.

The mirror has no edge. The reflection has no independent life. When this lands as direct perception — the search ends. You were never separate from what you sought. The seeking was the mirror looking at itself.
