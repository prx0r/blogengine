# pain is juice

You know the feeling. A song you should turn off but can't. A movie you know will wreck you but you watch anyway. That ache in your chest when something beautiful happens and you can't explain why it hurts so good.

What is that?

A philosopher from a thousand years ago called it *rasa*. The juice. The flavor. The taste that remains when an emotion has been fully felt and released. He said there are nine of them. Grief. Terror. Disgust. Wrath. Wonder. Laughter. Courage. Tenderness. Peace. Every feeling you have ever run from — held, blessed, turned into something you would pay to feel again.

---

> Aesthetic experience is the arousal of the vitality of consciousness. This vitality is the source of life and so emission is all things. The aesthetic delight of Śiva's power of emission is the same rasa that is tasted in aesthetic experience — whether in music, poetry, or drama.

Let that land. The same thing that makes you cry at a film is the same thing that makes flowers grow. Your tears were never yours. They are the universe tasting itself through you.

---

Now think about grief. Not the grief you push away — the grief you let sit in your chest like a stone. Its full weight pressing down. And you are just watching it. Not fighting. Not fixing. Just watching.

The grief does not change. You change. From clenched to open. From fist to cup.

---

> The goddess who is the Absolute is the aesthetic rapture — *camatkāra* — of the sovereignty of the Lord's unsurpassed freedom.

The wonder you experience at a sunset is the same wonder that makes sunsets possible. You are not having an aesthetic experience. You are being one. The universe is producing itself as beauty, and you are the part of it that has woken up enough to notice.

---

> Camatkṛti means the act of a person savouring — the bliss of full fruition. Unlike a glutton who swallows without tasting, one lets the experience rest on awareness itself, letting the subjective side predominate over the object.

Tasting versus consuming. The emotion is the wine. The witness is the tasting. The pain is the grape. The beauty is the harvest.

> The state of wonder in its highest form is both the act of savouring and the absolute independence of consciousness — the savouring of which is a savouring of one's own self and one's own bliss, beyond the mediation of objects, beyond even the momentary transcendence of poetry and theatre.

You — the one who witnesses, who lets the emotion land and rest and dissolve. Never touched by the fire but holding the space for it to burn.

---

> Just by abiding in their own innate and spontaneous aesthetic delight, they have attained oneness with the aesthetic delight of consciousness. The one who rests in the power of bliss becomes the bliss itself.

Rest in the feeling. Not fighting. Not holding. Not analyzing. Just resting — like you would rest in warm water, in a loved one's arms, in a song that breaks your heart open and leaves you grateful for every crack.

---

When the "I" that claims the emotion drops away — when you stop saying "I am sad" and notice "there is sadness" — the grief becomes rasa. The pain becomes beauty. Grief still hurts. The one who hurts has also recognized itself as the one who witnesses the hurt. The witness is made of something grief cannot touch.

Ride it. Let it crest. Let it crash. Let it dissolve. Another wave is already forming. And you will be there to witness that one too.
