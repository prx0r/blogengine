# the engine of consciousness

There is a pulse happening in you right now that is not your heartbeat. It is not your breath moving through your lungs. It is something more fundamental — the substrate of every thought you have ever thought, every sensation you have ever felt, every flicker of awareness you have ever known.

Most people go their entire lives without noticing it. The Tantric masters of Kashmir built an entire system around it. They called it Spanda — the hidden pulse that controls everything you experience. And once you learn to feel it, the way you experience reality changes completely.

> *yasyonmeṣa-nimeṣābhyāṃ jagataḥ pralayodayau*
> *taṃ śakti-cakra-vibhava-prabhavaṃ śaṅkaraṃ stumaḥ*
>
> "We praise the Beneficent Source of the all-pervasive activity of the Wheel of the Powers of Consciousness, the expansion of which is the arising of the world of one's experience and the contraction of which is its dissolution."

A wheel. Hub — awareness itself. Spokes — will, cognition, action. The hub in motion.

Kallata's own words: "The cause of the creation and dissolution of the world of your experience is none other than your own true nature, which is Divine; and this true nature, whose body is Consciousness, gives rise to the state of sovereignty over the Wheel of Powers."

Abhinavagupta, writing in the Tantraloka three centuries later, gave this pulse a precise anatomy:

> "It is called vitality — *prāṇanā*. It is the inner exertion which impels and fuels the activity of mind, senses and body. Also called life, it is just the bare vital force alone. This vitalizing power of the breath, which is the will of the inner exertion of consciousness, is variously called vibration — *spanda* — effulgence, repose, the living being, the Heart and intuition. This activity of the vital breath lays hold of the body in five ways, as the exhaled breath and the rest. It is because of this that the body appears to be full of consciousness."

Six names for one thing. When you are about to speak and the word is already there before your mouth moves — that pulse. When a thought arrives from somewhere you did not consciously send for it — that pulse. When you reach for a glass and the hand is already moving before the intention fully forms — that pulse.

Now: say a word out loud. Any word. Feel how the breath has to move, how the throat shapes the air, how the sound carries intention. Now imagine doing that with a syllable chosen specifically because its vibration matches the pulse you just felt. That is mantra — the pulse itself taking shape as sound:

> "All Mantras consisting of seed syllables and syllabic groups are the dynamic pulsing nature — *spandanātmatā* — of consciousness. Thus, a Mantra serves as a means to attain Supreme Consciousness because such is its nature. Mantra is the pulsation of consciousness within the flow of the breath. Purifying thought, it induces penetration and absorption into the energy of Śiva consciousness. The pure spontaneity of the freedom of this reflective awareness, free of objectivity because it generates it, effortlessly develops into the Inexplicable, where there is nothing to do."

The mantra works because it IS the pulse you started with — shaped into sound, riding the breath. The breath moves, the mantra moves, the pulse moves both. The syllable and the throbbing at your core are a single woven thrum. Say it again, slower this time. Feel where it resonates.

The Tantraloka traced this pulse into the body's most ordinary activity — perception itself:

> "The rhythm of sensory activity, the pulse of conjunction and separation of the senses with their objects, is essentially the pulse — *spanda* — of consciousness. That is its nature and root cause, intimately connected to the movement of the breath. The pulse of cognitive consciousness is essentially its movement from one object to another, which is thus conjoined and disjoined in the sphere of sensorial consciousness."

Every perception a pulse. Every thought. Every breath. *Unmesha* — opening. *Nimesha* — closing. The wheel turns, and you call it experience. Your eyes see because the pulse sees through them.

If the senses pulse with spanda, mantras work the same way — they attune to the same innate rhythm, the same thrum at the base of experience:

> "The universal pulse of the Heart of consciousness is the vitality of Mantra — *mantravīrya*. The self-awareness from which all things have flowed forth and dissolved away within the Heart, present in the beginning and end of perception, is called according to the Spanda teachings the universal vibration of consciousness — *sāmānyaspanda*. This vibration is that subtle motion which is the non-different radiance, the wave of the ocean of consciousness. Mantra is effective because it is one with this pulsation. Spanda Kārikā confirms: 'Mantras, attaining that innate power — which is unveiled Awareness — are endowed with divine power, omniscience and omnipotence, and thus perform their functions of bestowing grace. The senses of an embodied being function likewise, through accessing the innate power of awareness.'"

A tuning fork across a room begins to ring when its note is struck. The mantra meets spanda the same way — by resonance. The way your own voice makes a glass tremble without touching it.

What does this pulse feel like when it becomes constant, unbroken? The Tantraloka answers with two images that refuse spiritual abstraction:

> "The plane of the Belly of the Fish is that of the universal pulsation of consciousness. It is likened to the throb of the belly of a fish when it is pulled out of the water. Individual consciousness at this level, pulled out of the waters of transmigratory existence, pulses in unison with the rhythm of universal consciousness. The pulsation of the genitals of a female donkey when she is having an orgasm suggests that this pulsation is also experienced during sexual climax, although it is fleeting for those who are not accomplished Yogis. The essence of consciousness is the Lordship of its unlimited and completely full freedom."

A fish on the riverbank — the whole body one single throb, every part of it alive in the same rhythm. That is what awareness feels like when it stops identifying with its contents and becomes pure pulse. The second image is deliberately jarring: spanda is a raw, incandescent thrum that shudders through every living body at its most alive moments. The yogi stabilises this into permanence.

The same pulse connects the smallest breath to the largest architecture of reality:

> "This is the power of Time, established in the vital breath. The breath is established in the universal pulsation of consciousness, which is within the Void, which is within consciousness. Thus, all things are established within consciousness. The Void is the perceiver of the emptiness of deep sleep."

Time → Breath → Spanda → Void → Consciousness. The pulse: the middle link between the breath you feel right now and the stillness that holds it. The breath is time itself — condensed into rhythm, a thread connecting the smallest exhale to the architecture of stars.

And every pulse along this chain is real. A fact of the body. A fact of the breath:

> "Thus, the emanation and withdrawal of all things is established in consciousness, and so the innumerable pulsations of consciousness along the cosmic Path up to Śakti are real. Because time is serially established in consciousness itself, those pulsations of consciousness consisting of emanation and withdrawal along the Path encompass many secondary pulsations, in accord with the teaching imparted previously. The six-fold Path is grounded in consciousness in the same way as that of Time, mediated through the same series. Thus, he opens the door for the practice of awareness in the course of engaging in outer practices."

The heartbeat in your wrist. The thought forming behind your eyes. The light shaping itself into this page. Each is a real pulse of consciousness — a drum that has never stopped. The universe is a drum.

And all these pulses are waves of one pulse:

> "The centre is the Heart, the pulsing core of consciousness. Wave upon wave of its universal pulsation — *sāmānyaspanda* — arises from the ocean of the Heart and merges back into it. Each is an individual pulsation that forms as the beginning and end of the universal pulsation of which they are a part. The sixth is complete expansion — *pūrṇavikāsa* — which is experienced as the Cosmic Bliss of the universal activity of consciousness in the fully liberated state."

Your breath. Your thought. This room. A wave forms, peaks, dissolves. Another forms. All of it happens within the one ocean that never moves.

If the pulse is universal, is it mechanical? The tradition answers with a word that changes how you hear the entire teaching:

> "Play — *krīḍa* — is vibration (*spanda*) seeking out joy. Wonder — *camatkāra* — is a cosmic oscillation, *spanda*, a back and forth, giving rise eventually to intentionality that becomes language. The joy in playing is wonder, and wonder is the primordial vibration underlying all things, calling up simply the delight of life. No goal in mind for creation, just play. Somānanda illustrates this divine freedom with a simile of a king who pretends to be a limited foot soldier simply in order to experience the joy of play."

The universe pulses — pulsing is what joy does when it moves. The engine is a dancer — the dance sustains itself, needing nothing beyond its own motion.

The Spanda Karika says what happens when this recognition lands:

> "The One who ordains all things, when entreated with an impulse of pure desire, causes the moon and sun to rise and bestows those goals residing in the heart of an embodied being who is awake. In the same way, even while dreaming, that One residing in the Centre always clearly reveals all desired things and goals, because that One never disregards any loving entreaty."

When you know yourself as the pulse — desire becomes creative power. The sun rises because the pulse wills it. The moon follows because the pulse continues into everything.

Let Abhinavagupta have the last word: "The sixth bliss is complete expansion — the Cosmic Bliss of the universal activity of consciousness in the fully liberated state."
