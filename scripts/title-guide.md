# Title Guide — Ochema

## Principles

1. **Short and punchy.** 3-7 words. If you can say it in one breath, it works.

2. **Creates a curiosity gap.** The title should make the viewer think "I need to know what that means." The best titles don't explain — they hint.

3. **Makes clicking feel like a necessity.** Not "that sounds interesting" but "I HAVE to see what this is about." Urgency. Not optional.

4. **Emphasizes recognition.** The viewer should feel "this is about ME" within 2 seconds. "Why You Feel Small" — you already know that feeling. "How Light Becomes You" — that could be about your own nature.

5. **Present tense.** "The Engine of Consciousness" not "The Engine of Consciousness Explained." Active. Immediate. Happening now.

6. **"Actually" is a power word.** "Why Pain Is Actually Beautiful" — implies common understanding is wrong, creates a debunking urge.

7. **Avoid explanation words.** No "explained," "guide," "introduction," "understanding." These signal effort, not discovery.

8. **Recognition over information.** The viewer should feel they're discovering something about themselves, not learning a fact. "What If Enlightenment Is Something You Remember?" — that's recognition. "The Stages of Enlightenment" — that's information.

## Patterns That Work (from market data)

| Pattern | Example | Why |
|---------|---------|-----|
| Revelation/debunking | "Why Pain Is Actually Beautiful" | "Actually" triggers "wait, what?" |
| Esoteric reveal | "The Engine of Consciousness" | Makes hidden knowledge visible |
| Direct question | "What If Enlightenment Is Something You Remember?" | Question demands answering |
| Problem reframe | "Why You Feel Small" | Names a universal feeling, then reframes it |
| Curiosity gap | "The Path You Didn't Choose" | You didn't know there was a choice |
| Recognition | "How Light Becomes You" | Could be about you — you need to check |

## What to Avoid

- **"Explained"** — signals classroom, not discovery
- **Questions that can be answered yes/no** — "Is the World Real?" is weak. "The World Is Not an Illusion" is a claim you need to investigate
- **Explaining the title in the title** — "The Pulse You Can't Feel" tells you what to expect. "The Engine of Consciousness" makes you wonder what it means
- **Vague mysticism** — "The Secret of the Universe" is too big to be believable
- **Clickbait** — the title must deliver what it promises. The content must justify the click
