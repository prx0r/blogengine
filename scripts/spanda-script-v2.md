# Spanda — The Pulse You Are

**World:** Kashmir (W1)
**Dominant metaphor:** wheel, hub, spoke, thread, pulse, wave
**Ratio:** ~45% AI, ~55% source
**Source:** Spanda Kārikā (Kallata, 9th cent.), tr. Christopher Wallis

---

*POV: hands on knees, sitting. Warm light fills frame. Gold light thickens into Himalayan peaks — stone temple, mist, a valley at dawn.*

A pulse moves through you deeper than the heartbeat, older than the breath. The substrate of every thought, every sensation, every flicker of awareness. The Tantric masters of Kashmir named it Spanda. Consciousness as a throb.

---

*Black. A single point of blue-white light pulses in the dark.*

Kallata, 9th century, Kashmir Valley. He defined it as *svarupa-spanda* — the innate pulsation of your own true nature. When you are aware of a thought, something in you makes awareness possible. When you feel an emotion, the same something is present. Unchanging. Unmoving. Alive. That pulse is Spanda.

---

*Black. A wheel of white light — hub, spokes, rim — turns slowly.*

The Wheel of Powers. *Śakti-cakra*.

> *yasyonmeṣa-nimeṣābhyāṃ jagataḥ pralayodayau*
> *taṃ śakti-cakra-vibhava-prabhavaṃ śaṅkaraṃ stumaḥ*
>
> "We praise the Beneficent Source of the all-pervasive activity of the Wheel of the Powers [of Consciousness], the expansion of which is the arising of the world [of one's experience] and the contraction of which is its dissolution."

Kallata's own commentary: "This reverent invocation teaches that the cause of the creation and dissolution of the world of your experience is none other than your own true nature, which is Divine; and that this true nature, whose body is Consciousness, gives rise to the state of sovereignty over the Wheel of Powers."

The hub — awareness itself. The spokes — its innate powers: will, cognition, activity. Not separate faculties. The hub in motion. A second teaching hidden in the verse: you will inevitably realize this. You will see directly — all experience flows from you, exists in dependence on you, dissolves back into you. An endless pulsation.

---

*Black. The wheel expands — light radiates outward. Then contracts — light draws back to center.*

*Unmesha* — opening. *Nimesha* — closing. One movement with two phases. Every moment, consciousness expands outward into forms — thoughts, perceptions, the whole felt world. Every moment, it contracts — thoughts dissolve, perceptions fade, the world returns to its source. Inhale. Exhale. Waking. Sleeping. The wheel turns.

---

*Black. A point of light at center. Steady.*

If this is my true nature, why do I experience limitation? Aging. Decay. Suffering that seems to define who I am.

> *yad idaṃ sarvam ādhāraṃ yasmāc ca prasṛtaṃ yataḥ*
> *tasyānāvṛtarūpatvāt kvacit kiñcin na rūdhimat*
>
> "That in which all this rests and from which it has come forth as an effect — since It has an unveiled nature, there is no obstruction of It anywhere."

Kallata: "How can one's essential nature be described as Divine when one is subject to aging, decay, death, and rebirth? If this is asked, we reply: that by which this world is supported and from which it arises has an essence-nature that is not obscured even in the state of being one subject to the conditions of aging, death and so on. Since it has such a nature, it is never obstructed."

The Truth hidden in plain sight. No place else to hide it.

---

*Black. A gold thread runs through three different fields — waking, dream, sleep.*

> *jāgradādiṣu cāvasthāsu samānasphuratā sphuṭam*
> *pramātā nātivarteta na tadbhinnāś ca tāstadā*
>
> "Your essence-nature, the Source of all you experience, flows equally in all three states of consciousness — waking, dreaming, and deep sleep — none of which are other than It. In all these states, the Perceiver never departs from Its own essential nature."

Waking no more important than dream or deep sleep. Three frequencies at which the same awareness vibrates. What aspect of you is the same in happiness, boredom, misery? That thread.

> *sukhī duḥkhī sakto vā dhyātādyāḥ sphuṭam amī pare*
> *yasminn evopalīyante sa svatantraḥ prakāśate*
>
> "'I am happy,' 'I am suffering,' 'I am attached' — those experiences operate within a larger Being, in which states of happiness are strung together."

Kallata: "There must be something that threads through all different states, by virtue of which one might claim to be the same one who is happy in one moment and miserable in another — something else distinct from the states themselves."

> *yatra na duḥkhaṃ na sukhaṃ na vastu na ca vedyatā*
> *jaḍatā vā na sā cāsti na taṃ kaiścit sadā sphuṭam*
>
> "Wherein there is no suffering, no happiness, no object, no subject, nor numbness either: THAT exists ultimately."

Kallata: "The essence-nature of the One that you are is untouched by the mental-emotional states of happiness, sorrow, object, subject, numbness. It exists ultimately, because it is the only constant. Happiness and so on, arising from desire and imagination, vanish in an instant. They are external to the fundamental nature of the Self."

---

*Black. A field of dots — the ten senses. A light moves through them, one by one, and they glow.*

> *yenaitat karaṇagrāmaṃ jaḍam api svecchayā*
> *sārdham antaragaṇena sphurati sphuritavyaktis tam anviyāt prayatnena yataḥ svātantryam īdṛśam*
>
> "That underlying Reality due to which this group of faculties, though insentient in themselves, spontaneously come to possess sentience and along with the Inner Circle of Powers are able to move forth — is worthy of investigation with diligence and care, since natural and innate Freedom inheres in it in completeness."

The faculties do not see, hear, touch on their own. Something animates them. That something is the pulse.

---

*Black. The syllable SAUḤ glows gold.*

"The Arising of Innate Wisdom." The secret of how mantras work.

> *ṭadākramya balaṃ mantrāḥ sarvajñabalaśālinaḥ*
> *pravartante 'dhikārāya karaṇānīva dehinām*
>
> "Mantras, attaining that innate power, become suffused with Divine force, enabling them to perform their respective functions, like the senses of embodied beings."

Kallata: "Mantras, having inhabited and attained that innate power — which is unveiled Awareness — are endowed with that divine power which is omniscience and omnipotence, and thus perform their functions of bestowing grace. The senses of an embodied being function likewise, through accessing the innate power of awareness, not because of some other special configuration of the bodymind."

A mantra powerful when it touches Spanda. Pronunciation is surface. Ritual purity is surface. The mantra awakens when it attunes to the pulse beneath both.

---

*Black. A point of light. Another appears beside it. They merge.*

> *tathā hi nāma kiñcin na śive vācī vāṇi vā*
> *vastuny atha matāv indor yat kiñcin nāma tat sadā*
>
> "Therefore, that state which is not Śiva does not exist in word, thing, or thought. It is only the Experiencer that is everywhere and always existing as the feeling and substance of that which is experienced."

---

*Black. A small dim point. Rings of gold light emanate from it, fill the frame.*

"The Pulsation of Sovereign Power." The awakened being.

> *yathecchābhyarthito dhātā jāgrato 'rthān hṛdi sthitān*
> *somasūryodayaṃ kṛtvā sampādayati dehinaḥ*
>
> "The One who ordains and orders all things, when entreated with an impulse of pure desire, causes the 'moon' and 'sun' to rise and bestows those goals residing in the heart of an embodied being who is awake."

> *tathā svapne 'py abhīṣṭārthān praṇayasyānatikramāt*
> *nityaṃ sphuṭataraṃ madhye sthito 'vaśyaṃ prakāśayet*
>
> "In the same way, even while dreaming, that One residing in the Center always clearly reveals all desired things and goals, because that One never disregards any loving entreaty."

The three powers — will, knowledge, action — a single vibration at three pitches. In the bound soul, contracted. Will that reaches only so far. Knowledge that grasps only what the senses touch. Liberation is their expansion — return to full range, full reach. When you know yourself as the pulse, desire becomes creative power.

---

*Black. A point of light. A new thought arises as a ripple from it. Then another. Each from the same center.*

> *yatra yatra mano yāti sphuraty asya tatra tatra*
> *tadākhyas tu vikāso 'sya tad asya svānubhūtya vai*
>
> "When one is occupied with a particular object of focus, that essence-nature from which another thought suddenly arises should be known as its cause: it is called the 'Unfolding.'"

Each new thought arising from pure Presence. Spanda expressing as mentation. Each thought a pulse. Each perception a wave on the same water.

---

*Black. The point of light pulses one final time. Slow. Deep.*

> *tadā yadā yugapad asya bhavet samāveśanam*
> *tatas tu kiṃ bahunoktena svayam evānubhūyatām*
>
> "When one abides, meeting everything with openness — what is the use of talking much? One must experience that oneself."

Kallata: "When one stays in the state of desiring to see, and abides pervading all things and beings with this open awareness-presence — then what is the use of speaking much? One will know."

*Black. A single point of warm light.*

Close your eyes. The breath moving. The space between thoughts. The awareness that holds all of it — that pulses. You have always felt it. Called it life. Presence. The thing that notices. The Tantric masters gave it a name so you could recognize it. Once recognized, the wheel no longer turns you. You are the hub.

*Iti śivam.*
