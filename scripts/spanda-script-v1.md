# Spanda — The Pulse You Are

**Format:** Ochema video script
**World:** Kashmir (W1 — Himalayan peaks from gold light)
**Estimated duration:** ~16 min
**Source:** Spanda Kārikā (Kallata, 9th cent.), tr. Christopher Wallis

---

## SEGMENT 1 — DOORWAY + HOOK

*POV: hands on knees, sitting. Warm light fills frame. Gold light thickens into Himalayan peaks.*

A pulse moves through you right now — deeper than the heartbeat, older than the breath. Not the chambers of the heart contracting. Not air moving through the lungs. Something more fundamental. The substrate of every thought you have ever thought. Every sensation. Every flicker of awareness.

The Tantric masters of Kashmir called it Spanda. Consciousness as a throb.

---

*Black. A single point of blue-white light pulses in the dark.*

Kallata, a 9th-century master from the Kashmir Valley, defined it as *svarupa-spanda* — the innate pulsation of your own true nature. When you are aware of a thought, something in you makes awareness possible. When you feel an emotion, the same something is present. Unchanging. Unmoving. Alive.

That pulse is Spanda.

---

## SEGMENT 2 — THE WHEEL OF POWERS

*Black. A wheel of light appears — hub, spokes, rim — turning slowly.*

The Spanda Karika. A text composed 1,150 years ago in the Kashmir Valley. First Flow: "The Pulsation of One's True Nature."

It opens with the Wheel of Powers. *Śakti-cakra*.

> *yasyonmeṣa-nimeṣābhyāṃ jagataḥ pralayodayau*
> *taṃ śakti-cakra-vibhava-prabhavaṃ śaṅkaraṃ stumaḥ*
>
> "We praise the Beneficent Source of the all-pervasive activity of the Wheel of the Powers [of Consciousness], the expansion of which is the arising of the world [of one's experience] and the contraction of which is its dissolution."

Kallata's own commentary: "This reverent invocation teaches that the cause of the creation and dissolution of the world of your experience is none other than your own true nature, which is Divine; and that this true nature, whose body is Consciousness, gives rise to the state of sovereignty over the Wheel of Powers."

The wheel as metaphor. The hub — uncreated essential nature, awareness itself. The spokes — the powers innate to that awareness: will, cognition, activity. These are not separate faculties. They are the hub in motion.

A second teaching hidden in the verse. Kallata says you will inevitably realize your true nature. You will see directly that all you experience flows forth from you, exists in dependence on you, dissolves back into you. An endless pulsation.

---

*Black. The wheel expands, light radiating outward. Then contracts, light drawing back to center.*

*Unmesha* — opening. *Nimesha* — closing. The entire universe of your experience arises in dependence on what you are, as an expression of what you are. There is no universe apart from you. Everything you experience is an expression of your true nature.

---

## SEGMENT 3 — THE NATURE THAT CANNOT BE VEILED

*Black. A point of light at center. Steady. Unwavering.*

A question arises. If this is my true nature, why do I experience limitation? Aging. Decay. Death. The states of happiness, suffering, attachment that seem to define who I am.

Kallata answers directly:

> *yad idaṃ sarvam ādhāraṃ yasmāc ca prasṛtaṃ yataḥ*
> *tasyānāvṛtarūpatvāt kvacit kiñcin na rūdhimat*
>
> "That in which all this rests and from which it has come forth as an effect — since It has an unveiled nature, there is no obstruction of It anywhere."

His explanation: "How can one's essential nature be described as Divine when one is subject to aging, decay, death, and rebirth? If this is asked, we reply: that by which this world is supported and from which it arises has an essence-nature that is not obscured even in the state of being one subject to the conditions of aging, death and so on. Since it has such a nature, it is never obstructed."

The Truth is hidden in plain sight. There is nowhere else to hide it.

---

*Black. Three horizontal bands appear — waking, dream, sleep — faint, layered.*

> *jāgradādisu cāvasthāsu samānasphuratā sphuṭam*
> *pramātā nātivarteta na tadbhinnāśca tāstadā*
>
> "Your essence-nature, the Source of all you experience, flows equally in all three states of consciousness — waking, dreaming, and deep sleep — none of which are other than It. In all these states, the Perceiver never departs from Its own essential nature."

The waking state is no more important than dream or deep sleep. All three are frequencies at which awareness vibrates. The nature of awareness can be discovered through investigating what is the same in all three states. Or more simply: what aspect of you is the same in happiness, boredom, and misery?

The underlying Perceiver, formless Awareness-Presence, never deviates from its true nature in any mental-emotional state.

---

*Black. A thread — thin, gold — runs through three different colored fields.*

Kallata's verses:

> *sukhī duḥkhī sakto vā dhyātādyāḥ sphuṭam amī pare*
> *yasminn evopalīyante sa svatantraḥ prakāśate*
>
> "'I am happy,' 'I am suffering,' 'I am attached,' and so on — those experiences clearly operate within another, larger Being, in which states of happiness are strung together."

His commentary: "There must be something that threads through all different states, by virtue of which one might claim to be the same one who is happy in one moment and miserable in another, or desirous and then at peace — something else distinct from the states themselves."

> *yatra na duḥkhaṃ na sukhaṃ na vastu na ca vedyatā*
> *jaḍatā vā na sā cāsti na taṃ kaiścit sadā sphuṭam*
>
> "Wherein there is no suffering, no happiness, no object, no subject, nor numbness either: THAT exists ultimately."

His commentary: "The essence-nature of the One that you are is that which is untouched by the mental-emotional states of happiness, sorrow, object, subject, numbness, and so on. It exists in an ultimate sense, because it is the only constant. Happiness and so on, arising from desire and imagination, apt to vanish in an instant, are external to the fundamental nature of the Self."

---

## SEGMENT 4 — THE INNER POTENCY

*Black. A field of dots — the ten senses — then a light moves through them, one by one, and they glow.*

> *yenaitat karaṇagrāmaṃ jaḍam api svecchayā*
> *sārdham antaragaṇena sphurati sphuritavyaktis tam anviyāt prayatnena yataḥ svātantryam īdṛśam*
>
> "That underlying Reality due to which this group of faculties, though insentient in themselves, spontaneously come to possess sentience and along with the Inner Circle of Powers are able to move forth — is worthy of investigation with diligence and care, since natural and innate Freedom inheres in it in completeness."

Kallata: "There exists that inner Potency which infuses consciousness in that which is by itself unconscious, such as the group of sense-faculties, due to which they, along with the Inner Circle, are able to move forth, remain, and withdraw sensual experience."

The faculties do not see, hear, touch on their own. Something animates them. That something is the pulse.

---

## SEGMENT 5 — THE SECOND FLOW: MANTRA

*Black. The syllable SAUḤ glows gold.*

The Second Flow. "The Arising of Innate Wisdom." It reveals the secret of how mantras work.

> *ṭadākramya balaṃ mantrāḥ sarvajñabalaśālinaḥ*
> *pravartante 'dhikārāya karaṇānīva dehinām*
>
> "Mantras, attaining that innate power, become suffused with Divine force, enabling them to perform their respective functions, like the senses of embodied beings."

Kallata: "Mantras, having inhabited and attained that innate power — which is unveiled Awareness — are endowed with that divine power which is omniscience and omnipotence, and thus perform their functions of bestowing grace and so on. The senses of an embodied being function likewise, through accessing the innate power of awareness, not because of some other special configuration of the bodymind."

A mantra is powerful when it touches Spanda. Pronunciation is surface. Ritual purity is surface. The mantra awakens because it attunes to the pulse beneath both.

Abhinavagupta in the Tantraloka says all mantras — every seed syllable, every sacred formula — are the dynamic pulsing nature of consciousness. *Spandanātmatā*. When the last thought-construction falls away, the mantra becomes the reflective awareness of uncreated I-consciousness. It vibrates in the substrate — deeper than the throat, below the breath.

---

*Black. A point of light. Another appears beside it. They merge.*

> *tathā hi nāma kiñcin na śive vācī vāṇi vā*
> *vastuny atha matāv indor yat kiñcin nāma tat sadā*
>
> "Therefore, that state which is not Śiva does not exist in word, thing, or thought. It is only the Experiencer that is everywhere and always existing as the feeling and substance of that which is experienced."

Kallata: "This Self is indeed everything, by virtue of the fact that it is the source of all entities and states that it experiences. Because there is awareness only of that which is being experienced, one grasps all external things being experienced as aspects of oneself."

---

## SEGMENT 6 — THE THIRD FLOW: SOVEREIGN POWER

*Black. A small dim point. Rings of gold light emanate from it, fill the frame.*

The Third Flow. "The Pulsation of Sovereign Power." It describes the awakened being.

> *yathecchābhyarthito dhātā jāgrato 'rthān hṛdi sthitān*
> *somasūryodayaṃ kṛtvā sampādayati dehinaḥ*
>
> "The One who ordains and orders all things, when entreated with an impulse of pure desire, causes the 'moon' and 'sun' to rise and bestows those goals residing in the heart of an embodied being who is awake."

> *tathā svapne 'py abhīṣṭārthān praṇayasyānatikramāt*
> *nityaṃ sphuṭataraṃ madhye sthito 'vaśyaṃ prakāśayet*
>
> "In the same way, even while dreaming, that One residing in the Center always clearly reveals all desired things and goals, because that One never disregards any loving entreaty."

Kallata's commentary: "In precise accordance with the impulse of pure desire had in the waking state by a yogi whose true nature is not yet fully revealed, whatever thing or goal she desires is clearly seen in the midst of countless other things, the 'moon' and 'sun' having risen."

The three powers — will, knowledge, action — are a single vibration at three pitches. In the bound soul, contracted. Will that reaches only so far. Knowledge that grasps only what the senses touch. Liberation is the expansion of these powers — their return to full range, full reach.

When you know yourself as the pulse, your desire becomes creative power.

---

*Black. A figure stands. Luminous. Quiet.*

> *sarvaṃ paśyan sadā jāgra tatparaḥ sarvadā bhavet*
> *sarvatraikātmakaṃ kṛtvā nikṣipann ādadhad bhavet*
> *nodvegam upagacchet tu parasmai tasmai dīyate*
>
> "Perceiving the whole field with wisdom, that one remains ever awakened. Always directing everything to the One, depositing everything in the One, attributing everything to the One, she is never oppressed by another."

Kallata: "The state of being awakened is that state in which one abides at all times with the powers of awareness uncontracted."

---

## SEGMENT 7 — THE UNFOLDING

*Black. A point of light. A new thought arises as a ripple, then another, each from the same center.*

> *yatra yatra mano yāti sphuraty asya tatra tatra*
> *tadākhyas tu vikāso 'sya tad asya svānubhūtya vai*
>
> "When one is occupied with a particular object of focus, that essence-nature from which another thought suddenly arises should be known as its cause: it is called the 'Unfolding.'"

The arising of each new thought — from the pure Presence of your innermost being. That is Spanda expressing as mentation. Each thought a pulse. Each perception a wave.

---

*Black. The point of light pulses one final time. Slow. Deep.*

> *tadā yadā yugapad asya bhavet samāveśanam*
> *tatas tu kiṃ bahunoktena svayam evānubhūyatām*
>
> "When one abides, meeting everything with openness — well, what is the use of talking much? One must experience that oneself."

Kallata: "When one stays in the state of desiring to see, and abides pervading all things and beings with this open awareness-presence — then what is the use of speaking much? One will know and experience the Reality."

---

*Black. A single point of warm light.*

Close your eyes. Feel the breath moving. The space between thoughts. The awareness that holds all of it — that pulses.

You have always felt it. Called it life. Presence. The thing that notices. The Tantric masters gave it a name so you could recognize it.

Once recognized, it stays. Because it is what you are.

*Iti śivam.*
