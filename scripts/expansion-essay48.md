# the bodies you're already wearing

---

You have three bodies. You know only one of them. The second is made of life force — it grows, heals, and remembers every wound you have ever received. The third is made of pure consciousness — it travels while you sleep. Steiner mapped all three.

You wake in the morning and assume the body you feel — arms and legs, the ache from yesterday's work, the shadow in the sun — is the whole story. Steiner says the physical body is real, but it is the outermost layer of a structure that descends into levels of reality the physical senses cannot register. The second body — the etheric body, or life body — interpenetrates the physical completely and serves as its architect; all organs are preserved in their form and shape by the currents and movements of the ether body. The physical heart is based upon an 'etheric heart,' the physical brain upon an 'etheric brain,' and so forth.

The ether body is which is what we call death. The ether body is what distinguishes a living organism from a corpse. It is the reason a wound heals: the ether body remembers the original form and guides the cells back toward it.

This ether body, then, is a second member of the human entity. For supersensible cognition, it possesses a higher degree of reality than the physical body. The ether body penetrates the physical completely and it is to be looked upon as a kind of architect of the latter. All organs are preserved in their form and shape by means of the currents and movements of the ether body. The physical heart is based upon an 'etheric heart,' the physical brain upon an 'etheric brain,' and so forth. The ether body is organized like the physical body, only with greater complexity. Wherever in the physical body separated parts exist, in the ether body everything is in living, interweaving motion.

The human being possesses this ether body in common with the plants. The plant has no astral body, no consciousness, no inner subjective experience — but it lives. Its life is the ether body. When you cut a plant, the ether body works to heal the wound. When you cut yourself, the same principle operates at a higher level of organisation. The ether body is the life that resists death.

Picture it: a second you, made of organised life-currents, interpenetrating every cell of your physical body, holding the architecture of your form against the constant pressure of entropy. This second body ages differently from the physical body — it accumulates. Every wound, every illness, every trauma leaves a trace in the ether body. The ether body remembers everything that has ever happened to you.

---

Supersensible observation advances from the ether body to a further member of the human entity. In order to aid the student in forming a visualization of this member, it points to the phenomenon of sleep. All human endeavor rests upon activity in the waking state. This activity, however, is only possible if man again and again gathers new strength for his exhausted forces from sleep. Action and thought disappear in sleep; all suffering, all pleasure are submerged for conscious life. As though out of hidden, mysterious depths, conscious forces arise out of the unconsciousness of sleep as man awakens. It is the same consciousness that sinks into shadowy depths when we go to sleep and arises again when we awaken. The power that awakens life again and again out of a state of unconsciousness is, according to supersensible cognition, the third member of the human entity. We may call it the astral body.

The astral body is the carrier of consciousness. While the ether body is life itself, the astral body is the experience of that life — pleasure, pain, desire, fear, joy, sorrow. In sleep, the astral body withdraws from the physical and etheric bodies. This is why you lose consciousness: the organ of experience has left the building. The physical and etheric bodies remain, breathing, circulating, repairing — but the one who experiences is absent.

Just as the physical body is unable to retain its form by means of the mineral substances and forces contained in it, but only by being interpenetrated by the ether body, so likewise the forces of the ether body are unable, by themselves, to illuminate this body with the light of consciousness. An ether body, left entirely to itself, would have to remain in a continuous state of sleep. We might also say: it could only maintain a plant-existence within the physical body. An awakened ether body is illuminated by an astral body. For sense-observation, the activity of the astral body disappears when man sinks into sleep. For supersensible observation, the astral body still exists, but it appears to be separated or withdrawn from the ether body.

The astral body is the animal within you. Plants have ether bodies; animals have astral bodies. The animal feels, desires, fears, seeks. The human being has all of these — plus something else, something that no animal possesses. The fourth member of his being that supersensible cognition must ascribe to man has nothing in common with the world of the manifest surrounding him. It is what distinguishes him from his fellow-creatures and through which he is the crown of creation belonging to him. This is the ego — the I.

The fourth member is what makes self-consciousness possible. The animal feels pain but does this is the ego.

If the astral body were left to itself, pleasure and pain, feelings of hunger and thirst would take place in it; but what would not occur is the feeling that there is something permanent in all this.

The ego is the thread that runs through the changing states of the astral body. You were the same I yesterday as you are today, even though your feelings have changed. This permanence is the signature of the fourth member.

---

Steiner's sevenfold human constitution maps these four fundamental members — physical body, ether body, astral body, ego — plus three higher members that the human being is evolving toward: spirit self (or *manas*), life spirit (*buddhi*), and spirit man (*atma*). The spirit self is the transformed astral body: what the astral body becomes when it is purified. The life spirit is the transformed ether body. The spirit man is the transformed physical body — though at this level, the word "body" becomes almost misleading. These three higher members remain undeveloped in most of humanity — seeds of the future, the goal toward which initiation points.

---

To perceive the three bodies — etheric, astral, and ego — requires the development of higher cognitive faculties. Steiner calls them Imagination, Inspiration, and Intuition. These are trained modes of perception, as precise as scientific observation but directed at the supersensible. Imagination perceives the etheric and astral bodies as living images. Inspiration perceives the thoughts and intentions of spiritual beings. Intuition achieves direct union with them.

---

You stand now at the midpoint of evolution. You have the physical body, the ether body, the astral body, and the ego. The higher members are seeds that require cultivation. The three bodies you cannot see are the actual structure of your being, as real as your bones and blood.

The Doppelganger (double or twin likeness) of man must, according to a law of the spiritual world, emerge prior to everything else as his first impression in that world.

When a person begins to develop Imagination — the first stage of supersensible perception — the first thing they encounter is the Doppelganger: the sum total of everything the ether body has not yet healed, everything the astral body has not yet purified, everything the ego has not yet integrated.

In this abnormal life like a sort of Doppelganger — soul double — along side this higher ego. It is just in regard to these things that one should not surrender oneself to any sort of self-deception.

The Guardian is a teacher — rather than an enemy. It shows you exactly what you are. The unhealed wounds, the unexamined motives, the habits you thought you had overcome but had only suppressed. The being of light appears only after the double is recognised and integrated.

---

The three bodies you cannot see are the transformed etheric, astral, and physical bodies of a future humanity.

You need only notice that you have already experienced hints of Steiner's system — rather than believe in it. That sense of having travelled somewhere during deep sleep. That feeling of being held in form by something more than bone and muscle. That voice within you that says "I" and means the same self it meant yesterday. These are traces of the bodies you cannot see. They are the beginning — rather than the end of the story.

The path Steiner mapped — from the physical body through the etheric and astral to the ego, and from the ego through Imagination, Inspiration, and Intuition to the higher members — is a path that anyone can walk. It requires discipline, patience, and the courage to meet the Doppelganger without flinching. But the reward is direct perception of the being you actually are — rather than a belief: a fourfold creature standing at the threshold of a sevenfold destiny.
