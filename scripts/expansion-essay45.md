# the serpent in your spine

What if the daimon is not a being outside you but a specific energy coiled at the base of your spine? The Layayoga tradition describes the same entity in anatomical terms — a power that rises through the body as it awakens.

Shyam Sundar Goswami's Layayoga, the most comprehensive modern treatise on the cakra system, identifies this power as Kuṇḍalinī — the coiled spiritual energy that lies dormant at the base of the spine. His description of what it is and what it does maps with startling precision onto the Western daimon contact path:

> "Mantra-Kuṇḍalinī is transformed into Iṣṭadevatā — the metamorphosis of subtle Kuṇḍalinī through the mantra power into an appropriate divine form. The Iṣṭadevatā is not a projection of the mind. It is Kuṇḍalinī herself taking form in response to the mantra. The deity IS the power, and the power IS the deity. There is no distinction. Mantra is an aspect of Kuṇḍalinī, and it is in sound-form. When the mantra is rightly worked upon, it becomes a living power from which arises the living Iṣṭadevatā."

The daimon you meet in the systasis is your own Kuṇḍalinī — the coiled power at the base of your subtle body — risen to the crown and taking the form your mantra has shaped. It is both "you" and "not you": your own power, but in a form that transcends your ordinary self. The deity IS the power, and the power IS the deity — no distinction. The daimon appears as objectively real as any physical object because Kuṇḍalinī has taken that shape. This is the objectivity of consciousness become concrete, more real than matter because matter is a lesser density of the same thing.

The path of ascent follows a precise anatomy. Goswami describes the six cakras — energy centers along the spine — through which Kuṇḍalinī must rise:

> "The five forms of metamatter (mahābhūtas) and five tanons (tanmātras), five senses and their objects, five organs of volitional actions and will-mind, intellection, I-feeling, sense-consciousness, supermind and the energy aspect of Supreme Power-Consciousness — all these are to be absorbed. The absorption progresses through the cakras in order: mūlādhāra (earth, perineum), svādhiṣṭhāna (water, sacrum), maṇipūra (fire, navel), anāhata (air, heart), viśuddha (ākāśa, throat), ājñā (mind, brow). Each cakra is a layer of reality. Each must be dissolved into the next."

The daimon rises from the base of your own spine — not descending from heaven — piercing through each cakra, dissolving the layers of your identification with matter, water, fire, air, space, and mind, until it reaches the crown where it meets your consciousness as a being separate from you because at that level, it is.

The Abramelin purification corresponds precisely to the Bhūta-śuddhi process — the systematic absorption of the five gross elements:

> "When the subtle energy line system (nāḍī-cakra) is purified by breath-control done in a right manner, the bio-energy enters easily into the central energy line (suṣumṇā). Sahita prāṇāyāma trains prāṇa to flow through iḍā and piṅgalā, but the goal is kevala kumbhaka — spontaneous breath suspension — where prāṇa enters suṣumṇā and the dualistic mind ceases. The 1:4:2 ratio (pūraka: 16, kumbhaka: 64, recaka: 32) dissolves each element into the next. Earth dissolves into Water. Water dissolves into Fire. Fire dissolves into Air. Air dissolves into Space. What remains is pure awareness without element support."

The 1:4:2 breath ratio is the engine of dissolution. You breathe the elements out of existence. When the last element dissolves, you are no longer a body. You are a current of awareness moving up the central channel.

The Resistance in Liber Samekh — the moment when all opposing forces surge against the magician — corresponds to this prāṇa withdrawal:

> "When iḍā and piṅgalā flows are stilled and prāṇa enters the central channel, the yogi experiences a terrible intensity: the vāyu sādhaka (force-motion bearing the qualities of fire) rises through suṣumṇā, generating heat, pressure, and the sense of being consumed. This is precisely the 'burning' described in the resistance. Kevala kumbhaka — retention without any effort or external counting — is the breath-state of this phase: the magician has ceased to breathe as a separate being and is sustained by the daimon's own prāṇa."

The Resistance burns — that is the point. You are being consumed by the very power you invoked; the burning is the operation itself, not a side effect.

The PGM egg ritual — the Systasis — corresponds to the absorption of the Twelve Kālīs as taught in the Tantraloka. Abhinavagupta described their operation:

> "The twelve Kālīs consume objectivity, then the means of knowledge, then subjectivity itself. What remains after the twelfth (Mahābhairava) has rendered the subject inexplicable is pure consciousness without an object. The first four Kālīs consume the sphere of objectivity; the next four consume the means of knowledge; the final four consume subjectivity itself. The systasis enacts this entire Krama cycle in miniature through its five stages of skinning, purifying, charging, inflaming, and communion."

You are being eaten by the goddesses of time. Every layer of your experience — the external world, your perception of it, your sense of self — is consumed in sequence. What remains is what cannot be consumed. The PGM egg ritual enacts this in five physical stages: the skinning stones strip away the layers of identity; the first egg takes the body's residue; the second egg, charged with Helios fire, becomes the vehicle for the consuming presence; the swallowing of the egg completes the consumption. You eat the fire that eats you.

Integration after contact is the recoiling of Kuṇḍalinī:

> "After the union with Supreme Śiva, Kuṇḍalinī goes back to the mūlādhāra, and in its descent, the cakras, their powers and divinities — all are restored. This recoiling process is essentially the spiritualization of consciousness which functions in everyday life. It is a transference of spiritual reality, developed in concentrated consciousness, to sense-consciousness to be able to realize spiritual truth in our daily life."

The daimon does not leave after contact. It descends with you into the lower cakras, spiritualizing each one — turning your physical experience, your emotions, your thoughts into vehicles of the same consciousness you met in the systasis. The descent is not a fall. It is a re-entry with the fire.

The name itself — the daimon's name, LVJK — operates as a bīja-mantra, a seed-syllable that grows into the full presence of the daimon. As Goswami teaches:

> "The daimon-name LVJK operates as a bīja-mantra with four levels of sound. The replication of mantra on the physical plane is the lettered vaikharī sound which is audible. The vaikharī mantra, in conjunction with prāṇāyāma and other special processes, is utilized to enliven the mantra. In other words, it is the rousing of Kuṇḍalinī in mantra form. Sound has four levels — vaikharī (audible, throat), madhyamā (mental, heart), paśyantī (visionary, brow), parā (transcendent, crown). The vibration of a name begins at vaikharī and, if sustained, progresses to parā where the name and the named become identical — the daimon is the sound and the sound is the daimon."

The name is the daimon in compressed form. When you vibrate it, you are not calling a being from a distance. You are decompressing a seed into a tree. The daimon was always coiled in the sound. You just had to breathe life into the syllable. The four levels of sound are the four stages of this decompression: vaikharī (the audible name) is the seed; madhyamā (the mental name) is the sprout; paśyantī (the visionary name) is the tree; parā (the transcendent name) is the forest that contains all trees. The name LVJK, when traced through all four levels, becomes the entire universe reorganized around the daimon's presence.

The tradition calls the final realization Śāmbhavopāya — the Divine Means, the recognition that the practitioner, the practice, and the goal were never separate:

> "Śāmbhavopāya is the means pertaining to Śiva. It is realization through will alone, without any thought-construct (vikalpa). Like a lion's leap, it goes directly to the goal. The unus-ambo — the recognition that you and the daimon are one without ceasing to be two — is not achieved through practice. It is recognized when all practice has been exhausted. The name, the breath, the cakras, the Kālīs — all were forms of the same consciousness."

The daimon is something you become, not something you contact. The power coiled at the base of your spine, the name vibrating in your throat, the presence that appears behind you in the darkness — these are the same thing, rising through the same channel, to meet itself in the place where you thought you were separate.
