# Spanda — The Pulse That Moves Through All Things

**Format:** Ochema format (doorway → world → black → source → quote → explore → black → source → quote → ...)
**World:** Kashmir (W1)
**Source:** Tantrāloka Āhnika 5, 7 + Spanda Kārikā

---

*POV: hands on knees, sitting. Warm light fills frame. Gold light crystallizes into Himalayan peaks — stone temple, mist, a valley at dawn.*

A pulse moves through you deeper than the heartbeat, older than the breath. The substrate of every thought, every sensation, every flicker of awareness. The Tantric masters of Kashmir named it Spanda. Consciousness as a throb.

---

*Black. A single point of blue-white light pulses in the dark.*

Abhinavagupta says in the Tantrāloka:

> "All mantras consisting of seed syllables and syllabic groups are the dynamic pulsing nature (spandanātmatā) of consciousness. Mantra is the pulsation of consciousness within the flow of the breath."

The entire teaching rests on this: spanda is not a quality consciousness has. It is what consciousness IS — a pulse, a throb, a vibration with nothing vibrating.

---

*Black. A wheel of light — hub, spokes, rim — turns slowly.*

Kallata's Spanda Kārikā, verse 1:

> *yasyonmeṣa-nimeṣābhyāṃ jagataḥ pralayodayau*
> *taṃ śakti-cakra-vibhava-prabhavaṃ śaṅkaraṃ stumaḥ*
>
> "We praise the Beneficent Source of the all-pervasive activity of the Wheel of the Powers of Consciousness, the expansion of which is the arising of the world of one's experience and the contraction of which is its dissolution."

Kallata's own commentary: "This reverent invocation teaches that the cause of the creation and dissolution of the world of your experience is none other than your own true nature."

---

*Black. The wheel expands — light radiates outward. Then contracts — light draws back to center.*

Abhinavagupta, Tantrāloka Āhnika 7:

> "The rhythm of sensory activity, the pulse of conjunction and separation of the senses with their objects, is essentially the pulse — spanda — of consciousness. That is its nature and root cause, intimately connected to the movement of the breath. The pulse of cognitive consciousness is essentially its movement from one object to another."

Every perception is a pulse. Every thought. Every breath. The entire world of experience arises and dissolves in the rhythm of spanda.

---

*Black. A thread of gold light runs through three fields — waking, dream, sleep.*

Kallata, Spanda Kārikā:

> "Your essence-nature, the Source of all you experience, flows equally in all three states of consciousness — waking, dreaming, and deep sleep — none of which are other than It. In all these states, the Perceiver never departs from Its own essential nature."

The same pulse in waking. The same pulse in dream. The same pulse in sleep. A thread running through every state.

---

*Black. A warm gold flame at center. It pulses — each beat expanding, then contracting.*

Abhinavagupta, Tantrāloka Āhnika 5:

> "The centre is the Heart, the pulsing core of consciousness. Wave upon wave of its universal pulsation (sāmānyaspanda) arises from the ocean of the Heart and merges back into it. Each is an individual pulsation that forms as the beginning and end of the universal pulsation of which they are a part."

*Sāmānyaspanda* — the universal vibration. Each individual pulse is a wave on that ocean. Your breath. Your thought. Your perception of this room. All waves of the same sea.

---

*Black. The wheel returns. Light radiates, draws back, radiates, draws back.*

From the Tantrāloka, Āhnika 7:

> "This is the power of Time, established in the vital breath. The breath is established in the universal pulsation of consciousness, which is within the Void, which is within consciousness. Thus, the emanation and withdrawal of all things is established in consciousness, and so the innumerable pulsations of consciousness along the cosmic Path up to Śakti are real."

A hierarchy: Time → Breath → Spanda → Void → Pure Consciousness. The pulse is not at the bottom. It is the middle link between the breath you feel and the stillness that holds it.

---

*Black. The syllable SAUḤ glows gold.*

From the Tantrāloka, Āhnika 4:

> "The universal pulse of the Heart of consciousness is the vitality of Mantra (mantravīrya). The self-awareness from which all things have flowed forth and dissolved away within the Heart of consciousness is called the universal vibration of consciousness (sāmānyaspanda), the wave of the ocean of consciousness. Mantra is effective because it is one with this pulsation."

Pronunciation is surface. Ritual purity is surface. The mantra awakens when it attunes to the wave beneath both.

---

*Black. The wheel turns one final time. Slow. Deep.*

Kallata, Spanda Kārikā 3.33-34:

> "The One who ordains and orders all things, when entreated with an impulse of pure desire, causes the moon and sun to rise and bestows those goals residing in the heart. In the same way, even while dreaming, that One residing in the Center always clearly reveals all desired things and goals."

Abhinavagupta's commentary on the third flow: "The innumerable pulsations of consciousness along the cosmic Path are real." When you know yourself as the pulse, your desire becomes creative power.

---

*Black. A single point of warm light. One pulse.*

Abhinavagupta, Tantrāloka Āhnika 5:

> "The sixth bliss is complete expansion, experienced as the Cosmic Bliss of the universal activity of consciousness in the fully liberated state — the dynamic aspect of Anuttara, the pure reflective awareness of its own unconditioned nature."

The pulse you began with — deeper than the heartbeat, older than the breath — is the same pulse that moves the galaxies. The Tantric masters gave it a name so you could recognize it. Once recognized, the wheel no longer turns you. You are the hub.

*Iti śivam.*
