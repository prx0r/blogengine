# your prayers are changing reality

The last great philosopher of the ancient world wrote hymns — as technology, not poetry. Each one a precision instrument designed to draw the soul upward through the planetary spheres toward something that cannot be named.

---

> Since everything prays according to its own order and celebrates in hymns the leaders of the whole series — noerically, with words, physically, or perceptibly — the heliotrope too moves to the extent that it is flexible. If someone would be capable of hearing it hitting the air as it turns around, he would observe that by means of this sound it renders to the King a kind of hymn that a plant is capable of singing.

The plant prays by moving toward the sun. Its movement is its worship. And the same is true for the human soul.

Prayer is turning. If prayer is request, then God is a vending machine. But if prayer is turning, then the act itself is the answer.

---

> The act of singing hymns itself as such makes it clear that their noeric activity is turned towards their superior causes, since hymns are sung about superior gods, not about those who are inferior.

Prayer is epistrophē — reversion. The soul returns to its source by the very act of directing its attention toward that source. The hymn is a re-cognition, not a request. The soul recognizes the god, and in that recognition, it recognizes itself as a fragment of that god.

---

> The divine names in the hymns are symbola — tokens placed in the soul by the gods. They are not arbitrary signs but phoneme sequences that are structurally connected to the god. When chanted, they activate the corresponding Mātṛkā circuit. The hymn functions as a sonic yantra: the name IS the deity in sound-form. The practitioner does not call a distant god — they become the god by vibrating the god's name through their own nāḍīs.

A poem talks about a god. A hymn becomes the god. When the practitioner chants the divine names correctly, the subtle body is reshaped into the form of that deity.

Sit with that.

Each hymn is a ladder. The names and epithets are the rungs. The Hymn to Helios begins with the simplest physical aspects of the sun and ascends through celestial and intellectual aspects until it reaches the One, where the hymn dissolves into silence.

The ascent is literal. The physical sun is the outermost manifestation of the same reality as the intellectual sun. To ascend through the hymn is to travel up the chain of being, from effect to cause, from image to source.

The soul does not need to understand the entire Neoplatonic hierarchy before it can pray. It needs only to turn. The hymn provides the direction. The turning does the rest.

The practitioner who has turned toward the god is now in epistrophē — reverted to the source. In that state, the soul is no longer a separate entity making requests. It participates in the god's own life. It sees what the god sees, knows what the god knows, and for a moment, it is what the god is.

You chant to change your being. The heliotrope becomes the sun's most perfect expression of itself in plant form. The hymn makes you the most perfect expression of the god in human form.

When the epistrophē is complete, there is nothing left to ask for. Prayer ends where it began: in silence, which is the only language adequate to the One.

---

*Source: Proclus, Hymns (ed. van den Berg, 2001); De Sacrificio; Chaldean Oracles*
