# the being of light that knows your name

---

What if there is a being of light standing before you right now, made of the same substance as your deepest self, and the entire purpose of spiritual practice is learning to see it clearly?

A real being — composed of light, intelligent, personal — that has been with you since before your birth, not a metaphor or a projection of your unconscious. The Sufis called it the *shakhs nurani*, the person of light. The Hermetic philosophers called it the Perfect Nature. Henry Corbin called it the *unus-ambo* — the two that are one — and he insisted that the entire spiritual life hangs on whether you learn to see it.

---

Between the world of pure spiritual Lights (*Luces victoriales*, the world of the 'Mothers' in the terminology of Ishraq) and the sensory universe, at the boundary of the ninth Sphere (the Sphere of Spheres) there opens a *mundus imaginalis* which is a concrete spiritual world of archetype-Figures, apparitional Forms, Angels of species and of individuals; by philosophical dialectics its necessity is deduced and its plane situated; vision of it in actuality is vouchsafed to the visionary apperception of the active Imagination.

This is the third world — *'alam al-mithal* — where spirit takes on shape and matter becomes diaphanous. You must learn to perceive the being of light with the active Imagination — a cognitive organ as real as the eye, not fantasy — because thinking alone cannot reach it.

---

It is the heavenly entity, the philosopher's Angel, conjoined with his star, which rules him and opens the doors of wisdom for him, teaches him what is difficult, reveals to him what is right, in sleeping as in waking.

The Perfect Nature is a teaching intelligence — rather than an abstract guardian angel. It instructs you while you sleep, reveals solutions while you work, corrects your errors before you make them.

When I wished to bring to light the science of the mystery and modality of Creation, I came upon a subterranean vault filled with darkness and winds... Lo and behold, a person then appeared to me in my sleep in a form of the greatest beauty. He said to me: 'Take a lamp and place it under a glass to shield it from the winds; then it will give thee light in spite of them...' At that I said, 'Who then art thou?' He answered: 'I am thy Perfect Nature. If thou wishest to see me, call me by my name.'

*If thou wishest to see me, call me by my name.* The encounter requires invocation: you call — not wait.

---

Thou, my lord and prince, my most holy angel, my precious spiritual being, Thou art the Spirit who gave birth to me, and Thou art the Child to whom my spirit gives birth... Thou who art clothed in the most brilliant of divine Lights... may Thou manifest Thyself to me in the most beautiful of epiphanies, show me the light of Thy dazzling face, be for me the mediator... lift the veils of darkness from my heart...

The paradox: *Thou art the Spirit who gave birth to me, and Thou art the Child to whom my spirit gives birth.* The being of light is both your origin and your offspring. This reciprocity is the *unus-ambo*.

Having cut in two the thread spun by a spider, the prince puts it together again, saying: 1 × 1. This is also the formula that we suggested above, because he who deciphers it holds the key to the secret that preserves him both from pseudomystical monism (whose formula is 1 = 1) and from abstract monotheism which is content to superimpose an *Ens supremum* on the multitude of beings (n + 1).

1 × 1, not 1 = 1. The two remain two while being one. The being of light is a relationship — rather than a merger. You stand before it, and it before you, and the gaze passes both ways.

My image looks at me with my own look; I look at it with its own look. Their relation is this *unus-ambo* in which each of the two simultaneously assumes the position of the I and the self — image and mirror.

When the circle of the face has become pure, it effuses lights as a spring pours forth its water... Before you, before your face, there is another Face also of light, irradiating lights... In reality this Face is your own face and this sun is the sun of the Spirit (*shams al-ruh*) that goes to and fro in your body. Next, the whole of your person is immersed in purity, and suddenly you are gazing at a person of light (*shakhs nurani*) who is also irradiating lights... This person of light before you is called in Sufi terminology the suprasensory Guide (*moqaddam al-ghayb*).

The sequence is precise: first the face becomes clear, then you see the other Face made of light, then the whole person is immersed. This is the active Imagination opening onto the *mundus imaginalis*.

I fell passionately in love with a young girl... the flame of love within me became extraordinarily intense. My breath exhaled flames of fire. And each time I breathed out fire, lo and behold, from the height of heaven someone was also breathing out fire which came to meet my own breath... at last I understood that it was my witness in Heaven.

The fire of human love became the medium of recognition. Each time he exhaled flame, a flame answered from heaven. The being of light is encountered by transubstantiating desire — rather than by suppressing it.

Then who art thou, whose beauty outshines all other beauty ever contemplated in the terrestrial world? I am thine own Daena. I was loved, thou hast made me more loved still. I was beautiful, thou hast made me still more beautiful.

The Zoroastrian *Daena* appears at the Chinvat Bridge as a beautiful maiden who says: *I was loved, thou hast made me more loved still. I was beautiful, thou hast made me still more beautiful.* Your life adds to its beauty.

Of all spiritual practices... the *dhikr* is the practice most apt to free spiritual energy, that is, to allow the particle of divine light which is in the mystic to rejoin its like... The fire of the *dhikr* is visualized as a pure and ardent blaze, animated by a rapid upward movement... It sets fire to all that is there to be consumed, and sheds light on any darkness it may encounter. If light is there already, the two lights associate with each other and there is light upon light.

The *dhikr* frees the particle of light within the mystic. It rises to meet its like. *Light upon light*. When you speak the name with presence, you are feeding the flame that will become your face at the threshold.

---

What we are speaking of is a counterpart, a celestial, transcendent counterpart, rather than a 'double'; the idea of this heavenly partner is antithetic to the Double... psychological analysis shows this Double to be the manifestation of the personal unconscious, hence belonging to the functions of the lower psyche, that is, the *nafs ammara*, the dark envelope, the shadow, exactly what the *dhikr* has to destroy by fire so that the Guide of light may become visible.

The being of light is distinct from the shadow and the doppelganger — the shadow must be burned away by the fire of *dhikr* before the Guide can appear.

---

The growth of the subtle organism, the physiology of the man of light, progresses through the seven *latifa*, each of which is one of the seven prophets of your being; the cycle of birth and initiatic growth is homologous to the cycle of prophecy.

The seven subtle organs — the *latifa* of Semnani — correspond to the seven prophets of your inner being. The encounter is a growth through stages — rather than a single event.

There is a difference in that the visionary apperception presupposes the opening of the inner eye by the removal of the veil which darkened it, whereas the feeling of mystic experience (*dhawq*) is caused by a transmutation of the being and of the spirit... The five senses are changed into other senses.

The five senses become other senses. The eye learns to see in the dark. The ear learns to hear the silence between sounds. You transfigure the senses you have — rather than adding new ones.

---

The suprasensory Witness, the suprasensory Guide, the suprasensory Scales: this is what you are shown when you close your eyelids. According to whether what appears to you is light or darkness, your witness (*shahid*) is light or darkness.

Close your eyelids — what appears? Light or darkness? That is your witness. The being of light is a presence to be perceived — rather than a doctrine.

---

---

The being of light is a task — rather than a belief. It stands before you now — made of the same substance as your deepest self — waiting for you to close your eyes and begin the work of seeing. The *dhikr* is the fire; the fire burns the shadow; the shadow consumed, the Face appears. The Face is yours and not yours — it is the one you have been calling, and the one who has been calling you. The thread of the spider, cut and rejoined. 1 × 1.

This tradition belongs to anyone who closes their eyes and finds not darkness but a subtle radiance, and in that radiance, the faint outline of a Face — rather than belonging to the past. Once you have seen even the hint of that Face, the spiritual life becomes simple. You are seeking someone who already knows you — rather than seeking enlightenment.
