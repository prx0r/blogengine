# Egyptian Books of the Afterlife Reference Guide

> Based on Erik Hornung's *The Ancient Egyptian Books of the Afterlife*, translated by David Lorton. Cornell University Press, 1999. Written 2026-07-14.

---

## Overview

Hornung's work is the definitive scholarly survey of ancient Egyptian funerary literature, covering texts from the Pyramid Texts (c. 2350 BCE) through the Roman Period. The unifying theme: the **nocturnal solar journey** — the sun god Re travels through the 12 hours of the night, and the deceased king/soul accompanies him, being regenerated through this journey.

---

## Chronological Evolution of the Texts

| Period | Text | Key Innovation |
|---|---|---|
| Old Kingdom (c. 2350) | **Pyramid Texts** | Royal ascent to sky; spells for transformation |
| Middle Kingdom (c. 2050) | **Coffin Texts** | Democratized; Book of Two Ways (first netherworld map) |
| New Kingdom (c. 1550) | **Book of the Dead** | Judgment central; 190 spells |
| Dynasty 18 (c. 1540) | **Amduat** | First systematic 12-hour netherworld geography |
| Post-Amarna (c. 1300) | **Book of Gates** | 12 gates; judgment hall at 5th gate |
| Dynasty 19 (c. 1250) | **Book of Caverns** | 6 sections; Osiris/Re union; punishment emphasis |
| Dynasty 20 (c. 1150) | **Book of the Earth** | Solar barque atop Aker; regeneration |
| Dynasty 20+ | **Books of the Sky** | Solar journey through Nut's body |
| Greco-Roman | **Books of Breathing** | Identity card for the dead |

## The Major Books

### 1. Pyramid Texts (lines 612-816)
- Oldest Egyptian religious texts. 714-739 spells carved in pyramids of Dynasties 5-8.
- **Purpose**: Royal ascent to the sky — the king joins Re as a star.
- **Key features**: Cannibal Hymn (spells 273-274), earliest attestation of Osiris as netherworld ruler. Field of Reeds. Winding Waterway.
- **No fixed netherworld** — the Duat is not yet underground; it includes celestial regions.

### 2. Coffin Texts (lines 817-998)
- Middle Kingdom. 1,183 spells on coffins of officials.
- **Key innovation**: "Book of the Two Ways" — first cosmography of the hereafter.
- **Concepts introduced**: Apophis (sun's enemy), judgment of the dead, Rosetau ("locked in darkness and surrounded by fire"), Lake of Flames, seven guardians of gateways.

### 3. Book of the Dead (lines 1000-1308)
- New Kingdom onward. ~190 spells on papyrus. "Book of Coming Out by Day."
- **Purpose**: Practical magical aid, not description of the hereafter.
- **Central**: Judgment of the Dead / Negative Confession (spell 125) — heart weighed against Ma'at's feather before 42 assessor gods. Spells for transformation, gate-passing, ba-corpse reunion.
- **Hornung warns**: "Ongoing attempts to compare it with the Tibetan Book of the Dead fail because of the entirely different objectives of the two compositions" (lines 1139-1141).

### 4. Amduat — "What Is in the Duat" (lines 1450-1963)
- **First systematic description of the netherworld**. 12 hours of night, three registers. Solar barque in middle register.
- **The 12 Hours**:

| Hour | Name/Region | Key Event |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Interstitial realm | Entry; gate "Swallower of All" |
| 2-3 | Realm of abundance | Waters of Wernes; land assigned to blessed dead |
| 4 | Desert of Rosetau | "Land of Sokar" — snake-filled; barque towed |
| 5 | The West | Grave of Osiris; scarab emerging; Lake of Fire |
| **6** | **Deepest point** | **Union of ba and corpse — Re and Osiris unite** |
| 7 | Apophis defeated | Sun shines anew; serpent bound |
| 8 | Five crypts | Supplying clothes; ba-souls; animal cries |
| 9 | Court of law | Goddesses care for Osiris |
| 10 | The drowned | Saved even without proper burial |
| 11 | Preparations | Last Judgment; "who burns millions" |
| **12** | **Rebirth** | Enter serpent tail old, emerge mouth newborn |

### 5. Book of Gates (lines 2145-2587)
- 12 hours, each ending with a **gate**. Solar barque: only Sia (perception) + Heka (magic) with sun god.
- **Judgment Hall of Osiris** at 5th gate before 6th hour.
- **Distinguishes blessed from damned** — Lake of Fire for the wicked, provisioning for the good.
- **Four races** (Hour 5): Egyptians, Asiatics, Nubians, Libyans — all assured existence.

### 6. Book of Caverns (lines 2774-3101)
- 6 sections, 5 registers. No solar barque — sun disk instead.
- **"Caverns"** = beings enclosed in ovals/sarcophagi.
- **Emphasis**: Punishment of enemies. Osiris more prominent. Unity of Re and Osiris.

### 7. Book of the Earth (lines 3102-3437)
- No hour division. Aker (double sphinx) central. Creation of sun disk.

### 8. Books of the Sky (lines 3539-4288)
- After Amarna Period. Nightly journey transposed into body of sky goddess Nut.
- **Book of Nut**: Pictorial description; decans; stars born after 70 days invisible.
- **Book of the Day**: Diurnal journey; falcon-headed sun.
- **Book of the Night**: Nocturnal; 12 sections with gates; king tows barque.

## Key Egyptian Afterlife Concepts

| Concept | Nature | Role |
|---|---|---|
| **Ba** | Human-headed bird; soul-manifestation | Travels; rejoins corpse nightly |
| **Ka** | Life force | Sustains the deceased |
| **Akh** | Transfigured spirit | The blessed, effective dead |
| **Duat** | Netherworld | 12-hour nocturnal realm |
| **Maat** | Truth, cosmic order | Weighed against the heart |
| **Heart** | Seat of conscience | Testifies at judgment |
| **Field of Reeds** | Paradise | For the justified |
| **Lake of Fire** | Punishment | For the damned |
| **Place of Annihilation** | Second death | Complete destruction of the soul |

## Comparative Parallels

### To Steiner
- **Hour 6 (Amduat)** — union of Re and Osiris at midnight: the mid-point of Steiner's kamaloca where the soul confronts its essence
- **Apophis battle (Hour 7)** — the Guardian of the Threshold / encounter with one's shadow
- **Hour 12 — rebirth through serpent "World-encircler"** — entering tail old, emerging mouth newborn: the traversal of spheres before new incarnation
- **Gate guardians demanding names** — Steiner's soul being asked to recognize itself

### To Tibetan Book of the Dead
- 12 hours of night ↔ 3 intermediate bardos
- Gate guardians ↔ peaceful/wrathful deities (both require recognition)
- Apophis ↔ Yama / terrifying visions
- Judgment Hall ↔ Mirror of past actions
- Field of Reeds ↔ pure buddha fields
- Place of Annihilation ↔ worst hells
- **Key difference**: Egyptian = descriptive fixed geography; Tibetan = instructional mind-created visions

### To Tantraloka
- 12 hours = 12 *kalās* of the moon / 12 gate-deities
- Re-Osiris union at midnight = Śiva-Śakti union in central channel
- Solar barque through Nut's body = kuṇḍalinī through the cakras
- Place of Annihilation = *saṃhāra* (dissolution)

### To Daimon Path
- "Swallower of All" gate = the Threshold — contains everything the soul has been
- Sia (perception) + Hu (utterance) as crew of barque = the soul's cognitive and will faculties
- Four races = fourfold human constitution (physical, etheric, astral, ego)
- Maat appearing twice = the truth confronting the soul at the threshold
