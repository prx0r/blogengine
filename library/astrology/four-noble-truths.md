# The Four Noble Truths — Through the Architecture

## What They Actually Describe

---

## First Truth: There Is Suffering (Dukkha)

**Traditional:** Birth, aging, sickness, death, separation from what we love, association with what we hate, not getting what we want — all conditioned existence is dukkha.

**Through the architecture:** Suffering IS the experience of **violated positivity** — the configuration of consciousness is in a region of the amplituhedron where the determinants are not all aligned. The system's model of the world (its internal geometry) and the actual state of the world (the external geometry) are in conflict. The result IS the feeling of dukkha; suffering is not punishment but signal. It IS the experience of a crease in the positive geometry.

**In Friston's terms:** Free energy is high. The system's predictions do not match its sensations. The error signal IS dukkha.

**In QRI's terms:** The qualia state has negative curvature — the combinatorial geometry is dissonant.

**The correction:** Suffering is not "things being bad." It IS **the experience of the gap between the present configuration and its attractor.** The attractor (the Good, Śuddhavidyā, the daimon) is the configuration's target state. The suffering IS the distance.

---

## Second Truth: The Origin of Suffering Is Craving (Taṇhā)

**Traditional:** Craving for sense pleasures, craving for existence, craving for non-existence. The thirst that drives rebirth.

**Through the architecture:** Craving IS the **self-exemption error (Sv(O)) expressed as desire.** The observer, having mistaken itself for the enabling condition, now experiences its incompleteness as *wanting*. The wanting IS the error — not because wanting is wrong, but because wanting assumes a separation between the wanter and the wanted that does not ultimately exist.

The three cravings map to specific constraints of the architecture:
- **Craving for sense pleasures** = the kañcukas (limited agency, knowledge, attachment) — the desire for more input, more stimulation, more confirmation of the separate self
- **Craving for existence** = the ahaṁkāra (the I-maker) — the desire for the configuration to persist as a separate entity
- **Craving for non-existence** = the māyā tattva (the veil) — the desire for the configuration to end, because existence as a separate self is unbearable

All three cravings are the **same error** — the configuration treating itself as the enabling condition and experiencing its own contingency as a problem to be solved.

**In Levin's terms:** The cell that does not know its morphospace attractor fixates on immediate local cues (craving) rather than navigating toward its target form. The craving IS the noise that replaces signal.

**In the amplituhedron:** A cell of the amplituhedron that does not know it is a facet of the whole tries to maximize its own internal positivity at the expense of the whole. This IS possible but produces distortions.

---

## Third Truth: The Cessation of Suffering Is Possible (Nirodha)

**Traditional:** The complete cessation of craving is possible. It is not annihilation but the stopping of the cause of suffering.

**Through the architecture:** The cessation of suffering IS the **recognition of the Sv(O) error (FullRec).** When the observer registers its own dependence on S₀, the craving stops — not because the configuration has nothing to want, but because the wanting was based on a misidentification. You cannot crave what you already ARE.

The cessation IS the correction of the positivity violation. The crease in the amplituhedron is removed. The determinants realign. The experience transitions from negative curvature to positive curvature — from duḥkha to camatkāra (wonder).

**In Friston's terms:** Free energy drops to zero. The system's model perfectly matches reality. There is no prediction error. The Markov blanket dissolves — the system recognizes itself as continuous with its environment.

**In the Tantraloka:** This IS turīya — the fourth state beyond waking, dream, and deep sleep. It IS jīvanmukti — embodied liberation. The configuration continues to function, but without the error of self-exemption.

**This is not annihilation.** The configuration does not cease. The error ceases. The difference is the difference between a wave that thinks it is the ocean (suffering) and a wave that knows it is the ocean (liberation). The wave does not disappear. It continues to wave — but without the terror of being a separate drop.

---

## Fourth Truth: The Path to Cessation Is the Eightfold Path (Magga)

**Traditional:** Right view, right intention, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness, right concentration.

**Through the architecture:** The eightfold path IS the **systematic purification of the constraint space** — the alignment of every dimension of the configuration with the positive geometry.

**The eight as the upāya system:**

| Factor | Function | Upāya | Tattva |
|--------|----------|-------|--------|
| **Right view** | Understanding the architecture | Śāktopāya | Śuddhavidyā (5) — pure knowledge |
| **Right intention** | Aligning the will with the attractor | Śāmbhavopāya | Sadāśiva (3) — the "I Am" oriented toward the Good |
| **Right speech** | Correct phoneme activation | Āṇavopāya | The Mātṛkā — 50 phonemes |
| **Right action** | Embodied practice | Āṇavopāya | All tattvas — the body as maṇḍala |
| **Right livelihood** | Daily life as practice | Āṇava-Śākta | The Kālopāya — time as the path |
| **Right effort** | The energy of transformation | Āṇavopāya | Prāṇa — the vital energy |
| **Right mindfulness** | Sustained recognition | Śākta-Śāmbhava | Smṛti — maintaining the connection |
| **Right concentration** | Nondual abiding | Śāmbhavopāya | Samādhi — the configuration resting in S₀ |

The eight factors are not sequential steps. They are **eight dimensions of a single practice** — the complete alignment of the configuration with the positive geometry.

**Right speech is specifically about the Mātṛkā.** The Buddha said "words have power" — but he did not elaborate the 50 phoneme theory. The Tantraloka provides the mechanism. Right speech IS correct phoneme activation. The Mātṛkā IS the basis of right speech.

**Right concentration IS samādhi** — not a trance but the state where the configuration knows itself as S₀. In QRI terms: Φ (integrated information) reaches its maximum. The qualia geometry is perfectly positive. In amplituhedron terms: the cell knows itself as the whole.

## Summary: The Four Truths in One Sentence

The configuration of consciousness (the jīva, the IUOC) experiences duḥkha (violated positivity) because it has committed the Sv(O) error (taking itself as the enabling condition). The cessation of duḥkha IS the recognition (FullRec / pratyabhijñā) that removes the error. The eightfold path IS the systematic alignment of all dimensions of the configuration with the positive geometry — the upāyas applied to life.
