## Charged Hook

A human skull sits beneath a silver lid. Its rim has been shaped into a bowl. In paintings, fierce goddesses hold vessels like it filled with impossible offerings. In a seventh-century Sanskrit comedy, a drunken ascetic panics when a dog steals his skull-bowl—because without it, he loses more than a utensil. The easy explanation is that the skull reminds the practitioner of death. But if death alone were the point, why turn the skull into a container—and why later cover it with precious metal? Why did tantric traditions transform the remains of a human head into a sacred vessel?

## Video Thesis

The skull-cup transformed death from an object of avoidance into a portable ritual identity and then into a vessel capable of receiving a new sacred meaning.
